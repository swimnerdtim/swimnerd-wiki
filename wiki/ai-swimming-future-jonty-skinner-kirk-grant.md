# AI in Swimming: The Future of High Performance

**Source:** Podcast with Jonty Skinner & Kirk Grant (USA Swimming)  
**Date:** Early January 2025  
**Topic:** Artificial Intelligence implementation in competitive swimming

---

## Overview

Jonty Skinner (world record holder, legendary coach) and Kirk Grant (USA Swimming High Performance Specialist) discuss how AI will transform swimming over the next 5-10 years. This conversation emerged from discussions about hiring USA Swimming's new CEO and National Team Director - emphasizing the need for forward-thinking leadership that understands AI's role in the sport's future.

**Key insight:** "AI is really stupid until you fill it with information. The key to success is HOW we upload that information so it becomes smart and advises us effectively."

---

## The 8 Spokes of AI in Swimming

Jonty's framework identifies 8 critical areas where AI will impact high performance:

### 1. Training

**Current state (Jan 2025):**
- Coaches using AI to compile/organize workouts
- Beginning to identify training themes and gaps

**Future vision:**
- **Catalog everything:** Every Michael Phelps workout, every Bob Bowman session, all great training programs
- **Automated uploading:** Camera scans paper workout → instantly cataloged
- **Pattern recognition:** "Show me what it took to develop Phelps over 10-12 years"
- **Style diversity:** Capture high-volume (Bob Bowman), low-volume (Cameron McEvoy), and everything between

**Example use case:**
"What did Michael Phelps do to improve his weak stroke? Show me that year's progression."

### 2. Biomechanics

**Best current product:** Swim Better (Australia) - but information overload is an issue

**AI solution:**
- AI filter sits over biomechanics data
- Simplifies complex outputs: "This is exactly what you're looking at, here's where we need to go"
- **Goggles with pattern recognition:** AI watches stroke pattern repeatedly, identifies deviations

**Integration with anthropometry:**
Should be able to say: "Based on Kate Douglass's limb lengths, hand size, and current biomechanics, she SHOULD be producing a 50.5... but we're only getting 51.9. What's wrong?"

### 3. Coaching

**Key concept:** The best coaches have refined **decision-making trees** throughout their careers.

**AI advantage:**
- Accelerates that decision tree refinement
- Gives you leaps ahead of trial-and-error
- Provides specific indications faster than 30 years of coaching experience

**NOT a replacement - a tool to make better decisions faster.**

### 4. Anthropometry

**What to catalog:**
- Limb lengths
- Hand/feet sizes
- Girths (upper/lower body ratios)
- Torso shape
- Body type (ectomorph, endomorph, mesomorph)

**Why it matters:**
Combined with biomechanics data, should predict optimal performance potential and identify gaps.

**Example:** Kate Douglass (Gretchen Walsh mentioned in transcript) - height, size, limb ratios suggest she should "destroy world records in 50/100 free" with correct biomechanical adjustments.

### 5. Recovery

**Current monitoring:**
- Mood state questionnaires
- Blood testing
- Cortisol (saliva) testing
- Wearables (Whoop, etc.)

**Elite coach intuition vs AI:**
- **Eddie Reese example:** Could look at someone's face and know "Josh is gonna be a problem today"
- **Not every coach has that intuition**
- **AI can democratize it:** Catalog mood, fatigue markers, readiness indicators

**Challenge:** Still "a long way away" from fully documenting psychological/recovery variables - this is a weak area currently.

### 6. Athlete Physicality/Physiology

**What to measure:**
- Natural buoyancy/balance in water
- Muscle fiber type (fast-twitch aerobic, fast-twitch glycolytic, slow-twitch)
- Trainability indicators

**Ideal athlete profile:**
"Fast-twitch aerobic fiber athletes - great capacity to do anything, depends on durability"

**Current limitation:** Can't do muscle biopsies routinely, so much is anecdotal based on training response and recovery patterns.

### 7. Racing/Race Analysis

**Current state:**
USA Swimming working with USOPC on automated race analysis system (other countries already using)

**Future vision:**
"One camera at top of pool brings out EVERYTHING we want to know"

**Olympics already doing it:** Velocities, relative positions, stroke data visible on broadcast

**Post-race application:**
After prelims, get printout: "We're on track. Work on this one facet of the turn tonight."

### 8. Athlete Mindset

**Jonty's ranking:** #1 most important element

**Goal:** Take an athlete without a great mindset and develop a "Michael Phelps mindset" - steps on blocks never thinking they'll lose.

**Challenge:** Psychology is the furthest behind in AI integration currently.

**Kirk's take:** "I want people that hate to lose. How do we help coaches create an environment where MORE people on the team hate to lose?"

## Elite Coach vs AI: Who Does What?

### What Elite Coaches Already Do Well

**Qualitative assessment:**
- Reading mood/energy on deck
- Understanding when to push vs back off
- Creating competitive environments
- Managing team dynamics

### What AI Does Better

**Quantitative tracking:**
- Longitudinal athlete profiles (age 8 → professional)
- Pattern recognition across thousands of workouts
- Identifying what worked for similar athletes
- Faster, more accurate decision-making

### The Future Model (2030+)

**Not "coach vs AI" but "athlete-coach-AI collaboration":**

Jonty's vision:
> "The athlete has as much managerial control as the coach at the elite level. They might have 10-12 great athletes, some competitive in same races, all managed through an AI platform."

**Example workflow:**
- Coach: "Jim, go through all the AI readouts. Check our performance markers. Come to tomorrow's meeting and tell me where we're missing targets."
- Athlete does the analytical work
- Coach oversees, adds experience-based insights
- Training adjusted collaboratively

**Jason Lezak / Cameron McEvoy examples:** Athletes who succeeded largely on their own - AI makes this model scalable.

### The "I Control Everything" Coach

**Jonty's prediction:**
> "I don't see 'my way or the highway' coaches being successful at the elite level in 2030."

## The Decentralized Challenge (USA Swimming)

**Kirk's reality:**
> "We have pockets of elite athletes everywhere. Diverse training. As Olympic head coach, you're conducting a symphony for a concert you've never rehearsed."

**AI solution:**
- **Better information** for head coach
- **Accurate data** makes conversations more productive (less "I think..." more "here's what we know")
- **Continuity across transitions:**
  - Junior national team → national team
  - National team → professional
  - College transfers (transfer portal era)

"What are we keeping constant to help them swim fast consistently?"

## Age Group Implications

### The Critical Window

**Doug Leff (soccer) insight:**
> "If kids haven't learned to see the field right by age 10-11-12, they'll never see it."

**Jonty's swimming translation:**
> "If we put kids in compromised biomechanical patterns by age 12-14, we'll never get them to 100% of organic potential. That potential is LOST."

**Implication:** Everything learned at elite level MUST filter down to age 7-10 development.

### Parent Information Overload

**Brett Hawke's observation (coaching club in SoCal):**
- Parents have same AI access as coaches
- Taking in massive amounts of information
- Sometimes applying it inappropriately for child's age
- Making decisions based on incomplete understanding

**The balance:** Process over outcomes - not just "did they go faster?"

Kirk's teaching example: "All I get from parents is 'did they go faster?'" Need better metrics for progress.

## Key Principles for 2030

### 1. Athlete Ownership
Athletes must understand everything about the sport. Two-way communication. Educated athletes manage their own development (with coach guidance).

### 2. Decision-Making Speed
AI accelerates good decision-making. Small good decisions consistently = behavior change = performance gains.

### 3. Democratization of Expertise
Not every coach has Eddie Reese's intuition - AI makes elite-level insights accessible to all coaches.

### 4. Individual + Team Optimization
Can't sacrifice team dynamics for individual performance (or vice versa). Both matter.

### 5. Grassroots Matters Most
Elite insights MUST filter down to age 7-10 development or we lose the next generation.

### 6. Information Sharing
Social media, cameras, documentation - "Look at what we're doing" is GOOD for the sport. Share knowledge.
