# Characteristics of Champions
**Source:** Bob Bowman coaching clinic

## Core Philosophy

> "What makes champions great is what's up here and what's in here" (mind and heart)

Physical attributes matter at elite levels, but **mental and emotional characteristics separate champions from competitors.**

---

## The Seven Characteristics

### 1. Clear Plan for Success
Champions don't rely on talent alone - they have **specific, detailed plans** for achieving goals.

**Key insight:** Success is not random. It's engineered through planning.

### 2. Welcome Challenges as Growth Opportunities
Champions embrace failure as part of the process. What we see on TV is the end product of **years of ups and downs**.

**Bowman's perspective:** "A lot of becoming a champion is about failure. The process is very long and difficult."

### 3. Normal Performance in Abnormal Environments
Champions produce **predictable performances in unpredictable environments** (Olympics, World Championships, high-pressure meets).

**Example:** Olympics are "sporting events arranged around a big media event" - champions handle the circus.

### 4. Daily Rehearsal of Success
Champions rehearse success **mentally, physically, and emotionally every day.**

**Not just physical:** Visualization, emotional regulation, mental preparation are daily habits.

### 5. Value Process Over Outcome
> "The process is more important than the outcome because that's what is controllable."

**Nick Saban quote Bowman uses:** "Don't look at the scoreboard, play the next play."

**Application to swimming:** Don't worry about final place - focus on executing the first turn, the breakout, the finish.

### 6. Dream-Driven Passion
Champions start with a **dream** - an emotional vision that overcomes obstacles and carries them through tough times.

**Bowman's insight:** "Nobody's going to get out of bed in the morning to finish their 200 freestyle in 5:25. But somebody might get out of bed to be the most decorated Olympian of all time."

### 7. Demonstrated Ability Builds Confidence
> "Confidence comes from demonstrated ability. You can't dream it up."

**How it works:**
- Do something successfully in practice
- Next time you believe you can do it again
- Repeat thousands of times
- Confidence becomes automatic

## Michael Phelps' Dream Evolution

Bob Bowman tracked how Phelps' dream evolved over time:

### Age 12 (2000)
**Dream:** "I want to swim in the Olympics"

**Bowman's response:** "That's a good goal, but you better do a lot of butterfly if you're going to do that."

### Age 15 (2003)
**Dream:** "I want to change the sport of swimming. I want to see swimming on ESPN."

**Result:** Swimming is now regularly featured on SportsCenter.

### Age 21 (2006) - Two years before Beijing
**The defining moment:**

Meeting in Bowman's office with Phelps, his mother, and agent to discuss Beijing Olympics approach.

**Options presented:**
1. **Go big:** Pursue something no one's done before (8 golds) - huge expectations, huge pressure
2. **Play it safe:** "Happy to go and win some medals" - less pressure, less reward

**Phelps' response:** "I want to be the best ever."

**Bowman:** "I've never believed anything more in my whole life than when he said those words. It was like a bomb went off in my head about what I needed to do."

**Bowman's commitment:** "Are you in?" / "I'm already there."

## The Dream as Creative Catalyst

**The dream ignites the creative process** - once established, everything works toward it.

**Mental picture importance:**
- The dream establishes the forest
- Then you look for the trees
- Shows you direction
- Gets you out of bed on cold mornings

**Reality check:** "Nobody can be the best ever if they don't make the right decisions on a daily basis."

---

## Goal Setting Process

### The Hierarchy

**1. Long-term (8-10 years)**
- Forest-level vision
- Defines direction

**2. Olympic cycle (4 years)**
- Benchmark goals
- Thematic focus areas

**3. Annual (1 year)**
- Specific, plannable
- Daily training can be mapped

**4. Immediate (right now)** ← **MOST IMPORTANT**
- "Where the rubber meets the road"
- **The cookie in your hand decision**

### The Cookie Example

**Everyone agrees:** Eating healthy is good (longer life, better performance, etc.)

**Everyone can:** Find a plan, set a timeline

**The key decision:** What do you do when the cookie's in your hand?

> "That's where progress is made."

**Application to swimming:** The immediate goal - what are you going to do RIGHT NOW in this set, this lap, this turn - determines if you achieve the dream.

## Michael Phelps at Age 13: Goal-Setting Mastery

Bowman took 13-year-old Phelps through goal-setting exercise:
- Listed 3 events
- Target times to the **hundredth of a second**
- 3 things needed to achieve each goal
- Posted on refrigerator (saw it daily)

### Results at Year-End Meet:

| Event | Goal Time | Actual Time | Difference |
|-------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| 200 Fly | 2:04.68 | 2:04.68 | **0.00** |
| 1500 Free | 16:00.00 | 16:00.88 | +0.88 |
| 400 IM | 4:31.86 | 4:31.81 | -0.05 |

**Bowman's takeaway:** "If you have a very good eye for goal setting and you know how to turn that into daily training, you can predict your performance."

## Planning Under Adversity

### Broken Hand Story (December 2007)

**Context:** 9 months before Beijing Olympics, after historic World Championships (7 golds, 6 world records)

**Training:** Going perfectly - Michael getting better every day

**Crisis:** Michael broke his hand slipping on ice

**Initial reaction (both):** "I've just given away 3 gold medals"

**Bowman's response:** "Let's look at the facts. What CAN we do?"

**Doctor option 1:** Cast for 6 weeks  
**Bowman:** "That doesn't work for me. What's plan B?"

**Doctor option 2:** Surgery with pin, 10 days for stitches to heal  
**Bowman:** "We're done. Next morning he was in surgery."

**10-day solution:**
- 3 hours/day on stationary bike
- Day 7: Plastic bag over hand, kicking in pool
- Day 10: Back to training

**Lesson:** "This perfect plan that's rolling along comes off the tracks. How are we going to deal with this problem and how quickly?"

## Training Philosophy

### Worst Conditions → Best Performance

> "We want to teach swimmers to get the best possible performance in the worst possible conditions."

**Why:** Olympics = worst conditions (bad rooms, 8 people/suite, different routines, media circus)

**Training techniques:**
- Stepped on Phelps' goggles in practice → swam with goggles full of water
- Came back in Beijing 200 fly: goggles filled with water, **he just counted his strokes**

### Daily Confidence Building

**Every practice = opportunity to:**
- Build confidence
- See yourself as the person you want to be
- Rehearse success

**Key:** Visualization isn't separate from training - it's integrated into daily practice.

## Process vs. Outcome

**Coaching philosophy in one sentence:** "The process is more important than the outcome."

**Why:**
- Process is controllable
- Outcomes depend on others
- Process builds skill
- Outcomes are temporary

**Nick Saban's wisdom:** "Don't look at the scoreboard, play the next play."

**In swimming:** Don't worry about final place - execute the turn, the breakout, the finish.

---

## Repetition as the Foundation

Champions need **things to work on** - they all have weaknesses.

### Allison Schmidt Example

**Problem:** Not physically strong

**Solution:** Simple goal - pull-ups
- Started: 0 pull-ups
- Ended: 3 sets of 8-9

**Result:** 2012 London Olympics - 5 medals (3 gold) vs. 1 bronze in 2008

**Bowman:** "How did Allison improve so much? Very simple. She went from zero pull-ups to 3 sets of 8."

**Lesson:** Identify the weakness. Set clear goal. Work it consistently.

## Mental Discipline

### GPS Analogy

> "Think of me as a GPS. Swimmers drive the car. I'm that annoying thing on the dashboard saying 'turn right.'"

**When swimmers go off course:** "Recalibrating... recalibrating..."

**The job:** Keep bringing them back to the goal.

## Application for Coaches

1. **Help athletes define their dream** - not just times, but emotional vision
2. **Build goal-setting skills** - from 10-year vision down to "what are you doing right now"
3. **Focus on process** - outcomes will follow
4. **Create adversity in training** - champions perform under pressure
5. **Daily confidence building** - demonstrate ability, build belief
6. **Value the immediate goal** - the cookie in your hand moment

**Remember:** Physical talent gets you to the table. Mental and emotional characteristics make you a champion.
