# Bob Bowman's Coaching Essentials

**Source:** 2026 Pacific Coaches Clinic  
**Context:** Core philosophy lessons learned over 30+ years coaching Michael Phelps, Leon Marchand, Summer McIntosh

## Essential #1: Take Yourself Out of the Equation

### The Mindset Shift
**Wrong approach (early-career Bowman):**
- "I'm going to make this happen"
- "I'm going to get you into the top 10"
- Hammer mentality: everything looks like a nail

**Right approach (mature coaching):**
- "What is best for this athlete?"
- "What is best for their long-term development?"
- "What is best for their well-being?"

### The Emily Get Story (Cautionary Tale)

**Athlete:** Emily Get (Baltimore, ~13-14 years old, went to USC)  
**Situation:** Incredibly tall, talented, but passive and unaware (thought AAA time was her PR when it was actually Junior National standard)

**Bowman's approach:**
- Told her: "I'm going to get you into the top 10 in the world this year"
- Didn't say a kind word for 6 months
- She cried every day
- Result: Won nationals, 59-flat/100 fly

**The problem:**
- ✅ Short-term success
- ❌ Not sustainable for multiple athletes
- ❌ Not sustainable long-term
- ❌ Killed Bowman emotionally
- ❌ **She didn't learn to want it herself**

**The lesson:** "You can't be their motivation. You're their teacher. They have to want it."

### Servant Leadership in Practice
- You create the environment
- They do the work
- You can't make them want it
- You guide, show how cool things are
- People work together to achieve (not just you driving)

## Essential #2: Learn to Listen and Observe, Then Communicate

### The Eye Test
**Bowman's #1 diagnostic tool:** Look at their eyes
- **Bright and clear eyes** = ready to go, fully present
- **Dull or tired eyes** = not fully ready

**Application:**
- Walk-in diagnostic: "I can look at Michael's face when he walked in and tell you exactly how practice is going to go"
- Meet diagnostic: "First thing I look for in taper is when all their eyes are bright and clear"
- **If eyes never get bright during taper → not ready to race**

### What to Observe
- How they choose lanes
- Who they want to be around
- Why they go second instead of first
- All these are cues to coach them better

## The Shane Casas Story: Negotiated Commitment

### The Setup
**Bowman's assumption:** "I was 100% sure Shane and I would never work together because I was going to kick him off the team the first time he missed practice, which was like every day."

**Traditional Bowman rule:** You show up. Period. "We don't take attendance. Everybody shows up. If someone's not there, we know they're sick."

### The Meeting
**Bowman's strategy:** Do the exact opposite of what Shane expected

**Bowman's opening:** "Shane, I can deal with anything, but we need to agree on what you're going to commit to, and then you have to do it."

**Initial proposal:** 
- 5 practices/week: Mon-Fri afternoons
- Take off Saturdays
- Take off all mornings
- All high-risk commitment areas off the table

**Shane's response:** "Well, that's not nearly enough."

**Shane's counter-offer:**
- Monday-Friday afternoons
- Wednesday morning off
- Saturday mornings
- Total: **8 practices/week**

**The agreement:** "If you don't do it, you got to go."

### The Result
- Shane committed to 8x/week (from his own mouth, not Bowman's demand)
- "When you're super talented and you come 8 times, even if you don't do an amazing job every practice and you do this week in week out, you start to do these things at meets"
- Success at meets → more motivation → more buy-in

### The Principle
**"It was from him. It wasn't from me."**
- Learn your individual athletes
- Learn what makes them tick
- Provide the atmosphere where they can shine
- **You can't do it for them. They have to do it.**

## Key Takeaways for Coaches

1. **Servant leadership** > Dictator leadership
2. **Long-term development** > Short-term results
3. **Watch their eyes** before you coach anything else
4. **Individualize everything** - one size does NOT fit all
5. **Negotiate commitment** when needed (Shane model)
6. **Create environment** where athletes choose excellence
7. **You're not the driver** - you're the teacher
