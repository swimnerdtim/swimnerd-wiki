# Bowman's Motivation Tactics: Staying Passionate Over the Long Haul
**Source:** *The Golden Rules* by Bob Bowman (2016), Chapter 7

## The Problem: Swimming Is a Grind

> "Swimming isn't like baseball or golf, where you're out in the sunlight and checking out the views. No, with swimming you're wet, underwater, and using lots of raw muscle power. And any improvement you make comes in milliseconds, not minutes."

**The reality:**
- Twice-daily practices, year-round
- 7am before school, 3 hours after classes
- Staring at black lane line for hours, hundreds of times
- Wet, cold, exhausting
- Millisecond improvements (not minutes)
- Solo sport disguised as team sport
- Can't see competition during race (underwater)

**Analogy:** "Cubicle Village developing PowerPoint presentations or monthly sales forecasts" - same slog, different setting.

**The question coaches must answer:** How do you keep passion burning when work is repetitive, exhausting, and improvements are microscopic?

## Allison Schmitt's Secret: Fake It Till You Make It

**Schmitty's morning persona:**
- Shows up to 7am practice with cheerfulness of TV weatherperson
- Upbeat, talkative, smiling
- Sun barely up, chill in air, 7,000 meters awaiting

**vs. Michael Phelps' morning persona:**
- Half-opened eyes, scowl
- "Approach with caution"
- Bowman learned to save "Good morning" until after warm-up

**Reporter asked Allison:** Secret for combating bad days?

**Schmitty's answer:**
> "Yes, there are days when you don't want to go to practice, but there are days when most people don't want to go to work. I get by with a **fake-it-till-you-make-it attitude**. Eventually, if you tell yourself enough that it will be fun getting in the water, you'll trick yourself into being happy even when you don't want to."

**Bowman's validation:**
> "Some days you just have to work at going to work, especially if you're ultimately pursuing bigger prize. Regardless of mood, you must stay committed to Game Plan and daily goals."

**Does it always work?** Not at first. But it ALWAYS does eventually.

## Bowman's Personal Tactics

### Tactic 1: Countdown to Goal

**iPhone app:** Days until Rio

**Started:** September 2013 at 1,068 days  
**Ticks down:** Like Times Square New Year's Eve countdown

**On days needing kick:**
> "Pretty soon zero will be here. Bob, let's get to work. Show these swimmers you're as motivated as they are."

**Why it works:**
- Creates urgency
- Reminds of finite timeline
- Turns abstract goal into concrete countdown

**Allison Schmitt on Bowman's energy:**
> "You can use other people's energies to help goose you up or get you through rough spot. This team is very good about pushing one another. If they see someone that's down, they're going to help that person figure it out."

**Application:** Display your countdown publicly. Let others feed off your energy.

---

### Tactic 2: Visual Reminders

**Tom Luchsinger ("Mr. Perfect"):**
- Rendering of 5 Olympic rings hanging from doorway in home
- Every morning before workout: gets look at rings
- Instant reminder of why he's swimming 12,000 meters/day

**Michael Phelps (various times):**
- Wrote down vision on scrap of paper, kept in wallet
- Posted goals on refrigerator (saw daily)
- At age 13: times to hundredth of second for 3 events + 3 things needed to achieve each

**Bowman's recommendation:**
> "I recommend you codify your vision. Write a few words about it on scrap of paper and tuck it in your wallet or desk."

**Why it works:**
- Makes abstract vision concrete
- Daily visual reinforcement
- No effort required (just see it)

---

### Tactic 3: Theme Events to Break Monotony

**Friends Friday:**
- Swimmers bring buddy to work out
- Michael invited Ravens players
- When stars showed up, **everyone at pool got pumped**

**Half-Way to Rio Roast:**
- Celebrated how far they'd come toward Olympic vision
- Maryland pit beef dinner
- Highlighted progress, not just end goal

**Storytelling sessions (young swimmers):**
- 30 minutes before weekend practice
- Kids share something learned at school that week
- Let teammates get to know each other better

**Purpose:** "Break up routine while letting kids get to know their teammates a little better."

## John Harbaugh's Playbook: NFL Lessons for Swimming

**John Harbaugh (Baltimore Ravens coach):**
- Took over Ravens 2008
- Won Super Bowl 2012 (same year Michael won Olympic king title)
- **Only NFL coach to win playoff games in 6 of first 7 seasons**

**His secret:** Keeping players excited about coming to work every day.

**NFL season:**
- 6 weeks training camp
- 16 weeks regular season
- Playoff weeks
- Bruising to body AND mind
- "Kid's game" morphs into physical/emotional drudgery

### Harbaugh's Tactics (Applied by Bowman)

**1. Guest speakers from business/entertainment:**
- Sessions may have nothing to do with football
- Engage players, get them thinking beyond 100-yard field

**2. Players present:**
- Training camp 2012: Marshal Yanda addressed squad
- Summer heat threatened to sap drive
- Yanda: "We need to **embrace the grind** and not be defeated by it"
- Harbaugh loved message → gave all players "Embrace the Grind" T-shirts

**3. Field trips:**
- Training camp 2013: canceled practice one day
- Took rookies to Gettysburg, Pennsylvania battlefields
- History lesson rather than training session
- "Productive just the same"

**Results:** 2008-2014, Harbaugh's teams had **2nd most victories among NFL teams**

**Marshal Yanda post-Super Bowl:**
> "A Super Bowl is great goal, but it can't be why you play this game. It has to be for your love of the game and for the guy lined up next to you. In the end, it's about the competitiveness, the work you put in, and because you love it."

**Bowman's application:**
> "The more you can sprinkle motivational practices into regular vision quest, the easier it will be to keep passion for vision charged up."

## Tactic 4: Immediate Feedback (Not Meetings)

### The Meeting Problem

**USA Swimming directive (2013 World Championships in Barcelona):**
- Hold daily team meeting
- Cover: who's swimming, bus times, etc.
- Information that could be emailed/texted

**After first meeting:**
- Guys barely listened
- Spent time checking iPhones
- Didn't need to hear to-do list

**Bowman's solution:**
- Met every day at 3:45pm as directed
- **Finished by 3:48pm** (to rousing cheer from swimmers)

**Results:** American men won 15 medals, total well ahead of any other country.

### The Better Way: In-the-Trenches Feedback

**Bowman's approach:**
- Every hour of practice, walking pool deck giving feedback
- Shows swimmers he cares about their effort
- On-the-spot correction/reinforcement

**Why it works:**
> "The best way to get somebody to improve or to reinforce good behavior or to extinguish bad behavior is in the trenches. If I can convey my interest in their effort at moment's notice, and not at mandatory meeting, they will stay sharp (and so will I)."

**Application:** 
- Limit scheduled meetings (they zap motivation)
- Give immediate feedback where work happens
- Show interest in the moment, not later at scheduled review

## Tactic 5: Find Secondary Passion

### Bowman's Thoroughbreds

**Year:** 1996, working with coach Paul Bergen

**Discovery:** Paul owned thoroughbreds

**Bowman's immersion:**
- Read every book on horse training/breeding
- Watched every video
- Eventually bought horses of own

**Why horses?**
- Rhythm of horse's training mimicked swimming
- Shared early-rising lifestyle
- Smell of hay/barn reminded him of grandparents' South Carolina farm

**Most important:**
> "What the horses did was make me a better swim coach. They provided diversion from my No. 1 passion; I wasn't always thinking about swimming, or overthinking about swimming. They gave me balance."

### Michael's Outlets

**Away from pool:**
- Golf
- Video games
- Card games
- Sitting in chair, eyes staring ahead, thinking

**Debbie Phelps:**
> "He can find joy by simply sitting in a chair, eyes staring ahead, thinking. He's actually rather deep."

**Bowman's caution:**
> "Don't let vision become so important that you neglect other interests or fail to develop new ones. These 'secondary' skills can provide much-needed perspective."

**Application:** Distance from passion recharges passion.

## Tactic 6: Know What Motivates People Beyond Their Vision

### Debbie Phelps on Bowman

**At dinner near Meadowbrook:**
> "What I love about Bob is that as he and Michael trained Bob realized how much he encompassed every aspect of a kid's life—from sports to school to physical and emotional well-being. Bob's philosophy was very well-rounded. He didn't try to parent a child, but he also wasn't just the swim coach. He became part of their world. I think that when you're working with people, to gain their respect you have to have that sense of interest. Bob was interested in everyone in his practice."

**Parallel to teaching:**
> "I parallel it to classroom. When I'm teaching 30 kids in classroom, you can't teach them all same way. It has to be individualized."

**Bowman's response:**
> "I was bit embarrassed by Debbie's words, but I also wouldn't disagree with them. People are emotional beings, and part of helping someone achieve vision is understanding what motivates person, what moves person."

### Know Your Swimmers' Interests

**Tom Luchsinger:** History buff (carried JFK biography around pool deck)

**Matt McLean:** Restores old cars

**Jessica Long:** Developing side career as model

**Yannick Agnel:** Curious about everything
- Visited DC museums and historical monuments
- Took online courses
- Read literature, watched films
- **Why he came to Baltimore:**

> "Everybody has his own personality, but something that's really important to me is being balanced in life, and when you're swimming, when you're practicing for 6-8 hours a day, it's really, really hard to find balance. Bob is definitely focused on making you grow up as a man and become more than just a swimmer."

**Bowman's approach:**
> "While I'm not going to have my swimmers over for weekly barbecue, I do look for ways to connect with them and discover what makes them tick. With such insight, I think I can help them grow both as individuals and as athletes—and give them motivation to come to practice for more than just challenging workout."

## Tactic 7: Passion Must Be Managed (Not Overwhelming)

### The 2000 Blowup

**Setting:** June day, Baltimore scorcher (98°, 100% humidity)

**Context:**
- 4pm, hundreds of kids/adults using outdoor pool to cool off
- Loud, kids screaming
- Michael and group using indoor pool for hard practice
- **One month until Michael's first Olympic Trials**

**Michael's behavior:** Messing around with buddies (acting like 15-year-old on hot summer day)

**Bowman's reaction:**
- "Get out this second!"
- Everyone at Meadowbrook could hear
- Launched into Michael: "You have to do what I tell you! You're going to work harder, and you're going to listen to me!"

**Michael:** Started talking back

**Bowman:** "Be quiet! And do what I say!"

**Michael:** Started walking away, shouting "I'm getting out of here!"

**Bowman:** "Oh, no you're not!"

**Next minute:** Cussing at each other

**Finally:** "Get the hell out of here!"

**Aftermath:**
- Michael headed to locker room
- Bowman on pool deck
- Meadowbrook packed with people
- Bowman felt alone

> "Needless to say, this was not one of my finer moments. I'd like to say it was last time I ever yelled at Michael or at any of my swimmers. Safe to say, it wasn't."

### Lessons Learned (Following Bob Baffert)

**Bob Baffert:** Legendary horse trainer, 2015 Triple Crown winner

**Baffert's approach:**
- Doesn't demand daily improvement
- Doesn't employ extreme intensity every day
- "Steely level-headedness built upon experience and insight"
- Demands excellence but doesn't supersede long-term goal

**Too many trainers (and coaches):**
- Never let up on charges
- Extreme intensity each day
- Unsustainable and unproductive

**Bowman's evolution:**
> "As young coach, I used hammer for every nail that popped up, often to detriment of my swimmers. I don't want them to feel that failure—the inevitable lapse that comes with pursuit of goal—will nullify chance to achieve what they want. That's just too much pressure, and it's pressure that will ultimately harm their growth. It depletes, rather than inspires, their passion."

**Allison Schmitt on change:**
> "It's funny. He's not as loud as he was, and because of that I think he's better. He's mellowed, but in a good way."

**Application:** Discover what athlete needs to stay passionate/charged, and what can be done to solve problems, not exacerbate them.

## Tactic 8: Dick Ebersol's Tears (Passion Endures)

### Beijing 2008, Post-8th Gold

**Scene:** Dick Ebersol's office/apartment at NBC Sports headquarters

**Dick Ebersol:**
- NBC Sports president
- Oversaw every Olympics broadcast 1992-2008
- Connection to Olympics dates to late 1960s (ABC Sports researcher)
- Convinced Olympic organizers to move swimming finals to morning (live prime time in U.S.)

**When Michael arrived:** Dick rushed over, bear-hugged him

**Why?** 
- Ecstatic about ratings from Michael's 8-gold run
- But more: Dick had become mentor to Michael (lost son Teddy in 2004 plane crash)

**The video:**
- Dick showed Michael 4-minute Olympic tribute that aired first night
- Beautiful, compelling
- Michael/Bowman hadn't seen it

**Midway through:** Bowman turned to Dick, saw tears dripping from eyes

**Dick:**
> "Every time I see this I cry. And I've probably seen it hundreds of times!"

**Bowman's realization:**
> "Right then I realized how much this man loved what he did. Yes, he was in high-pressure position. Yes, his network had huge dollars at stake. Yes, days and nights during Games were long, tense, and often sleepless ones. And yet for all challenges and pressure, Dick Ebersol still maintained same passion for Olympics that he had when Games originally lured him into TV sports business 40 years earlier."

**Lesson:** Passion often separates high-level achievers from mere dreamers.

## Summary: The Motivation Toolkit

### For Athletes:
1. **Fake it till you make it** (Schmitty's secret)
2. **Visual reminders** (Olympic rings on doorway, goals on fridge)
3. **Countdown displays** (days until goal)
4. **Theme events** (Friends Friday, Half-Way celebrations)
5. **Secondary passions** (horses, golf, history, cars, modeling)
6. **Team energy** (use others' positivity when yours lags)

### For Coaches:
1. **Immediate feedback** (not scheduled meetings)
2. **Know your people** (interests, motivations, what makes them tick)
3. **Theme events** (guest speakers, field trips, presentations)
4. **Manage passion** (don't overwhelm, don't deplete)
5. **Model enthusiasm** (your energy feeds theirs)
6. **Balance intensity** (Baffert approach, not hammer-every-nail)

### Universal Truths:
- **Some days require work to go to work** (commitment to Game Plan)
- **Passion must be sustained** (not just initial excitement)
- **Diversions recharge passion** (distance makes vision clearer)
- **People feed off collective energy** (teams elevate individuals)
- **Process must be satisfying** (not just reward)

## The Bottom Line

**Best schoolteachers when asked why they stay despite bureaucracy/long hours/low pay:**
> "It's for the joy we get out of inspiring young minds. That's what fuels our passion."

**Best sportswriters when asked why they stay despite turmoil/layoffs:**
> "We love covering sports. Every day there's a new story to write. That's what fuels our passion."

**Michael Phelps quote:**
> "You can't put a limit on anything. The more you dream, the farther you get."

**Marshal Yanda (Ravens) post-Super Bowl:**
> "A Super Bowl is great goal, but it can't be why you play this game. It has to be for your love of the game and for the guy lined up next to you."

**Bowman's mission:**
> "In pursuit of your goals and your dream vision, one critical ingredient you'll need is passion—that intense interest or affection that starts to develop once you hit upon something you're drawn to. Passion often separates high-level achievers from mere dreamers."

**The grind is real. The tactics work. The passion endures.**
