# Leon Marchand - Paris 2024 Olympics Training
**Source:** Bob Bowman SwimSwam Podcast (Post-Paris 2024)

## The 4 Gold Medal Performance

**Paris Olympics 2024 Results:**
- 🥇 400 IM - 4:02.95 (near World Record)
- 🥇 200 Butterfly - 1:51.21
- 🥇 200 Breaststroke - 2:05.85  
- 🥇 200 IM - 1:54.06
- 🥉 4x100m Medley Relay

## Pre-Olympic Preparation

### Training Location Progression

**After US Olympic Trials:**
1. **Austin, TX** (1 week) - Training US athletes before they left for Raleigh
2. **Toulouse, France** (2 weeks) - Leon's hometown, with coach Nico Castell at club facility
3. **Vichy, France** (8-9 days) - French team training camp
4. **Paris** (3 days) - Pre-competition acclimatization
5. **Competition**

### Vichy Training Camp Setup

**Facility:** "Like a tiny Colorado Springs Olympic Training Center"
- Brand new dorms
- Good food
- Gym on-site
- 10-minute walk to pool
- Streamlined, simple setup

**Team vibe:** Relaxed, excited, not uptight
- Small team (30 total vs USA's ~50)
- Everyone knew each other well
- Positive energy
- Focus on important things, filter out noise

**French Team Director:** Julien Jacquier ("Joo")
- Super positive
- Calm leadership
- Empowered coaches
- Created great team environment

## The "Never Great" Taper

### Leon's Taper Pattern

> "Leon will definitely be very average going into a big meet... for his two best meets (Paris & Minnesota NCAAs), he was just really not that good for about the whole time going in."

**Characteristics:**
- Workmanlike, not spectacular
- Little flashes of good, then doubts
- **Not terrible, not great** - just steady work
- Then explodes at the meet

**Bowman's philosophy:** 
> "If the work before it has been good, and Leon's work had been VERY good... you just have to pay attention to it and realize the goal is to have them ready at the meet, not two weeks before."

### Blood Lactate Monitoring

**What it showed:**
- Swimming pretty fast
- Low lactate readings
- Quick clearance

**Interpretation:** Leon needed MORE recovery to drive anaerobic system

**Action:** Gave him extra rest in final 2 weeks → paid off massively

## The Public Day in Vichy - Race Rehearsal

**Setup:** Public viewing day, ~1000 people in attendance

**Workout:**
- 50m build + 100m at pace progression
- Round 1: 50 fly build, 100 back at pace
- Round 2: 50 back build, 100 breast at pace  
- Round 3: 50 breast build, 100 free at pace
- **Suited up**, race simulation

**Results:** Well under goal paces

**Crowd:** Chanting "LEON! LEON!" - perfect rehearsal for Paris pressure

**Bowman's realization:** "That's when I felt like okay, it's there. Just not ready to be here all the time yet."

**Lesson:** "That's just kind of how he does it. But it's hell on coaches... He makes it work."

## Sleep as Performance Tool

**After the double (2 fly + 2 breast in one day):**
- Didn't sleep well that night (adrenaline, media, excitement)
- Next morning (200 IM prelim): "I don't need this... I just cannot"
- **Big nap** going into next evening
- **Night before 200 IM final:** Slept 11 hours

**Bowman:** "When he sleeps, he's good."

**Comparison to Phelps:** Michael would come back before Olympic final: "Yeah I slept 3 hours this afternoon"

**High performer trait:** Ability to sleep under Olympic pressure

## The Double Decision (200 Fly + 200 Breast Same Day)

### Initial Plan

**BEFORE 400 IM:** Would wait to decide on the double

**AFTER 400 IM gold:** "Yeah we should go for it"

**Process:**
1. Try morning semifinals, see how it goes
2. Morning semis: Nothing for him (easy)
3. Night semis: Pretty good

**Blood lactate test (200 breast semi):**
- Recovered in **10 minutes** (!!)
- Re-tested to confirm
- "We knew after that he should definitely go for both finals"

**Bowman:** "I didn't think there was any way he would do the double"

### Race Strategy - 200 Butterfly Final

**The challenge:** Kristof Milak
- Amazing swimmer
- Has speed
- Great 50/100 start

**The plan:**
> "Leon's hope would be to be as close as possible at the 150. If he was somewhere in the neighborhood at 150, we felt like he would kick out and finish really well."

**Key insight:** Milak hadn't shown strong finishes in 2024

**Execution:**
- Great underwater at 150m
- Started moving
- Got there with ~7 meters to go
- Touched first

**Result:** All last 3 x 50m splits = **28s** ("That's really good")

### Race Strategy - 200 Breaststroke Final

**Strategy:** "Be as far ahead as you could be and hope Zac Stubblety-Cook didn't catch you at the end"

**Leon's preference:** Loves to go out fast in breaststroke

**Why it worked:**
- 400 IM breaststroke was very good
- Prelims/semis: Under control, using legs, very together
- Could take it out and hang on

## The 2004 Parallel - Michael Phelps' Triple

**Long Beach Trials 2004:**
- Finals: 200 IM, 200 Back  
- Semi: 100 Fly
- All in one session, all swam well

**The next morning:** "I just cannot walk"

**Result:** Dropped 200 back for Athens Olympics

**Leon's experience:** Nearly identical
- Double went well the night of
- Next morning 200 IM prelim: Really hurting, couldn't walk

**Bowman's instruction:** "Just do it, focus in, don't be too conservative, be out fast, then if you want to shut it down at the end you can."

**Leon's prelim strategy:** Out faster, then relax at end (much better than going out easy and having to overdo the finish)

## Media Management - The Budapest Lesson

**Budapest 2022:** Leon went 4:04 in 400 IM

**Problem:** Spent **1 HOUR in mixed zone** talking to media before coming out

**Bowman:** "They just let him stay in there and talk and talk"

**Education session:** How media obligations should work

**Outside obligations:** Sponsors, etc. waited until after meet (smart)

## Training Philosophy - Repetition Over Variety

### The Principle

> "Repetition is the highest form of learning. The answer is always no when they ask to do different strokes. Repetition makes it automatic under stress."

**Why:** Makes execution automatic under pressure

**Application:** Don't let swimmers switch strokes during sets just because they're bored

---

## Confidence Through Demonstrated Ability

> "Confidence comes from demonstrated ability. You can't dream it up."

**The Process:**
1. Do something successfully in practice
2. Next time, believe you can do it again  
3. Repeat thousands of times
4. Confidence becomes automatic

**NOT:**
- Visualization alone
- Positive thinking
- Coach-given confidence

**IS:** Earned through successful repetition in training

---

## Work:Rest Ratios - Maximum Variety

### Different Purposes:

**3:1 (work:rest) - Lactate Tolerance**
- Example: 100s on 4:00 (swim 1:00, rest 3:00)
- Lactate peaks ~3min after effort
- Teaches buffering and metabolizing

**1:1 - Neuromuscular + Endurance**
- Example: 10x100 on 1:20, climb out and dive
- 51s swim, rest until 1:20
- Build speed with fatigue management

**Bowman's example set:**
- 10x100 on 1:20 (climb out/dive, best average)
- Into: 10x50 fly on 1:00 (climb out/dive, best average)
- Heart rate spikes on every jump
- College guys holding 51s

**Conclusion:** Vary ratios throughout training for complete development

## Coaching at Olympics - Compensation Discussion

### Current Reality

**USA Olympic staff:** Volunteer (unpaid except performance bonuses)
- Give up 5-6 weeks
- 24/7 commitment
- Leave paying jobs

**Performance bonuses:** Gold medal bonuses (not staff salary)

**Athletic trainers/massage therapists:**
- Leaving their businesses
- No income while gone
- Same volunteer status

### Bowman's Position

> "You're not just volunteering some time, you're volunteering 24 hours a day for weeks and weeks... to accomplish something very valuable. I do think it should be remunerated somehow."

**Suggestion:** All Olympic staff (coaches, trainers, support) should receive compensation for participation, not just performance bonuses

**Applies to:** Every country, not just USA

## Decision to Coach French Team vs USA

### Why French Team for Paris 2024

> "I just can't imagine what it would have been like for me to be there in a USA shirt in the middle of the Leon thing. It would just not be tenable."

**Benefits:**
- Could focus fully on Leon  
- Removed distraction from USA team
- Natural and free at the meet
- USA staff very cooperative
- Could still work with USA athletes

### The Leon Effect on France

> "What I loved about it most was... the French people aren't really known for coming together and being happy, they're kind of the opposite... but here everybody in that arena, everybody outside, the people in hotels, everybody was just so excited about it. Leon was kind of the catalyst of that."

**Rare moment:** Unified French celebration

### Future

**Bowman's plan:** Back to USA full-time for LA 2028

**Reasoning:** "Leon can do some things on his own now, he doesn't need me to hold his hand through all this stuff"

**Thursday Practice (December 2003 - Phelps age 17):**

## Championship Performance Under Adversity

### 2007 Broken Hand (Phelps, 9 months before Beijing)

**Crisis:** Slipped on ice after perfect training progression

**Initial reaction:** "Gave away 3 gold medals"

**Solution:**
- Surgery with pin (not 6-week cast)
- 10 days for stitches
- 3 hours/day stationary bike
- Day 7: Kicking with plastic bag over hand
- Day 10: Back to full training

**Lesson:** Perfect plans derail - how quickly can you adapt?

### Beijing 2008 - Goggles Filled

**Training prep:** Bowman stepped on Phelps' goggles in practice, swam with goggles full of water

**Competition:** 200 fly finals, goggles filled  
**Response:** Counted strokes, won gold

**Result:** Automatic execution under any conditions

**Leon's equivalent:** Prepared for any scenario through training variability
