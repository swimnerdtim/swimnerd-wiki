# Bob Bowman: Leon Marchand Training & Methodology

*Source: Podcast transcripts 2024-2026*

## Coaching Leon vs. Michael: The Critical Difference

> "If I coached Leon like I coached Michael, he would quit in like two days. If I coached Michael like I coached Leon, he wouldn't have done anything near what he did." - Bob Bowman, Unfiltered Waters 2024

### Personality & Approach

**Michael Phelps**:
- Needed to get **really intense, almost angry** to perform best
- External motivation worked
- Pre-race: Stoke competitive fire, create enemies
- Example: "Michael Cavic said there's no way you should win. You're the one to stop yourself. Go."
- Thrived on pressure, conflict, challenge

**Leon Marchand**:
- Needs to be **really laid-back** to swim fast
- Gets worse when intense
- Pre-race: "Have fun out there"
- Indifferent to trash talk or external motivation
- Best when calm, relaxed, enjoying himself

**Bob's Adaptation**: "Every time I do something with him before races, it's just 'have fun out there, you know.' With Michael, I'd wind him up like a toy."

## Paris 2024 Olympics

### The Historic Performance

**Results**:
- 4 Gold Medals
- 1 Bronze Medal
- Multiple Olympic Records
- Home Olympics in Paris
- Defeated world record holders
- "A landmark Olympics at home, an event that will never be replicated"

### Pre-Olympics Preparation

**Bob's Concern (2023 Worlds)**:
- Leon won multiple golds at Fukuoka Worlds 2023
- Media obsession: "Will Leon break Michael's record?"
- Bob's connection to Leon became distraction for Team USA
- First question at press conference: "Bob, will Leon break Michael's record?"

**Decision**: Bob chose NOT to be on USA coaching staff for Paris 2024
- "My connection to him was really a distraction for Team USA"
- "If I had been wearing the USA shirt in the middle of all this stuff he was doing, it would just be a lot"
- Let Leon and France have their moment without Michael Phelps comparisons

### The Last Night Before Finals

**Bob's Warning**:
> "I wish we had more time to go over this, but things are not going to be the same for you. Just remember: stay connected to the people you know that are close to you and your family. Take a breath sometimes. We'll figure it out together."

Leon was about to be slammed.

## Post-Olympic Transition (2024-2025)

### The Problem: Post-Olympic Stress

**Bob's Historical Mistakes**:
> "I have poorly managed the period after the Olympics historically. I got it wrong with Michael like three times. Allison struggled."

**What Actually Happens**:
1. **Physical & Emotional Exhaustion** from the performance itself
2. **Celebration Period** (as it should be)
3. **But then**: Before you can even reflect on what happened...
   - Making big decisions about A LOT of money
   - Being pulled in 100 different directions
   - Mother signing autographs in the grocery store in Toulouse
   - Can't go anywhere in France without pictures/autographs
   - Complete loss of privacy (will never have it again)
   - New people entering your life
   - Agents, sponsors, media demands

**Bob's Realization**: "The time after is probably MORE stressful than the Olympics themselves for these swimmers."

### Leon's Specific Post-Olympic Reality

**In France**:
- Cannot go anywhere without being recognized
- Every public appearance = autographs, photos, conversations
- "A wonderful thing that they love him so much, but he has lost this level of privacy he'll probably never have again"
- Similar to Michael Phelps' experience

**Emotionally Exhausted**: By Short Course Worlds 2024 (December), Leon was drained

### The Transition Plan (Bowman's New Approach)

**Phase 1: Immediate Post-Olympics (July-August)**
- Time with family
- Celebration
- Processing what happened

**Phase 2: World Cup Tour (October)**
- Singapore, Shanghai, Incheon
- "Light training disguised as competition"
- Fun travel experience
- Kept him swimming without rigorous training demands
- Not competing in all events, selective schedule

**Phase 3: Short Break** (November)
- Skipped Short Course Worlds in Budapest (December)
- **Correct decision** according to Bob
- Needed more recovery time

**Phase 4: Australia with Dean Boxall** (December-February estimated)
- Training with Australian National Team
- Completely different environment
- New stimulus, new culture
- Dean is "an amazing coach, he'll learn some things, I'll get to learn some things"
- Different from Texas program structure

**Phase 5: Return to Austin** (Spring 2025 estimated)
- Fresh: mentally, physically, emotionally
- Ready to begin next Olympic quad
- Relative anonymity in Austin ("People care about football players, not swimmers")

**Full Program Timeline**: Not back to 100% until ~9-12 months post-Olympics

### Why Australia?

**Origin of Idea**:
- Leon did World Cup tour
- Connected with Italian swimmers also training with Dean Boxall
- They suggested it
- Leon approached Bob with the idea

**Bob's Initial Reaction**: "Well, you know, I don't know..."
- Coach's instinct: Get athletes back to training ASAP
- "I'm always itchy to get him back into things because I know the further you go, the further you go"

**Why Bob Said Yes**:
1. Leon was emotionally exhausted before Short Course Worlds
2. Needs something to refresh him
3. Needs something NOT in France/Europe
4. Needs to go far away
5. Then can return to Austin with relative anonymity
6. Similar to how Phoenix worked pre-Olympics

**Bob's Philosophy Shift**: "It just makes sense for him where he is right now."

### The "Inescapable Reality" Problem

**Leon's Training History with Bob**:
- 3 years in Bob's program (2021-2024)
- Led to historic Olympic performance
- But: "The inescapable reality of these training programs is they don't really change that much"
- Another 3+ years coming up of same program structure
- Risk of staleness, burnout

**Australia Solution**:
- Different stimulus
- Learn new things
- Mix it up
- Then return refreshed and ready for familiar program
- "He'll need that to make the next step"

## Lessons from Leon for Coaches

### 1. Individualization is Non-Negotiable

- What worked for Michael would destroy Leon
- What works for Leon would have limited Michael
- Know your athlete's psychology

### 2. Post-Championship Management Matters

- The work doesn't end with the medal
- Plan for 6-12 month transition
- Protect athlete from exhaustion
- Allow space to be human

### 3. Environment Selection is Strategic

- Austin's relative anonymity = asset
- Australia's novelty = refreshment
- France's adoration = overwhelming
- Choose training location for mental health, not just physical facilities

### 4. Long-term > Short-term

- Bob could have pushed Leon back to training immediately
- Would have been physically fine
- Would have been mentally damaging
- Patience now = longevity later

### 5. Core Group Stability

- Keep tight circle during chaos
- New people will come
- Old people provide grounding
- Trust the ones who were there before success

## Bob's Growth as a Coach (Through Leon)

**What Leon Has Taught Bob**:
1. Patience with post-Olympic transitions
2. Value of varied training environments
3. Calm > intensity for some athletes
4. Importance of fun for optimal performance
5. When to let go and trust the process

**Bob's Reflection**:
> "Hopefully we learn, right?"

## Summary: The Leon Marchand Method

1. **Keep it fun** - performance follows joy
2. **Stay calm** - intensity hurts his swimming
3. **Extended post-championship recovery** - 9-12 months transition
4. **Varied environments** - Australia, Austin, strategic planning
5. **Core group stability** - family, Nico, Bob
6. **Process focus** - not recreating past glories
7. **Strategic anonymity** - training in places where he can be normal
8. **Professional independence** - he owns his career decisions
9. **Long-term vision** - another 3+ years, sustainable approach
10. **Trust the athlete** - he knows when he needs what

**Bottom Line**: Leon isn't Michael 2.0. He's Leon 1.0. And Bob has learned how to coach him accordingly.
