# Bob Bowman: Texas NCAA Championship 2025

*Source: SwimSwam Podcast with Coleman Hodges - Post-NCAA Championship Interview*

**Historic Achievement:** First coach to win back-to-back NCAA team titles with two different teams (ASU 2024, Texas 2025)

---

## The Integration Strategy: How to Win Year One

### Gradual Implementation (60-70% → 100%)

Bob didn't come in and blow up everything. **He started at 60-70% of his system and gradually built up.**

**What They Kept (Texas Traditions):**
- Friday clap meeting
- Team dinner every Friday
- Dress up for NCAAs + cowboy hats
- Long-standing Texas cultural elements

**What They Changed:**
- Practice structure (everything on paper when you arrive)
- Set-to-set rest time (shortened dramatically)
- Strength training protocols
- Fall morning volume (7,000-8,000 yards in 90-100 minutes)

### The Transition Team

**Key people who bridged the gap:**
1. **Hubert Kós** - Knew Bob's system, could explain the "why" behind workouts
2. **Chase Kalisz** - Training as a pro at Texas, helped younger swimmers understand expectations
3. **Senior leadership** - Bought in first, everyone else followed

---

## NCAA Teams vs. International: Different Math

### International Preparation
- All about YOUR time
- Hit specific time at specific competition
- Individual focus

### NCAA Team Championship
- **It's a numbers game** - scoring points, not just swimming fast
- Everybody plays a role
- **Texas wouldn't have won without diving**
- **Texas wouldn't have won without ALL the swimmers who scored**

**Holistic experience:**
- Perform under pressure
- Go above expectations
- Come back from setbacks
- Grind it out when it's not going your way

## The Meet: How Texas Won

### Seeding Situation
Seeded to score 200+ points more than next team (similar to ASU 2024)

**Bob's confidence came from:**
- Swam well at SECs on NOT a full taper
- Top 4 scorers (Kós, Luke Hobson, Rex Maurer, etc.) were **unshaved at SECs**
- Knew they had room to improve

### Why Bob Gave Them More Rest Earlier

**Bob gave more taper for:**
1. **Fall invite** - needed to see where they were
2. **Indiana dual meet** - needed confidence boost

**The difference from ASU:**
- ASU was confident but inexperienced at NCAAs
- Texas had gotten 7th the previous year
- Eddie Reese told Bob at breakfast: **"I'm so sorry. They're way better than that."**
- Texas has to walk the hallways and see championship banners every day

> "I was trying to coach them mentally as well as physically. If I just did a straight physiological plan, our invite I'd probably just rolled through it with most people, but I felt like we needed to see where they were and have them feel good about what we were doing so far." — Bob Bowman

### Indiana Dual Meet: Going to the Well?

**Ray Looze (Indiana coach):** "You can only go to the well so many times."

**Bob's response:** They got TWO DAYS of fresher training before Indiana. Bob's philosophy: **"I don't see the point in competing so far away from where you're going to be."**

- Warmed up the day before
- No lifting (because Indiana flew in, so they didn't lift either)
- "We saw the well over there somewhere. I don't know. You know what I mean? We kind of saw it."

Result: Way better than Bob expected.

> "What's the point of swimming a 500 fifteen seconds slower than your best time? Maybe 5 or 10 seconds is good. But Michael [Phelps] was always relatively close to his speed and we trained 80,000 a week. When we went to a meet, we didn't just keep that going till the day we got there. It seems stupid to do that. Why would we even go? Just keep practicing." — Bob Bowman

## The Championship Meet: Day-by-Day

### Night 1: Amazing
Exceeded expectations. Very good.

**Texas's advantage:** First four events are their best events.

### Day 2 Morning: Body Blows
- 200 medley relay DQ in the final (every point counts)
- Taking punches, just trying to hang in
- **But somehow still increased the lead slightly**

### Day 3 Morning (Last Prelims): Struggle
"We didn't swim as well as we needed to. A lot of that's inexperience. A lot of that was they'd given a lot in the first three days."

**Bob's response:** Gave them an **objective assessment** of what they needed to do.

### The Emotional Control Lesson

Bob's first inclination: "All right, come on, we gotta—"

**But that's wrong.** They're already feeling the pressure. They don't need MORE pressure from the coach.

> "The reality is if we want them to perform, they're already feeling the pressure. They don't need pressure from me. I need to take it away if anything. I need to mitigate it so they can perform at their best." — Bob Bowman

**The seniors had a team meeting alone after Night 3.** Whatever they said worked. The team hung in there.

> "The team very much like the ASU team kind of motivated itself. We didn't have to give them too much." — Bob Bowman

### Night 3 (Finals): The Comeback

**What Texas needed:**
- A few people move up 3-5 places
- Get point swings in their direction

**Key performances:**
1. **Luke Hobson** - Moved up to 9th in 100 breast (substantial move)
2. **Nate Germonprez** - Went from lane 1 to **winning the consolation final** (biggest difference maker)
3. **Jacob Welch** - Moved up in platform diving (weren't sure they'd have a platform diver)

**The 400 Free Relay:**
- Texas had to finish top 12
- Had to be within a certain margin of Cal
- Bob: "I would have liked to have had it done before the relay. At ASU we did that, then you can send them and they can do amazing times. But in our situation, we had to have everybody go."

**Relay starts:** "I said, be deliberate. Maybe not THAT deliberate, but it was great."

Result: **Texas wins NCAA Championship**

## Building Discomfort Tolerance

### Low-Hanging Fruit Strategy

Bob immediately looked for ways to make them **uncomfortable**, then make them deal with it.

### Example 1: Practice Pace

**Eddie Reese's system:**
- Decide what he was going to do
- Tell group what they're doing
- Go to next lane, deliver to them
- Amazing practices, but **lots of time between sets**

**Bob Bowman's system:**
- Everything on paper when you arrive
- Between sets: **about 1 minute**
- "Sometimes I try to make it less. They finish one set, I'll be like 'On the top, start the pulling.'"

**Swimmers' reaction:** "Completely flummoxed in the beginning."

But that alone put them in an uncomfortable state. After a few days, they adapted.

### Example 2: Fall Morning Volume

**Bob's favorite fall routine:**
- Build general aerobic work (kicking, pulling, swimming)
- Short rest
- **7,000-8,000 yards in 90-100 minutes**

"They're just completely not used to it, not really comfortable with it in the beginning, but after a while they'd say, 'Oh yeah, I can do this.' And that made them more confident."

### Example 3: The Christmas Sledgehammer

**After Christmas break, long course meters:**

- 10x100 freestyle from a dive, on 2:00 (maximal effort)
- Straight into 10x50 fly from a dive, on 1:30 (maximal effort)
- Everyone climbs out after every one
- **Everyone does it** - sprinters, distance, doesn't matter
- Coaches yelling and screaming: "Come on, you can do it!"

Guys got into it. "Wow, I don't even know if I can do this."

Then they do it.

> "That muscle starts to get bigger. They get a little more strong in the brain. I was reading where there's a part of your brain that when you get yourself to do something you either don't want to do or don't think you can do, every time you do that a little area of your brain grows and gets better at it the next time." — Bob Bowman

## The Hubert Kós Story

### SECs: Lost Every Event
Got beat in every event at SECs (unshaved).

### NCAAs: Dominant
**SEC Swimmer of the Year** but **All-SEC Second Team** (because you have to WIN an event to make first team).

Bob: "It's pretty cool. Keeps him humble. Keep working."

### The 200 Back: 1:34-low

**Coleman:** "The swim of the meet to me."

**Bob:** "He was mad he didn't go 1:33. We had talked about going 1:33."

**The setup:**
- Week before NCAAs, Bob does suited swims (broken swim or effort)
- Kós swam 150 back: **1:10.0**
- Exactly what he split at NCAAs: **1:10.0**
- After that 150, Kós: "Man, I could go like 1:33 or something."
- Bob: "Yeah, feel free. Go ahead. Do it. Please do."

**Bob's assessment:** "He's capable of that for sure."

### Long Course: "He's got a lot of work to do"

"He's far away [from world-class long course times], so we need to start dropping this stuff by a lot."

### Hungarian Nationals (Spring 2025)

Kós was sick before the meet. Bob had no idea how he'd do.

**Result:** Great. First day he swam 5 times (relay + individual events). All of it pretty good.

**Bob's takeaway:** "That tells me he was in good condition right at NCAAs. Maybe he could have rested more for NCAAs."

**Two-year progression:**
- 2024 (ASU): Bob "really blew it with him" - let him get way too tired, shaved him for Pac-12s (should NOT have done that)
- 2025 (Texas): **Got it right** - swam SECs, got beat in every event, then peaked perfectly for NCAAs

## Long Course Transition

### Start Long Course IMMEDIATELY After Short Course

"They're already in shape. We just roll right into it."

**2025 Timeline:**
- Trials: June 3
- ~40 days away (at time of interview)
- Serious trials athletes: **4 days off, then back in full training**

"Use the speed you have and roll that into the next training phase."

### Long Course During College Season

**Tuesday/Thursday/Saturday long course** most of the time (maybe not the last month before championships).

**After NCAAs:**
- Add Monday afternoons
- Tuesday morning
- Thursday morning
- Saturday morning
- Add Wednesday
- **At least 5 long course sessions** per week

### Bob's Philosophy on Long Course Training

> "Excellence in long course is about your fitness level and your technical level, and there are many ways to get that. I don't think it's just swimming long course every time because if you swim long course all the time, they just get so sloggy and it's hard to get speed. I kind of keep a good mix. I want them to have enough long course that they know the pace and the stroke counts and those things and they get the conditioning to do it, but I also like to keep enough short course in there so they maintain their speed and their strokes are just better in the short course." — Bob Bowman

### Short Course Meters

**At NBAC (North Baltimore):** Did it all the time in the outdoor pool.

**Benefits:**
- "Nobody knows what they're doing, so it's kind of a great relief."
- By the time you figure out the intervals, it's over
- Swim things similar to long course (200 SCM is way faster than 200 LCM, but still meters)
- **Varies the stimulus**
- Swimmers get a little confidence

## On Olympic 50s of Stroke

**Bob's position:** Fine with the events themselves, but **watch the roster implications.**

**Concerns:**
- Hard to cover all those events
- **Relay alternates probably won't happen anymore** (Bob's okay with this)
- Need a good selection plan
- Need to think about how it affects relays

**Benefits:**
- Exciting events
- Big population of world swimmers can participate
- USA should excel in these

### Shane Casas and the 50s

**When 50s were announced:**

Shane texted Bob immediately: "50s! 🎉"

Bob: "It's awesome. We got a set this afternoon. It's going to get you ready for these 50s."

Shane: All pumped.

## The Benefits of Racing

> "I like that we have more racing now. I think I like that you see more coaches adopting that philosophy of staying sharp and racing more. You get some benefits from racing you don't get from training, in all kinds of ways—psychological, physiological, all of those things are there. And it's kind of fun. Let's face it, the majority of stuff we do is not that fun. Trust me, I've been doing it for a long time. You just watch them going up and down all the time." — Bob Bowman

**2025 Worlds issue:** 6-7 weeks between trials and Worlds.

**Bob's solution:** "We have to have something that's like a meet, whether we just make it our own or we go somewhere. There has to be a competition in there. That's a long time."

## Coaching Evolution: The Blowup That Saved a Medal

**2024 Paris Olympics - Chase Kalisz, 400 IM:**

Chase had been with Bob since age 7. Michael Phelps was his role model the whole way.

For a long time, Chase "had not been doing it right."

**Bob dropped a nuclear bomb on him about a flip turn.**

> "I know I seem really sweet and nice as I talk to you today, but I can get a little bit forceful." — Bob Bowman

Bob thought Chase was actually going to leave.

**Michael Phelps called Chase:**
> "Look, I know Bob is hard on you. It's only because he wants you to succeed. And if you don't change that turn, you don't have a chance to win a medal in the Olympic Games." — Michael Phelps

Chase came back. **Never did a bad turn again.**

**Paris Olympics 400 IM:** Chase wins silver.

**First thing Chase said after:** "You know what? That turn really saved me about 5-10ths. That's why I won the silver medal."
