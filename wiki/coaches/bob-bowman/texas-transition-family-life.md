# Bob Bowman: The Texas Transition & Family Life

## The Dream Job

### When the Dream Started

> "Anybody who's involved in coaching kind of looked at this as the ultimate job."

**The recurring thought**: For years, Bowman would come to Texas for meets, walk back to his car or hotel, look around and think: *"Wow, I wonder what it's like to be the coach at Texas."*

**Immediately followed by**: *"Well, it's never going to be you, so just stop thinking about it."*

**His first Olympic Trials was in Texas** - 1988, before most current swimmers were born.

**What attracted him:**
- The resources
- The facility (iconic)
- The history and expectations
- The standard of excellence

## The Secret Search

### How It Really Went Down

**Timeline**: Monday before Women's Pac-12s (5 weeks before NCAAs)

**The unexpected call**: From a head hunter Bowman knew from helping with previous coaching searches.

Bowman assumed he was calling for recommendations for the Texas job.

**Head hunter**: "No, they want to talk to you."

**Bowman's reaction**: "What?" (Genuinely shocked)

### The One Condition

**First call with Sarah Bomgardner (Texas AD)**:

**First question Bowman asked**: "Will I have the ability to go back to Phoenix anytime I want and see my grandchildren?"

**If she had said no**: He would have said "thanks" and ended the conversation right there.

**She said yes**: "Absolutely." → The conversation continued.

### The Secret Calls

**Every week after that**: More calls, getting closer to a decision.

**Week 1**: General discussion - "Am I really going to do this? I'm really happy at ASU. We're about to win. I'm not sure I'm up for it."

**But**: Something kept pulling at him.

**Week 2**: "What would it look like for you to be Director of Swimming and Diving at Texas?"

**Week 3**: Money discussions - things getting real.

**Week 4**: Sarah flew out and met with Bowman during Women's NCAAs (all very secretive).

### The Only People Who Knew

During the entire process, Bowman talked to **only four people**:
1. His parents
2. Michael Phelps
3. Nicole Phelps

**That's it.** Nobody else.

### The Non-Negotiable Condition

**Bowman told Sarah from the beginning**:

> "If any part of this search compromises ASU's chance to win a championship, my answer is no right there. It's just done."

**His biggest fear**: Someone would leak it and the ASU team would know before NCAAs, affecting their performance.

**Texas's credit**: They kept it completely secret. It didn't leak.

## The Week That Changed Everything

### The Term Sheet

**Location**: Hotel in Indianapolis, Tuesday before NCAAs (meet starts Wednesday)

**The call with Chris Del Conte (Texas AD)**:

**Del Conte**: "Everything good with this?"  
**Bowman**: "Yep."  
**Del Conte**: "Congratulations, you're the new head coach at Texas."

**Bowman's reaction**: Sitting in a hotel room, looking like he wasn't the least bit excited. Just: "Uh, okay."

**He had just taken the job** - right before NCAAs started.

### The Meet

**Nobody knew** - not the team, not the staff.

**ASU killed it** at NCAAs - exactly as they knew they could.

**Saturday night**: Amazing celebration with the ASU team after winning the championship.

**Sunday**: Bowman signed the actual contract.

**Monday**: Had to tell the team and ASU. This is why it was so abrupt.

### Why No Negotiation

**Bowman chose not to**:
- Play both ends against the middle
- Use Texas offer to get a better deal from ASU
- Do any long negotiating

**Why?** If he had done that, it would have come out. People talk. He couldn't risk the ASU guys knowing before the championship meet.

**So he just took the Texas job** - no back and forth, no haggling.

## The Transition Plan

### Why It Had to Be April 1st

**Bowman begged Texas** to give him some time after NCAAs.

**Texas couldn't do it** - had to be the Monday after the championship.

**The timing was dictated by**:
- Recruiting cycles
- Transfer portal timelines
- Staff hiring needs
- Spring recruiting period

### The Olympic Year Pattern

> "I always do everything on Olympic years. I went to Michigan - Olympic year. I went to ASU - the year of the Olympics. I always do all of that. I can't do it in a lowkey year."

### Telling Herbie

**Monday morning** after returning from NCAAs:

**Bowman to team**: "Team meeting tomorrow at 2:00 for everybody. Everybody needs to be there."

**Bowman to Herbie**: "I'd like to meet with you about 9:00 tomorrow morning."

**That morning conversation**:

**Bowman**: "Well, today is your day."  
**Herbie**: "What do you mean?"  
**Bowman**: "In about 4 hours you're going to be the head coach of this program. So go across the street and work out your deal."

**Herbie's reaction**: "Whoa, okay."

**Bowman's feeling**: Super happy for Herbie and Megan. Herbie was **always the long-term solution for ASU**. He loves it, he's a Sun Devil, he was perfectly ready to take over.

### Telling the Team

**2:00 meeting**: Very short. They were super excited about Herbie.

**Bowman literally**:
- Told the team
- Walked out
- Got on a plane
- Came to Texas

**The team meeting went much better than expected.**

## Managing Two Groups Through Olympics

### The College Team

**Problem**: Once he took the job at Texas, NCAA rules prevented him from talking to anyone at ASU.

**Exception for pros**: Leon was different (pro status) - compliance allowed Bowman to talk to him a little.

**The college athletes**:
- If Hubie or Liam wanted to talk to Bowman, they had to go in the transfer portal first
- Bowman couldn't recruit them or communicate with them
- Created a lot of anxious people
- Bowman couldn't really help - had to wait and see how things played out

**Eventually**: Everybody kind of smoothed out.

### The Pro Group

**The immediate aftermath was tough** for pros:

**Logistics**:
- Had leases in Phoenix
- Had to move to Austin
- Complete pain in terms of moving and stuff

**The swimming was actually easy**:
- Eddie Reese basically coached the college team until after Trials
- While setting things up at Texas, Bowman just coached the pros
- Didn't coach any Texas college guys all summer
- Almost the whole Phoenix pro group came to Austin

**The problem**: Bowman couldn't tell the pros until he told the college team.

**When he told the college team**: 30 seconds later, Texas put out a release (timed for 2:00 PM).

**Then had to call the pros**: "Hey, um..."

**Eric (Bowman's assistant)** was with the pros at altitude. Bowman called ahead: "We're going to do this, just support them a little bit."

**Next day**: Had to Zoom with the pros to really go over it.

> "That part was not great. But I didn't have a choice in that. It's just how it had to be."

## Life in Austin

### Still Getting Settled

**At time of interview** (early in first season):
- Still renting a place
- Looking at houses
- Figuring out the commute (learning it can be terrible)
- Priority: Close to campus, good commute, nice kitchen

**Not looking at horses**: Done with that right now.

### The Standard

**The Longhorn Experience** (new coach orientation):

Attended with the new baseball coach and new golf coach. Part of it is touring the athletic Hall of Fame.

**Eddie Reese's display**:
- 15 national championships
- Don't even know how many consecutive conference championships

**Golf coach looks at Bowman**: "Oh wow..."

**Bowman**: "Yeah, no pressure at all here. I'm fine. Totally fine."

**The good news**: "I'll never surpass that, so I just try to keep it going. I'm just trying to add some things to it probably."

### The Transition Experience

> "The transition to the team, the athletic department, and the university as a whole has been amazing. It's just an incredible place to be. The standard obviously is quite High."

**The kids have been great** - really enjoying working with them.

## The Roster Cuts: The Hardest Challenge

### The Brutal Reality

**Current situation**:
- Team of 41 swimmers
- Going down to 22
- Welcome to the SEC

> "It's like taking a test, a multiple choice test, where every answer is wrong. There is no right answer. Every answer gives somebody some pain, and every answer gives me total pain."

### The Approach: Transparency

**From the first meeting**: Talked about it openly. Didn't put heads in the sand and act like it wasn't happening.

**Made it as objective as possible**:
- Everybody has had a chance to put their best foot forward over 6 months
- Looking at the numbers
- If you haven't put up a time that would score points at SECs by this point, you're not moving forward
- "That's just how it is."

**Helping with transitions**: Proactively working over the next month or so. By end of month, will probably have most of roster in place so other people have **longer on-ramp to wherever they're going to go**.

### The Philosophy

> "My job is to take us from here to there and try to do it in a way that's respectful. I always try to, in the back of my mind—this sounds crazy—but just do it with love. I love these guys, I really do, and I know they love Texas."

**For the people that won't be with us**: Try to be as respectful as possible, but also honest.

**Everyone on the team**:
- Has been amazing in attitude
- How they've approached it
- How they've accepted what's going on
- Culture of the team hasn't suffered - quite good

### Real-Life Lessons

**Using it as a real-life example**:

> "Stuff like this happens all the time out there, in every job. How you handle it has a big part on the effect it has on you."

**Focus on control**:
- A lot is out of your control
- What IS in your control: Come in here, do the best job you can in training, put forward the best performances you can in meets
- See where that takes you
- If it takes you somewhere else, that's what's going to happen
- If it keeps you here, that's what's going to happen

**"We love you either way"** - it's not subjective, it's objective. **Thank God swimming is objective.**

### The Phased Approach

**Doing it in phases**:
- Corresponds with different meets
- Giving full preparation for these meets
- Letting athletes put their best foot forward
- Not waiting until April (can't - portal opens March 19th)
- Athletes have to know by then so they can enter portal if they need to

**Hope**: Get some people who know they're transferring into portal early (NCAA waiver). Would help them since portal will be full.

### The Brutal End

> "The most brutal will be when we get to the end and there's like two or three people left. When we get to number 22 but number 23 and 24 definitely deserve to be on the team. That's going to be brutal."

## Grandpa Bob: The Michael Phelps Family

### How "Grandpa" Happened

**The pregnancy announcement (2016)**:

**Bowman's first thought**: "What a wonderful thing - they belong together." (They'd gone through a long process to get Michael and Nicole together, and they finally were.)

**Bowman's second thought (as the coach)**: "I'm worried about what kind of stressor the baby will be on Michael's swimming going into the Olympics."

Bowman felt huge responsibility to help Michael finish well - he'd come out of retirement, made a lot of changes, gone through hard things. Wanted him to end on a high note.

### The Colorado Springs Ordeal

**Six weeks at altitude** (Bowman: "We went for like six weeks, which oh my God it was horrible. I would never do it again.")

- Three weeks up
- Went down for a meet
- Back for three more weeks

**During this time**: Boomer was supposed to come.

### The Birth Call

**Michael was in practice** when the call came that Nicole was going to be induced.

USOPC had helped set up a plane for Michael to get there fast.

**Bowman**: "Let's just do one more... one more 100."

**Looking back**: "I was like what are you doing?"

**Michael**: "I got to go."

**Bowman**: "Okay, yeah go go go."

**Michael ran out**, got in the plane, went to Phoenix.

### The Name Reveal

**That night** (around 7:00 PM):

Bowman sitting in his little room in the basement in Colorado Springs. Hadn't heard from Michael all day. Getting nervous.

**Texted Michael**: "Any news?"

**Immediate response**: "He's here."

**Bowman**: "Awesome. Well what's his name?"

**Michael**: "Boomer."

**Bowman**: "Okay, okay."

**Then Michael texted**: "Robert."

**Pause.**

"Robert Oh Phelps."

**(Michael always calls Bowman "Robert")**

**Bowman's reaction**: Sat there and cried for a while. It was a beautiful thing. Overwhelmed.

### The Burt's Bees Package

**Two days later**: Michael comes back to Colorado Springs (had to - couldn't stay forever with the baby).

**Bowman**: "I've got to do something."

**Went online to Burt's Bees** (they have amazing organic baby clothing - "it's really good, it's awesome").

**Found stuff and sent it** to their house.

**The label**: "To Boomer, from **Grandpa**."

**Bowman thought it was a joke**: "Grandpa Bob" - because nobody thought of him as a grandpa. Everybody thought of him as "just this jerk."

### The Name Stuck

**When they got back from Colorado**:

First time Bowman held Boomer:

**Nicole and Michael**: "Hey Grandpa, you're going to hold the baby?"

**Even for them it was kind of a joke at first.**

**But they never stopped.**

**All Boomer ever knew** was his Grandpa.

**So now Bowman is just Grandpa** - and he absolutely loves it.

> "It wasn't like we'd planned it or anything. It just sort of came from that."

### The Daily Relationship

**Bowman's routine**: "Every night I go over there for dinner pretty much. I leave here, go play with the kids while Michael fixes dinner. Michael cooks dinner most nights."

**What they do**: Bowman plays with the four boys while Michael cooks.

**Favorite thing now**: If Bowman is there and it's not like they go do things together, they'll just be in the house together and chat:
- Michael talks about golf a lot
- They talk about whatever
- Just hanging out

> "That's my favorite. I absolutely love that."

### The Soccer Games

**Boomer is really into soccer** - plays competitively.

**He's really good at it**:

**Last game Bowman watched**:
- Team won 8-0
- Boomer scored 5 goals
- Assisted the other 3
- Coaches the team during the game: "Johnny, over here! You, no, move this way, move this!"

**Probably too into it**, but that's the Phelps competitive nature.

### The Family Soccer Game

**The Setup**: Before the game, Boomer meticulously writes who's on the teams at dinner.

**The iron law**: If Michael's not on your team, you're losing. Period. Michael's team always wins because he'll just take over the game.

**This particular game**:

**Michael's team**: Michael + two little boys (Beckett and Maverick)

**Bowman's team**: Bowman + Boomer + the nanny (Emo - "actually pretty good")

**The rules**: Play to 3 goals, have goals set up in backyard.

**How it went**:
- Boomer sneaks one past Michael (up 1-0)
- Michael gives him a hard time but kind of lets it go
- Somehow Michael slipped down and Bowman's team sneaks another (up 2-0)
- Michael comes back, scores 2 (tied 2-2)
- "By some miracle" Bowman's team gets the third goal
- **Final**: 3-2, Bowman and Boomer win

**Michael's reaction**: Furious.

**Boomer and Bowman**: Boomer takes his shirt off, running around the backyard, chest-bumping Grandpa.

**Michael's comeback**: "Well that's what you taught me. You should always win, shouldn't make it easy on them."

**Bowman**: "Guilty as charged."

### Same as Losing

**Another family moment**:

Bowman over after ASU's big meet at Cal (tied 150-150, crazy fast dual meet).

**Bowman telling Michael about the meet**: "Yeah, we went up there, crazy weather, we had this meet and we tied."

**Michael**: "What? How does that happen?"

**Bowman**: "I don't know, it's like 150 to 150 were the points."

**Right about that time, Boomer (age 7) walks in**:

**Boomer**: "You tied?"

**Bowman**: "Yeah."

**Boomer**: "Same as losing." *[Walks off]*

**Michael**: "That's right, Boom. That's exactly what I would say."

**Bowman**: "Can Grandpa get a little love around here?"

**The culture**: Everything in that house is a competition. You either win or lose - there aren't any ties.

**Everything has a "world record"** - no matter what it is.

### The Impact on Coaching

**Asked if relationship with the boys has impacted his coaching**:

> "Without question. Whenever I think I'm going to start getting pretty hard on somebody, I picture Boomer. I talk to them like I would talk to Boomer."

**Also applies to self-talk**:

> "I talk to myself like I would talk to them. I used to talk to myself very badly—'you're so dumb, why don't you do this,' all the time, all day long."

**Read**: *Permission to Feel* by Marc Brackett (professor at Yale)

**Started thinking about**: How I speak to myself.

**The principle**: You should speak to yourself like somebody you love, not like some idiot.

**This has changed** - giving himself some grace, being kinder to himself.

## Evolution as a Coach

### The Tool Box Metaphor

> "My coaching toolbox is much bigger now. In the beginning, all I had was a hammer, so everything looked like a nail. I just hammered everything."

*[To Katie Meili, referring to 2009]: "That was my worst. I always feel bad about that. I don't think any of us were at our best in 2009. We were kind of both collectively at a not great place."*

### The ASU Story

**Practice one day**: People got off on the intervals.

**Bowman had to explain**: "Okay, you got to watch the interval, we're going to be going on this every time, da da da."

**One lane got off. Another lane got off.** Next thing: all six long course lanes going on different intervals.

**Bowman stopped everybody**: "Look, we went over this. Do it again, on the top. Start over."

**He said it basically like that** - calmly, directly.

**ASU kids after practice** (talking to Chase): "Wow, Bob really yelled at us."

**Chase's response**: "What? You don't know anything."

**The truth**: Bowman doesn't do any of that anymore. Texas swimmers have **never heard him lose his control**.

### How He's Changed: Delivery

**The basic knowledge is the same**:
- Science of swimming
- How to put together a practice
- Learned from David Marsh, Murray Stevens, Jon Urbanchek and other Olympic-level coaches
- Took the best from each and combined them

**What's changed most**: How he delivers it.

> "If you ask the kids... these people here have never heard me ever kind of lose my control. That's the best part that I have done."

**More patient**:
- Realizes he can't control everything
- Can control himself
- Can't control swimmers
- Can influence, try to get them to do things

**The shift**: From pushing incessantly every second of every day to creating an environment where swimmers are **motivated to do it themselves**.

### Building Trust Through Honesty

**The biggest part of trust is honesty**:

Never had a problem letting people know who he is and what he's about. Used to do it in an aggressive way, like a machine gun.

**Now**: Does it in a more respectful and tactful way.

**Example**:

**Old way**: "Just do this." (Command)

**New way**: "Look, if we really want to achieve these goals, we're going to have to do this. Right now you're here, really need to see this. In my opinion, I believe we got to see this. What do you think about that?"

**Swimmer**: "Yeah, probably need to do that."

**The result**: They know if he's telling them something they don't want to hear:
1. He's trying to do it in a way where they're more receptive
2. He's being honest about what's needed
3. It's a conversation, not a command

**Athletes say the word that keeps coming up**: **TRUST**

They all have so much trust in Bowman as a coach, and that being the foundation.

## The Three Words: Empathy

**Final question**: If you had to pick one word that represents something you're working on, focusing on, trying to do as you move forward, what would that word be for you and why?

**Bowman's answer (after long pause)**: **"Empathy."**

**Elaboration**: "Try to have it. Try to live that. For not only others, but also for myself and others."

**The full circle**:
- Started as a hammer
- Now building empathy for athletes
- Also building empathy for himself
- Treating himself with the same kindness he'd give the boys
- Speaking to himself like somebody he loves

---

## Key Themes

1. **The dream job required sacrifice** - Couldn't negotiate, had to keep it secret, had to act fast
2. **Family shaped the coach** - The Phelps boys (especially being "Grandpa") fundamentally changed how Bowman coaches and talks to himself
3. **Evolution through empathy** - From "hammer" to having a full toolbox, prioritizing understanding over control
4. **Trust through honesty** - Direct, respectful communication builds the foundation
5. **Leadership in crisis** - Managing roster cuts with transparency, love, and objectivity
6. **The competitive culture persists** - Even in family life, the "same as losing" mindset prevails (but with joy and playfulness)
