# Writing Swim Workouts: Bob Bowman's Approach

**Source:** SwimSwam Podcast with Bob Bowman, Michael Bohl, Vince Raleigh  
**Context:** Three Olympic gold medal coaches discuss workout design philosophy  
**Date:** Oct 2022 (ASU era)

## Bowman's Core Workout Structure

### The 3-Part Formula

**1. Warm-Up (30% of practice)**
- Gradually warm joints, especially shoulders
- Reinforce good technique
- Get swimmers mentally ready for main challenge
- May include: swimming, kicking, pulling, drilling

**2. Main Set (50% of practice)**
- Primary objective for that practice
- Target specific energy system:
  - Threshold/endurance
  - Anaerobic/high-quality
  - Mixed systems
- Consider: energy system, strokes, event specificity
- "This is where the magic happens"

**3. Cool Down/Secondary Work (20% of practice)**
- Small series of sprints
- Technical work
- Very long recovery swim
- Keep it light after main effort

---

## The "Long Preliminary" Strategy

**Bowman's Wednesday (ASU):** 3,500y preliminary work before main set
- Extensive warm-up
- Underwater kicking
- Pulling set

**Why?** "We do these long preliminary things so we can get most of the stuff done before the main set, because then after we don't have to put in too much stuff."

**Benefit:** Swimmers leave practice having done quality main work without excessive fatigue afterward

---

## Progression Philosophy: When to Build Up vs. Break Down

### Early Season: Build Up (2-4-6-8)
- Goal: **Speed endurance**
- Start with 2 reps to establish stroke/speed
- Add reps each round while holding quality
- "Trying to extend it, not get faster every round"

### Late Season/Spring: Break Down (8-6-4-2)
- Goal: **Pure speed**
- Start with volume, get faster each round
- Reverse fatigue → sharpen turnover
- "Try to get them to go faster as they went through"

**Bowman:** "I do sometimes [reverse it], and probably in the spring it would be backwards."

## Famous Sets: The Grant Hackett Set

### Structure
**40 × 50s (can be any stroke)**
- 16 × 50 on :45 — every 4th fast
- 12 × 50 on :50 — every 3rd fast
- 8 × 50 on :55 — every 2nd fast
- 4 × 50 on 1:00 — all out

### Why Bowman Loves It
1. **Develops pace** — Gradually harder to maintain as rest decreases
2. **Super versatile:**
   - Single stroke (all fly, all free, etc.)
   - Split strokes (16 fly + 12 back + 8 breast + 4 free)
   - IM rotation (fly-back-breast-free repeating)
3. **Kids love it** — Always get good performances

**Origin:** Dennis Cotterell created it; Grant Hackett made it famous

**Why not "Michael Phelps Set"?** Bowman: "That's just too much." 😂

## Breath Control: Purpose & Application

### Not for Conditioning
"When we're doing a breathing pattern, it's **not so much for conditioning** as it is for **stroke discipline**."

### Primary Uses
1. **Balance** — Breathing every 3/5/7 to breathe both sides
2. **Focus** — Have to think about what they're doing instead of autopilot
3. **Underwater conditioning** — Learn to do it right (in controlled fashion)

### Example: Underwater Work
**ASU set:** 25s starting on :30, descend to :20
- 2 fly (2 strokes right, 2 strokes left)
- 2 underwater kick
- Several rounds on :20

**Key:** "Challenging but certainly doable, not something that puts them in jeopardy"

## Group Structure: Homogeneous vs. Mixed

### Club Days (North Baltimore)
- Large mixed-ability group
- Many different things going on
- Had to split constantly

### College/Elite (ASU/Texas)
- More homogeneous group (~16 athletes)
- Usually 2 things happening max
- Example: "IMs doing breaststroke, non-breaststrokers doing fly"

**Bowman:** "It's not quite as many things going on now as it used to be."

## Michael Bohl's Approach (Emma McKeon's coach)

### Group Breakdown
- **Distance group:** 1500m swimmers + open water
- **Middle distance (main):** 11-12 athletes (primary focus)
- **Sprint group:** 3-4 athletes

### Philosophy
"Really try and give swimmers work that's going to **promote and get them better in their particular event**."

**Old days:** Everyone did the same thing (Bill/Laurie era)  
**Now:** Highly individualized

**Common elements:**
- Warm-up together
- Sometimes dry land before practice as a group
- Main sets split by event group

## Vince Raleigh's Approach (Zac Stubblety-Cook's coach)

### 200 Breaststroke Set Example
**Heart rate zones by stroke:**
- 3 × 100 on 2:00 @ aerobic 2
- 2 × 100 @ aerobic 3
- 1 × 100 @ threshold
- 6 × 50 on :65 @ 200 pace + race rate
- 100 easy
- **Repeat 3-4 rounds**

**50s target:** Pushing 32-low or better

### Individual Variation
"Not everybody in breaststroke can do the **same amount of breaststroke**."
- Zac Stubblety-Cook: Great range of motion, can handle 4 breaststroke sessions/week
- Brenton Rickard (2008-09): Less propensity for breaststroke volume, only 2 sessions/week

**Lesson:** Find each breaststroker's capacity

## Emma McKeon Front-End Speed Set (Michael Bohl)

### Block of 3s (repeated 4x)
1. **25 dive + 25 easy** on 1:30
2. **Push 35 + 15 easy** on 1:15
3. **50 fast (push)** trying to hit back-end speed
4. **Easy 300** between rounds

### Performance Standards (Emma)
- 25 dive: 11.2-11.3
- 35 push: 17-high to 18-low
- 50 push: Under 26.5

**Purpose:** Develop front-end speed AND back-end speed together

---

## The Hawkeye Step Test (Michael Bohl)

**Format (short course meters):**
- 4 × 100 on 1:30 → 100 easy
- 3 × 100 on 1:45 (faster than previous) → 200-300 easy
- 2 × 100 on 2:00 (max effort) → 300-400 easy
- 1 × 100 max from push

**Purpose:** Monitor improvement over season  
**Track:** Average times in each block get faster as season progresses

## Australian Sprint Development (Bohl)

### Why So Many Sub-53 Women?
1. **History** — Shannon Rawlinson/Jody Henry era (2004): 4-6 women at 53-54
2. **Standard setting** — To make Paris team: sub-52
3. **National event camps** — Simon Cusack-led sprint camps
4. **Modeling** — Kate & Bronte Campbell showed the non-negotiables
5. **Coach education** — Female coaches saw exactly what top girls did in training

**Upward pressure:** "You can either complain about it or do something about it"

---

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Structure:** 30% warm-up, 50% main, 20% cool/secondary  
✅ **Progression:** Build up early season, break down late season  
✅ **Breath control:** For stroke discipline, not conditioning  
✅ **Versatility:** Use sets like Grant Hackett that work multiple ways  
✅ **Individualize:** Different events = different training needs  
✅ **Long preliminary:** Get volume done before main quality work
