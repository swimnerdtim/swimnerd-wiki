## Who Is Cam McEvoy?

Cameron McEvoy (born 1994) is an Australian sprint freestyle swimmer who revolutionized sprint training through an ultra-low-volume, strength-based approach. After retiring post-Rio 2016, he returned in October 2022 with coach/partner Tim Lane and went from 22.8 to Olympic gold (21.25) in under 2 years.

**Career highlights:**
- **2024:** Olympic gold 50m freestyle (21.25) — Australia's first ever
- **2023:** World Champion 50m freestyle (21.10)
- **2025:** World Champion 50m freestyle (defended title at age 31)
- **Training:** 3-4x/week swimming, 1-2k per session
- **Philosophy:** Quality over quantity, strength-based, technique-obsessed

---

## Core Philosophy

**The revolution:**
- 3-4 swim sessions per week (vs traditional 9-10)
- 1-2k per session (vs traditional 30-40k/week)
- Zero aerobic base work
- Heavy strength training (pull-ups toward 2.0x bodyweight)
- Resistance swimming as bridge from gym to pool
- All work at or near race pace

**The principle:**
> "Traditional volume creates fitness for finishing a 9-session week, not fitness for finishing a 21-second race."

## Quick Reference

### Training Week (Race-Specific Phase)
- **Monday:** Gym + suited 35m sprints (3-5 reps)
- **Tuesday:** OFF or technique
- **Wednesday:** Technique session
- **Thursday:** Gym + suited 25m sprints (4-6 reps)
- **Friday:** Technique or OFF
- **Saturday:** Technique or light work
- **Sunday:** OFF

**Total volume:** 300-500m sprint volume per week

### Strength Benchmarks
- **Pull-ups:** Progressing toward 2.0x bodyweight (77.5kg at 85kg BW)
- **Vertical jump:** 48cm → 63cm (direct correlation to 15m time)
- **Trap bar deadlift:** Heavy singles for maintenance
- **Gym frequency:** 3x/week (strength phase), 2x/week (race phase)

### Technique Anchor Points
1. **Hollow-body core activation** — never breaks through entire stroke
2. **Overhead stretch position** — hit at every full extension
3. **Rib rotation** — not just hip rotation
4. **Kick timing** — deep kick links to opposite arm entry
5. **Diagonal pressure** — down and back, not just back

### Resistance Guidelines
- **Heavy block:** 2-3x/week, 150-300m per session
- **Power phase:** 2x/week, 100-200m per session
- **Race phase:** 0-1x/week, 50-100m optional
- **Recovery:** Minimum 48 hours between resistance sessions

## The Paradigm Shifts

### Old thinking → New thinking

**Volume:**
- Old: More is better (30-40k/week)
- New: Quality over quantity (1-2k/week at race pace)

**Age:**
- Old: Peak at 22-24
- New: Peak when optimized (30+ possible)

**Coaching:**
- Old: Coach writes program, athlete executes
- New: Athlete leads, coach guides

**Taper:**
- Old: Rest into trials, build to Olympics
- New: Extend race-pace exposure post-trials

**Recovery:**
- Old: Push through mental fatigue
- New: Mental breaks are part of training
