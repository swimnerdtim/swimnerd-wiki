# Cam McEvoy: The Paris 2024 Olympic Journey
**From retirement to Olympic gold — the complete story**

**Source:** Brett Hawke podcast, Inside with Brett Hawke (2024)  
**Result:** 21.25 Olympic gold, Australia's first-ever men's 50m freestyle champion  
**Age:** 30 years old

---

## The Comeback Timeline

**2016:** Rio Olympics disappointment → retirement  
**2022 (October):** Return to swimming, first 50m: 22.8  
**2023 (May):** Breakthrough 21.8 at Sydney Open  
**2023 (July):** 21.10 World Champion (Fukuoka)  
**2024 (February):** 21.06 heat at Doha (beat by Bokov in final)  
**2024 (June):** 21.3 at Australian Trials  
**2024 (August):** 21.25 Olympic gold (Paris)

---

## The Doha Learning Experience (February 2024)

### What Happened

**The races:**
- **Heat:** 21.06 (PR) — went ALL OUT
- **Semi:** 21.13 — went ALL OUT again
- **Final:** 21.17 — beaten by Bokov (21.14)

**The mistake:**
> "It doesn't matter how fit you are. If you're at 100% max and go max again 12 hours later, then max again, fitness can't repair that."

### What They Learned

**The "rep in reserve" concept:**
- Can't go max in every round
- Heat/semi: Controlled effort, qualify for final
- Final: NOW go for gold

**Ben Proud's model:**
- Always gets faster from heat → semi → final
- That's strategy, not luck
- Must have energy in reserve for the final

**Parallel to gym:**
> "You do a 5RM squat, come back 12 hours later and hit it again—you'll probably still do it. But come back the next day for a third time at 100% max? Not happening. Same in the 50m."

## The Pre-Paris Training Decision

### The Standard Approach (Rejected)

**Traditional path:**
1. Taper into trials
2. Rest after trials (recovery from competition)
3. Travel to Europe with team
4. Slowly build back up over weeks
5. Taper again for Olympics

### The McEvoy/Lane Approach (Chosen)

**Their path:**
1. Taper into trials
2. Hit 21.3-21.4 at trials (first exposure to low-21 speed)
3. Get back in pool 2-3 days later
4. Train AT that pace for 3-4 weeks (maximum time at race speed)
5. Stay in Brisbane (don't travel to Europe)
6. Then rest/freshen 2-3 weeks into Olympics

**The rationale:**
> "Trials is the first time we get exposure to low-21 speed. We don't want to let that go. Extend trials out for 3-4 weeks, get as much volume as we can at that speed, THEN freshen up."

### Getting Permission

**The conversation with Rowan Taylor (Swimming Australia head coach):**
- Cam and Tim presented their plan
- Not about "doing their own thing"
- About maximizing Cam's specific performance needs
- Rowan: "Performance-first approach will be taken when merited"
- Approval given

**Tim Lane:**
> "It could have easily been viewed as the wrong choice if Paris wasn't successful. But Rowan's willingness to approach it this way was greatly appreciated."

**The outcome:**
- Arrived in Paris 2 weeks after the rest of the team
- Seamless integration
- Gold medal

## The Mental Break (April-May 2024)

### The Problem

**Post-Doha burnout:**
- Cam was mentally fried from constant microscope on training
- Motivation to suit up and chase times was declining
- Olympics approaching in 3-4 months
- Traditional thinking: "It's an Olympic year, grind through it"

### The Counterintuitive Decision

**What they did:**
- Took mental break from timed work
- Continued physical training
- Stopped analyzing every rep
- Swam in regular suit, not tech suit
- Reduced pressure

**Tim's philosophy:**
> "We had two options: grind through because it's an Olympic year, or take a step back mentally. We chose the mental break."

**Cam's Bulgarian method parallel:**
- Weightlifters using Bulgarian method (multiple daily max lifts)
- Expected to go through 18-24 month depressed periods
- Not psychological weakness — biological consequence of neural fatigue
- Swimming max sprints has same neurological impact

### The Scary Part

**Mid-May doubts:**
- "Did we make the wrong decision?"
- Olympics in 3 months
- Everyone else grinding
- Going against conventional wisdom

**The result:**
- Refreshed by June
- Peak performance at trials
- Gold at Olympics

## The Race Strategy

### Heat: 21.32 — Rep in Reserve

**Execution:**
- Controlled first 25m (NOT all-out like Doha)
- Smooth, efficient
- Qualified easily
- Kept energy for semi

**Post-race feeling:**
- Shocked at how comfortable it felt
- Same 25m split as Olympic final would be
- Validation of the approach

### Semi: 21.28 — Still in Reserve

**Execution:**
- Slightly faster than heat
- Still controlled
- Still had gears left
- Mission: Get lane in final with max energy

**Strategic goal:**
> "Hit the heat, hit the semi, and you just want to get that lane in the final with as much energy in reserve as you can."

### Final: 21.25 — Go for Gold

**Pre-race:**
- Flow-Lévy getting the crowd clapping
- Cam: "90-95% of the crowd was eyes on Flo, so I can just sit back and do what I've trained for"
- Crowd energy was incredible
- Zero pressure—all eyes on someone else

**Execution:**
- First 49 meters: "Felt pretty nice"
- Last meter: "Finish was horrific"
  - Crossed over
  - Long reach
  - Panic
  - "Pure heart and soul, just spin and get your hand on the wall"

**The result:**
- 21.25
- 0.02 ahead of Ben Proud (21.27)
- Australia's first-ever Olympic men's 50m freestyle gold

## The Significance

### For Australia

**Historic firsts:**
- First Australian man to win Olympic 50m freestyle
- Oldest Australian to win World Championship 50m freestyle (32 at Singapore 2025)
- Proved low-volume, high-quality approach works at highest level

### For Sprint Swimming

**Paradigm shifts proven:**
1. **Volume:** Less can be more (3-4x/week vs. 9-10x/week)
2. **Age:** 30+ swimmers can peak (with right approach)
3. **Coaching:** Athlete-led programming can work
4. **Taper:** Extending race-pace exposure works
5. **Recovery:** Mental breaks are training

### For Cam Personally

**Redemption:**
- Rewrote Olympic narrative after Rio disappointment
- Proved his approach to himself and the world
- "Took the chip off my shoulder"

**Family:**
- Got engaged post-Paris
- Had first son (Hartley)
- Balanced elite performance with life
- "I just wanted to kick back and relax"

**Legacy:**
> "There's a lot of joy in packaging this and putting it out for others to learn from. Seeing people hit lifetime PBs—people in their mid-40s who retired 20 years ago coming back and loving the sport. That's massively rewarding."

## Post-Paris: The Singapore Defense (August 2025)

### The Training Block

**October-December 2024:**
- Extended gym block (heaviest ever)
- 1-2x/week swimming: 6 x 10m with fins + 3kg resistance
- Total weekly volume: ~120m
- Body weight: 87kg (Paris) → 92kg (Singapore prep)
- Focus: Maximum strength and mobility

**December test:**
- Japan Open: 21.70 (fastest in-season ever)
- 25m split = same as Olympics
- Proved top speed maintained with minimal swimming

**January-July 2025:**
- Last week of March: Back to swimming "properly"
- 4x/week swimming, 3x/week gym
- Monday/Thursday: Suited sprinting
- Tuesday/Friday: Technique + bodyweight adaptation
- Total volume per session: 1-2k

### Singapore World Championships

**Result:** Successfully defended world title  
**Significance:** Proved Paris wasn't a fluke

## Lessons from Paris

### 1. Trust the Process

**Even when it seems crazy:**
- Training 3-4x/week when others do 9-10x
- Taking mental breaks 3 months before Olympics
- Staying home when team travels to Europe
- All felt counterintuitive—all worked

### 2. Relationship > Outcomes

**The foundation:**
- Tim's initial goal: Help Cam leave sport with better relationship
- Not about medals or times
- About sustainable excellence
- About wellbeing first

**The result:**
- Got the medals AND the relationship
- Cam is still enjoying the sport
- Planning to continue to LA 2028

### 3. Strategy Matters in the 50m

**The myth:** "The 50m is a crapshoot"

**The reality:**
- Pacing strategy is critical
- Rep in reserve through rounds
- Energy management over 3 swims
- Execute the plan under pressure

### 4. The Athlete Can Lead

**What worked:**
- Cam designed the training
- Tim provided expertise and guidance
- Collaborative refinement
- Cam made final calls
- Tim supported execution

**Why it worked:**
- Clear roles
- Mutual respect
- No ego
- Shared values
- Trust
