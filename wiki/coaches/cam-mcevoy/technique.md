# Cam McEvoy's 50m Freestyle Technique
**The revolutionary technique that won Olympic gold**

**Source:** Multiple interviews and technical discussions (2023-2024)  
**Context:** Technique development October 2022 → May 2023, refined through Paris 2024  
**Result:** 21.10 world championship, 21.25 Olympic gold

---

## The Starting Point: October 2022

**First 50m back:** 22.8 — "basically a sped-up 200 freestyle stroke"

**The problem:**
- No 50m-specific technique
- Stroke rate ~55-56 (vs race 60-63)
- Swimming "long" like a 200 swimmer
- No aggression, no power application

**Tim Lane's immediate cues:**
- Open up the stroke
- Get above the water
- Add aggression
- Increase stroke rate

**Timeline:** Took 8 months (Oct 2022 → May 2023) to develop race-ready 50m technique.

## The Two Core Anchor Points

### Anchor Point #1: Hollow-Body Core Activation

**Origin:** Gymnastics hollow-body hold

**The setup:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet on ground
2. Lift tailbone off ground
3. Engage core and lats
4. Feel the full-body tension

**In the water:**
1. Float with pool buoy
2. Scull downward
3. Replicate the hollow-body lift
4. Extend legs only as far as you can maintain activation

**The goal:**
- Maintain this activation THROUGH THE ENTIRE STROKE
- Core is the anchor point for all limb movement
- Limbs move around a rigid, stable core

**Most difficult part:** Holding activation through the recovery phase
- Easy to activate during pull
- Easy to lose during recovery
- Must maintain the "isometric contraction" throughout

**McEvoy:**
> "There's like an isometric part to the movement where the whole body is under one contraction that doesn't break throughout the whole 50. And then a dynamic part on top where your body moves around that isometric point."

### Anchor Point #2: Overhead Stretch Position

**The mobility drill:**
1. Thumbs into armpits (deep)
2. Lift shoulders as high as possible
3. Elevate scapulae
4. Expand ribs outward
5. Let go of one arm, extend forward
6. Feel the activation and stretch

**In freestyle:**
- Hit this position at EVERY full extension
- Both arms, every stroke
- Maintain the rib expansion and scap elevation
- This is the "stretch flat" position

**Training the feeling:**
- 30 seconds on the wall before every push-off
- Head underwater, arms in position
- Find the activation
- Push off ONLY when you can hold it
- Try to maintain for as many strokes as possible

**Progressive development:**
- Week 1-2: Hold for half a rep
- Week 3-4: Hold for one full rep
- Week 5-8: Hold for 5+ reps
- Months later: Maintain through entire 50m

## The Rotation Pattern

### NOT Traditional Hip Rotation

**Traditional teaching:** "Rotate your hips"

**McEvoy's approach:** Rib rotation with hip rotation as byproduct

**The movement:**
- Flare one rib outward sideways ("like a bracket")
- This creates body rotation
- Hips follow, but aren't the driver

**Visual:**
- Neutral: | (straight up and down)
- Right side rotation: ) (rib expands right)
- Left side rotation: ( (rib expands left)

**Why this matters:**
- Allows overhead stretch position to be maintained
- Creates more torso involvement
- Engages lats and core differently than hip-driven rotation

## The Kick: Timing Over Propulsion

### McEvoy's Kick Pattern

**6-beat kick with variations:**
- 2 shallow kicks
- 1 deep kick
- 2 shallow kicks
- 1 deep kick

**Timing:**
- Deep kick on same side as pulling arm
- Right deep kick → right arm catch
- Left deep kick → left arm catch

**The deep kick timing:**
> "It's like a single-arm butterfly kick at the end of my stroke. The deep kick happens just before the opposite arm enters."

### Purpose of the Kick

**NOT for forward propulsion (in the 50m)**

**Primary purposes:**
1. **Balance** — maintaining body position
2. **Timing** — linking upper body rotation
3. **Anchoring** — creating reaction point (like ground in sprinting)
4. **Torque generation** — stretch-shortening cycle in rotation

**The stretch-shortening cycle:**
- Body is neutral
- Deep kick provides torque
- Body bends into rotation
- Snaps back out quickly
- Like using kick as the "ground" to push off

**McEvoy's underwater position:**
- Flat from head to hips
- Hips drop slightly
- Knees dig deeper than most sprinters
- "I dig my knees into the bottom of the pool"

**Rationale:**
> "Surely that creates more drag, but the cost-benefit of the lift I get from that downward pressure... if I flattened out and couldn't have that semi-downward pressure, I might go slower."

### Kick Training

**Amount:** ZERO isolated kick training

**McEvoy:**
- No kickboard work
- No kick sets with snorkel
- Only kick exposure is within full-stroke swimming

**Exception:** 50m kick with fins in warmup
- Not for kick development
- For body position activation
- Hands on board, get into the position, activate

**Why no kick training for 50m:**
- Not an aerobic effort (don't need metabolic kick conditioning)
- Kick timing must be practiced with full stroke
- Isolated kick can interfere with coordination
- Time better spent on full-stroke volume

**For 100m freestylers:** Kick training makes sense (aerobic/anaerobic conditioning)

## Pressure and "Lift" in the Water

### Sculling Downward, Not Just Backward

**Traditional focus:** Press backward (horizontal force)

**McEvoy's focus:** Press diagonally downward

**The feeling:**
- Constant downward pressure toward pool bottom
- Creates lift in the water
- Gets body higher on surface

**Visual result:**
- At Singapore warmup: "I've never seen a human body that high out of the water"
- Brett Hawke: "I could see your hamstrings. With fins I swear I saw your quads."

**The trade-off:**
- Downward force = less backward force
- But the lift enables better body position
- Better body position = less drag
- Net result: Faster despite "losing" backward force

## The "Falling" Concept

### What Is "Falling"?

**Santos Silva's term:** "Stay on top" vs. "I fell"

**What it means:**
- Losing the isometric core activation
- Breaking away from the anchored position
- Usually happens during recovery phase

**When it happens:**
- Stroke 3-4 in a 50m when fatigued
- When you "miss" a stroke (hand wobbles)
- When you push too hard too early

**How to avoid it:**
- Maintain core activation through recovery
- Don't overspin the first 25m
- Stay "on top" of the water throughout

**McEvoy:**
> "The most difficult thing was holding onto the activation in the recovery. It was super easy to let go and refind it upon entry. But holding through the entire recovery—that's the technique."

## Stroke Rate and Tempo

### McEvoy's Stroke Rate

**Training (submax):** ~58 SPM  
**Racing:** 60-63 SPM

**The narrow window:**
- Only 2-5 SPM difference between training and racing
- Staying close to race tempo even in practice
- Not training at 50 SPM and racing at 63

### Tempo vs. Force

**The balance:**
- Can't just spin your arms faster (tempo without force)
- Can't just pull harder (force without tempo)
- Must increase both simultaneously

**Development progression:**
1. Build max strength (gym work)
2. Learn to apply strength at speed (resistance swimming)
3. Maintain strength while increasing tempo (bodyweight sprints)
4. Peak both for race day

## Technique Under Fatigue

### The 35-Meter Breakdown

**Universal sprint phenomenon:**
- ~35m mark: technique coordination breaks down
- Feels like crossing a threshold
- Last 15m is a different zone than first 35m

**McEvoy's goals:**
1. Extend the breakdown point further (push from 35m to 40m)
2. Make the breakdown less severe (hold stroke better)
3. Improve 5m splits in the last 15m

**Training for this:**
- Sprint-float-sprint protocol (max 0-15, easy 15-25, max 25-35)
- Teaches coordination at speed when semi-fatigued
- Often produces faster 35m times than all-out 35m

### The Finish

**Paris final:**
- McEvoy's finish was "horrific"
- Long reach, crossed over, panicking
- "Just get your hand on the wall"

**Channeling Dan Wiffen's 800m last 15m:**
> "Everything went out the window. Pure heart and soul for every stroke. Just spin and hope for the best."

**The takeaway:** Even perfect technique breaks down. That's okay—everyone experiences it.

## Technique Development Process

### The Obsessive Phase (Oct 2022 - May 2023)

**Daily process:**
1. Arrive at pool
2. 30 seconds on wall finding activation
3. Won't push off until the feeling is there
4. Push off (sometimes not even in streamline to hold activation)
5. Swim as long as activation can be held
6. Repeat

**Frequency:** 5x/week during initial development

**Volume:** Every single rep was technique-focused
- No "garbage yardage"
- No easy swimming
- Every push-off was intentional

### Maintenance Phase (May 2023 onwards)

**Frequency:** 3-4x/week

**Focus:**
- Submax swimming (7-8 RPE)
- Stroke rate ~58 (vs race 60-63)
- Maintaining feel and coordination
- Preventing degradation

**Why less frequency works:**
> "If you're adapted to 3-4x/week, 48 hours off feels normal. The myth of losing feel comes from swimming 9-10x/week, then taking 48 hours off—of course that feels bad."

## Dryland for Technique

### The Ab Wheel (Standing)

**McEvoy's challenge:**
- Can't do full standing ab wheel without band
- Wants to maintain shoulder elevation + rib expansion
- Locking lats down makes it easier, but defeats swimming purpose

**Regression:** Wall tap ab wheel
- Ab wheel close to wall
- Get in elevated shoulder position
- Decrease ROM by adjusting distance from wall
- Tap wall, return
- Progress by backing up

### Overhead Dumbbell Shrug (Single-Arm)

**Setup:**
- One dumbbell overhead
- Shrug straight up
- Can add rib expansion (sideways)

**Why it works:**
- Mimics overhead position in stroke
- Builds isometric strength at that angle
- Can be paired with cable internal rotation

### Squat + Overhead Press (Banded)

**Setup:**
- Full squat (slant board for ankle mobility)
- Resistance band around wrists
- Hold light weight (5-10kg)
- From bottom of squat: fully extend overhead into stretch position

**Why it works:**
- Band pulls arms back (must press forward and down)
- Squat position challenges mobility
- Hip flexion prevents arching back
- Forces movement from ribs and shoulders

### Front Lever

**McEvoy connection:**
- Isometric lat strength
- Core engaged
- Similar to mid-pull position
- Holding body rigid while creating force

## The Final Technique Evolution

### What Changed From Fukuoka to Paris

**Fukuoka (July 2023):** 21.10  
**Paris (August 2024):** 21.25

**Differences:**
1. Better pacing (heat/semi/final strategy)
2. More consistent technique under pressure
3. Refined first 15m execution
4. Better finish (despite "terrible" Paris finish—still won)

**The reality:** Technique is never "done"
- Always evolving
- Small refinements each session
- Context-dependent (fresh vs. fatigued)
- Individual to body type and strengths

## Key Technique Principles (Summary)

### ✅ **Maintain Core Activation Throughout**
- Hollow-body engagement
- Don't "fall" during recovery
- Anchor point for all movement

### ✅ **Hit Overhead Stretch Every Stroke**
- Full extension with rib expansion
- Scap elevation maintained
- This is the power position

### ✅ **Rib Rotation, Not Just Hip Rotation**
- Expand one rib sideways
- Hips follow as byproduct
- Engages lats and core differently

### ✅ **Kick for Timing and Balance**
- Deep kick links to opposite arm entry
- Not for propulsion in 50m
- Creates anchoring and torque

### ✅ **Press Diagonally Down**
- Not just horizontal backward
- Creates lift
- Enables higher body position

### ✅ **Develop Technique Obsessively, Then Maintain**
- 8 months to build the pattern
- 3-4x/week to maintain
- Every rep with intention
