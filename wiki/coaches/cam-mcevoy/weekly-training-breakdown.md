# Weekly Training Breakdown

A detailed look at Cam McEvoy's training week leading up to his world record 50m freestyle performance.

## Overview

McEvoy's training week consists of extremely low volume, high-intensity sessions totaling around 1,500m per week—less than most competitors swim in a single warm-up. This represents a radical departure from traditional high-volume training programs.

## Monday: Pool Session - Sprint Speed Work

**Location:** Queensland Academy of Sport pool
**Duration:** ~1 hour
**Total Distance:** ~5km

### Session Structure

**Warm-up:**
- 300m loosen
- 2 warm-up reps:
  - 1x25 at body weight (submax speed)
  - 1x20m with 2kg resistance on 1080 machine

**Main Set:**
- 1x25 max effort off blocks (body weight, no resistance)
- 4 reps with resistance:
  - 2x20m @ 1kg resistance
  - 2x20m @ 2kg resistance
- Optional finisher (depending on feel):
  - 1x25 body weight max, OR
  - 2x20m @ 5kg resistance

### Key Metrics Tracked
- 10m splits
- 15m splits
- 20m splits
- 25m times
- Stroke rates (target: 62+ strokes per minute)
- Segment times (target: under 43 seconds for certain reps)

### Training Partners
- **Grayson:** Breaststroker
- **Josh:** Junior sprint freestyler (one of the hottest in the world)
- **I (from Japan):** Butterfly sprinter

### Coaching Staff
- **Tim Lane:** Head coach on deck
- **Gerine:** Sports scientist tracking metrics

### Session Notes
- McEvoy didn't hit target times until the 5th rep
- Stroke rate initially low at 60, improved to 62+ on final two reps
- Session ended when McEvoy started feeling good (9:50 on final rep) - Tim Lane shut it down to preserve energy for the week
- Recovery in hot tub post-session

## Tuesday: Gym Session - Resistance Training

**Focus:** Singles only (never doubles or higher reps)
**Philosophy:** Maximum strength development without hypertrophy

This session focuses on heavy compound movements at low reps to build pure strength without adding muscle mass, which would increase drag in the water.

## Wednesday-Sunday

Additional sessions throughout the week follow similar principles:
- Extremely low volume
- Maximum intensity
- Heavy resistance work
- Precise metric tracking
- Strategic recovery periods

## Training Volume Context

**Traditional high-volume programs:** Up to 70,000m per week

**McEvoy's current approach:** Never exceeds 1,500m per week

This represents a 98% reduction in swimming volume while maintaining (and improving) world-class speed.

## The "McEvoy Method" Philosophy

The approach is built on the principle that **quality dramatically outweighs quantity** for sprint events. By focusing exclusively on race-pace and faster swimming, McEvoy eliminates all "junk" volume that doesn't directly translate to 50m performance.

### Key Differentiators from Traditional Training:
- Zero aerobic base work
- No middle-distance sets
- No threshold or "red zone" training
- Only race pace or faster
- Heavy use of resistance technology (1080 machine)
- Extensive strength training
- Strategic recovery prioritization

## Facility Technology

The Queensland Academy of Sport pool is a 4-lane, 25m facility equipped with:
- 1080 Sprint resistance machines
- Advanced timing systems
- Real-time metric tracking boards
- Video analysis capabilities

Despite being a smaller facility, the technology integration allows for precise measurement and optimization of every aspect of sprint performance.
