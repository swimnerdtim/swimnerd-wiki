## About This Collection

This is a complete transcription of Jon Urbanchek's daily workout plans from the 1995 NCAA Championship season at University of Michigan. This season ended Michigan's 34-year championship drought and stands as one of the greatest team performances in NCAA swimming history.

**What makes these workouts special:**
- Distance-focused training philosophy that proved you could win NCAAs without sprint emphasis
- Color-coded energy system (white/pink/red/blue/purple/green)
- Threshold-based training with individualized zones
- Multiple lane groups with customized intervals
- High volume (6,000-8,000+ yards per session)
- Mental/psychological coaching notes embedded in workouts
- Team culture building through structure

## The Color-Coded Energy System

Urbanchek's revolutionary visual training intensity system:

| Color | Zone | Description | Heart Rate | Lactate | Usage |
|-------|------|-------------|------------|---------|-------|
| **WHITE** | Aerobic Easy | Warm-up pace, easy aerobic | ~120-140 | <2 mmol | Recovery, technique |
| **PINK** | Moderate Aerobic | Building effort, conversation pace | ~140-160 | 2-3 mmol | Base building |
| **RED** | Threshold | Hard sustained effort, high HR | ~160-175 | 3-5 mmol | Threshold work |
| **BLUE** | Above Threshold | Crossing anaerobic threshold, lactate accumulation | ~175-185 | 5-8 mmol | VO2 max intervals |
| **PURPLE** | Maximum Effort | Near collapse, all-out | ~185+ | 8-12 mmol | Race pace, testing |
| **GREEN** | Lactate Tolerance | Fast until you puke! (3-10 min rest) | Max | 10-20+ mmol | Buffering capacity |

**Training Philosophy:**
- All intensities calculated from individual anaerobic threshold pace
- Threshold determined through testing (T30, 10x200 @ :20-30", 8x300 averaging)
- Color zones allow visual communication across pool deck
- Athletes learn to feel and recognize their zones

## Lane Group System

Urbanchek used multiple lane groups with customized intervals:

- **DIST:** Distance specialists (primarily freestylers, 500/1650 focus)
- **MIO / M.D.:** Mid-distance (200/500 free, 200/400 IM specialists)
- **BE:** Breaststroke specialists
- **BK:** Backstroke specialists
- **BT:** Butterfly specialists
- **OT:** Other/General group
- **Lanes 7-8:** Often designated for rookies or first-day-back swimmers

**Intervals varied by group:**
- Example: Same set might be @3:40 for DIST, @3:50 for MIO, @4:10 for BK, @4:30 for BT
- Customization allowed simultaneous training of different specialties
- Created internal competition and racing opportunities

## Training Methodology Highlights

### Volume Philosophy
- Regular 6,000-8,000 yard sessions
- Distance emphasis: 20-30 minute steady-state threshold swims
- High volume maintained through championship phase

### Threshold Testing Protocols
1. **T30 Test:** 30-minute continuous threshold swim
2. **10x200 @ :20-30":** Averaging pace for threshold
3. **8x300 Descending:** Threshold endurance
4. **Broken Swims:** Race pace with short rest (5-10")

### Set Structure Patterns
- **Warm-up:** 600-900 yards, technique emphasis ("LONG & PRETTY")
- **Pre-set:** Skills, drills, kick (often 400-600 yards)
- **Main Set:** Multi-part, lane-group specific (3,000-5,000 yards)
- **Cool Down:** Usually minimal, sometimes integrated into main set

### Psychological Elements
- Motivational notes written into workouts
- "GET ANGRY" mentality
- Team event announcements (Apple Party, etc.)
- International swimmer integration notes
- "COOPERATE!" - data collection emphasis

- Building team culture among elite competitors
