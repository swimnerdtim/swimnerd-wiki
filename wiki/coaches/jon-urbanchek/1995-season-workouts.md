# 1995 Michigan Championship Season - Workout Collection

**Coach:** Jon Urbanchek  
**Team:** University of Michigan Men's Swimming  
**Season:** 1994-1995 (NCAA Championship Season)  
**Source:** Original 189-page workout book  
**Championship Result:** 1995 NCAA Champions (561.5 - 475.5 over Stanford)

---

## About This Collection

This is a complete transcription of Jon Urbanchek's daily workout plans from the 1995 NCAA Championship season at University of Michigan. This season ended Michigan's 34-year championship drought and stands as one of the greatest team performances in NCAA swimming history.

**What makes these workouts special:**
- Distance-focused training philosophy that proved you could win NCAAs without sprint emphasis
- Color-coded energy system (white/pink/red/blue/purple/green)
- Threshold-based training with individualized zones
- Multiple lane groups with customized intervals
- High volume (6,000-8,000+ yards per session)
- Mental/psychological coaching notes embedded in workouts
- Team culture building through structure

---

## Season Overview

**Key Dates:**
- **October 3, 1994:** First practice - "172 DAYS" until NCAAs

- **Early Season (Fall):** 6 weeks aerobic base building

## Transcribed Workouts

### October 1994 - Complete Month Analysis

#### Overview: "172 Days" - The Foundation

October 1994 was the base-building phase of Michigan's championship season. Starting with "DAY #1" on October 3rd, Jon Urbanchek methodically constructed the aerobic and threshold foundation that would support championship performances 172 days later.

**Month Statistics (from transcribed workouts):**
- **Total Practices:** 60+ sessions (mostly two-a-days)
- **Daily Volume:** 7,000-12,000 yards (4K AM + 8K PM typical)
- **Weekly Volume:** 60,000-70,000 yards
- **Dominant Zone:** RED (threshold) - 50-60% of quality work
- **Testing Frequency:** 2-3 threshold tests per week
- **Rest Days:** Minimal (possibly Sundays only)

#### Week 1: October 1-8 - "Zone Establishment"

**Theme:** Find threshold, build aerobic base, integrate team

**[Saturday, October 1](./workouts/1994-10-01-practice.md)** - Pre-Season Testing
- 1,500 pull threshold test
- Lane group assignments: DIST, MIO, BE, BK, BT
- Long aerobic sets (3x600, 3x500, 3x400)
- **Team Event:** Apple Party @ Saye Farm Pool

**[Monday, October 3 - DAY #1](./workouts/1994-10-03-practice.md)** - "THE QUEST IS ON!"
- **Significance:** First official practice, 172-day countdown begins
- **AM:** Pull sets by lane (15x100, 12x100, 6x100)
- **PM:** Threshold test sets
  - DIST: 3x300 @ 3:40 + 1x800 @ 9:30
  - MIO: 3x300 @ 3:50 + 1x700 @ 9:00
  - BK: 3x300 @ 4:10 + 1x600 @ 8:00
  - BT: 3x300 @ 4:30 + 1x500 @ 7:30
- **Culture:** "BREAK IN THE ROOKIES" (lanes 7-8 for first-years)

**Tuesday, October 4 - DAY #170**
- Multi-lane complexity increases
- Pull: 4x100 @ 2:40-3:00 by group
- Swim: 4x200 @ 2:45-4:40 by group
- PM: 3x500 @ 6:20-7:20 → 4x300 → 3x200 (descending pyramid)
- Yardage: ~8,000 yards

**Wednesday, October 5 - DAY #169**
- Moderate volume, technique emphasis
- 4x200 @ threshold by lane
- Vertical kicking drills
- "LONG & PRETTY" coaching cues throughout

**[Thursday, October 6 - DAY #168](./workouts/1994-10-06-practice.md)** - The Grinder
- **Main Set:** Descending pyramid (3x500 → 4x400 → 3x300 → 2x200 → 3x100)
- ~4,000 yards threshold work
- 5 customized interval schemes by lane
- Total: ~8,000 yards

**Friday, October 7 - DAY #167**
- Recovery after Thursday's grinder
- Continuous 900 FR + 800 IM
- Volume: ~6,500 yards (lighter day)

**Saturday, October 8 - DAY #166**
- Long course work
- DIST: 3x800 @ 6:30 threshold
- MIO/Stroke groups: 400s and 300s
- Yardage: ~7,500 yards

**Week 1 Outcomes:**
- ✅ Threshold zones established
- ✅ Lane groups finalized
- ✅ Color-coded system introduced
- ✅ Team culture events (Apple Party)
- ✅ International swimmers integrated (Gustavo, Derya)

#### Week 2: October 9-15 - "Threshold Validation"

**Theme:** Confirm zones, increase quality, maintain volume

**[Monday, October 10 - DAY #165](./workouts/1994-10-10-practice.md)** - Threshold Sandwich
- **AM:** 4,000 yards - Descending pyramid
- **PM:** 8,000 yards - Threshold mastery
  - 1,500m threshold time trial (GET TIME!)
  - 3x400 @ trial pace (confirmation)
  - 5x300 @ above threshold (progression)
  - 6x100 "crispness" (speed preservation)
- Daily Total: ~12,000 yards
- **Culture:** "BREAKFAST CLUB" (AM crew identity)

**Tuesday, October 11 - DAY #164**
- Broken swim focus
- 4x200 @ 2:45 stroke specialists
- PM: Lactate tolerance introduction

  - 4x150 RED to BLUE zones

**Wednesday, October 12 - DAY #163 - COLUMBUS DAY**
- Full practice despite holiday
- Technique emphasis: "LONG & PRETTY"
- Main: 2x400 PUSH @ 5:20-6:40
- **Culture:** "CAPTAIN'S PRACTICE"

**Thursday-Saturday, October 13-15 - DAY #162-160**
- *Pattern:* Heavy Thursday (8K+), moderate Friday (6-7K), long Saturday (7-8K)
- Threshold work continues
- BLUE zone percentage increasing

**Week 2 Outcomes:**
- ✅ Threshold validated through time trials
- ✅ Two-a-day structure solidified
- ✅ BLUE zone work introduced

## The Color-Coded Energy System

Urbanchek's revolutionary visual training intensity system:

| Color | Zone | Description | Heart Rate | Lactate | Usage |
|-------|------|-------------|------------|---------|-------|
| **WHITE** | Aerobic Easy | Warm-up pace, easy aerobic | ~120-140 | <2 mmol | Recovery, technique |
| **PINK** | Moderate Aerobic | Building effort, conversation pace | ~140-160 | 2-3 mmol | Base building |
| **RED** | Threshold | Hard sustained effort, high HR | ~160-175 | 3-5 mmol | Threshold work |
| **BLUE** | Above Threshold | Crossing anaerobic threshold, lactate accumulation | ~175-185 | 5-8 mmol | VO2 max intervals |
| **PURPLE** | Maximum Effort | Near collapse, all-out | ~185+ | 8-12 mmol | Race pace, testing |
| **GREEN** | Lactate Tolerance | Fast until you puke! (3-10 min rest) | Max | 10-20+ mmol | Buffering capacity |

**Training Philosophy:**
- All intensities calculated from individual anaerobic threshold pace
- Threshold determined through testing (T30, 10x200 @ :20-30", 8x300 averaging)
- Color zones allow visual communication across pool deck
- Athletes learn to feel and recognize their zones

## Lane Group System

Urbanchek used multiple lane groups with customized intervals:

- **DIST:** Distance specialists (primarily freestylers, 500/1650 focus)
- **MIO / M.D.:** Mid-distance (200/500 free, 200/400 IM specialists)
- **BE:** Breaststroke specialists
- **BK:** Backstroke specialists
- **BT:** Butterfly specialists
- **OT:** Other/General group
- **Lanes 7-8:** Often designated for rookies or first-day-back swimmers

**Intervals varied by group:**
- Example: Same set might be @3:40 for DIST, @3:50 for MIO, @4:10 for BK, @4:30 for BT
- Customization allowed simultaneous training of different specialties
- Created internal competition and racing opportunities

## Training Methodology Highlights

### Volume Philosophy
- Regular 6,000-8,000 yard sessions
- Distance emphasis: 20-30 minute steady-state threshold swims
- High volume maintained through championship phase

### Threshold Testing Protocols
1. **T30 Test:** 30-minute continuous threshold swim
2. **10x200 @ :20-30":** Averaging pace for threshold
3. **8x300 Descending:** Threshold endurance
4. **Broken Swims:** Race pace with short rest (5-10")

### Set Structure Patterns
- **Warm-up:** 600-900 yards, technique emphasis ("LONG & PRETTY")
- **Pre-set:** Skills, drills, kick (often 400-600 yards)
- **Main Set:** Multi-part, lane-group specific (3,000-5,000 yards)
- **Cool Down:** Usually minimal, sometimes integrated into main set

### Psychological Elements
- Motivational notes written into workouts
- "GET ANGRY" mentality
- Team event announcements (Apple Party, etc.)
- International swimmer integration notes
- "COOPERATE!" - data collection emphasis
