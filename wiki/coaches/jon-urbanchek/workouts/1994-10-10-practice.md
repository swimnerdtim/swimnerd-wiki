## Main Set #1: Threshold Time Trial (~1,500 yards)

```

BK:       1 x 1,200 @ 15:00-17:00 (threshold)
```

## Main Set #2: Threshold Maintenance (~2,400 yards)

### Part A: 3 x 400 @ threshold-based intervals

| Lane Group | Interval | Pace Target | Zone |
|------------|----------|-------------|------|
| DIST | @ 5:00 | Match 1500 pace | RED |
| MIO  | @ 5:20 | Threshold hold | RED |
| BK   | @ 6:00 | Controlled effort | RED |
| BT   | @ 6:30 | Technique + pace | PINK/RED |

**Purpose:** Verify threshold pace is sustainable across multiple reps

### Part B: 5 x 300 @ progressive intervals

**Set Structure:**
```
DIST: 5 x 300 @ 3:40 (descend 1-3, 4-5)
MIO:  5 x 300 @ 3:50 (steady RED zone)
BK:   5 x 300 @ 4:30 (build each 300)
BT:   5 x 300 @ 4:50 (stroke focus)
```

**Progression:**
- Reps 1-3: Descending effort (build into threshold)
- Reps 4-5: Hold or exceed threshold pace
- Color: PINK → RED → BLUE (if achieving)

## Training Insights

### Volume Breakdown
- **AM Session:** ~4,000 yards (30% of daily volume)
- **PM Session:** ~8,000 yards (70% of daily volume)
- **Daily Total:** ~12,000 yards
- **Quality Work:** ~4,500 yards at threshold or above

### Two-A-Day Philosophy
- **AM:** Lower volume, technique focus, moderate intensity
- **PM:** High volume, threshold emphasis, quality work
- **Recovery:** ~6 hours between sessions for glycogen replenishment

### Physiological Targets
1. **Threshold Testing:** 1500m time trial establishes pace zones
2. **Threshold Confirmation:** 400s verify sustainability
3. **Threshold Progression:** 300s push beyond base threshold
4. **Speed Preservation:** 100s maintain neural pathways
5. **Base Building:** 12K total volume builds aerobic engine

### Color Zone Distribution (PM Session)
- **WHITE:** 10% (warm-up, cool down)
- **PINK:** 20% (pre-set, easy kick)
- **RED:** 60% (majority of main sets - threshold work)
- **BLUE:** 10% (crispness 100s, odd reps)
- **PURPLE/GREEN:** 0% (not yet introduced in October base phase)

### Lane Customization
Every main set has 4-6 different interval schemes:
- **DIST (Distance):** Tightest intervals, highest expectations
- **MIO (Mid-distance):** Moderate intervals, pace focus
- **BE (Breaststroke):** Stroke-specific intervals
- **BK (Backstroke):** Technical emphasis, reasonable rest
- **BT (Butterfly):** Most rest, stroke efficiency priority

### Coaching Cues & Philosophy

**Data Collection:**
- "GET TIME!" on 1500m threshold trial
- "COOPERATE!" for pulse checks and time sharing
- Building database of individual threshold paces

**Mental Elements:**
- Day #165 countdown (constant championship awareness)
- "BREAKFAST CLUB" (morning session culture/identity)
- "BEST AVERAGE" mentality (consistency over single fast swim)

**Threshold Obsession:**
- Three different threshold formats in one session:
  1. Long trial (1500m)
  2. Broken threshold (400s)
  3. Progression above threshold (300s)
- Teaching athletes to FEEL and RECOGNIZE their zones

## Notable Patterns

### The "Threshold Sandwich"
1. **Test** threshold (1500m trial)
2. **Confirm** threshold (400s at trial pace)
3. **Push** threshold (300s above trial pace)
4. **Speed** work (100s for crispness)

This structure appears repeatedly in Urbanchek workouts - establish, confirm, exceed, preserve speed.

### Week 2 Progression
- Week 1 (Day #172-166): Zone establishment, technique, moderate volume
- Week 2 (Day #165-159): Threshold validation, quality increases, volume holds
- This workout marks a shift from "finding zones" to "working zones"
