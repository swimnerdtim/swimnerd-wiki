# Mark Schubert: Sample Workouts

**Source:** *Competitive Swimming: Techniques for Champions* (1990, 1996)  
**Author:** Mark Schubert (USC coach, multiple Olympic teams)  
**Context:** Practical workout sets for sprinters, middle-distance, and distance swimmers  
**Significance:** Real training sessions from one of swimming's greatest coaches — copy these verbatim or adapt

---

## How to Use This Page

### Workout Structure
Each workout follows this pattern:
1. **Warmup** — Easy swimming, stroke drills, technique focus
2. **Main Sets** — The meat of the workout (kick, pull, swim)
3. **Loosen down** — Easy recovery swimming

### Shorthand Legend
- **K** = kickboard
- **P** = pull buoy
- **f** = fast
- **e** = easy
- **alt. br.** = alternate breathing
- **descend** = each rep faster than the previous
- **buildup** = increase speed throughout the rep
- **on [time]** = interval (e.g., "on 1:30" = 1:30 per 100)

**Example:** "16 × 50 K on 1:10, 3f, 1e"  
= 16 sets of 50 yards kick with kickboard, on 1:10 interval, swim 3 fast then 1 easy

## Sprint Workouts (50-100 Events)

### Philosophy for Sprinters
- Start season with aerobic foundation (long, slow swims)
- Progress to short-rest aerobic sets (weeks 1-3)
- Add pure speed work starting week 4 (25s → 50s)
- Mid-season: Increase anaerobic work to 3× per week
- Championship: Reduce volume 20%/week, emphasize quality starts/turns

---

### Early Season Sprint Workout (SCY)

**Focus:** Building aerobic base, introducing speed

**Warmup:**  
600 yards easy with emphasis on stroke technique  
(Use stroke drills as warmup device)

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
16 × 50 K on 1:10, 3f, 1e

**Pull:**  
5 × 200 P on 2:50, buoy, alt. br., descend  
(Each 200 faster than the previous)

**Swim:**  
12 × 100 free on 1:30  
(Hold comfortable aerobic pace)

**Speed:**  
8 × 50 free on 1:10, 1st 25 fast

**Loosen Down:**  
300 stroke drill, easy

**Total:** ~4,000 yards

---

### Mid-Season Sprint Workout (SCY)

**Focus:** Maintain speed, increase anaerobic capacity

**Warmup:**  
8 × 100 on descending intervals:
- 2 on 1:40
- 2 on 1:35
- 2 on 1:30
- 2 on 1:25

**Main Sets:**

**Kick (no breaks between sets):**  
- 4 × 75 on 1:40, last 25 fast
- 4 × 75 on 1:35, last 50 fast
- 4 × 75 on 1:30, whole 75 fast  
(Looks like 12 × 75)

**Pull:**  
"Locomotive" up to 4 laps, freestyle, on :30 intervals:
- 1 lap easy, 1 lap fast
- 2 laps easy, 2 laps fast
- 3 laps easy, 3 laps fast
- 4 laps easy, 4 laps fast  
(Total: 20 laps = 500 yards)

**Swim (broken 100s):**  
9 × 100 yards:
- First 3: Continuous, very fast (3-5 min rest between)
- Second 3: Broken at 50 (:05 rest at wall)
- Last 3: Broken at each 25 (:05 rest at each wall)

**Loosen Down:**  
8 × 100 on 1:45, alternating left arm only / right arm only, with paddles, easy

**Total:** ~4,500 yards

---

### Championship Season Sprint Workout (SCY)

**Focus:** Quality over quantity, race simulation

**Warmup:**  
400 stroke drills

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
12 × 50 K on 1:15:
- 1 easy
- 1 buildup
- 1 fast (90% effort)

**Pull:**  
5 × 100 free on 1:30, last 25 strong

**Race Pace:**  
8 × 50 free on 2:00:
- 1 easy
- 1 FAST from dive start (90% of best effort)

**Neuromuscular Speed:**  
8 × 25 free on 1:00:
- First 4: Fast first 12.5
- Last 4: Fast last 12.5

**Loosen Down:**  
400 stroke drill

**Total:** ~2,800 yards  
(Volume reduced ~30% from mid-season)

## Middle-Distance Workouts (200-400 Events)

### Philosophy for Middle-Distance
- Larger aerobic base than sprinters (first 3 weeks)
- Aerobic work emphasized throughout season
- Anaerobic training 2× per week mid-season
- Championship phase: 3-week taper, 20% volume reduction per week
- Race-pace sets to simulate competition

---

### Early Season Middle-Distance Workout (SCY)

**Focus:** Building aerobic foundation

**Warmup:**  
600 stroke drill

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
5 × 200 on 4:10, descending

**Pull:**  
Ladder (50-100-150-200-150-100-50) with :15 rest between, repeat 2×  
(Total pull: 1,400 yards)

**Swim:**  
12 × 150 on descending intervals:
- 4 on 2:15
- 4 on 2:10
- 4 on 2:05  
Descend 1-4 within each group

**Speed:**  
8 × (75, 50, 25):
- 75 on 1:10
- 50 on :50
- 25 on :30  
Last 25 of each swim is fast

**Loosen Down:**  
3 × 200 stroke drill with :30 rest

**Total:** ~5,500 yards

---

### Mid-Season Middle-Distance Workout (SCY)

**Focus:** Aerobic maintenance + anaerobic development

**Warmup:**  
10 × 100:
- 5 on 1:30
- 5 on 1:20

**Main Sets:**

**Kick (with fins):**  
3 rounds of:
- 150 on 2:15
- 100 on 1:30
- 50 on :45

**Swim (descending pyramid):**  
- 4 × 200 on 2:30, descending 1-4
- 1 × 100 on 1:30
- 3 × 200 on 2:35, descending
- 2 × 100 on 1:25, descending
- 2 × 200 on 2:40, descending
- 3 × 100 on 1:20, descending
- 1 × 200 on 2:45
- 4 × 100 on 1:15, descending

**Pull:**  
6 × 300:
- 2 on 4:00
- 2 on 4:10
- 2 on 4:20  
Second of each pair faster

**Pace Work:**  
10 × 50:
- 1 easy
- 1 fast on 1:00  
Fast swims hold 200 pace or faster

**Loosen Down:**  
400 stroke drill

**Total:** ~6,500 yards

---

### Championship Season Middle-Distance Workout (SCY)

**Focus:** Race simulation, reduced volume

**Warmup:**  
600 stroke drill

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
4 × 100 on 2:00, last 25 strong

**Pull:**  
4 × 200 on 3:00, buildup (going faster throughout)

**Race Pace:**  
8 × 100 on 1:45:
- 1 easy
- 1 at race pace

**Loosen Down:**  
300 stroke drill

**Total:** ~3,200 yards

## Distance Workouts (800-1500 Events)

### Philosophy for Distance
- Largest segment of aerobic training early season
- Long, slow swimming + easy intervals (weeks 1-4)
- Mid-season: Continue aerobic work with shorter rest, anaerobic 2× per week
- Championship: Reduce volume, maintain intensity, race-pace sets
- Speed work throughout season to retain neuromuscular ability

---

### Early Season Distance Workout (SCY)

**Focus:** Over-distance aerobic base

**Warmup:**  
800 stroke drill

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
6 × 200 on 4:00, descending

**Pull:**  
2,000 continuous pull with buoy  
(Negative split: second 1,000 faster than first)

**Swim:**  
20 × 100 on 1:40, holding consistent pace

**Speed (neuromuscular):**  
8 × 25 on :45, fast

**Loosen Down:**  
400 easy

**Total:** ~6,000 yards

---

### Mid-Season Distance Workout (SCY)

**Focus:** Threshold work, pace discipline

**Warmup:**  
10 × 100 on 1:35

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
5 × 300 on 5:30, descending

**Pull:**  
3 × 500 on 7:00, negative split each

**Swim (threshold set):**  
16 × 150 on 2:20, holding pace

**Anaerobic:**  
8 × 100 on 1:30, descending 1-4, repeat

**Speed:**  
10 × 50 on 1:00:
- 1 easy
- 1 fast (200 pace or faster)

**Loosen Down:**  
400 easy

**Total:** ~7,500 yards

---

### Championship Season Distance Workout (SCY)

**Focus:** Race-specific pace, reduced volume

**Warmup:**  
600 stroke drill

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
4 × 150 on 3:00, last 50 fast

**Pull:**  
3 × 300 on 4:30, buildup

**Race Pace:**  
6 × 200 on 3:00, holding race pace

**Speed:**  
6 × 50 on 1:10, fast

**Loosen Down:**  
400 easy

**Total:** ~4,000 yards

## Stroke-Specific Workouts

### Breaststroke & Butterfly (Senior Swimmers)

**Focus:** Stroke-specific endurance and power

**Warmup:**  
500 choice stroke drill

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
10 × 100 stroke-specific kick on 2:00

**Pull (with buoy):**  
5 × 200 stroke on 3:30, descending

**Swim:**  
12 × 75 stroke on 1:30:
- 25 drill
- 25 swim
- 25 fast

**Broken 100s:**  
8 × 100 stroke broken at 50 (:10 rest), very fast

**Speed:**  
6 × 25 stroke on 1:00, maximum effort

**Loosen Down:**  
300 easy choice

**Total:** ~4,200 yards

## Individual Medley Workouts

### IM Workout (Senior Swimmers)

**Focus:** All four strokes, transitions, endurance

**Warmup:**  
400 IM drill (100 each stroke)

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
4 × (4 × 50) on 1:10:
- Round 1: Fly kick
- Round 2: Back kick
- Round 3: Breast kick
- Round 4: Free kick

**Pull:**  
3 × 300 IM pull on 5:00

**Swim:**  
5 × 200 IM on 3:00, descending

**Broken IM:**  
4 × 100 IM broken at each 25 (:05 rest), fast

**Choice:**  
8 × 50 on 1:00, choice stroke, alternate easy/fast

**Loosen Down:**  
200 easy choice

**Total:** ~4,600 yards

## Age-Group Workouts

### Ages 10 and Under

**Focus:** Fun, technique, aerobic base

**Warmup:**  
200 kick/drill/swim (by 50s)

**Main Set:**  
10 × 50 on 1:15, choice stroke

**Game:**  
Relay races (4 × 25)

**Cool Down:**  
100 easy

**Total:** ~800-1,000 yards  
**Frequency:** 3-5× per week

**Key principle:** Keep it fun, emphasize technique, NO anaerobic suffering

---

### Ages 11-14

**Focus:** Technique refinement, increased volume, introduction to pace work

**Warmup:**  
400 stroke drill

**Main Sets:**

**Kick:**  
8 × 50 on 1:20, 2 easy, 2 fast

**Pull:**  
4 × 150 on 2:45, descending

**Swim:**  
10 × 100 on 1:45, holding pace

**Speed:**  
6 × 25 on :45, fast

**Loosen Down:**  
200 choice

**Total:** ~2,500-3,500 yards  
**Frequency:** 5-7× per week

**Key principle:** Still mostly aerobic, begin introducing threshold work

## Special Sets & Concepts

### The "Locomotive"

**What it is:** Increasing distances, alternating easy/fast

**Example:**  
- 1 lap easy, 1 lap fast (on :30 per lap)
- 2 laps easy, 2 laps fast (on :30 per lap)
- 3 laps easy, 3 laps fast (on :30 per lap)
- 4 laps easy, 4 laps fast (on :30 per lap)

**Total:** 20 laps (500 yards)

**Why it works:** Builds endurance while teaching pace control

---

### Broken Swims

**What they are:** Swim with brief rest at walls to simulate racing without full fatigue

**Example 1:** 3 × 100 continuous, very fast (3-5 min rest)  
**Example 2:** 3 × 100 broken at 50 (:05 rest)  
**Example 3:** 3 × 100 broken at each 25 (:05 rest)

**Progression:** More rest = faster times = neuromuscular speed training

**Why it works:** Allows swimmers to experience race pace without the lactic acid buildup that would prevent proper technique

---

### Descending Sets

**What it is:** Each rep faster than the previous

**Example:**  
5 × 200 pull on 2:50, descending:
- #1: 2:30
- #2: 2:28
- #3: 2:25
- #4: 2:22
- #5: 2:19

**Why it works:** Teaches pacing discipline and builds confidence as swimmer gets faster

---

### Negative Split Sets

**What it is:** Second half faster than first half

**Example:**  
3 × 500 pull on 7:00, negative split each:
- First 250: 3:35
- Second 250: 3:25

**Why it works:** Mimics smart race strategy, builds back-end speed

## Practical Tips for Coaches

### Setting Intervals (The 75% Rule)

**If 75% of your swimmers cannot complete the set at the prescribed pace, the interval is too fast.**

**Adjust by:**
- Adding :05-:10 per rep
- Reducing reps (12 × 100 → 8 × 100)
- Breaking into smaller chunks

**Example:**  
- Planned: 10 × 100 on 1:20
- Only 3 of 10 complete it
- Better: 10 × 100 on 1:30 (now 8+ complete it)

---

### Adapting for SCM/LCM

**These workouts are written for SCY (25-yard pool).**

**To convert to LCM (50-meter):**
- Reduce total reps by ~20%
- Add :10-:20 per 100m compared to 100y interval
- Focus more on underwater work (longer walls)

**To convert to SCM (25-meter):**
- Similar volume to SCY
- Add :02-:05 per 100m compared to 100y interval

---

### Workout Design Philosophy

**Schubert's principles:**
1. **Warm up properly** — Prepare joints, ligaments, muscles
2. **Progress logically** — Kick → Pull → Swim
3. **Vary intensity** — Hard sets followed by recovery
4. **End with speed** — Short, fast efforts while fatigued (race simulation)
5. **Cool down** — Help body clear lactate, prevent injury

**Weekly pattern:**
- Monday: Hard aerobic
- Tuesday: Moderate + speed
- Wednesday: Hard anaerobic
- Thursday: Moderate + technique
- Friday: Hard aerobic or anaerobic
- Saturday: Moderate or race
- Sunday: Easy or off
