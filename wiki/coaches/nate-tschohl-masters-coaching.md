## Coaching Philosophy

Nate Tschohl's coaching approach is built on **sustainable quality over unsustainable quantity**. Across 66 workouts spanning 7 months, the data reveals a coach who prioritizes consistent structure, threshold mastery, and technical foundation while maintaining realistic volume expectations for Masters athletes.

### Core Principles (Data-Driven)

**1. Consistency Over Chaos**
- 98% of workouts begin with 300m choice warmup
- 98% end with 100m easy cooldown
- Average workout distance: 2,154m (range: 1,200-2,900m)
- Minimal volume fluctuation month-to-month (2,000-2,300m range)

**2. Threshold is King, Speed is Icing**
- 28% of total season volume at threshold intensity
- 75 @ 400 pace / 25 EZ pattern appears in 11 workouts (primary threshold tool)
- Only 5% sprint/max speed volume (opportunity area)

**3. Technical Foundation Before Power**
- Every workout includes 500-1,000m warmup with drills
- Butterfly appears in 68% of workouts (45/66) but always aerobic/technique focus
- Equipment progressions: Buoy only → Paddles only → Both/Neither
- Kickout drills (15m underwater + free) in 35% of workouts (23/66)

**4. Smart Equipment Use for Injury Prevention**
- Pull buoy present in 53% of workouts (35/66)
- Paddles in 32% (21/66) - rarely used for max speed
- Fins in 15% (10/66) - primarily for technique/speed development
- Resistance bands in 5% (3/66) - upper body isolation work

**5. Realistic Volume for Masters Athletes**
- No 5,000m college-style mega-sets
- Focus on what can be done **well** in 60-90 minutes
- Quality threshold work over junk yardage

### Equipment Philosophy

**Pull Buoy (53% of workouts - 35/66)**
- Primary threshold training tool
- Often paired with breathing patterns (bilateral 3/5/7/9)
- Average pull volume per workout: 600-900m
- **Philosophy:** Build upper body strength while reducing lower body fatigue

**Paddles (32% of workouts - 21/66)**
- Typically introduced mid-to-late set
- Progressive loading: Buoy only → Paddles only → Both → Neither
- **Rarely used for sprint work** (coach discourages paddles for max speed)
- **Philosophy:** Gradual resistance increase prevents shoulder injury

**Fins (15% of workouts - 10/66)**
- Used for: Sprint touch development (100 FAF w/fins), technique drills, speed work
- Often "anything goes" rounds in FAST sets
- **Philosophy:** Speed assistance, not crutch

**Kickboards (27% of workouts - 18/66)**
- Pure kick sets: 200-400m ranges
- "Kick with board FAST" + sprint swims pattern popular
- Flutter kick emphasis
- **Gap:** Limited vertical kicking or fin variety

**Resistance Bands (5% of workouts - 3/66)**
- Used with pull buoy for upper body isolation
- Typical: 300m pulls with band + free swimming threshold combo

## Development Opportunities

### Areas for Improvement (Data-Driven Gaps)

#### ⚠️ **Limited Race-Specific Speed Work**
**Current State:**
- Only 5% sprint volume across season
- Most "FAST" sets are 25s on 1:00 (not truly max speed)

**Recommendation:**
- Add **1x/month dedicated sprint day:**
  - 6-8x50 @ 3:00-5:00 (true max effort)
  - Dive/turn work
  - Race-start simulations

**Impact:** Would improve race finishes and develop top-end speed

---

#### ⚠️ **Stroke Balance - Breaststroke & Backstroke**
**Current State:**
- Breaststroke: 5% of volume (mostly in IM context)
- Backstroke: 5% of volume (rarely trained outside IM)
- No dedicated breaststroke or backstroke technique sets

**Recommendation:**
- **Monthly breaststroke focus day:**
  - 8x50 Breast pull + kick combinations
  - Underwater pullout progressions
- **Monthly backstroke focus day:**
  - Backstroke rotation drills (body position, hip drive)
  - 8x50 Back with underwater focus

**Impact:** Create more well-rounded IM racers; prevent stroke imbalance injuries

---

#### ⚠️ **Minimal Kicking Volume**
**Current State:**
- Only 8-10% of total yardage is pure kick
- Some workouts have zero kick beyond drills
- Limited equipment variety (mostly flutter kick with board)

**Recommendation:**
- Add **400-600m kick block every 2nd workout**
- Vertical kicking sets (30sec on/30sec off)
- More varied kick equipment (fins, Zoomers, not just boards)

**Impact:** Improve body position, ankle flexibility, and race turns

---

#### ⚠️ **No Pace Variance in Threshold Sets**
**Current State:**
- Most threshold work is steady-state or descending
- Lacks simulation of mid-race surges or negative splits

**Recommendation:**
- Add **variable pace sets:**
  - 8x100 (2 @ 90%, 2 @ 95%, 2 @ 90%, 2 @ 100%)
  - Broken swims with 10sec rest to simulate race pacing

**Impact:** Better race-day execution; mental toughness under fatigue

---

#### ⚠️ **Lack of Active Recovery Weeks**
**Current State:**
- Volume stays consistent (2,000-2,300m) even during high-intensity phases
- Could lead to cumulative fatigue

**Recommendation:**
- **Every 4th week: Drop volume to 1,600-1,800m**
- Maintain intensity but reduce reps
- More drill/technique focus

**Impact:** Prevent overtraining; allow adaptation; improve long-term consistency

---

#### ⚠️ **Limited Long Course Exposure**
**Current State:**
- Only 2/66 workouts in LCM
- 92% of training in SCM (25m pool)

**Recommendation (if athletes race LCM):**
- Monthly LCM session
- Focus on turn endurance, pacing, breathing patterns

**Impact:** Better LCM race preparation if competing in long course meets
