## Coaching Philosophy

Nate Tschohl runs a **threshold-centered, athlete-empowering** program focused on sustainable race-pace development. His approach prioritizes quality execution over survival mentality, building complete swimmers through comprehensive stroke development while respecting individual differences.

### Core Principles

- **Build race pace through volume, not just intensity** - Threshold work dominates (33.6% of total volume)
- **Develop complete swimmers** - Heavy IM emphasis in 80% of workouts
- **Individualization within structure** - "Make the Interval" philosophy with interval ranges
- **Swimming over drilling** - 80-85% straight swimming, minimal drill dependency
- **Consistent structure, sustainable training** - Predictable warmup/cooldown patterns
- **Race-skill integration** - Underwater work, transitions, pullouts emphasized

**Ideal for:** Distance swimmers (200-500m), IM swimmers, technique-focused athletes  
**Training focus:** Aerobic foundation + threshold development + stroke versatility

## Signature Sets

### 1. **"Make the Interval"** ⭐ Primary Philosophy

**Frequency:** 3 explicit uses, philosophically present in most workouts  
**Purpose:** Individualized threshold development within group structure

**Examples:**
```
• 2x (4x50 Free @ :40-:55 Make the Interval) = 400y
• 12x100 Free - Make the Interval @ 1:20-1:30 = 1,200y
```

**Coaching Pattern:**
- Interval RANGES (not fixed times) = respect for individual capabilities
- Athletes self-select sustainable pace within provided range
- Emphasizes quality over survival, athlete ownership over dictation
- Mature coaching approach that builds pacing discipline

**Coaching Pattern:**

- Develops butterfly endurance without fatigue compromise
- Smart dual-stroke development

### Stroke Emphasis

**Freestyle:** ~60-65% (dominant stroke)

**Individual Medley:** Heavy emphasis (~25-30%)
- IM transitions (12x50 @ 1:00-1:10)
- Full IM sets (5x100, 3x100)
- "The IM 75's" (12x75 @ 1:30)

- Stroke combinations (EF/FE/FF/EE)

**Butterfly:** Significant secondary focus
- Always aerobic or technique-based (never threshold fly alone)
- Paired with freestyle threshold work

**Backstroke/Breaststroke:** Integrated into IM work, minimal isolation

**Philosophy:** Freestyle is the primary race focus, but **IM development is a core training philosophy**. Coach believes in comprehensive stroke development for all swimmers, not just IMers. Builds better overall swimmers and prevents overuse injury.

## Development Opportunities

### Areas for Program Balance

#### ⚠️ **1. VO2 Max Severely Underutilized (4.9%)**

**Current State:** Only 1,100 yards across 4 workouts

**Missing Stimuli:**
- 8x100 @ 1:20-1:30 (threshold-to-VO2 transition)
- 16x50 @ :50-:55 (classic VO2 set)
- 200 pace work with short rest
- Lactate tolerance development

**Recommendation:**
- **Target:** Increase to 10-12% of weekly volume
- **Format:** 1-2 VO2 workouts per week
- **Sample:** 2x (8x50 @ :50 @ 200 pace + 200 EZ between rounds)
- **Impact:** Improved 200/500 racing, better lactate clearance, higher top-end speed

---

#### ⚠️ **2. Sprint Development Limited (7.2%)**

**Current State:** 1,600 yards across 5 workouts, often very short (4x25)

**Missing Stimuli:**
- Dive sprints (15m-25m all-out)
- 6-8x50 @ 3:00+ (full recovery sprint work)
- Relay-start simulation
- True max-speed work

**Recommendation:**
- **Target:** Increase to 12-15% of weekly volume
- **Format:** 1 dedicated sprint workout per week
- **Sample:** 8x50 @ 3:00 (15m BLAST, cruise home) + 6x25 all-out @ 2:00
- **Impact:** Improved top-end speed, better 50/100 performance, explosive power

---

#### ⚠️ **3. Kicking Work Underrepresented**

**Current State:** Very limited dedicated kicking, mostly warmup kickouts

**Missing Stimuli:**
- Vertical kicking (power development)
- 10x100 kick sets (kick endurance)
- Board vs. FKOB variety
- Underwater kicking emphasis

**Recommendation:**
- **Target:** 300-500 kick yards per workout
- **Format:** 1 dedicated kick set per session
- **Sample:** 8x100 Kick (50 board, 50 FKOB) @ 2:00
- **Impact:** Stronger underwaters, better turns, improved leg power

---

#### ⚠️ **4. No True Distance Sets**

**Current State:** Longest continuous swim is 300y (warmup)

**Missing Stimuli:**
- Mile swims (1650/1500)
- 500s for aerobic development
- 400 IMs
- Pacing practice on longer swims

**Recommendation:**
- **Target:** 1 distance set every 7-10 days
- **Format:** 1x500, 3x400, or 1x1000 aerobic
- **Sample:** 3x400 Free @ 6:00 (descend 1-3 to threshold)
- **Impact:** Better aerobic capacity, pacing skills, mental toughness

---

#### ⚠️ **5. Limited Pacing Variety**

**Current State:** Most sets are straight (same pace throughout)

**Missing Stimuli:**
- 6x100 descend 1-6
- Ladder sets (50-100-150-200-150-100-50)
- Negative-split practice

**Recommendation:**
- **Target:** 1-2 descending/ascending sets per week
- **Sample:** 6x100 @ 1:30 (descend 1-3, repeat 4-6)
- **Impact:** Better race pacing, mental engagement, variety
