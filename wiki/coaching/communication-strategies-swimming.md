# Communication Strategies for Swim Coaches

**8 practical tools to build buy-in, motivate swimmers, and create championship culture.**

---

## Why Communication Matters More Than Programming

**The reality:**
- Perfect training program + poor communication = mediocre results
- Good training program + great communication = championship results

**The gap:**
- Coaches obsess over periodization, energy systems, and volume/intensity
- Coaches neglect communication, trust-building, and emotional intelligence

> "There is no gadget that can inspire and ignite an athlete like an emotionally-tuned coach can."  
> — Brett Bartholomew, *Conscious Coaching*

**Your job:** Master BOTH the science AND the art.

---

## The 8 Communication Strategies

### 1. Know Yourself First

**Principle:** You cannot effectively lead others if you don't understand yourself.

**Self-awareness questions:**
- What is MY communication style? (Direct? Encouraging? Data-driven?)
- What are MY triggers? (What makes me frustrated/angry?)
- What are MY biases? (Do I favor sprinters over distance swimmers?)
- How do I handle stress/pressure?

**Why it matters:** Your emotional state affects your swimmers.

**Swimming example:**

**❌ Bad:**
> Coach has a bad morning → Snaps at swimmer during warm-up → Swimmer shuts down, loses trust

**✅ Good:**
> Coach recognizes their stress → Takes a breath → Separates personal issues from coaching → Engages positively with team

---

#### **Action Steps:**

1. **Journal after practice:** What went well? What triggered me? How did I handle it?
2. **Ask for feedback:** "How do I come across when I'm stressed?"
3. **Identify patterns:** Do you coach differently on Mondays? After meets?

**Goal:** Self-aware coaches are consistent coaches.

---

### 2. Seek to Understand Before Being Understood

**Principle:** Listen first, coach second.

**Steven Covey (*7 Habits*):** "Seek first to understand, then to be understood."

**Application:**
- Before correcting a swimmer, ask questions
- "What were you thinking during that set?"
- "How did that feel?"
- "What's going on today?"

**Why it works:** Swimmers are more receptive to coaching when they feel HEARD.

---

#### **Swimming Example:**

**Scenario:** Swimmer is off pace on a threshold set.

**❌ Bad approach:**
> "You're way off pace! What's wrong with you?"

**✅ Good approach:**
> "I noticed you were 3-4 seconds slower than usual. What's going on? How are you feeling?"

**Possible responses:**
- "My shoulder's been bugging me" → Adjust training load
- "I didn't eat breakfast" → Nutrition conversation
- "I'm stressed about exams" → Empathy + support
- "I don't understand what 'threshold pace' means" → Educate

**Key insight:** The root cause determines the solution.

---

### 3. Educate with the 3 R's

**The 3 R's:**
1. **Relevance** — Why does this matter to YOU (the swimmer)?
2. **Reason** — What's the science/rationale behind it?
3. **Results** — What will you get out of it?

**Why it works:** Swimmers buy in when they understand the "why."

---

#### **Swimming Example: Teaching Bilateral Breathing**

**❌ Bad explanation:**
> "Breathe every 3 strokes."

**✅ Good explanation (3 R's):**

**Relevance:**
> "You want to drop time in your 200 free, right? And you said you always fade on the last 50."

**Reason:**
> "Breathing every 3 helps you maintain better body position and reduces drag. It also trains both sides of your body equally, which improves balance and prevents shoulder overuse injuries. Plus, it gives you more flexibility in races — if you're swimming into the sun or waves, you can breathe away from it."

**Results:**
> "Swimmers who master bilateral breathing typically see 1-2 second drops in their 200 within 6-8 weeks. And you'll finish races feeling more balanced and less fatigued."

---

#### **The 3 R's Template (Fill in for ANY technique/set):**

**Relevance:**
> "You want to [goal], right?"

**Reason:**
> "This [technique/set] works because [science/mechanism]."

**Results:**
> "[Evidence/outcome] — you'll see [specific benefit]."

---

### 4. Make 'Em Laugh

**Principle:** Humor builds connection and diffuses tension.

**Why it works:**
- Laughter releases endorphins (reduces stress)
- Creates positive associations with hard training
- Makes you more approachable
- Builds rapport

**Caution:** Authenticity only. Don't force it.

---

#### **Swimming Examples:**

**After a brutal lactate tolerance set:**
> "Well, that was fun... said no one ever. But you crushed it. Great work, warriors."

**Before a tough morning practice:**
> "Good morning! I know you all want to be here at 5:30 AM more than anything in the world. Your enthusiasm is overwhelming." (Delivered with a smile)

**During a long set:**
> "Only 3 more rounds! That's like... nothing... if you don't think about it... which you definitely will."

---

### 5. Be Authentic

**Principle:** Don't pretend to be someone you're not.

**Athletes can smell BS from a mile away.**

**Examples:**

**❌ Bad:** Quiet coach tries to be a loud, intense motivator like Bob Bowman → Feels fake

**✅ Good:** Quiet coach leans into calm, thoughtful leadership (John Wooden style) → Authentic

---

**❌ Bad:** Coach mimics another coach's language/style → Swimmers see through it

**✅ Good:** Coach finds THEIR voice → Swimmers respect it

---

**Your job:** Be the best version of YOURSELF, not a copy of another coach.

**How to find your authentic style:**
- What do swimmers say they appreciate about you?
- When do you feel most natural coaching?
- What's your communication "default"? (Data-driven? Storytelling? Direct?)

**Remember:** There's no "right" style. Bowman, Urbanchek, Reese, Marsh — all different, all effective.

---

### 6. Empathy (Without Lowering Standards)

**Definition:** Understanding and sharing the feelings of another.

**Key:** Empathy ≠ lowering standards. You can be empathetic AND hold swimmers accountable.

---

#### **Swimming Examples:**

**Scenario 1: Swimmer stressed about school**

**❌ Bad:**
> "Everyone has stress. Suck it up."

**✅ Good:**
> "I know you're stressed about exams this week. Let's adjust practice so you can recover mentally and physically. But when exams are done, we're going HARD to make up for it. Deal?"

---

**Scenario 2: Swimmer dealing with injury**

**❌ Bad:**
> "If you can't swim, don't bother showing up."

**✅ Good:**
> "I know this injury is frustrating. Let's focus on what you CAN do — core work, kicking, visualization. When you're cleared, you'll be ahead of where you were."

---

**Scenario 3: Swimmer nervous before big meet**

**❌ Bad:**
> "Don't be nervous."

**✅ Good:**
> "It's normal to be nervous. That means you care. Let's channel that energy into focus. You've done the work. Trust your training."

---

### 7. Delivery & Persuasion

**Principle:** HOW you say something matters as much as WHAT you say.

**Elements of effective delivery:**
- **Tone of voice** (firm vs. encouraging vs. calm)
- **Body language** (open posture, eye contact, positioning)
- **Timing** (when you give feedback matters)
- **Word choice** (positive vs. negative framing)

---

#### **Example 1: Correcting Technique**

**❌ Negative framing:**
> "Don't drop your elbow."

**✅ Positive framing:**
> "Keep your elbow high. Yes, like that! Perfect."

**Why it works:** The brain processes positive commands faster than negative ones.

---

#### **Example 2: Motivating Before a Set**

**❌ Flat delivery:**
> "Okay, 10×100 on 1:20. Go."

**✅ Energized delivery:**
> "Alright, this is where we separate from the competition. 10×100, all-out effort. Show me what you've got. Let's GO!"

---

#### **Example 3: Timing Matters**

**❌ Bad timing:**
> Correcting technique DURING a race-pace set (swimmer can't process mid-effort)

**✅ Good timing:**
> Correcting technique AFTER the set (when swimmer can focus and absorb feedback)

---

#### **Persuasion Principle: Gains > Losses**

People are more motivated by GAINS than LOSSES.

**❌ Loss framing:**
> "If you don't fix your technique, you'll get injured."

**✅ Gain framing:**
> "Fixing your technique will make you faster AND keep you healthy for the long season."

---

### 8. Autonomy (Ownership = Motivation)

**Principle:** People are more motivated when they have ownership.

**Application:**
- Give swimmers choices (within boundaries)
- Ask for input on training
- Let them problem-solve

**Why it works:** Autonomy → sense of control → higher buy-in → better effort.

---

#### **Swimming Examples:**

**Example 1: Main Set**

**❌ No autonomy:**
> "Do 10×100 freestyle on 1:20."

**✅ With autonomy:**
> "We need 1000 yards of threshold work today. You can do 10×100, 5×200, or 2×500. What do you prefer?"

---

**Example 2: Stroke Choice**

**❌ No autonomy:**
> "Everyone swims fly today."

**✅ With autonomy:**
> "Today's focus is max-effort intervals. Pick your best stroke and commit to it."

---

**Example 3: Goal Setting**

**❌ Coach-imposed goals:**
> "Your goal is to go 1:50 in the 200 free."

**✅ Collaborative goals:**
> "Where do you want to be by the end of the season? Let's map out a plan to get you there."

---

**Autonomy Guidelines:**
- ✅ Give choices within structure (not chaos)
- ✅ Let swimmers own their process
- ❌ Don't give autonomy to manipulators (they'll exploit it)
- ❌ Don't overwhelm novices with too many choices

## Putting It All Together: Real-World Scenarios

### **Scenario 1: New Swimmer (First Week of Practice)**

**Goal:** Build trust quickly.

**Actions:**
1. **Learn their name** (use it often)
2. **Ask about their background** ("Where did you swim before?" "What events do you like?")
3. **Set clear expectations** ("Here's what practice looks like. Here's what I expect from you.")
4. **Encourage** ("You're going to do great here. We're excited to have you.")

**Communication strategy:** Seek to understand + empathy + authenticity

---

### **Scenario 2: Swimmer Underperforming**

**Goal:** Identify root cause, provide support.

**Actions:**
1. **Ask questions** ("What's going on? I noticed you've been off this week.")
2. **Listen** (don't interrupt, don't judge)
3. **Empathize** ("That sounds tough. I get it.")
4. **Problem-solve together** ("How can we adjust to help you get back on track?")

**Communication strategy:** Seek to understand + empathy + autonomy

---

### **Scenario 3: Championship Meet Preparation**

**Goal:** Build confidence, manage nerves.

**Actions:**
1. **Remind them of their preparation** ("You've put in the work. You're ready.")
2. **Visualize success** ("See yourself executing your race plan perfectly.")
3. **Focus on process, not outcome** ("Swim YOUR race. The time will come.")
4. **Be calm** (your energy sets the tone — if you're anxious, they'll be anxious)

**Communication strategy:** Empathy + delivery & persuasion + authenticity

---

### **Scenario 4: Teaching a New Technique**

**Goal:** Help swimmer understand and adopt the technique.

**Actions:**
1. **Explain the 3 R's** (Relevance, Reason, Results)
2. **Show a demo** (video or in-person)
3. **Let them try it** (give autonomy to experiment)
4. **Provide feedback** (positive framing)

**Communication strategy:** Educate + autonomy + delivery & persuasion

## Common Communication Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Coaching in anger** → Swimmers shut down  
✅ **Pause, breathe, coach calmly**

❌ **One-size-fits-all communication** → Misses individual needs  
✅ **Adapt to each swimmer's archetype**

❌ **Negative language** ("Don't do X") → Brain focuses on the mistake  
✅ **Positive language** ("Do Y") → Brain focuses on the solution

❌ **Talking AT swimmers** → No buy-in  
✅ **Talking WITH swimmers** → Collaboration

❌ **Ignoring emotional state** → Swimmer can't absorb feedback  
✅ **Addressing emotional state first** → Creates space for coaching

❌ **Giving feedback mid-effort** → Swimmer can't process it  
✅ **Giving feedback after the set** → Swimmer can integrate it

❌ **Using jargon without explanation** → Confusion  
✅ **Breaking down concepts** → Clarity

❌ **Being fake** → Swimmers see through it  
✅ **Being authentic** → Swimmers respect it

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Communication is a SKILL** — it can be learned and improved  
✅ **Know yourself first** — self-awareness enables better coaching  
✅ **Listen before instructing** — understanding drives buy-in  
✅ **Educate with the 3 R's** — Relevance, Reason, Results  
✅ **Use humor** — builds connection and reduces tension  
✅ **Be authentic** — don't fake it  
✅ **Show empathy** — without lowering standards  
✅ **Focus on delivery** — how you say it > what you say  
✅ **Give autonomy** — ownership = motivation  

> "Conscious Coaches don't coach TO athletes — they coach WITH athletes."  
> — Brett Bartholomew

## Daily Practice: The 5-Minute Communication Check

**Before practice:**
- How am I feeling today? (Self-awareness)
- What's my energy/tone? (Delivery)
- Which swimmers might need extra attention? (Empathy)

**During practice:**
- Am I listening or just waiting to talk? (Seek to understand)
- Am I giving positive or negative feedback? (Framing)
- Am I coaching WITH or TO my swimmers? (Collaboration)

**After practice:**
- What went well?
- What could I improve?
- Which communication strategy did I use? Which one should I use tomorrow?
