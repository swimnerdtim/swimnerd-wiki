# Cueing Strategies for Swimming — Practical Translation
**Source:** *The Language of Coaching* by Nick Winkelman (2020)  
**Application:** Research-Based Cueing → Stroke-Specific Implementation

---

## The One Rule of Cueing

> **One rep, one cue, one focus.**

**Why?**  
The brain can only consciously process **one thing at a time**. Multiple cues = cognitive overload = paralysis by analysis.

---

## The Anatomy of an Effective Cue

### 1. **Accurate**
Captures the **most relevant feature** of the movement in context of the **desired outcome**.

❌ "Extend your elbow" (nested detail)  
✅ "Drive your hand to the wall" (outcome that requires elbow extension)

---

### 2. **Vivid**
Creates a **clear, powerful mental image** that represents both **what** to do and **how** it should feel.

❌ "Maintain body position" (vague)  
✅ "Be a missile locked on target" (vivid + actionable)

---

### 3. **External**
Directs attention to the **effect on the environment**, not body parts.

❌ "Rotate your hips and shoulders together" (internal)  
✅ "Roll like a log down a hill" (external + analogy)

---

### 4. **Simple**
Uses **minimal words** to convey maximum meaning.

❌ "Make sure to maintain a high elbow position throughout the pull phase while keeping your forearm vertical" (12 words, 4 concepts)  
✅ "Grab and pull" (3 words, 1 image)

---

## Stroke-Specific External Focus Cues

### FREESTYLE

#### Entry & Extension
**Error:** Short reach, dropped elbow on entry  
**❌ Internal:** "Extend your arm fully, keep your elbow high"  
**✅ External:** "Spear the water 2 feet in front of your head"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Reach over a barrel"

---

#### Catch
**Error:** Slipping water, pushing down  
**❌ Internal:** "Bend your elbow to 90 degrees, engage your lat"  
**✅ External:** "Grab the water and anchor your hand"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Climb a ladder underwater"

---

#### Pull
**Error:** Weak pull, arm drifts wide  
**❌ Internal:** "Keep your forearm vertical, pull your elbow back"  
**✅ External:** "Pull the water from your head to your hip"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Rake leaves from front to back"

---

#### Finish
**Error:** Short finish, elbow stops early  
**❌ Internal:** "Extend your tricep fully"  
**✅ External:** "Throw the water to the wall behind you"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Snap a towel at the finish"

---

#### Rotation
**Error:** Flat, no body roll  
**❌ Internal:** "Rotate your hips and shoulders 45 degrees"  
**✅ External:** "Roll your belly button to the side wall"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Swim like you're in a tube"

---

#### Kick
**Error:** Bent knees, bicycle kick  
**❌ Internal:** "Keep your knees straighter, flex your ankles"  
**✅ External:** "Drive your heels to the wall behind you"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Crack a whip with your legs"

---

### BACKSTROKE

#### Entry
**Error:** Thumb-first entry, crossed midline  
**❌ Internal:** "Enter pinky-first at 11 o'clock"  
**✅ External:** "Slice a knife into the water"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Punch through the surface at shoulder-width"

---

#### Pull
**Error:** Straight-arm pull, weak catch  
**❌ Internal:** "Bend your elbow, pull with your lat"  
**✅ External:** "Scoop the water from over your head to your hip"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Dig a shovel deep and pull"

---

#### Rotation
**Error:** Flat, shoulders don't roll  
**❌ Internal:** "Rotate to 45 degrees on each stroke"  
**✅ External:** "Show your armpit to the ceiling"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Roll like a rotisserie chicken"

---

#### Kick
**Error:** Knees breaking surface, weak kick  
**❌ Internal:** "Kick from your hips, keep your knees down"  
**✅ External:** "Boil the water at your feet"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Kick like your feet are glued together"

---

### BREASTSTROKE

#### Pull
**Error:** Wide, shallow pull  
**❌ Internal:** "Keep your elbows high, pull to your chest"  
**✅ External:** "Scoop ice cream toward your chin"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Part the water like opening curtains"

---

#### Kick
**Error:** Narrow kick, feet not turned out  
**❌ Internal:** "Turn your feet out, snap your legs together"  
**✅ External:** "Kick a ball behind you"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Squeeze water between your heels like a clap"

---

#### Timing
**Error:** Simultaneous pull and kick  
**❌ Internal:** "Pull, breathe, kick, glide in that order"  
**✅ External:** "Pull → shoot → glide"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Row the boat, then kick it forward"

---

#### Streamline
**Error:** Head up, hips dropped  
**❌ Internal:** "Keep your head down, squeeze your core"  
**✅ External:** "Be a torpedo shooting through a tunnel"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Glide downhill on a sled"

---

### BUTTERFLY

#### Entry & Press
**Error:** Hands enter wide, press down  
**❌ Internal:** "Enter shoulder-width, press forward and out"  
**✅ External:** "Stab the water and push it back"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Grab two walls in front and rip them apart"

---

#### Catch
**Error:** Weak catch, arms collapse  
**❌ Internal:** "Keep your elbows high, engage your lats"  
**✅ External:** "Catch the water with your palms and pull it under your chest"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Hug a giant beach ball"

---

#### Kick Timing
**Error:** One big kick, no small kick  
**❌ Internal:** "Two kicks per stroke—small at entry, big at finish"  
**✅ External:** "Pop your hips when your hands enter, then DRIVE when they exit"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Whip your body like a dolphin: up → DOWN"

---

#### Breathing
**Error:** Head lifts too high, hips sink  
**❌ Internal:** "Keep your chin low, look forward"  
**✅ External:** "Sneak a breath through a crack in the water"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Press your chest down to lift your mouth"

---

### STARTS & TURNS

#### Dive Entry
**Error:** Belly flop, feet enter late  
**❌ Internal:** "Keep your body tight, extend through your fingertips"  
**✅ External:** "Dive through a hoop"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Be an arrow piercing the water"

---

#### Underwater Kick
**Error:** Weak kick, no power  
**❌ Internal:** "Kick from your hips, use your glutes"  
**✅ External:** "Drive your feet to the wall behind you"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Rocket off the blocks"

---

#### Breakout
**Error:** Pops up too early, loses speed  
**❌ Internal:** "Hold your streamline longer, stay deep"  
**✅ External:** "Don't break the surface until you're at the flags"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Ride the underwater wave as long as possible"

---

#### Flip Turn
**Error:** Slow rotation, pushes off shallow  
**❌ Internal:** "Tuck your knees, drive your heels over"  
**✅ External:** "Snap your feet to the ceiling, then explode off the wall"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Do a backflip, then shoot like a cannonball"

---

#### Open Turn (Breaststroke/Butterfly)
**Error:** Hands pull down, feet don't plant  
**❌ Internal:** "Touch with both hands, spin your hips"  
**✅ External:** "Slap the wall and whip your body around"  
**✅ Analogy:** "Touch and tornado"

## Analogy Cues: The Ultimate External Focus

**Why analogies work:**
- Bypass technical jargon
- Create instant mental images
- Embed movement **feeling** + movement **outcome**
- Simplify complex coordination

### Freestyle Analogies
- **"Swim downhill"** (body position: head down, hips up)
- **"Unzip your jacket"** (hand entry to finish pulls tight to body)
- **"Paddle like a kayak"** (long, powerful strokes)

### Backstroke Analogies
- **"Walk a tightrope"** (straight line, no snaking)
- **"Stir a pot overhead"** (circular pull pattern)

### Breaststroke Analogies
- **"Heart hands to prayer hands"** (pull recovers narrow)
- **"Rubber band snap"** (explosive kick)

### Butterfly Analogies
- **"Swim like a mermaid"** (fluid, wave-like motion)
- **"Keyhole pull"** (path of hands: out → in → out)

---

## Advanced Cueing: Action Sounds

**What:** Use onomatopoeia to accent tempo, rhythm, or explosive moments.

**Examples:**
- **"BANG"** (start reaction)
- **"Pop-pop"** (butterfly kick timing: light-HEAVY)
- **"ZIP"** (fast hand recovery)
- **"SNAP"** (breaststroke kick finish)
- **"Ssssss"** (long glide, hold streamline)

**When to use:** During execution (rare exception to the "silence during DO it" rule) to reinforce tempo.

## The Coaching Loop Applied to Swimming

### Scenario: Teaching Better Freestyle Catch

#### **Step 1: DESCRIBE it**
"We're working on grabbing the water earlier. This gives you more power and speed through the middle of your stroke."

---

#### **Step 2: DEMONSTRATE it**
(Coach or demo swimmer shows catch with high elbow)  
Optional A/B: Show slipping vs. grabbing

---

#### **Step 3: CUE it** ⭐
"Anchor your hand and pull your body past it."

**Rule:** Only ONE cue. Don't add "and keep your elbow high and rotate your body." That's 3 cues = overload.

---

#### **Step 4: DO it**
Swimmer executes 1 x 50 with that focus.  
**Coach:** Silent. Observes from optimal angle.

---

#### **Step 5: DEBRIEF it**
**Coach:** "How did that feel?"  
**Swimmer:** "I felt like I was grabbing more water."  
**Coach:** "Good. Did you feel it more at the beginning or middle of your pull?"  
**Swimmer:** "Beginning."  
**Coach:** "Perfect. Same focus, extend that feeling through the whole pull."

**Loop restarts at CUE it (Short Loop: CDD)**

## Progression: From Cue-Dependent to Self-Organized

### Week 1: Coach-Led Cueing
- **Coach provides cue before every rep**
- **Swimmer executes**
- **Feedback after every rep or every set**

---

### Week 2: Athlete Self-Cueing
- **Coach:** "What's your focus for this one?"
- **Swimmer:** "Anchor and pull."
- **Coach:** "Go."
- **Feedback:** After set, not every rep

---

### Week 3: Silent Execution
- **Minimal cue or silent**
- **Swimmer internalizes focus**
- **Feedback:** Bandwidth only (if major error)

---

### Week 4: Competition Transfer
- **No cues, no reminders**
- **Technique evaluated under race conditions**
- **If holds → learned. If not → back to Week 2**

## Red Flags: When Your Cue Isn't Working

### 🚩 Swimmer looks confused
**Problem:** Cue is too abstract or uses unfamiliar imagery

**Fix:** Simplify. Use something they've experienced (kayak > "sculling motion")

---

### 🚩 Swimmer improves for 2 reps, then regresses
**Problem:** Cue dependency or blocked practice

**Fix:** Introduce variability. Mix distances/speeds. Fade cues.

---

### 🚩 Swimmer says "I can't think about all that"
**Problem:** You gave 3+ cues in one sentence

**Fix:** **ONE CUE.** That's it.

---

### 🚩 Technique looks good, but swimmer looks tense
**Problem:** Internal focus cue or overthinking

**Fix:** Switch to analogy or outcome-based cue. Add rhythm/tempo cue.

---

### 🚩 Cue works in drills, fails at race pace
**Problem:** Didn't practice under race conditions

**Fix:** Test cues during fast swimming, not just drills.

## Cueing Do's and Don'ts

### ✅ DO:
1. **Default to external focus** ("pull the water" not "bend your elbow")
2. **Use vivid analogies** ("swim like a dolphin" not "undulate your body")
3. **One cue per rep** (more = cognitive overload)
4. **Ask before telling** ("What's your focus?" promotes autonomy)
5. **Embed the goal** ("drive to the wall" not just "drive")
6. **Stay silent during execution** (don't interrupt flow)
7. **Use action sounds sparingly** (rhythm/tempo only)

---

### ❌ DON'T:
1. **Chain multiple cues** ("Reach, grab, pull, push, finish, kick" = 6 things)
2. **Use anatomy lessons** ("Engage your latissimus dorsi" = internal + jargon)
3. **Correct every rep** (feedback dependency)
4. **Talk during execution** (breaks focus)
5. **Give cues that require mirrors** ("See if your elbow is high" = impossible in water)
6. **Use negative cues** ("Don't drop your elbow" → brain ignores "don't")

## Sample Practice Set with Cueing

### Set: 8 x 50 Free on 1:00

**Goal:** Improve catch

#### Reps 1-2: **DDCDD (Long Loop)**
- **DESCRIBE:** "We're working on early grab"
- **DEMONSTRATE:** Show catch with high elbow
- **CUE:** "Anchor your hand like grabbing a rung on a ladder"
- **DO:** Swim
- **DEBRIEF:** "Did you feel the grab at the beginning or middle of your pull?"

---

#### Reps 3-4: **CDD (Short Loop)**
- **CUE:** "Same focus: Grab the ladder"
- **DO:** Swim
- **DEBRIEF (after both reps):** "Rank those two reps. Which felt stronger?"

---

#### Reps 5-6: **Athlete Self-Cue**
- **COACH:** "What's your focus?"
- **ATHLETE:** "Grab the ladder."
- **DO:** Swim
- **DEBRIEF (after both reps):** "How consistent was your grab across those two 50s?"

---

#### Reps 7-8: **Silent Execution + Pressure**
- **CUE:** None, or "Bring everything together. Race pace."
- **DO:** Swim at race pace
- **DEBRIEF (end of set):** "Did your catch hold at speed? What fell apart, if anything?"

## Implementation Checklist

- [ ] **Audit current cues:** Are they internal or external?
- [ ] **Rewrite top 10 cues** using external focus and analogies
- [ ] **Test with swimmers:** "What were you thinking about?"
- [ ] **Track retention:** Does technique hold in competition?
- [ ] **Fade cues over 3-4 weeks:** Coach-led → Athlete-led → Silent
- [ ] **Practice under pressure:** Test technique at race pace and when fatigued
- [ ] **Limit to ONE cue per rep:** Fight the urge to add "and also..."

## Key Quote

> "A vivid image is one that is both clear and powerful in the feelings it incites. A cue is vivid if it encourages an image that accurately represents the target features of the movement (the what) while also capturing the essence or feeling one should have while performing the movement (the how)."  
> — Nick Winkelman

---

**Bottom Line:**  
The best cue is the one the swimmer **understands**, **feels**, and can **execute without thinking about it**.

If you need 3 sentences to explain your cue, it's not a cue—it's a lecture.

**One rep. One cue. One focus.**
