# Hannula's Coaching Philosophy

**Source:** *Coaching Swimming Successfully* by Dick Hannula (2003)

## Core Philosophy Tenets

Dick Hannula, with over 50 years of coaching experience (Wilson High School & Tacoma Swim Club), built his program on six equally important principles:

### 1. **No Substitute for Hard Work**
- Success = direct result of work invested
- No shortcuts for athletes OR coaches
- Consistent, persistent effort is prerequisite for achievement

### 2. **Every Athlete Counts**
- Team only as strong as weakest link
- Concern for ALL swimmers' attitude & improvement
- **Winning redefined:** Measured against yourself, not just placement
- Every swimmer can be a winner through goal attainment

### 3. **Responsibility Required**
- Greatest power = ability to choose
- Coaches responsible for team results
- Swimmers responsible for their choices (study habits, sleep, training commitment)

### 4. **Teach First, Train Second**
- **CRITICAL:** Establish good technique before training effectively
- Keep returning to basics
- "Fundamentals must be learned before training can take place" —Bill Sweetenham

### 5. **Change is Necessary**
- Athletes change, methods evolve
- Variation prevents coach burnout
- Don't repeat same program just because it worked last year

### 6. **Enthusiasm is Essential**
> "Nothing can be accomplished without enthusiasm." —Lincoln High School motto

**Key insight:** Hannula's Olympic swimmer told him after graduation: *"Coach, don't ever lose your enthusiasm!"* — this had the greatest effect on him as a swimmer.

## Communication Principles

### Tell Swimmers What They Do RIGHT
- Strengthen positive mental pictures
- Praise correct technique, don't just correct faults
- "Great competitors" statement reinforces belief

### KISS Method (Keep It Short & Simple)
Examples of concise coaching:
- "Elbows up!"
- "Lengthen your reach!"
- "Finish your stroke!"

Focus on ONE technique point at a time.

### Never Reprimand Learners
- Reprimands don't teach skills
- Be persistent but patient with willing learners
- Discipline = gradual development of self-control, NOT punishment

## Building Team Pride

### Pride Sources
1. **Discipline in training** — completing every challenging set
2. **Teammate challenges** — pushing each other in practice
3. **Winning close races** — toughest/strongest at the finish
4. **Excelling in relays** — relay times better than individual splits
5. **Tradition** — honor rolls, records, All-American boards

### Standards vs. Rules
- Hannula prefers **standards** (something to attain) over rules (things to break)
- Standards build pride
- Example: school attendance required for competition, passing grades minimum requirement

## Discipline Philosophy

> "Discipline has more to do with accomplishing things that need to be done than avoiding things that violate team standards."

- Self-control over extended periods
- Personal/social habits that contribute to team success
- Suspended athletes could ALWAYS return — never permanent
- Focus: what you ACHIEVE, not what you avoid

## Action Items for Coaches

✅ **Define your 6 core principles** — write them down, share with team
✅ **Communicate what swimmers do RIGHT** — shift from negative to positive feedback
✅ **Teach technique before training intensity** — fundamentals first
✅ **Keep enthusiasm high** — it's contagious and affects everything
✅ **Set standards, not just rules** — give athletes something to achieve
