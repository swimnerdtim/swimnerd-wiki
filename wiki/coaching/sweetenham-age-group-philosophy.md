# Bill Sweetenham's Age-Group Development Philosophy
**Source:** *Championship Swim Training* by Bill Sweetenham & John Atkinson (2003)

## Core Philosophy

> "We should develop swimmers younger than age 12 as 200m IM competitors. Age-group swimmers aged 13-14 should be developed as 400m IM, middle distance, and distance freestyle competitors."

**The theory:** Young swimmers who develop four strong strokes and a good endurance base will have more options and better foundations as seniors.

**The alternative:** Specializing too early limits long-term potential.

---

## The Competitive Swimming Commitment Gap

### Three Levels of Involvement

**8 Hours Per Week:**
- ✅ Participation, fun, involvement, health benefits
- ❌ **NOT competition swimming**
- ❌ **Never produces competitive results**

**Reality check:** This is recreational swimming, not competitive development.

---

**10-12 Hours Per Week:**
> "The competitive swimming twilight zone"

- ❌ Too much training to be fun
- ❌ Not enough training to produce results
- ❌ Swimmers in this middle ground never feel good
- ❌ Frustration builds over time

**The trap:** Athletes do just enough to feel tired but not enough to see improvement. This is where dropout happens.

---

**18-24 Hours Per Week:**
- ✅ **THIS is competitive swimming**
- ✅ Athletes are COMMITTED (not just involved)
- ✅ Satisfaction from improved results
- ✅ Meaningful progress occurs

**The coaching challenge:** 
> "Sell the dream to the 10-12 hour swimmers and persuade them to commit to the 18-24 hours they need to become successful competitive swimmers."

---

## Squad System Structure

### Philosophy of Progression

**Key principle:** Fast-track talented swimmers. Don't make them wait.

**Talent identification:** 
- Essential for clubs and lesson programs
- Talented swimmers move DIRECTLY from lessons to competitive section
- Avoid risk of dropout before reaching competitive level

### The Squad Levels

| Squad Level | Focus | Sessions/Week | Distance/Session |
|-------------|-------|---------------|------------------|
| **Level 1** | Junior | 2-3 | 1000-2000m |
| **Level 2** | Intermediate | 5 | 2500-4000m |
| **Level 3** | Development | 8 | 4000-5000m |
| **Level 4** | Junior National / Club Squad | Variable | Variable |
| **Level 5** | National Squad | Variable | Variable |

**Note:** Larger clubs have more squad levels. Movement between levels requires meeting set criteria.

---

## Squad Level 1: Junior Development

### When Swimmers Enter Level 1

**Reality:** One Level 1 session = two swimming lessons in volume

**Initial commitment:** 2 sessions/week for 3-4 weeks, then increase to 3 sessions/week

### Training Focus (Level 1)

**Technical foundations:**
1. ✅ Stroke technique on all four competitive strokes (coach-controlled sets, single laps)
2. ✅ Stroke drill progressions with fins (Chapters 4-7 method)
3. ✅ Endurance and speed prep for next level
4. ✅ Introduction to IM swimming

**Race skills:**
5. ✅ Turns for all four strokes and IM
6. ✅ Starts on all four strokes
7. ✅ Relay takeovers
8. ✅ Correct finishes

**Water sense:**
9. ✅ Sculling and games (wrist-up, wrist-down, flat-wrist)

**Training culture:**
10. ✅ Using a pace clock
11. ✅ Group training and lane discipline
12. ✅ Rules of the sport
13. ✅ Club time trials every 6 weeks
14. ✅ Low-level competition introduction
15. ✅ Punctual start times
16. ✅ Basic stretching

**Required equipment:** Kickboard, drink bottle, flippers

### Sample 6-Week Plan (Level 1)

**3 sessions/week, 1 hour each, 1000-2000m per session**

| Session | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 | Week 4 | Week 5 | Week 6 |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| **1** | BF tech/drills + Relays | FS tech/drills + Turns | IM + FS long + Sculling | BF tech/drills + Finishes | FS tech/drills + Relays | All strokes starts/turns/finishes |
| **2** | BK tech/drills + Sprints | IM 50s + Finishes | BR tech/drills + FS 100s | BK tech/drills + Turns | IM 100s + Sculling | All strokes starts/turns/finishes |
| **3** | BR tech/drills + Sculling | FS tech/drills + Sprints | BK tech/drills + FS long + Sprints/turns | BR tech/drills + Sprints | FS tech/drills + Relays | Timed swims |

**Each box = main set + secondary set** (warm-up/cool-down not shown)

### Criteria to Move to Level 2

Must demonstrate:
1. ✅ 200m FS with tumble turns and bilateral breathing
2. ✅ 200m BK with tumble turns and bent arm pull
3. ✅ 200m BR with correct underwater pullouts
4. ✅ 100m BF with fins and correct turn
5. ✅ 100m IM and turns
6. ✅ Competition racing start
7. ✅ Correct finishes on all four strokes

**No passing = stay in Level 1 and keep developing**

## Squad Level 2: Intermediate Development

### Training Commitment

**Sessions available:** 5 per week, 1-1.5 hours each

**Weekly volume:** 12.5-20 kilometers

**Session volume:** 2500-4000m

### Training Focus (Level 2)

**Technical progression:**
1. ✅ Develop/improve stroke technique (all strokes)
2. ✅ Learn basic stroke drill progressions
3. ✅ More complex drill progressions introduced
4. ✅ Monitor training with test sets (Chapter 2)

**Event development:**
5. ✅ IM swimming sets
6. ✅ Perfect starts, turns, finishes (all strokes + IM turns)
7. ✅ Perfect relay takeovers

**Training literacy:**
8. ✅ Independent use of pace clock
9. ✅ Well-balanced program: builds, negative splits, descending sets
10. ✅ Basic swimming speed, pacing, overdistance
11. ✅ Rules of the sport

**Competition:**
12. ✅ Club time trials (variety of strokes/distances)
13. ✅ Local meets, carnivals, scratch meets

**Athlete development:**
14. ✅ Goal setting process
15. ✅ Training logbook (Chapter 16 format)
16. ✅ Basic stretching + body-weight exercises + core strength

**Equipment responsibility:** Pull buoy, drink bottle, flippers

### Sample 6-Week Plan (Level 2)

**5 sessions/week, 1-1.5 hours, 2500-4000m per session**

| Session | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 | Week 4 | Week 5 | Week 6 |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| **1** | BF tech/drills + Starts | FS 100 repeats + BK tech/drills | IM 50 link set + Starts | BR tech/drills + Relays | IM 200 repeats + Fly drills/FS tech | All strokes starts/turns/finishes |
| **2** | BR tech/drills + Relays | FS 200 builds + BF tech/drills | BK/BR set + Sculling | FS tech/drills + BK drills | FS 400 neg split + Finishes | IM set |
| **3** | FS tech/drills + Build swims | FS 400 neg split + BR tech/drills/sprints | BF/BK set + Finishes | IM tech + BR drills + Sculling | 100 IM set + FS drills + Starts | Timed swims + All stroke turns + Sprints |
| **4** | BK tech/drills + Finishes | IM 100 repeats + Neg-split FS 100s | BR/FS set + Turns | BK tech/drills + Finishes | FS 100 repeats + FS drills + Descending swims | All stroke turns + Sprints mixture |
| **5** | IM 100 repeats + Sprints | 25m repeats all strokes + Sculling | All strokes mixture (coach's choice) + All stroke turns | FS tech/drills + Relays | 25m repeats all strokes + Sculling | Timed swims race night |

### Criteria to Move to Level 3

Must demonstrate:
1. ✅ 10 × 100m FS on 2:15 with correct turns
2. ✅ 10 × 100m IM on 2:45 with correct turns
3. ✅ Confident swimming 200m IM in competition
4. ✅ Attend all 5 sessions available in Level 2 each week

**Attendance is non-negotiable for advancement**

---

## Squad Level 3: Development Squad

### Purpose

**Goal:** Prepare to race at state/district age championships OR make first national age qualifying time

**Commitment level increases:** This is where "competitive" becomes real

### The Commitment Decision Point

**At conclusion of Level 3, swimmers choose one of two paths:**

**Path 1: Junior National Squad**
- ✅ Has state final OR national age qualifying time
- ✅ Attended 8 sessions/week in Level 3
- ✅ Willing to commit to required sessions in next squad
- ✅ Results + commitment = advancement

**Path 2: Club Squad**
- ❌ Late developer (no state final/national age time by age 14)
- ❌ Has results but unwilling to commit to Junior National training load
- ❌ Did not attend 5 sessions/week in Level 2

**The distinction:** Talent without commitment = club squad. Talent + commitment = junior national squad.

## Coaching Age-Group Swimmers: Key Principles

### Training Philosophy for Young Athletes

**Under age 13:**
- ❌ **Do NOT need** specific high-level endurance sets
- ✅ **DO need** aerobic focus with skill enhancement
- ✅ **Little need** for critical speed training (Zone 3)
- ✅ **Emphasis on** short rest and overdistance with varying stimulus

**The challenge:**
> "People say that with aerobic training, athletes become bored before they get tired. Good coaching will make the difference."

### Event Focus by Age

**Age 12 and under:**
- Primary event: **200m IM**
- Why: Develops four strong strokes + endurance base
- Result: More options as senior swimmer

**Age 13-14:**
- Primary events: **400m IM, middle distance, distance freestyle**
- Why: Builds aerobic capacity during key growth period
- Result: Strong foundation for any specialization later

### Gender Differences in Training

**Female swimmers:**
- ✅ Use MORE volume and intensity of aerobic training
- Why: Different recovery patterns than males
- Application: Higher A2 percentage, less A1

**Male swimmers:**
- Follow standard progression in training zones

## Competitive Philosophy for Age-Group

### Competition Frequency

> "Competing too frequently is a problem within our sport. Good coaching can mean swimmers need fewer competitions."

**The midseason paradox:**
- Less committed swimmers may have MORE success at midseason meets
- Why: No training taper vs. committed swimmers still in heavy training
- Danger: Creates false sense of progress for uncommitted athletes

**The coaching response:**
- Construct training to meet needs of individual swimmers
- 9-year-old session ≠ 20-year-old session
- Fewer, more focused competitions for serious swimmers

### Training vs. Racing Balance

**For age-group swimmers:**
- ❌ Too much racing = not enough training adaptation
- ✅ Strategic competition = assessment + motivation
- ✅ Club time trials (every 6 weeks) = controlled testing environment

## Talent Identification & Fast-Tracking

### The Fast-Track Philosophy

**Old way:**
1. Lessons → Pre-competitive → Age-group competitive (slow progression)
2. Risk: Talented swimmers drop out before reaching competitive section

**Sweetenham way:**
1. Identify talent in lessons
2. Move IMMEDIATELY to competitive section
3. Skip unnecessary steps

**Why this works:**
- Talented kids need challenge
- Peer group matters (train with other talented swimmers)
- Early commitment creates culture

### What to Look For (Talent ID)

**Physical markers:**
- Feel for water
- Coordination
- Body position awareness
- Fast twitch capacity

**Mental markers:**
- Responds positively to challenge
- Enjoys training
- Competitive fire
- Coachable

**Don't wait for "the right time" - fast-track immediately**

## Practical Coaching Tips

### Session Design for Age-Group

**What works:**
1. ✅ Short rest intervals (builds aerobic without boredom)
2. ✅ Overdistance swims (not sprints)
3. ✅ Varying stimulus (don't do same 20×100 every week)
4. ✅ Games and sculling (water sense development)
5. ✅ Stroke drills with fins (technique + fitness)

**What doesn't work:**
1. ❌ High-level endurance sets (Zone 3 critical speed for <13)
2. ❌ Excessive lactate work (Zone 4)
3. ❌ Pure sprint training (Zone 5)
4. ❌ Monotonous training (same sets weekly)

### The Punctuality Standard

**From Level 1 onward:**
- ✅ Sessions start on time (no waiting)
- ✅ Athletes arrive early
- ✅ Warm-up begins at posted time

**Why this matters:**
- Sets culture of professionalism
- Teaches time management
- Eliminates excuses

## Parental Education

### The Commitment Conversation

**What parents need to understand:**

**8 hours/week:**
"Your child is doing recreational swimming. They're healthy and having fun, but they won't be competitive."

**10-12 hours/week:**
"This is the danger zone. Too much to enjoy, not enough to succeed. Let's either go to 8 hours for fun or 18+ hours for results."

**18-24 hours/week:**
"This is what competitive swimming requires. Your child needs to be all-in, and so do you."

**The ask:** Don't sugarcoat it. Parents need to know what "competitive" actually means.

---

## Winter Training Philosophy

### Seasonal Training Approach

**Winter training emphasis:**
- ✅ Less quality, more quantity
- ✅ Less racing, more training
- ✅ More dryland + cross-training ON TOP OF swimming
- ✅ More workout frequency

**Purpose:** Build base that summer competitions will reveal

**Key insight:** Age-group training MUST vary by season, not stay flat year-round

---

## The Bottom Line

> "There is a place for the 8 hour-a-week swimmer. The job of the coach is to sell the dream to the athletes doing 10-12 hours a week and persuade them to commit to the 18-24 hours a week they need to become successful competitive swimmers."

**Sweetenham's age-group philosophy in one sentence:**
Develop young swimmers as 200m IM / 400m IM athletes with strong aerobic bases and all four strokes, fast-track talented swimmers immediately, and make the commitment gap crystal clear to athletes and parents.

**It's not about being nice. It's about being honest.**
