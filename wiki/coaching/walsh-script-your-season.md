# Bill Walsh: Script Your Season — Planning Under Pressure
**Source:** *The Score Takes Care of Itself* by Bill Walsh (2009)  
**Cross-sport application:** NFL → Swimming

---

## The Big Idea

> "Informed preplanning—looking perceptively into the future and getting ready for it—gave us a distinct advantage."  
> — Bill Walsh

**Walsh's innovation:** He scripted the first 20-25 plays of every game **before kickoff**, complete with contingency plans for every situation.

**Why it worked:**
- Removed panic-driven decisions during the chaos of competition
- Allowed calm, clinical thinking on Thursday/Friday instead of reactive choices on Sunday
- Gave players confidence (they knew what was coming)
- Extended preparation into performance

**The motto:** *"Be Prepared"* (Boy Scouts) became *"Be Pre-Scripted"* (Bill Walsh).

---

## Walsh's Scripting System

### The Original Method (NFL)

**What Walsh scripted:**
- First 20-25 plays of the game
- Contingency plays for different situations (tied, ahead, behind)
- Adjustments based on opponent behavior
- Backup plans for injuries, weather, momentum shifts

**The Process:**
1. **Thursday-Friday:** Clinical atmosphere, no pressure
2. **Write the script:** Detailed play calls with options
3. **Review with team:** Players get the script, start thinking
4. **Practice the script:** Friday/Saturday walk-throughs
5. **Sunday:** Execute. Adjust as needed, but from a prepared baseline.

**The Result:**
- 49ers scored on their opening drive **8 out of 11 games** in one season (league average: 1-2 times/season)
- Walsh could "close his eyes and literally see all twenty-two men running" before the play happened
- Reduced panic, increased precision

**Quote:**
> "Planning even one day ahead was usually much better than trying to make a decision in the heat of the contest amid the clatter and chaos. I reduced the possibility of panic-driven, ill-conceived decisions."

## Translating Scripting to Swimming

### 1. **Script Your Practices (Weekly Planning)**

**The Problem:**  
Most coaches wing it. Monday: "Let's do some kick." Friday: "Uh... sprint day?"

**The Walsh Way:**  
Plan the entire week on Sunday night. Know exactly what you're doing and why.

**Sample Week Script:**

**Monday: Aerobic Base + Stroke Refinement**
- Warm-up: 1000 choice (400 free, 300 kick, 200 pull, 100 drill)
- Main: 4×400 IM order @ 5:30 (descend 1-4)
- Drill: 8×50 fly/back (25 drill, 25 swim) on 1:00
- Cool-down: 300 easy

**Tuesday: Lactate Tolerance + Speed**
- Warm-up: 800 (build every 100)
- Main: 5×200 @ 3:00 (target: 2:10-2:15), 100 easy between
- Speed: 8×25 from blocks @ 1:00 (all-out)
- Cool-down: 200 easy

**Wednesday: Active Recovery + Technique**
- Warm-up: 600 choice
- Technique: 20×50 @ 1:00 (stroke count challenge: <16/25)
- Kick: 10×50 @ 1:10 (vertical kicking between)
- Cool-down: 400 easy

**Thursday: Threshold + Underwaters**
- Warm-up: 1000 (every 4th 25 underwater kick)
- Main: 12×100 @ 1:30 (hold 1:15-1:18)
- Underwaters: 4×25 underwater kick for distance (record best)
- Cool-down: 300 easy

**Friday: Taper / Race Prep**
- Warm-up: 600 (include starts/turns)
- Race simulation: 2×100 @ 5:00 (race pace, full technique)
- Reaction drills: 6×15m from blocks (work reaction time)
- Cool-down: 400 easy

**The Walsh Insight:**  
By planning Thursday-Friday's practice on Sunday, you can **anticipate fatigue** from Monday-Wednesday and adjust. You're not making decisions in the moment when you're tired and reactive.

### 2. **Script Your Taper (Meet Preparation)**

**Walsh's Super Bowl Prep:**  
He knew exactly what plays he'd call in which situations. Players could "sleep a little better because I had alleviated some of the deep anxiety caused by uncertainty prior to competition."

**Swim Translation: Script the Final 2 Weeks Before Championship**

**14 Days Out:**
- Full volume, but stroke refinement emphasis
- Test underwaters (how many kicks to 15m?)
- Video stroke technique, identify fixes

**10 Days Out:**
- Volume drops 30%
- Maintain intensity (speed work, threshold sets)
- Perfect race starts & turns (10 min/practice)

**7 Days Out:**
- Volume drops 50%
- Race pace efforts (3×100, 4×50 at goal pace)
- Mental rehearsal (visualize races)

**3 Days Out:**
- Light technique work
- Reaction drills
- Mobility & activation

**Day Before:**
- 10-15 min easy swim
- Visualization
- Sleep protocol (electronics off by 9pm)

**Meet Day:**
- **Scripted warm-up** (see below)

**Why this works:**  
Removes guesswork. Swimmers know the plan. Anxiety drops. Execution improves.

## 3. **Script Your Meet Warm-Up (The Most Overlooked Tool)**

**The Problem:**  
Most swimmers show up and do "whatever feels good." Result: Inconsistent preparation, suboptimal performance.

**The Walsh Solution:**  
Every warm-up is scripted. Same every time. No decisions, just execution.

**Sample Championship Meet Warm-Up Script:**

**60 minutes before race:**

1. **Dry-land activation (10 min):**
   - Arm circles (10 each direction)
   - Leg swings (10 each leg)
   - Band pull-aparts (20 reps)
   - Bodyweight squats (15 reps)
   - Dynamic stretching (not static)

2. **Pool warm-up (30 min):**
   - 400 easy mixed strokes (get loose)
   - 200 kick (build intensity)
   - 8×50 @ 1:00 (odds: drill, evens: swim, build)
   - 4×25 descend (25%, 50%, 75%, 90% effort)
   - 2×15m underwater kick (prime the nervous system)
   - 100 easy cool-down

3. **Mental prep (10 min):**
   - Visualization (see your race, feel it)
   - Breathing protocol (box breathing: 4-4-4-4)
   - Review race plan (splits, strategy)

4. **Final activation (5 min before race):**
   - 2×25 sprint (get heart rate up)
   - Shoulder activation (bands or dynamic stretches)
   - Deep breathing (calm the nerves)

**Walsh's principle:**  
"The players wanted those plays so they could start thinking about them. Right away the expectation level would pop up."

**Translation:**  
Give swimmers the warm-up script Friday before the meet. They'll visualize it, rehearse it mentally, show up ready.

## 4. **Script Your Race Strategy (Race Plans)**

**Walsh's approach:**  
"If this situation arises, we do this; if this happens, we do that. On and on. It was almost by the numbers."

**Swim Translation: Create Race Plans for Every Event**

**Example: 200 Freestyle Race Plan**

| **Split** | **Target Time** | **Strategy** | **Technique Cues** |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|-------------------|
| **50** | 27.5 | Controlled aggression, establish rhythm | Long strokes, 6-beat kick, breathe every 3 |
| **100** | 56.8 | Hold pace, stay relaxed | Maintain stroke count (<16/25), efficient turns |
| **150** | 1:27.0 | Begin acceleration, mental toughness kicks in | Increase tempo, not stroke count. Tight streamlines. |
| **200** | 1:56.5 | All-out, finish strong | Last 15m: sprint through the wall, no breathing |

**Contingency Plans:**

- **If ahead at 100:** Don't coast. Maintain rhythm, build confidence.
- **If behind at 100:** Stay patient. Stick to the plan. Others may fade.
- **If legs are dead at 150:** Mental toughness. Shorten stroke, increase tempo. Fight.

**Walsh's wisdom:**  
"I defy you to think as well—as clearly—under great stress as you do in normal circumstances. Unless you've planned and thought through the steps."

**Result:**  
Swimmers don't make panic decisions mid-race. They execute the pre-planned strategy.

## 5. **Script Your Season (Periodization Planning)**

**Walsh's long-term thinking:**  
He didn't just script games. He scripted entire seasons. He knew when to peak, when to rest, when to experiment.

**Swim Season Script (Sample 20-Week Plan):**

| **Phase** | **Weeks** | **Focus** | **Volume** | **Intensity** |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| **Base** | 1-6 | Aerobic capacity, technique | High (7-9k/practice) | Low-Moderate |
| **Build** | 7-12 | Lactate tolerance, strength | Moderate-High (6-8k) | Moderate-High |
| **Peak** | 13-16 | Race pace, speed, power | Moderate (5-7k) | High |
| **Taper** | 17-18 | Recovery, sharpening | Low (3-5k) | High (short bursts) |
| **Championship** | 19-20 | Race execution | Very Low (2-3k) | Race only |

**Microcycle within Macrocycle (Weekly Script):**

**Week 10 (Build Phase):**
- Monday: Threshold (12×100 @ 1:30)
- Tuesday: Lactate tolerance (5×200 hard @ 4:00)
- Wednesday: Recovery (technique drills, easy distance)
- Thursday: Speed (10×50 @ 1:30, descend)
- Friday: Active recovery or OFF
- Saturday: Long swim (3000 straight, moderate pace)

**Walsh's key:**  
"With the 49ers I began asking my offensive coaches to give me their twenty-five scripted plays; then I'd revise and add my own to their ideas."

**Translation:**  
Involve assistants. Get athlete feedback. Refine the plan. But have a plan.

## The Psychological Advantage of Scripting

**From the book:**

> "The players and coaches could sleep a little better because I had alleviated some of the deep anxiety caused by uncertainty prior to the competition; they were somewhat relieved because they could anticipate what we'd do in the opening stages of the battle."

**For swimmers:**

**Without a script:**
- "I hope I swim fast."
- "I don't know what to expect."
- "What if I go out too hard?"

**With a script:**
- "I know my splits. I know my strategy."
- "I've visualized this race 20 times."
- "If X happens, I do Y. I'm prepared."

**Confidence = Demonstrated Ability + Preparation**

**Scripting provides:**
- Clarity (no guessing)
- Confidence (you've rehearsed this)
- Calmness (less anxiety, more execution)

## Walsh's Contingency Planning Template

**The Question:**  
*"What do I do if...?"*

**Swim Coaching Examples:**

**What do I do if...**

- **...a swimmer gets sick during taper?**  
  → Script: Light technique work only. No volume. Focus on hydration & sleep.

- **...the pace clock breaks mid-practice?**  
  → Script: Switch to timed sets (coach calls intervals). Or: Use pool clock + mental math.

- **...a key swimmer is injured 2 weeks before champs?**  
  → Script: Rehab protocol already written. Backup athlete knows their role.

- **...the team is way behind at a meet and morale tanks?**  
  → Script: Huddle up. Refocus on execution, not scoreboard. Celebrate effort & technique.

- **...a swimmer false starts in finals?**  
  → Script: Mental reset protocol. Deep breaths. Focus on next race. Move on.

**Walsh's insight:**  
"Far-reaching contingency planning gave me a tremendous advantage because I was no different from anyone else; it was almost impossible for me to make quick and correct decisions in extreme emotional and mental upheaval."

**Coaching under stress is hard. But if you've already made the decision...**  
You just execute the script.

## How to Start Scripting (Practical Steps)

### Step 1: Script Next Week's Practices

- Sunday night: Write Monday-Saturday practices
- Include: Warm-up, main set, drill work, cool-down
- Add one "contingency" (e.g., if pool time is cut short, what gets dropped?)

### Step 2: Script Your Next Meet Warm-Up

- Write a repeatable warm-up protocol
- Share it with swimmers 48hrs before the meet
- Enforce it (no freelancing)

### Step 3: Script 3 Race Plans

- Pick your top 3 events
- Write target splits, strategy, technique cues
- Include contingencies ("if behind at halfway...")

### Step 4: Script Your Taper

- Work backwards from championship date
- Plan volume, intensity, rest days
- Include non-swim factors (sleep, nutrition, mental prep)

### Step 5: Review & Refine

- After each meet: What worked? What didn't?
- Adjust scripts based on data
- Keep what works, discard what doesn't

**Walsh's wisdom:**  
"Scripting took years for other teams to fully implement the concepts I had been developing for a long time."

**Translation:** Start small. Build your system. Refine over seasons.

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Plan in calm, execute in chaos** — Decisions made Thursday > decisions made Sunday

✅ **Reduce anxiety through preparation** — Scripts give swimmers confidence

✅ **Anticipate contingencies** — "What do I do if...?" should have answers

✅ **Consistency breeds excellence** — Same warm-up, same race plan, same preparation = reliable results

✅ **Involve your team** — Share scripts. Get feedback. Refine together.

✅ **Flexibility within structure** — Scripts aren't rigid. Adjust as needed. But start with a plan.
