# Dave Salo: The Case Against Traditional Aerobic Base

**Category:** Training Philosophy, Physiology, Sprint Training, Aerobic Development  
**Coach:** Dave Salo  
**Influenced by:** Dr. Ronald Costill (research from ~1973)

## Summary

Dave Salo challenges traditional aerobic base training not by avoiding aerobic development, but by developing it through race-pace training instead of submaximal volume. His approach focuses on recruiting 100% of muscle fibers at maximum intensity to increase aerobic capacity across all fiber types, rather than the traditional model of long, slow distance work.

---

## Core Argument

### Traditional Aerobic Base Problem

**Issue:** Low-intensity aerobic training only recruits slow-twitch muscle fibers sequentially

If you have 100 muscle fibers:
- At low intensity: 10 fibers firing
- Then next 10 fire
- Then next 10 fire
- To engage all 100 fibers, intensity must be very high

**Result:** Fast-twitch fibers never get developed aerobically in traditional base training

---

## Salo's Solution: Race Pace Training

### The Science

1. **All muscle fibers have aerobic capacity** - Including fast-twitch (contrary to some coaching mythology)
2. **Maximum effort = maximum recruitment** - 100% of fibers recruited simultaneously
3. **Sprint training increases aerobic capacity** - Supported by Ronald Costill research (~1973)

## Terminology: Race Pace vs. Sprint

Salo distinguishes his approach:
- **NOT "sprint training"** (implies only short work)
- **Race Pace Training** - Includes:
  - Short sprint (50s)
  - Long sprint (100-200s)
  - ALL work at race pace or faster

## Physiological Adaptations

### What Increases with Race Pace Training:
- **Aerobic capacity** across all fiber types
- **Cytochrome C** (mitochondrial enzyme)
- **Mitochondrial density** in fast-twitch fibers
- **Lactate clearance** ability
- **Power per stroke**

### Research Basis
**Ronald Costill Study (~1973)**
- Examined sprint training effects on aerobic markers
- Showed aerobic capacity increases with sprint work
- Studied animals (allowing controlled variables)
- Measured full aerobic profile including cytochrome C

## Practical Application

### Salo's Workout Structure

**After warm-up:**
1. **Kicking** - Focus on posture, speed elements
2. **Power development** - One stroke at a time
3. **Progressive intensity** - 1-2 strokes → 5 strokes → full length → 50+ meters
4. **Resistance work** - Cords, parachutes, wet cords
5. **Detail-oriented** - Every element done at maximum capacity

**NOT traditional:**
- Long aerobic sets
- Moderate pace threshold work
- High volume, low intensity

---

## Key Principles

1. **Recruit all muscle fibers** - Maximum effort ensures this
2. **Rest allows quality** - Recovery between reps maintains race pace
3. **Specificity** - Train the systems you'll use in racing
4. **Fast-twitch aerobic development** - Don't leave any fiber type behind

---

## Common Misconceptions

### MYTH: "Fast-twitch fibers don't have aerobic capacity"
**REALITY:** They do - but you must recruit them to develop it

### MYTH: "You must do long slow distance for aerobic base"
**REALITY:** Aerobic capacity improves through maximal recruitment, not just duration

### MYTH: "Salo only coaches sprinters"
**REALITY:** Coached Ous Mellouli to 1500m gold medals (2008, 2012) and 10K open water gold

## Coaching Philosophy

Salo describes himself as:
- **Academically trained** (Master's and PhD)
- **Not experientially driven** - Challenged by professors to rethink traditional methods
- **Detail-oriented** - Every aspect of training has purpose
- **Progressive workout design** - Building power stroke by stroke

> *"I'm not avoiding the aerobic base. I developed the aerobic base in a completely different manner."* - Dave Salo

---

## Training Focus Areas

### What Salo Emphasizes:
- **Posture** - Reducing drag, maximizing efficiency
- **Speed into walls** - Habit formation
- **Speed off walls** - Passive glide, minimizing resistance
- **Power per stroke** - Maximizing each individual stroke
- **Surge feeling** - Swimmers should feel speed change with each stroke
- **Detail execution** - Everything done correctly at maximum capacity

## For Coaches

**Consider:**
- Are you developing aerobic capacity in all fiber types?
