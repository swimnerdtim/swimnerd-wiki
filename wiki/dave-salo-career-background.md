# Dave Salo: Career Background and Achievements

**Full Name:** Dr. David Salo  
**Location:** Irvine, California (Orange County)  
**Current Roles:** Irvine Novaquatics Head Coach/GM, Orange Coast College Assistant Coach  
**Education:** Master's and PhD (academic background in exercise physiology)

## Summary

Dave Salo is one of the most accomplished and innovative swim coaches in history, known for revolutionizing sprint and breaststroke training. With an academic foundation (PhD), he challenged traditional training methodologies and developed race-pace training protocols that produced Olympic champions across all distances and strokes. His career spans club coaching (Irvine Novaquatics since 1990), college coaching (USC), and Olympic coaching, with a resume that includes world record holders and gold medalists.

## USC (University of Southern California)

**Tenure:** Multiple years (retired)  
**Role:** Head Coach  
**Status:** Retired from USC, now at Orange Coast College

### Key Accomplishments:

**Athletes Coached at USC:**
- Rebecca Soni - 200m breaststroke WR, Olympic gold (2012)
- Vlad Morozov - Sprint star
- Large roster (80-100 athletes between college team and pro group)

**Challenges:**
- Managing college team + elite pro group simultaneously
- Compliance rules
- Recruiting pressures (pre and post transfer-portal)
- International athletes with varying backgrounds

**Why He Left:**
*"I don't miss NCAA Division I swimming - the landscape of recruiting and that process... you've got to recruit juniors, you've got to recruit seniors, then the transfer portal and they don't have to sit out, you've got to keep track, you've got to keep recruiting your own kids... you're just kind of always on edge about that whole thing."*

## Orange Coast College

**Started:** ~2022-2023  
**Role:** Assistant Coach

### Why Assistant Coach?

**The Dream:**
> *"My entire career except for the five years I coached with Peter Daland I've been a head coach. It was kind of in the back of my mind a dream of being an assistant coach for a change."*

**What He Does:**
- Sink the clocks
- Fix lane lines ("really good at tight lane lines")
- Be a sounding board for Anthony
- Take practices when needed
- Allow head coach to focus on coaching
- Handle parental issues
- No recruiting pressure
- No compliance paperwork

**Inspiration:**
- John Urbanchek retired from Michigan
- Became Salo's volunteer assistant at USC
- "Great to have John on the deck and as a friend as a mentor"
- Salo wanted to do the same for a younger coach
