### Detail Orientation

**Consistent Focus Areas:**

**Posture:**
- Off every wall
- During every glide
- Throughout stroke cycle
- Back flat, streamlined

**Wall Speed:**
- Into walls (last 10m fast)
- Off walls (passive glide, then surge)
- Habit formation
- Race-specific

**The Surge:**
- Feel body move with each stroke
- Speed change sensation
- Not just moving fast, but feeling acceleration
- Proprioception development

**Technique Under Load:**
- Elbow position maintained
- Stroke integrity never compromised
- Race tempo even when tired
- Quality over survival

---

### Progressive Development

**Stroke-by-Stroke Philosophy:**

Appears in:
- Workout structure (1 stroke → 2 → 5 → 25 → 50+)
- Power development (maximize one stroke at a time)
- Resistance work (feel → tempo → speed)
- Set progression (easy → moderate → hard)
- Seasonal training (build to taper)

**Why It Works:**
- Athletes master fundamentals first
- Quality maintained as volume/intensity increases
- Technical integrity never sacrificed
- Confidence builds progressively

### Race-Pace Training Across All Topics

**Universal Application:**

1. **Aerobic Development:** Through race pace, not slow volume
2. **Resistance Work:** Race tempo under load
3. **Stroke Development:** At race pace from early in set
4. **Breaststroke:** Drill work at race pace
5. **Fine-Tuning:** X+Y+Z full speed efforts

**Philosophy:**  
If you're not training at race pace, you're not training specifically for racing
