# Dave Salo: Friday Speed Practice Protocol

**Category:** Speed Training, Resistance Work, Practice Design, Weekly Programming  
**Coach:** Dave Salo  
**Venue:** Irvine Novaquatics  
**Day:** Friday (speed emphasis)

## Summary

Dave Salo's Friday practices at Irvine Novaquatics represent his race-pace training philosophy in action. The session progresses from stroke development with paddles and fins, through various speed drills emphasizing posture and surge, to resistance work with parachutes, culminating in race-pace application. Every element builds toward maximum effort strokes at race tempo, with detail-oriented focus on technique under load.

---

## Practice Philosophy

### Typical Friday Structure

**Total Time:** 90 minutes to 1:45  
**Warm-up:** Minimal (heart rate stays low - "bunch of pansies")  
**Main Work:** Progressive power development → resistance → race pace  
**Cool-down:** Active recovery

### Weekly Pattern

**Fridays:** Resistance work (parachutes)  
**Tuesdays/Thursdays:** Wet cords and other resistance  
**All Days:** Race pace or faster emphasis

---

## Phase 1: Stroke Development

### Goal: Maximize Power Per Stroke

**Progressive Build:**
1. One stroke at a time
2. Two strokes
3. Five strokes
4. Whole length (25m/y)
5. 50+ meters

---

## Set 1: Knuckle Paddles + Fins

### 75s - Knuckle Paddle Work

**Equipment:** Knuckle paddles + fins  
**Focus:** THREE FAST STROKES per 25

**Purpose:**
- Grab paddle with knuckles (not palm)
- Moves pressure point back into mid-arm
- Forces elbows to stay up/high
- Feel the water differently

**Salo's Coaching Cue:**  
"Surge" - feel your body move with speed

**Technical Elements:**
- Posture on side kick
- Side kick surging on pull
- Grab that water
- Feel body move with speed
- "If they find that, then they're finding their stroke"

---

## Set 2: Regular Paddles

### 50s - Full Paddles + Fins

**Focus:** FOUR STROKES coming off wall

**Contrast Work:**
- Just came from knuckle paddles
- Now open to full paddle
- Feel the difference
- More power available

**Technical Emphasis:**
- Posture coming off wall
- Speed coming off wall
- Passive speed maximized
- Then surge into four strokes

**The Surge:**  
"Body should surge with a stroke. When you're doing minimal stroke count like three strokes or four strokes, you should feel that change in speed. Then you settle down into slower speed or less effort."

---

## Set 3: Full Speed 25s

### Regular Paddles + Fins

**Focus:** FULL SPEED, one breath

**Breathing Control:**
- Pick a spot to breathe
- Only one breath
- Same spot every rep
- Not random breathing

**Why It Matters:**
*"I think it's important to teach sprinters to pick their spot. If they're not going to hold their breath for a whole 50, then they should learn to pick a spot and take that spot as their breathing point rather than just random breathing. There's some control in this element."*

---

## Phase 2: Speed Into/Off Walls

### Rotation Kick + Breakout Drill

**First Rounds (2×25s):**

**Part 1:**
- Push off on back
- Glide to line 4 on surface
- Scrunch scull to wall
- Reverse turn
- Push off

**Part 2:**
- Three underwater pulldowns (fast flutter kick)
- Break out into flip turn
- 360° rotation

**Part 3:**
- Superman kick at flags (stop, lower head, 3-count)
- Fast swim into wall

**Later Rounds (2×25s):**
- Push off back, glide to line 4
- Fast kick to line 5
- Fast swim to wall
- **5-second fast kick against wall**
- **Fast flip turn**

### Purpose: Wall Speed Habit

**Into Wall:**
- Fast kick last 10m
- Good posture
- Maintain velocity approaching

**Off Wall:**
- Passive glide maximized
- Reduce resistance/drag
- Posture perfect
- Then power into strokes

---

## Phase 3: Distance Per Stroke (DPS)

### One Stroke Per Black Line

**Setup:**
- Push and glide to line 3
- No stroke until head reaches line 3
- Then ONE powerful stroke per black line
- Head reaches next line = next stroke

**Equipment:**
- 75s: Knuckle paddles
- 50s: Regular paddles
- 25s: Full speed, one breath

**Gears (By Time):**
- 3rd gear
- 4th gear
- 5th gear
- Chart shows targets based on best 100 time

**Coaching Points:**
- Avoid catch-up drill
- Every stroke = full power
- Long, powerful strokes
- Strong kick
- Good posture
- Grab the water effectively

---

## Phase 4: Resistance Work (Parachutes)

### The Main Event: 4 Rounds

**Structure Per Round:**
- 100 (max resistance)
- 4×25 (max resistance)
- 75 (various intensities)
- 25 + fins (active recovery)

---

### 100s - Maximum Resistance

**Parachute:** Fully closed  
**Purpose:** Feel stroke, posture, mechanics

**NOT About:**
- Going fast
- Time
- Speed

**IS About:**
- Accentuating stroke feel
- Maximizing proprioception
- Posture awareness
- Using resistance to enhance feeling

**Coaching:**
*"I'm just using the resistance to really accentuate the feel for the stroke. So it's mostly posture and stroke feel."*

---

### 4×25 - Maximum Resistance, Race Tempo

**THE KEY SET:**  
Salo's "mantra the last few years"

**Protocol:**
- Parachute fully closed (max resistance)
- Only FOUR STROKES per 25
- Somewhere within the 25 (athlete chooses where)
- **RACE TEMPO** required
- Maximum effort

**The Mantra:**

> *"My mantra the last few years is trying to get race tempo when loaded. I think that's the key to that next development."*

### The Problem This Solves

**Common Issue:**
- Athletes put load on
- Slow things down
- Compromise stroke to manage resistance
- Elbows drop
- Stroke integrity lost

**Salo's Goal:**
*"The focus is to get the tempo up - race tempo - without compromising the stroke. That's more specific training than letting the elbows drop and stuff like that."*

**Coaching Cue (Mid-Set):**
*"When you turn it on, race tempo! Gotta dig into the corners. Gotta get here [high elbow], not here [low elbow]. And when you go to light load on that freestyle, you're still on that right side dropped in the elbow. You gotta still hold it even on the casual part. Hold that elbow position."*

---

### 4th × 25 - Minimum Resistance

**Parachute:** Fully open  
**Purpose:** Accommodating resistance

**Protocol:**
- Whole 25 at race tempo
- Get across pool as quickly as possible
- Feel the difference from max resistance
- Still maintaining stroke integrity

---

### 75s - Progressive Max Effort

**First 3 Rounds:**
- Max effort on ANY TWO of the 25s (athlete choice)
- Other 25 = controlled

**Last Round:**
- ALL THREE 25s max effort
- Whole 75 maxed out

**Purpose:**
- Build to full intensity
- Choice within structure
- Progressive overload

---

### Plus 25 - Active Recovery

**Equipment:** Fins  
**Effort:** Easy  
**Purpose:** Active recovery between rounds

**Salo:** *"Oh yeah, we don't have to work fast."*

## Parachute Equipment Details

### Why Singapore Parachutes?

**1. Customization**
- Can brand with Nova logo
- Team identity

**2. Durability**
*"We've had others in the past that just don't hold up very well. They use a lot of metal in the framing and then they rot out."*

**3. Adjustability**
- Can open or close
- Variable resistance
- Though "you can't control it that well"

### Two Settings Used

**Fully Closed:**
- Maximum resistance
- 100s (feel work)
- First 3×25 (race tempo under load)

**Fully Open:**
- Minimum resistance
- 4th×25 (race tempo with accommodation)
- 75s (speed application)

## The "Nightmare" Philosophy Applied

### Creating Stress

**By This Point in Practice:**
1. **Physically fatigued** - Resistance work is cumulative
2. **Mentally challenged** - Constant variety, detail demands
3. **Technically stressed** - Must maintain integrity under load
4. **Psychologically pushed** - "Where dreams become nightmares"

### The Race Simulation

**What Late-Race Feels Like:**
- Fatigued from cords/parachutes
- Still must execute perfectly
- Tempo can't drop
- Stroke can't break down
- Must be your best when feeling worst

**Result:**  
When race gets hard (and it will), athletes have been there before under even worse conditions

## Practical Applications

### For Coaches:

**DO:**
- Progress from stroke development to race pace
- Use resistance to enhance feel first, then build tempo
- Demand race tempo under load
- Don't let athletes slow down with resistance
- Use equipment progressively (knuckle → regular paddles)
- Emphasize walls (speed into/off)
- Build in packages (3-4 rounds, not 7-8)

**DON'T:**
- Let elbows drop under load
- Allow tempo to slow with resistance
- Skip the feeling/posture work
- Forget the surge emphasis
- Overload with too many rounds

### For Swimmers:

**Focus On:**
- Feeling the surge every stroke
- Maintaining posture under load
- Race tempo even when tired
- Elbow position (dig into corners)
- Speed into walls (last 10m)
- Speed off walls (passive, then surge)
- Four strokes max effort when loaded

## Sample Friday Practice

### Complete Breakdown

**WARM-UP:** (Minimal - not detailed)

**SET 1: STROKE DEVELOPMENT**
- 3×75 knuckle paddles + fins (3 fast strokes per 25)
- 3×50 regular paddles + fins (4 strokes off wall)
- 3×25 regular paddles + fins (full speed, one breath, pick spot)

**SET 2: WALL SPEED**
- 2×25 rotation kick + breakout
- 2×25 push/glide + fast kick + fast swim + 5s kick wall + fast turn

**SET 3: DPS + GEARS**
- 3 rounds of 3×25 (one stroke per line, 3rd/4th/5th gear by time)
- Using knuckle paddles → regular paddles → no equipment

**SET 4: PARACHUTE RESISTANCE**  
4 rounds of:
- 100 max resistance (feel stroke)
- 4×25 max resistance (4 strokes race tempo)
- 75 progressive max effort
- 25 + fins (active recovery)

**COOL-DOWN:** Active recovery

**Total Time:** ~90-105 minutes  
**Volume:** Not tracked ("I don't track volume")  
**Focus:** Maximum effort strokes at race pace

## Key Takeaways

**1. Progressive Build**
- Stroke by stroke
- One element to next
- Power → Speed → Resistance → Race Pace

**2. Race Tempo Under Load**
- The mantra
- The key to development
- Cannot compromise

**3. Detail Orientation**
- Posture
- Elbow position
- Surge feeling
- Wall speed
- Every element matters

**4. Resistance as Tool**
- Enhance feeling (max resistance, slow)
- Build power (max resistance, race tempo)
- Apply speed (min resistance, race tempo)

**5. Creating Stress**
- Physical (fatigue)
- Mental (complexity)
- Technical (integrity under load)
- Psychological (nightmare scenario)

---

## Bottom Line

**Friday at Nova = Race Pace Training in Action**

Every element serves the goal: Maximum effort strokes at race tempo with perfect technique, even under maximum fatigue and resistance. This is how Dave Salo produces Olympic champions - not through volume, but through quality execution under stress.

**The Result:** Athletes who can maintain stroke integrity, race tempo, and mental toughness when the race gets hard, because practice was harder.
