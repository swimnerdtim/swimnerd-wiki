# Dave Salo X+Y+Z Set

**Category:** Training, Set Design, Individualization, Fine-Tuning  
**Coach:** Dave Salo  
**Created:** ~2000s (20+ years of use)

## Summary

The X+Y+Z set is a signature Dave Salo training methodology that allows individualized distance, stroke, and equipment choices within a structured interval framework. It's primarily used for fine-tuning during taper phases but is also effective as an in-season training tool. The set provides coaches with insight into what athletes believe they need while maintaining group cohesion on deck.

---

## Set Structure

**Base Intervals:**
- **X** = 1:00
- **Y** = 1:30  
- **Z** = 2:00

**Athlete Choices:**
- Distance (minimum typically 50 yards/meters)
- Stroke
- Equipment (sometimes)

**Effort:** Full speed throughout

**Rounds:** Variable
- Taper/fine-tuning phase: 4-5 rounds
- In-season: can be increased

---

## How It Works

Athletes choose how far they swim on each interval based on what they feel they need:

### Example Progressions:

**Jason Lezak** (sprinter, needs more rest):
- X: 25
- Y: 25
- Z: 25

**Ous Mellouli** (distance swimmer, less rest needed):
- X: 100
- Y: 150-200
- Z: 200

---

## Coaching Benefits

1. **Reveals athlete self-awareness** - Shows what each swimmer thinks they need for recovery vs. intensity
2. **Individualization within structure** - Different training needs accommodated while maintaining group cohesion
3. **Diagnostic tool** - Coaches can observe patterns and adjust training accordingly
4. **Energy on deck** - Athletes send off together, creating "rowdy" group energy even while doing different work
5. **Athlete ownership** - Swimmers take responsibility for their choices

---

## Variations

Dave mentions using this set with creativity throughout the season:
- Varying minimum distances
- Adding equipment requirements
- Adjusting number of rounds
- Modifying intervals based on training phase

---

## Philosophy

This set embodies Salo's belief in:
- **Individualized training** - Recognizing different athletes have different needs
- **Athlete autonomy** - Trusting swimmers to understand their bodies
- **Race pace focus** - Full speed effort, not threshold or aerobic work
- **Fine-tuning mastery** - Precise preparation in critical taper phases

---

## Practical Applications

**For Coaches:**
- Use during taper to let athletes dial in their race prep
- Track what athletes choose - it reveals their race mentality
- Adjust future training based on patterns observed
- Create variations for different points in the season

**For Swimmers:**
- Be honest about what you need, not what others are doing
- Full speed means full speed - don't sandbag
- Use this to learn about your recovery needs
- Experiment during training season, commit during taper

---

## Key Quote

*"Some kids think they need very little rest so they're going to maximize the distance that they go. Some think they need a lot of rest... As a coach you get to find out what they think they need in this critical phase of fine-tuning and then you can adjust your training throughout that period of time."* - Dave Salo
