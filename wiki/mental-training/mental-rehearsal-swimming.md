# Mental Rehearsal for Swimming

**Source:** *With Winning in Mind* by Lanny Bassham (Olympic champion — won gold thousands of times in his mind before 1976 Montreal)  
**Definition:** Thinking about an action without physically doing it — also called visualization, mental imagery  
**Significance:** Creates neural pathways in the brain, paves roadways for actual performance  
**Application:** Race prep, skill acquisition, error correction, confidence building

---

## Why Mental Rehearsal Works

### The Science

**When you vividly rehearse an action, neural pathways are created in the brain.**

- These pathways = small tracks engraved in brain cells
- Similar to physical practice tracks
- Enable the athlete to perform the actual task
- **Mental rehearsal literally paves the roadways you must travel to perform**

**Research:** Shane Murphy (sports psychologist, Olympic Training Center) found mental rehearsal enhances performance across all Olympic sports.

---

## The Bassham Reality: You Don't Need Perfect Visualization

### What Mental Rehearsal ISN'T:

❌ Must see vivid colors  
❌ Must hear sounds  
❌ Must be like a movie  
❌ Must take hours  
❌ Must be done lying down in silence  

### What Mental Rehearsal IS:

✅ Thinking about the action  
✅ Can be a little blurry (Bassham's was)  
✅ Can be done in seconds or minutes  
✅ Can be done anywhere (waiting, exercising, sitting, walking)  
✅ **FREE and EASY**

**Bassham's rehearsal:** Blurry, no sounds, but took him to Olympic gold.

**Key insight:** More often you rehearse = more effective. Vividness helps, but consistency matters more.

## Mental Rehearsal Applications for Swimming

### 1. **Race Preparation**

**When:** Daily in the week leading up to meet, 10 min before race

**How:**
1. Close eyes or soft focus
2. Picture the pool, blocks, water
3. Walk through race from start to finish:
   - Hear the beep
   - Feel the dive
   - See first stroke entry
   - Count strokes underwater (5-7)
   - Breakout tempo
   - Turn execution (approach, flip, push-off, streamline)
   - Finish (stroke into wall, fast hands)

**Duration:** 30 seconds to 5 minutes

**Example (100m Freestyle):**
- Rehearse explosive start (arms back, knees bent)
- Picture 6 dolphin kicks underwater
- See breakout at 15m mark
- Feel tempo: "1-2-3" rhythm
- Picture turn: long into wall, tight flip, feet on wall, push
- See finish: accelerate last 5m, touch with speed

**Frequency:** 3-5 times per day in meet week

---

### 2. **Skill Acquisition (Learning New Technique)**

**Use case:** Teaching high elbow catch, underwaters, new start technique

**Process:**
1. **Watch video** of elite swimmer (Leon Marchand underwater, Dressel start)
2. **Mentally rehearse** performing the skill yourself (first-person view)
3. **Feel the movement** in your mind (kinesthetic rehearsal)
4. **Physical practice** with mental cue
5. **Repeat mental rehearsal** after practice

**Example: Learning High Elbow Catch**
- Picture hand entry (fingertips first, elbow high)
- Feel forearm vertical position
- See water catch (fingertips pointing down, palm back)
- Pull through (elbow stays high, hand accelerates)
- **Rehearse 10 times mentally BEFORE getting in pool**
- Then practice physically
- After practice: rehearse correct version 5 more times

**Why this works:** Brain creates pathway before body tries it = faster learning, fewer mistakes

---

### 3. **Error Correction (After Mistakes)**

**Problem:** Swimmer keeps breathing into the turn, gliding into finish, dropping elbow

**Traditional approach (WRONG):**  
- Coach says: "Stop breathing into the turn!"
- Swimmer pictures: breathing into the turn (imprints mistake)
- Mistake becomes habit

**Mental rehearsal approach (RIGHT):**

**Step 1:** Acknowledge mistake (don't replay it)  
**Step 2:** Immediately rehearse CORRECT version 5 times  
**Step 3:** Write correct version in performance journal  
**Step 4:** Rehearse again before bed

**Example: Fixing Breathing Into Turn**
1. Mistake happens in practice
2. Coach: "Next time, hold breath last 3 strokes into wall"
3. Swimmer stops, closes eyes
4. **Rehearses 5 times:** Long stroke, long stroke, long stroke, HIT (no breath)
5. Tries again physically
6. After practice: rehearse correct version 10 more times
7. Write in journal: "I hold my breath into every turn. That's like me."

**Result:** Correct version imprints stronger than mistake

---

### 4. **Pre-Performance Mental Program**

**When:** On deck, before race (part of mental program — see Mental Management page)

**Rehearsal sequence (2-3 minutes):**

**Phase 1: Calm (30 sec)**
- Deep breath
- Picture yourself confident, ready
- Affirmation: "I am prepared. I am ready."

**Phase 2: Rehearse Race (60-90 sec)**
- Start: explosive reaction
- Underwaters: tight streamline, dolphin kicks
- Breakout: fast hands, rhythm
- Mid-race: hold tempo, stroke count
- Turn: attack wall, fast feet
- Finish: accelerate, touch with speed

**Phase 3: Trigger (10 sec)**
- One deep breath
- Cue word: "Fast" or "Go" or "Smooth"
- **Release** — let subconscious take over

**After race:**
- If good: "That's like me!" (reinforce)
- If bad: refocus immediately, rehearse correct version later

---

### 5. **Building Confidence (Rehearsing Success)**

**When:** Daily, especially during taper, before big meets

**Process:**
- Rehearse standing on podium
- Picture your name on scoreboard
- Feel the medal around your neck
- Hear crowd cheering
- **See yourself winning**

**Why this works:**
- Brain doesn't distinguish between vivid rehearsal and real experience
- Imprints winning identity ("It's like me to win")
- Builds Self-Image of champion

**Bassham example:** "I won the Olympics thousands of times in my mind before entering the competition in Montreal in 1976."

**For swimmers:**
- Rehearse touching first
- See your time (goal time)
- Picture coach celebrating
- Feel the excitement of victory

**Frequency:** Daily for 21 days before big meet

## Practical Rehearsal Drills for Coaches

### Drill 1: **Course Walking (Before Race)**

**Application:** Dog agility handlers and practical pistol shooters walk the course, rehearsing movement

**Swimming version:**
1. Swimmer walks to blocks
2. Looks at pool, lane, turn wall
3. Closes eyes
4. Rehearses entire race (start to finish)
5. Opens eyes
6. Walks back
7. Repeats 2-3 times

**When:** 10-15 min before race

---

### Drill 2: **Post-Practice Rehearsal**

**After hard workout:**
1. Cool down
2. Sit on deck
3. Rehearse one skill from practice (turn, underwaters, tempo)
4. Rehearse it 10 times mentally
5. Write in performance journal

**Why:** Solidifies learning, imprints correct version

---

### Drill 3: **Morning Rehearsal Routine**

**Before school/work:**
1. Sit quietly (2 min)
2. Rehearse race or key skill
3. Affirmation: "I am a champion swimmer. I train with purpose. I race with confidence."
4. Visualize success
5. Start the day

**Builds:** Winning mindset, consistency in mental preparation

---

### Drill 4: **Bed-Time Rehearsal**

**Before sleep:**
1. Lie down
2. Replay best swim/practice moment from today
3. Rehearse tomorrow's workout or race
4. Affirmation: "I am getting better every day"
5. Fall asleep picturing success

**Why:** Brain processes during sleep, imprints overnight

## Mental Rehearsal for Different Phases of Season

### **Early Season (Building Skills)**
- Focus: Technical rehearsal (stroke mechanics, turns, starts)
- Frequency: Daily, 5-10 min
- Content: Rehearse new skills being learned

### **Mid-Season (Racing)**
- Focus: Race rehearsal (strategy, pacing, tempo)
- Frequency: 3-5x per week, 10-15 min
- Content: Full race from start to finish

### **Taper (Peak Performance)**
- Focus: Confidence building, winning rehearsal
- Frequency: Daily, 15-20 min
- Content: Perfect races, podium finishes, championship performances

### **Off-Season (Rest & Visualization)**
- Focus: Goal-setting rehearsal, long-term vision
- Frequency: 2-3x per week, 5 min
- Content: Next season goals, new techniques to learn

## Common Mistakes in Mental Rehearsal

### ❌ Mistake #1: Rehearsing Failure
- Replaying mistakes over and over
- Picturing worst-case scenarios
- **Fix:** Only rehearse what you WANT to happen

### ❌ Mistake #2: No Consistency
- Rehearsing once before big meet
- No routine or structure
- **Fix:** Daily practice, same time each day

### ❌ Mistake #3: Too Vague
- "I picture myself swimming fast"
- No specific details (stroke count, tempo, splits)
- **Fix:** Be specific — see, feel, hear details

### ❌ Mistake #4: Only Rehearsing Outcomes
- "I see myself on the podium" (only)
- Not rehearsing the PROCESS
- **Fix:** Rehearse the race itself, not just the result

### ❌ Mistake #5: Waiting Until It's Too Late
- Only rehearsing the night before race
- No time to build neural pathways
- **Fix:** Start rehearsal weeks before competition

## The Mental Rehearsal Toolbox

### **Quick Rehearsal (30 sec - 2 min)**
- Before race
- Between sets in practice
- After mistake
- **Use:** Focus on one specific skill or moment

### **Full Rehearsal (5-15 min)**
- Daily practice routine
- Pre-meet preparation
- Skill acquisition
- **Use:** Complete race or detailed technique work

### **Extended Rehearsal (20-30 min)**
- Championship preparation
- Major skill overhaul
- Confidence building
- **Use:** Deep work, multiple races/scenarios
