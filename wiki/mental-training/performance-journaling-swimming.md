# Performance Journaling & Self-Image Building for Swimming

**Source:** *With Winning in Mind* by Lanny Bassham (Olympic champion)  
**Significance:** Writing imprints 10x stronger than thinking — changes Self-Image in 21 days  
**Application:** Track progress, build winning identity, accelerate improvement  
**Tools:** Performance journal + directive affirmations

---

## Why You MUST Keep a Performance Journal

### Three Reasons (Bassham)

#### 1. **You Cannot Manage What You Do Not Measure**

**Problem:** Swimmer says "I've been swimming OK, I guess"  
- No precision, no record, can't identify what works
- Can't tell if plan failed or swimmer failed to work the plan

**Solution:** Performance journal shows:
- How often practicing
- How long each session
- What worked / what didn't
- Equipment changes
- Competition results
- Weather, start time, conditions

**Result:** Data-driven improvement, not guesswork

---

#### 2. **Coaching Efficiency**

**Bassham's rule:** "No journal, no coaching from me."

**Why:**
- Without journal, coach wastes time asking questions
- Memory is unreliable (coach's and athlete's)
- Journal shows objective reality
- Critical details captured immediately

**What coach learns from journal:**
- Training frequency & duration
- What went on in each session
- What worked / what didn't
- Equipment used & changes
- Competition results
- Weather, conditions, mental state

**For swim coaches:**
- Swimmer brings journal to practice
- Coach reviews weekly
- Adjusts training based on data
- Holds swimmer accountable

---

#### 3. **Building Self-Image (PRIMARY BENEFIT)**

**Hierarchy of imprinting power:**

| **Method** | **Imprint Strength** | **Example** |
|------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Thinking | 1x | "I should work on my turn" |
| Talking | 3x | "I always mess up my turns" |
| **Writing** | **10x** | **"I execute perfect turns. That's like me."** |

**Why writing is powerful:**
- Every thought imprints and shapes Self-Image
- Talking about bad performance = makes it a habit
- **Writing down what you want = you become what you write**
- Mental images written down have greatest impact on Self-Image

**Warning:** Do NOT write about anything you don't want to happen.

## Journal vs. Diary: Critical Difference

### ❌ **Diary (What NOT to Do)**

**Definition:** Record your impressions, good and bad, of what happened today

**Example diary entry:**
> "Today sucked. I couldn't hold my tempo. My turns were terrible. I got beat by Sarah again. I'm never going to qualify for States. Coach is mad at me. I hate this."

**Result:** Imprints losing identity, builds negative Self-Image

---

### ✅ **Performance Journal (What TO Do)**

**Definition:** Record objective data + what you want to reinforce

**Example journal entry:**
> "3/15 — AM practice, 6:00-7:30am  
> Main set: 10×100 free on 1:20, goal 1:08-1:10  
> Times: 1:09, 1:10, 1:09, 1:08, 1:09, 1:10, 1:09, 1:08, 1:07, 1:06 (descended!)  
> Stroke count: 16-17 (goal: under 17) ✓  
> **What worked:** Held tempo, strong finish on last 3  
> **That's like me:** Fast finisher, consistent stroke count  
> Mental state: Focused, confident  
> Equipment: Tempo trainer set at 1.4 sec/stroke  
> Tomorrow: Same set, add turns focus"

**Result:** Imprints success, builds champion identity

## The Directive Affirmation: Change Any Habit in 21 Days

### What It Is

**A paragraph written in first person present tense that:**
1. Describes your goal
2. Lists pay-value (why you want it)
3. Outlines plan to achieve it
4. Reinforces habits/attitudes supporting the goal

**Rehearsed repetitively = Self-Image changes**

**Bassham:** "Greatest single impact on building Self-Image for my Olympic success"

---

### How to Write a Directive Affirmation (7 Steps)

#### Example: "I Want to Be the Fastest 100 Freestyler on My Team"

---

**Step 1: Define the Goal**

Write in first person present tense as if you ALREADY have it:

> "I am the fastest 100 freestyler on my team."

---

**Step 2: Set a Time Limit**

**21 days from start date** (minimum to change Self-Image)

**Target date:** April 27, 2026

---

**Step 3: List Personal Pay-Value**

**Why do you want this? What's in it for you?**

> "I score points at championship meets. I am a team leader. I earn a college scholarship. I feel confident and proud."

---

**Step 4: Outline a Plan**

**Specific actions you'll take:**

> "I run a mental program before every race. I count strokes (stay under 17). I hold my tempo through 75m. I finish strong into the wall. I say 'That's like me!' after every fast swim. I record my performance journal daily. I read and visualize my directive affirmation every morning and night."

---

**Step 5: Write Complete Affirmation**

**Format:**
- Start with target date
- Begin with "I"
- State goal (as if you have it)
- List pay-value
- List plan
- Restate goal

**Example:**

> **04/27/2026**  
> I am the fastest 100 freestyler on my team. I score crucial points at championship meets and I am a leader my teammates look up to. I run a mental program before every race. I count my strokes and stay under 17. I hold my tempo through 75 meters and finish strong into the wall. After every fast swim I say "That's like me!" I record my performance journal daily and read my directive affirmation every morning and night. I am the fastest 100 freestyler on my team.

---

**Step 6: Make 5 Handwritten Copies**

**Write it out 5 times on 5x7 index cards** (your own handwriting)

**Why handwriting?** Stronger imprint than typing

---

**Step 7: Place Cards in 5 Prominent Locations**

**Examples:**
1. Bathroom mirror
2. Swim bag
3. Bedroom wall (above bed)
4. School locker
5. Car dashboard / backpack

**Goal:** See it multiple times per day

---

### How to Use Directive Affirmation (Daily Routine)

**Every morning:**
1. Read the card out loud (1 min)
2. Close eyes
3. Visualize yourself performing (2 min)
   - See yourself winning
   - Feel the tempo
   - Picture perfect execution
4. Say: "That's like me."

**Every night (before bed):**
1. Read the card out loud
2. Visualize success
3. Fall asleep picturing yourself as the person in the affirmation

**Duration:** 21 days minimum (to change Self-Image)

**Advanced:** Continue for 60-90 days for deep imprint

## The "That's Like Me!" Strategy

### Why It's Powerful

**Every time you say "That's like me!" after a success:**
- Imprints winning identity
- Reinforces good performance
- Builds Self-Image as champion

**Bassham's rule:** Winners reinforce success immediately.

---

### How to Use It

**After EVERY good performance:**
- Great turn? → "That's like me!"
- Hit your tempo? → "That's like me!"
- Fast 50 in practice? → "That's like me!"
- PR at meet? → "That's like me!"

**Write it in journal:**
> "I hit 15 out of 16 turns today. Perfect streamline, fast feet. **That's like me!**"

---

### What NOT to Do After Mistakes

❌ **Bad:** "I always mess up my turns. That's so like me."  
- Imprints losing identity

✅ **Good:** Immediately refocus on next rep  
- Do NOT replay mistake
- Later: rehearse CORRECT version 5 times mentally
- Write correct version in journal

## Integration: Journal + Affirmation + Mental Program

### The Complete Mental Management System for Swimmers

**Daily routine:**

**Morning (5 min):**
1. Read directive affirmation
2. Visualize yourself as that person
3. Set intention for today's practice

**Practice (during):**
- Run mental program before race-pace sets
- Say "That's like me!" after good reps
- Refocus immediately after mistakes

**After practice (10 min):**
- Write performance journal entry
- Record "That's like me!" moments
- Identify what worked
- Set goal for next session

**Night (5 min):**
- Read directive affirmation
- Visualize success
- Fall asleep picturing best version of yourself

**Total time investment:** 20 min/day  
**Result:** Self-Image transformation in 21 days

## Performance Analysis: What to Track

### Quantitative Data (Numbers)

**Times:**
- Main set times
- Race times
- Splits (50s, 100s)
- Best times / PRs

**Stroke counts:**
- Per length (25m/50m)
- Per race (100m, 200m)
- Underwater kick count

**Heart rate (if tracked):**
- Resting HR
- Max HR in sets
- Recovery HR

**Volume:**
- Total yards/meters per session
- Weekly volume
- Monthly volume

---

### Qualitative Data (Observations)

**What worked:**
- Technique focus that clicked
- Mental cue that helped
- Equipment that improved performance

**What didn't work:**
- Where technique broke down
- When focus was lost
- What caused bad performance

**Mental state:**
- Confident / anxious
- Focused / distracted
- Motivated / tired
- Excited / stressed

**Physical state:**
- Fresh / fatigued
- Strong / weak
- Healthy / sore

---

### Long-Term Tracking (Monthly Review)

**Every month, review journal and note:**
1. Best performances (times, races, sets)
2. Patterns (what consistently works)
3. Progress toward goals
4. Areas needing focus
5. Self-Image shifts ("That's like me!" themes)

**Use this data to:**
- Adjust training plan
- Update directive affirmations
- Set new goals
- Celebrate progress

## Common Mistakes in Journaling

### ❌ Mistake #1: Writing About Failures
- "I sucked today. Couldn't hold pace. I'm slow."
- **Imprints losing identity**

### ✅ Fix: Write About What You Want
- "Today I practiced holding pace. Goal: be more consistent. Next session: focus on stroke count."

---

### ❌ Mistake #2: No Consistency
- Journal 3 days, skip 2 weeks, journal again
- **No pattern to analyze, weak imprinting**

### ✅ Fix: Daily Entries (Even Short Ones)
- 5 min after every practice
- Even if just times + "That's like me!" moment

---

### ❌ Mistake #3: Too Vague
- "Practiced freestyle. It was fine."
- **No data, no learning, weak imprint**

### ✅ Fix: Be Specific
- "10×100 free on 1:15, descended 1-10. Times: 1:10, 1:09, 1:08, 1:07, 1:06. Stroke count stayed under 17. **That's like me!**"

---

### ❌ Mistake #4: Forgetting the "Why"
- Just writing times, no context
- **Data without meaning = no motivation**

### ✅ Fix: Connect to Goals
- "Today's set = race pace for States. Times show I'm ready. **That's like me to race well when it matters.**"

## Quick Start Guide: 21-Day Mental Transformation

**Week 1: Setup**
- Write 1 directive affirmation (your biggest goal)
- Make 5 handwritten copies
- Place in 5 locations
- Buy/create performance journal
- Read affirmation 2x daily, visualize

**Week 2: Build Habit**
- Continue affirmation (2x daily)
- Write journal entry after EVERY practice
- Start saying "That's like me!" after good reps
- Track progress toward affirmation goal

**Week 3: Integration**
- Affirmation becomes automatic
- Journal reveals patterns
- "That's like me!" feels natural
- Self-Image shifting

**Week 4: New Identity**
- You ARE the person in the affirmation
- Performance reflects new Self-Image
- Write next directive affirmation (new goal)
- Repeat cycle
