# Focus & Distraction Management for Swimmers

**How to control your attention, block out negativity, and stay task-focused under pressure — using techniques from elite athletes.**

---

## The Mind Can Focus on Only ONE Thing at a Time

**Core principle:** You cannot concentrate on two things simultaneously.

**Coaching application:**
- Rather than suppress what you DON'T want to happen...
- Replace it with a NEUTRAL or POSITIVE thought

**Why it matters:** Actions follow thoughts and images.

---

## The Placekicker Problem (NFL Story)

**Scenario:** NFL kicker to sports psychologist:

> "I'm a great field goal kicker. But I just can't kick when Gene [Stallings, head coach] is watching me."

**Psychologist's response:**
> "Well, I think he's going to be at all the games."

**The issue:**
- Kicker was **self-conscious** and **coach-conscious**
- He was NOT **task-conscious**
- His mind was on his boss (negative thought)

**The fix:** Create a focus word/phrase that blocks negative thoughts.

## Al Del Greco's Focus Word: "Birdie"

**Context:** Al Del Greco (Tennessee Titans kicker, Super Bowl XXXIV)

**His word:** "Birdie"

**Why it works:**
- Al is a scratch golfer (best golfer in the NFL)
- "Birdie" creates the FEELING of success
- Reminds him of the FUN he has on the golf course
- Blocks out negative thoughts
- Relieves tension

**Swimming application:** Create YOUR focus word.

## Your Focus Word: Design Exercise

**Instructions:**

1. **Choose a word that:**
   - Creates a feeling of SUCCESS
   - Is NEUTRAL or POSITIVE (not performance-related)
   - Reminds you of JOY or FUN
   - Is SHORT (1-2 syllables)

2. **Swimming examples:**
   - "Flow" (feel of moving through water)
   - "Smooth" (technique focus)
   - "Glide" (efficiency)
   - "Wave" (natural, powerful)
   - "Shark" (predator mentality)
   - "Go" (simple, aggressive)

3. **Practice using it:**
   - When negative thoughts creep in
   - Behind the blocks before your race
   - During tough sets when doubt appears

**How to use:**
- Negative thought appears: "I'm too tired"
- Replace with focus word: "Flow, flow, flow"
- Mind refocuses on the task (stroke, pace, execution)

## Don't Look Where You Don't Want to Go

**The Water Hole Problem (Golf)**

**Scenario:** Weekend golfer arrives at a water hazard.

**What he does:**
1. Fishes an old ball (water ball) out of his bag
2. Steps to tee, tells himself: **"Don't hit it in the water"**

**What his mind hears:**
- **"Water, water, water"**
- Looking at water
- Thinking about water
- Focused on water

**Law of dominant thought:** Your mind remembers the MOST DOMINANT THOUGHT.

**Result:** Ball goes in the water.

---

## Tell Your Mind WHAT TO DO (Not What NOT to Do)

**The fix:**

**❌ DON'T say:** "Don't hit it in the water"  
**✅ DO say:** "Land the ball 10 yards to the right of the pin"

**Why it works:** The mind works most effectively when you tell it WHAT to do (not what NOT to do).

## Swimming Examples: Positive Self-Talk

### **During Practice:**

**❌ DON'T say:**
- "Don't breathe too early"
- "Don't drop your elbow"
- "Don't kick from your knees"
- "Don't glide too long"

**✅ DO say:**
- "Breathe when hand enters"
- "High elbow recovery"
- "Kick from hips"
- "Quick tempo off the wall"

---

### **Before a Race:**

**❌ DON'T say:**
- "Don't go out too fast"
- "Don't mess up the turn"
- "Don't get disqualified"
- "Don't blow this"

**✅ DO say:**
- "Controlled first 50, build into the race"
- "Tight streamline, powerful push-off"
- "Execute the race plan"
- "Trust my training"

---

### **During a Race:**

**❌ DON'T think:**
- "Don't let them catch me"
- "I can't keep this pace"
- "Don't fade"

**✅ DO think:**
- "Hold my tempo"
- "One more stroke, one more stroke"
- "Finish strong"

## The Cubs Pitcher Story (Negative → Positive)

**Context:** MLB pitcher called sports psychologist from Montreal after getting rocked.

**His negative self-talk on the mound:**
- "Don't hang your curve"
- "Don't walk this guy"
- "The ump won't give me a call"
- "If I don't get through the fifth inning I'm going to lose my spot in the rotation"

**The fix:** Create a 3x5 card with:
- **Personal keys to success** (one side)
- **Performance keys to success** (other side)

**Performance keys:** (What he does when he's on his game)
- "Locating my fastball"
- "Throwing first-pitch strikes"
- "Changing speed"

**Physical keys:** (How he executes)
- "Good balance"
- "Shoulder back"
- "Drive through"

**Result:** Focused on those 3 things before his next start (5 days later).

**Outcome:** Threw a complete-game shutout.

**Proof:** He couldn't have changed that much PHYSICALLY in 5 days. He changed MENTALLY.

## Herbert Benson's Breathing Technique (Harvard Cardiologist)

**Finding:** Focusing on breathing + repeating a word lowers blood pressure and heart rate.

**The technique:**

1. **Breathe deeply** (4 counts in, 6 counts out)
2. **Repeat the word "one"** (on each exhale)
3. **Continue for 1-2 minutes**

**Effect:** Calms the nervous system, reduces anxiety, sharpens focus.

**Swimming application:**

**When to use:**
- Behind the blocks (too much nervous energy)
- Between heats (need to reset)
- During taper (overanalyzing, anxious)

**How to use:**
1. Find a quiet corner
2. Sit or stand comfortably
3. Close your eyes
4. Breathe: 4 in, 6 out
5. Repeat "one" (or your focus word)
6. Do this for 2 minutes
7. Open eyes, feeling calm and ready

## Mark McGwire's Approach: Mind Over Arms

**Quote:**
> "It's hard work, mentally and physically. Everybody looks at my body, but I use my mind more than my arms."

**His routine:**
- Moments before his at-bat, sat in the dugout with eyes closed
- NOT napping — MENTALLY REHEARSING
- Visualizing the pitcher, the pitch, the swing
- By the time he stepped in the box: focused, relaxed, ready

**Result:** Hit 70 home runs in 1998 (broke Roger Maris' record).

**Swimming translation:**
- Behind the blocks, close your eyes for 10 seconds
- See your perfect race
- Block out distractions (crowd, competitors, anxiety)
- Open eyes, step up, compete

## Yogi Berra's Wisdom on Mental Training

**Quote:**
> "Baseball is 90% mental. The other half is physical."

**Translation for swimmers:**

**The problem:** Most swimmers spend 0% of their time training the mental game.

**Questions to ask yourself:**
- How many books about sports psychology have you read?
- How much time do you spend practicing mental skills?
- Do you have a pre-race mental routine?

**The reality:** The mind is like a VCR — it records sights and sounds, and the tape plays continuously.

**Key insight:** The human body treats every vivid thought and image as if it is REAL and happening NOW.

**Your job:** Control what plays on that tape.

## Practical Exercises for Swimmers

### **Exercise 1: Create Your Focus Word**

**Time:** 5 minutes

1. Brainstorm 5-10 words that create positive feelings
2. Test them (say each one aloud, notice how it feels)
3. Pick ONE
4. Practice using it:
   - During practice (when you're struggling)
   - Before races (when anxiety creeps in)
   - In competition (when negative thoughts intrude)

---

### **Exercise 2: Build Your 3x5 Card**

**Time:** 10 minutes

1. **Front side:** Personal keys to success (mindset, attitude)
2. **Back side:** Performance keys to success (technique, tactics)
3. Keep it SHORT (3-5 items per side)
4. Review daily
5. Carry it with you to meets

---

### **Exercise 3: Reframe Negative Self-Talk**

**Time:** Ongoing practice

**Catch yourself:**
- When you hear "don't" in your head
- When you're focused on what NOT to do

**Reframe it:**
- Replace with what TO do
- Focus on the SOLUTION (not the problem)

**Examples:**
- "Don't false start" → "Wait for the beep, explode"
- "Don't go out too fast" → "Hit my splits, build into the race"
- "Don't miss the wall" → "See the T, count strokes, attack the finish"

---

### **Exercise 4: Pre-Race Breathing Routine**

**Time:** 2 minutes before your race

1. Find a quiet spot (away from chaos)
2. Close your eyes
3. Breathe deeply: 4 in, 6 out
4. Repeat your focus word (or "one")
5. Continue for 2 minutes
6. Feel your heart rate slow, mind clear
7. Open eyes, step up, compete with calm confidence
