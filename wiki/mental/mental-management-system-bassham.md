# Mental Management® System for Swimming (Bassham)

**Source:** *With Winning in Mind* by Lanny Bassham (Olympic Gold Medalist, Rifle Shooting)  
**Adapted for:** Swim coaches seeking to build championship mental skills  
**Key concept:** Performance = Conscious Mind + Subconscious Mind + Self-Image (all in balance)

---

## The Triad State: When Performance Flows

**The Triad State** = When all three mental processes are balanced and working together.

```
┌─────────────┐
│  CONSCIOUS  │ ← What you picture/think about
│    MIND     │
└─────────────┘
┌─────────────┐
│SUBCONSCIOUS │ ← Your automated skills
│    MIND     │
└─────────────┘
┌─────────────┐
│ SELF-IMAGE  │ ← "What's like me" (habits + attitudes)
└─────────────┘
```

**When balanced:** Performance feels effortless. You're calm, confident, and "in the zone."

**When unbalanced:** Frustration. Over-trying. Inconsistency.

---

## Three Mental Processes Explained

### 1. Conscious Mind

**Function:** Controls what you picture and think about (RIGHT NOW)

**Key principle:** Can only concentrate on ONE thing at a time.

**Swimming application:**
- ✅ "I'm going to nail my underwater" (positive picture)
- ❌ "Don't breathe into the wall" (negative picture — you just pictured breathing in!)

**What coaches should say:**
- ✅ "Drive your head down off the start"
- ❌ "Don't lift your head"

**Why it matters:** You move toward what you picture. If you picture failure, your Self-Image moves you to create it.

---

### 2. Subconscious Mind

**Function:** The source of all mental power and automated skills

**Key principle:** Performs MANY things simultaneously (unlike Conscious Mind)

**How skills become subconscious:**
1. Conscious repetition (drills, technique work)
2. Eventually automated (no longer think about it)
3. Subconscious execution (faster, smoother, more powerful)

**Swimming example:**
- **Beginner:** Thinks about every part of their stroke (slow, choppy)
- **Elite:** Body executes stroke automatically while mind focuses on race strategy

**Danger: Conscious override**
- Trying TOO HARD in big meets
- "I've got to make this turn perfect!" → stiffens up → performance drops
- **Solution:** Trust your training. Let it flow.

**Cross-reference:** [Performance Under Pressure](./performance-under-pressure.md) — handling nerves

---

### 3. Self-Image

**Definition:** The total of your habits + attitudes. Makes you "act like you."

**Key principle:** Performance and Self-Image are ALWAYS equal.

**Examples in swimming:**

| Swimmer says... | Self-Image belief | Result |
|----------------|------------------|--------|
| "I always fall apart in the 3rd 50" | It's LIKE me to fade | Fades in races |
| "I'm not a morning swimmer" | It's LIKE me to suck at prelims | Poor prelim swims |
| "I crush it when it matters" | It's LIKE me to perform under pressure | Big meet PRs |

**To change performance, you MUST change Self-Image first.**

## The Comfort Zone

**Your Self-Image keeps you in a "comfort zone" of performance.**

**Example:**
- Swimmer averages 55-57 seconds in 100 free
- Swims a 53 in practice (ABOVE zone) → Self-Image thinks "that's not like me" → next swim is 58 (back in zone)
- Swims a 59 in prelims (BELOW zone) → Self-Image thinks "I need to get back to normal" → finals is 56 (back in zone)

**The problem:** If you want to consistently swim faster, you must **expand your comfort zone** by changing your Self-Image.

**How to expand the zone:**
1. Rehearse faster swims (mental imagery)
2. Reinforce good swims ("That's like me!")
3. Stop reinforcing bad swims (don't dwell on them)

## 8 Core Principles of Mental Management®

### Principle 1: Conscious Mind = One Thing at a Time

**You cannot think positive and negative simultaneously.**

**Drill for swimmers:**
- Before a race, picture ONLY what you want to happen
- "See" yourself executing perfectly, not worrying about competitors

---

### Principle 2: What You PICTURE Matters (Not What You Say)

**Coach says:** "Don't false start!"  
**Swimmer pictures:** False starting.

**Better:** "Hold the wall until the beep."

**Swimmer pictures:** Staying controlled, then exploding.

---

### Principle 3: Subconscious = Source of All Power

**Elite performances happen subconsciously.**

**Champions think about VERY LITTLE while competing.**
- Not "I need to kick harder"
- More "Flow. Trust. Execute."

**Cross-reference:** [Bob Bowman — Mastery and Motivation](../coaches/bob-bowman/mastery-and-motivation.md) — "Do your work" philosophy

---

### Principle 4: Self-Image Moves You Toward What You Picture

**If you picture yourself swimming slow → Self-Image makes it happen.**

**If you picture yourself dominating → Self-Image makes it happen.**

**Training application:**
- Every rep in practice = opportunity to imprint "I crush this"
- Bad rep? Forget it. Don't rehearse failure.

---

### Principle 5: Performance = Self-Image (Always)

**You perform at the level of your Self-Image.**

**To improve performance:**
1. Change habits (how you train, recover, eat, think)
2. Change attitudes ("I'm a closer" vs. "I always fade")

**Nothing changes unless YOU change.**

---

### Principle 6: You CAN Replace Your Self-Image

**The Self-Image you have ≠ the Self-Image you're stuck with.**

**You can replace:**
- The income you have → the income you want
- The times you have → the times you want
- The YOU you have → the YOU you want

**Tool:** Directive Affirmation (see below)

---

### Principle 7: Reinforcement = Repetition

**The more you think/talk/write about something, the more likely it becomes.**

**What NOT to do:**
- "I had THREE terrible starts today" (just imprinted 3 more bad starts)
- "I always choke in finals" (just imprinted choking again)

**What TO do:**
- "My underwater was perfect on that rep" (imprint success)
- "I owned that finish" (imprint confidence)

**Bassham:** "I don't remember how I got my bad shots. I only remember my tens."

---

### Principle 8: Self-Image Can't Tell Real vs. Imagined

**Your brain treats vivid mental rehearsal like a real performance.**

**Bassham example:**
- Rehearsed shooting a perfect 400 kneeling for 2 months (never done it in real life)
- First competition after → shot perfect 400 and set national record
- Self-Image believed "it's like me to shoot 400"

**Swimming application:**
- Rehearse perfect race execution DAILY
- Your Self-Image will believe "it's like me to swim that way"
- When race day comes, your body knows what to do

**Cross-reference:** [Mental Training Techniques](./mental-training-techniques.md) — visualization protocols

## The Mental Program (Pre-Performance Routine)

**Definition:** A series of thoughts that, when pictured, trigger the Subconscious to perform.

**Purpose:**
1. Occupy the Conscious Mind (keeps it from interfering)
2. Transfer power to Subconscious (automated execution)
3. Duplicable (same every time = consistency)

**Format for swimming:**

### Point of Initiation
**Physical cue that starts the routine**
- Step on the block
- Pull goggles down
- Deep breath

### Point of Direction
**Picture what you want to happen**
- Visualize explosive start
- See yourself underwater, streamlined, kicking hard

### Point of Focus
**Last thought before action**
- Single word: "Attack" or "Go" or "Explode"
- Or: Silence. Trust. Let it happen.

**Example (50 free):**
1. **Initiation:** Pull goggles down
2. **Direction:** Picture driving off the block, tight streamline, 6 dolphin kicks
3. **Focus:** "ATTACK"
4. **Action:** GO (subconscious takes over)

**Key:** Same routine. Every time. Builds mental consistency.

## Three Phases of Every Task

### 1. Anticipation Phase (Before)

**What you think about before you perform.**

**Swimmer checklist:**
- Visualize race plan
- Run mental program
- Positive self-talk ("I'm ready. I've done the work.")

---

### 2. Action Phase (During)

**What you think about WHILE performing.**

**Key:** Think as LITTLE as possible. Trust your training.

**Elite swimmers during races:**
- Not: "Kick harder! Breathe less! Is she ahead?"
- More: "Execute. Flow. Next stroke."

**Bassham in 1974 World Championships (Switzerland):**
- Snowing so hard he couldn't see the target
- Adapted instead of panicking
- Said to himself: "I'm the best snow shooter here. I love this."
- **Won the match.**

**Lesson:** When your best isn't enough, do what's required to win.

---

### 3. Reinforcement Phase (After)

**What you think about AFTER you perform.**

**Champions:**
- ✅ Talk about what went RIGHT
- ✅ "That turn was perfect. What did I do?"
- ❌ Never reinforce bad performances by dwelling on them

**Jack Writer (Olympic Champion):**
- Only talked about his HIGH scores
- Never mentioned errors in front of competitors
- "I don't want to give you an edge"

**Margaret Murdock (Olympic Silver Medalist):**
- Rarely talked about her own performance
- Only praised others
- **Every time she praised someone else, she built her own Self-Image**

**Application for coaches:**
- Praise good technique IMMEDIATELY
- Correct errors by focusing on the SOLUTION, not the mistake
- "Let's get your head down" (solution) vs. "You lifted your head again" (mistake)

## Over-Trying: The #1 Mental Problem

**Definition:** Trying TOO HARD, especially in big meets.

**Why it happens:**
1. Afraid to trust Subconscious skill
2. Thinking about OUTCOME instead of PROCESS
3. Belief that "trying harder = better performance" (FALSE)

**Signs of over-trying:**
- Tight shoulders
- Rushed tempo
- Conscious override ("I've GOT to make this wall!")

**Solution:**

### 1. Work hard in PRACTICE, easy in COMPETITION

**Champions:**
- Train: High effort, perfect reps, build the skill bank
- Compete: Trust, flow, let it happen

### 2. Focus on PROCESS, not OUTCOME

**Outcome thinking (BAD):**
- "I need a 1:45 to make finals"
- "If I don't win this, I'm done"

**Process thinking (GOOD):**
- "Nail my underwater on every wall"
- "Execute my race plan"

### 3. Dial it back

**If you're swimming worse in meets than practice → you're over-trying.**

**Try:** 90% effort with 100% trust.

**Bassham:** "The best performances seem effortless."

## Building Subconscious Skills: 7 Training Guidelines

### 1. Catch Yourself Doing Something RIGHT

**Most swimmers:** Focus on fixing errors  
**Champions:** Focus on repeating successes

**After a rep:**
- ❌ "What did I do wrong?"
- ✅ "What did I do right?"

**Self-Image grows when you reinforce success.**

---

### 2. Train 4-5 Days/Week

**Less than 4 days/week:** Maintenance (or decline)  
**4-6 days/week:** Growth

**Why:** Skill development requires consistent imprinting.

---

### 3. Wherever You Are, Be ALL There

**At practice:** 100% focused on swimming  
**At home:** Leave practice at the pool

**Bringing problems from one area to another = lost focus = wasted training.**

---

### 4. Rehearse Match Day Often

**Bassham:** "Treat training days as if they were competition days."

**Swimming application:**
- Practice race pace under pressure
- Simulate prelims/finals in training
- Build Self-Image of "I perform well when it matters"

---

### 5. When You're Swimming Well, Swim A LOT

**Jack Writer:** "When I'm shooting well, I keep shooting."

**Application:**
- Crushing a main set? Do extra 50s at the end.
- Having a great training block? Add an extra session.
- On a roll? Keep rolling.

**Opposite:** Having a terrible day? STOP. Don't practice losing.

---

### 6. Train with People Better Than You

**You rise to the standard you're around.**

**Bob Bowman trains Leon Marchand in the same pool as NCAA champions.**  
**Result:** Leon sees elite work every day. His Self-Image believes "this is normal."

---

### 7. Plan Your Year

**Break season into quarters:**
1. **Q1:** Evaluate, set goals, test new ideas
2. **Q2:** Conditioning, hard work, build the engine
3. **Q3:** Competition, peak performance, taper
4. **Q4:** Rest, reflect, recover

**Track everything:** Times, sets, technique changes, mental breakthroughs

## Performance Journal: The Secret Weapon

**Why 95% of swimmers don't keep one:**
1. Don't know HOW
2. Think it's just a diary (recording mistakes = BAD)
3. Lazy

**Why champions DO:**
1. You can't manage what you don't measure
2. Coaches can't help you without data
3. **Writing down success imprints it deeper than thinking or talking**

**What to record:**

### Competition Log
- Dates, results, key learnings

### Equipment Log
- Suit, goggles, paddles, anything that changes

### General Data
- Date, workout type, duration, what you accomplished

### Solution Analysis
- Problems + solutions discovered today
- "Looking for a solution to: breathing pattern on 200 fly"

### Success Analysis
- **ONLY write what you did WELL**
- "My underwater was perfect on all 50s"
- "My finish was aggressive every time"

### Goal Statement (Daily)
- Write in first-person present tense as if ALREADY achieved
- "I often swim under 1:45 in the 200 free"
- "I am the most consistent swimmer on our team"

**Bassham:** "You tend to become what you write about."

## The Directive Affirmation (21-Day Self-Image Change Tool)

**What it is:** A paragraph written to REPLACE an old Self-Image with a new one.

**Format:**
1. Goal (as if already achieved)
2. Pay-value (why it matters)
3. Plan (how you'll do it)
4. Restate goal

**Example for swimmer who "always fades in the 3rd 50":**

---

**4/30/2026**

I am a swimmer who finishes STRONG in every race. I feel the power in my legs when others are fading. I love the feeling of passing people in the last 50. I negative-split my races because I focus on process, not outcome. I rehearse strong finishes daily. I visualize executing my race plan perfectly. I say "That's like me!" after every good rep. I read and visualize this affirmation daily. I am a swimmer who finishes STRONG in every race.

---

**How to use:**
1. Write on 5 index cards
2. Place in 5 locations (bathroom mirror, phone case, locker, desk, car)
3. Read AND VISUALIZE every time you see it (5-10 times/day)
4. Run for 21 days, rest 9 days, repeat if needed

**What happens:**
- Old Self-Image ("I fade") conflicts with new Self-Image ("I finish strong")
- Self-Image can't stand conflict
- One must go
- Through repetition, NEW Self-Image wins
- Performance changes PERMANENTLY

## Practical Drills for Coaches

### 1. Pre-Race Mental Program Practice

**Teach swimmers to build a 3-step routine:**
1. Initiation (pull goggles down)
2. Direction (visualize perfect start + underwater)
3. Focus (one word: "GO")

**Practice in training:** Every rep starts with the routine.

---

### 2. "That's Like Me!" Reinforcement

**After EVERY good rep:**
- Swimmer says (out loud or internally): "That's like me!"
- Imprints success immediately
- Builds Self-Image of excellence

---

### 3. Success-Only Debrief

**After practice:**
- Ask: "What did you do WELL today?"
- NOT: "What went wrong?"

**Builds Self-Image through positive reinforcement.**

---

### 4. Weekly Directive Affirmation Assignment

**Have swimmers write ONE goal as a Directive Affirmation**  
**Read it daily for 21 days**  
**Report results**
