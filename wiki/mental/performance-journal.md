# Performance Journal System for Swimmers

**Source:** Lanny Bassham's *With Winning in Mind* (Performance Analysis method)  
**Purpose:** Track progress + build Self-Image simultaneously  
**Swimming relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (mandatory for serious competitors)

---

## Why Keep a Performance Journal?

**Bassham:** "You cannot manage what you do not measure."

### Three Reasons It's Mandatory:

#### 1. Accountability
Without a journal, you can't answer:
- How often am I actually training?
- Am I working my plan or just showing up?
- What worked? What didn't?

**Result:** Without records, you're flying blind.

#### 2. Coaching Efficiency
**Bassham:** "I will not coach an individual without a Performance Journal."

**Why?**
- Coach knows exactly what you've been doing
- No wasted time on "So, how's it going?"
- You know what you've been doing (memory = unreliable)

#### 3. Self-Image Growth (The Primary Benefit)
**Bassham:** "The primary benefit is to build Self-Image by imprinting both real and rehearsed images."

**The imprinting hierarchy:**
- Thinking = weak imprint
- Talking = stronger imprint
- **Writing = strongest imprint**

**Bassham:** "You tend to become what you write about."

**The trap:** Most swimmers keep a **diary** (records failures) instead of a **journal** (records successes).

---

## Diary vs. Journal: The Critical Difference

### ❌ Diary (Don't Do This)

**What it is:** Recording everything, good and bad

**Example entry:**
> "Terrible practice today. Missed 3 of 8 intervals on the 100s. Felt slow and tired. Stroke fell apart on #5. Coach was disappointed. I'm so inconsistent."

**What just happened:**
- Imprinted failure 5+ times
- Increased probability of repeating it
- Shrunk Self-Image

**Bassham:** "If you record your mistakes, you are making a huge mental error and the Self-Image suffers."

**Result:** Swimmers who keep diaries often see performance deteriorate, then (correctly) throw the diary away.

---

### ✅ Journal (Do This)

**What it is:** Recording only solutions and successes

**Example entry:**
> "Hit 5 of 8 intervals strong today. #3, #5, #7 felt powerful — long stroke, tight core. Solution for #2, #4: start streamline sooner off wall. Working on it. I am a 1:42 200 freestyler."

**What just happened:**
- Reinforced success 3 times
- Identified solution (not dwelling on problem)
- Restated goal (imprinting desired identity)
- Grew Self-Image

**Bassham's definition:** "A Performance Journal has no references to bad shots, bad experiences, or poor performances."

## The 6 Elements of a Performance Journal

### 1. Competition Log
**What to record:**
- Date of meet
- Event(s) competed
- Result/time
- Page number in journal for full entry

**Purpose:** Quick reference to see progress over season

---

### 2. Equipment Log
**What to record:**
- All equipment currently using (suit, goggles, cap, fins, paddles, snorkel, etc.)
- Any changes made (date + reason)
- Repairs

**Purpose:** If something changes (performance or equipment), you know when/why

---

### 3. General Data Section

**What to record:**
- Date
- Location (pool/meet)
- Event (practice / competition / dryland / film)
- Time of day + duration
- What you did (sets, drills, yardage, focus)
- Result/times (if practice test set or meet)

**Example:**
> **4/6/26** — CRWC, Practice, 6:00-8:00am (2hrs)  
> Main set: 8x100 free @ 1:20, target 1:02-1:03  
> Hit: 1:02.5, 1:02.8, 1:02.3, 1:02.9, 1:02.7, 1:02.4, 1:02.6, 1:02.2  
> Total: 5,500 yards

**Purpose:** Know exactly what you've been doing (no rationalizations)

**Bassham:** "It is easy for us to say we are training when in fact we are spending most of our time visiting with other competitors."

---

### 4. Solution Analysis

**What to record:**
- Solutions to problems you discovered today
- Problems you're looking for solutions to (stated as questions)

**Examples:**

✅ **Good:**
> "Solution: Tightening core 2 strokes before wall = faster turn speed"  
> "Looking for: How to maintain tempo in last 50 of 200 fly?"

❌ **Bad:**
> "I fell apart on the last 50 again. My tempo sucks."

**Purpose:** Build a library of solutions. When you face the same challenge again, you have the answer.

---

### 5. Success Analysis

**What to record:**
- Anything you did well today
- Personal records (be very descriptive)
- Good feelings, strong swims, technical wins

**Examples:**

> "Held 1:02 pace on all 8x100s. Felt controlled and strong. Breathing pattern (3-2-2-2) worked well."

> "PR in 100 free: 50.8! Nailed the start, dolphin kicks felt powerful, tempo stayed high through finish."

> "Coach said my stroke looked the best he's seen it. Long extension, high elbow, clean entry."

**Bassham:** "When you do this, you improve the probability that you will repeat the success."

**Personal record days:** Write extensively. Capture everything about how it felt, what you did, conditions, mindset.

---

### 6. Goal Statement (Most Important Step)

**What to write:**
- First person, present tense
- As if you already achieved it
- One goal per day (can rotate)

**Examples:**
- "I often swim under 1:42 in the 200 free."
- "I am a NCAA finalist."
- "I am the most consistent swimmer on our team."
- "I perform better in meets than in practice."

**Bassham:** "Every time we write down a goal, we are that much closer to its attainment. Only two things are possible. Your goal will be reached, or you will stop writing it down. As long as you continue to write down your goal statements, you are moving toward their attainment."

**Key rules:**
- Currently out of reach, but **not out of sight**
- You must believe it's possible
- Only goals YOU can control (process, not outcome)

---

## What NOT to Write (Critical)

**Bassham:** "Never write about anything you do not want to have happen."

### ❌ Don't Write:
- "I had a terrible practice"
- "I'm so slow in the 200 breast"
- "I always choke at conference"
- "I can't hold my splits"
- "My underwater is weak"
- Complaints about coach/teammates/pool
- Excuses (tired, sick, stressed)
- Detailed descriptions of failures

**Why:** Every time you write about something, you imprint it deeper into your Self-Image.

### ✅ Do Write:
- "I hit 6 of 8 intervals strong today"
- "I'm getting faster in the 200 breast"
- "I perform well at conference championships"
- "I'm improving my split consistency"
- "I'm working on underwater efficiency"
- Gratitude for coach/teammates/opportunity
- What you learned
- Solutions to challenges

---

## When to Write (Daily Discipline)

**Best time:** Immediately after practice/meet (while it's fresh)

**Minimum:** 5 minutes/day

**Bassham:** "Keep your journal where you can record this information immediately after performing. You can remember things only so long, so do your recording before you leave the competition or training area if possible."

**What if you miss a day?** Don't write about it. Just start again.

---

## The Bassham Journal Format (One Page Per Day)

```
DATE: 4/6/26
LOCATION: CRWC
EVENT: Morning Practice
TIME: 6:00-8:00am (2hrs)

GENERAL DATA:
[What you did — sets, yardage, times]

SOLUTION ANALYSIS:
[Solutions found or questions for next time]

SUCCESS ANALYSIS:
[What you did well]

GOAL STATEMENT:
[First person, present tense]
```

## Why Most People Don't Keep Journals (And How to Beat It)

### Reason 1: They Keep Diaries (Not Journals)
**Fix:** Only write solutions + successes. Never failures.

### Reason 2: They Don't Know How
**Fix:** Use the 6-element format above. Copy it exactly.

### Reason 3: Laziness
**Bassham's response:**
> "If you are playing just to have fun, this section may not be for you. But if you want to win, you must separate yourself from the others in your dedication."

**What's not acceptable:**
- Being unable to remember what you've done
- Making the same mistakes repeatedly
- Not knowing if your plan is working
- Being beaten by someone who keeps a journal
- Losing because you're too lazy to do what's needed to win

---

## Advanced: Coaching with Journals

**For coaches who require Performance Journals from athletes:**

**Benefits:**
1. Know exactly what athletes have done
2. Track progress without interrogation
3. Athletes become accountable to themselves
4. Painless documentation system
5. Both parties can review together

**Bassham:** "Ask a player who keeps a Performance Journal 'How is it going?' and see if he doesn't pull out his Performance Journal and show you."

---

## The Cumulative Effect

**What happens after 6 months:**
- 180+ entries of success reinforcement
- 180+ goal statement imprints
- Library of solutions for every challenge
- Visible progress (times, technique, consistency)
- Massive Self-Image growth

**What happens after 4 years:**
- 1,400+ success imprints
- Clear record of entire journey
- Proof you can do hard things
- Unshakeable confidence

**Bassham:** "A Performance Journal provides the athlete and coach with a valuable resource for improvement, without burdening either with unnecessary paperwork."

## Sample Entry (Good Practice Day)

```
DATE: 4/6/26
LOCATION: CRWC
EVENT: Morning Practice
TIME: 6:00-8:00am (2 hours)

GENERAL DATA:
Warmup: 1000 mixed (drill/swim)
Main set: 8x100 free @ 1:20, target 1:02-1:03
  - 1:02.5, 1:02.8, 1:02.3, 1:02.9, 1:02.7, 1:02.4, 1:02.6, 1:02.2
  - All made the send-off, felt controlled
Pull set: 6x200 @ 2:40, paddles/buoy
  - Focused on high elbow catch
Cooldown: 500 easy
Total: 5,500 yards

SOLUTION ANALYSIS:
- Solution found: Tightening core 2 strokes before wall gives me better turn speed
- Breathing 3-2-2-2 pattern (instead of 2-2-2-2) helped me stay relaxed on back half

SUCCESS ANALYSIS:
- Hit all 8 intervals in the 1:02s — most consistent set in weeks
- #8 (1:02.2) was fastest AND felt easiest — trusting my fitness
- Coach said my stroke looked "locked in" on the pull set
- High elbow catch is becoming automatic

GOAL STATEMENT:
I am a 1:42 200 freestyler.
```

## Sample Entry (Championship Meet)

```
DATE: 4/8/26
LOCATION: Conference Championships
EVENT: 200 Free Final
TIME: Evening Finals

GENERAL DATA:
Warmup: 1200 yards, felt good
Pre-race: Mental program (3 breaths, "explode, streamline, press")
Split: 24.1 / 52.3 (1:21.8) / 1:43.2
Place: 3rd
Result: PR by 0.6 seconds, All-Conference

SOLUTION ANALYSIS:
- Start felt great (explosive off blocks, tight streamline)
- Looking for: How to negative split the race (out 52.3, back 50.9 = positive split of 1.4s)

SUCCESS ANALYSIS:
- PR by 0.6! New best time: 1:43.2
- Stayed calm under pressure (3rd in conference is like me)
- Held tempo through final 50 better than last year
- Mental program worked — felt automatic, not overthinking
- Finished strong, didn't die like past years
- Breathing pattern (3 per 50 yard) was perfect

GOAL STATEMENT:
I am a 1:42 200 freestyler. I am an All-American.
```

> "I control my process, not the outcome."

## Action Steps

1. **Buy a notebook** — Dedicated journal, not loose papers
2. **Use the 6-element format** — Copy it exactly
3. **Write daily** — 5 minutes after practice/meet
4. **Follow the rules:**
   - Only successes + solutions
   - Never failures/complaints
   - Goal statement every day (first person, present tense)
5. **Review weekly** — Flip through past week, see progress
6. **Show your coach** — "Here's what I've been working on"
7. **Keep forever** — Your legacy, your proof

**Bassham:** "It is not acceptable to beat yourself."
