# Pre-Race Mental Preparation for Swimmers

**How Olympic champions like Janet Evans, Susie O'Neil, and Summer Sanders used mental preparation routines to win races — and how your swimmers can too.**

---

## Why Pre-Race Mental Prep Matters

**Research finding:** In surveys of 1,200+ Olympic track and field athletes, those who made the Olympic team did **more mental practice in final preparation stages** than those who nearly qualified but missed.

**Key insight:** Physical training gets athletes to the pool. Mental preparation determines who wins.

**Quote — Janet Evans (4x Olympic gold medalist):**
> "I have never been to a competition, including the Olympic Games, where I didn't see myself win in my mental images before I got there. It is just part of the whole training package."

---

## The PTEZ: Perfect Timing and Energy Zone

**What it is:** A pre-race mental state 30 minutes before competition where swimmers visualize, breathe, and prepare.

**How to use it:**

**30 minutes before race:**
1. **Check your environment** — crowd, weather, noise, competitors
2. **Practice relaxed breathing** — slow, deep breaths
3. **Visualize the race** — see yourself surging, leaving the pack, finishing strong
4. **Positive self-talk** — "I will do well. I've trained for this."
5. **Pre-race rituals** — anything that makes you comfortable (adjust cap, shake out arms, etc.)

---

## Pre-Race Routine: Crissy Perham's Method

**Background:** Crissy Perham (1992 Olympic swimmer, gold in 400 free relay, silver in 100 fly) used a **consistent pre-race cue system**.

**Her routine:**
1. **Enter pool deck**
2. **Find parents in bleachers** — make eye contact
3. **Throw "pumpers"** — raised fist in the air (energy cue)
4. **Make eye contact with coach** — connection and confidence
5. **Walk to podium** — touch the first-place spot
6. **Visualize receiving the first-place award** — kinesthetic cue

**Why it worked:** Consistent ritual = reduced anxiety, increased focus.

**Swimming application:**
- **Create YOUR routine** — What calms you? What energizes you?
- **Practice it at every meet** — Make it automatic
- **Visual + kinesthetic cues** — See it, feel it, do it

## Nicole Haislett-Bacher's Focus Formula

**Background:** Triple gold medalist (1992 Olympics) who structured relaxation before racing.

**Her pre-race protocol:**

**1. Warm-up** (physical + mental prep)

**2. Find a quiet spot away from team**
- Put feet up
- Remove distractions

**3. Headphones + music**
- Block external noise
- Get into mental zone

**4. Mental preparation**
- Visualize game plan
- See yourself winning strong
- Focus on:
  - Accelerating off the wall
  - Perfect turns
  - Strong kick
  - Fast finish

**Key principle:** "The winner is the one with the fewest doubts."

**Confidence builders:**
- Good starts
- Clean turns
- Trusting your training

## Summer Sanders' Pre-Race Strategy

**Background:** 4 medals at 1992 Olympics (silver in 200 IM, gold in 200 fly and relay, bronze in 400 medley).

**Her approach:** "Competitive swimming is just like a hard practice."

**Mental frame:**
> "It is just a swim race. If you just think about it, it is something you have done hundreds of times before."

**What she did:**

### **Before First Olympic Race (400 Medley, Barcelona 1992):**

**Challenge:** Very tense, extremely excited

**Mental shift:**
- "I have nothing to lose"
- "I made it to the Olympics — the pinnacle"
- "Just jump in and have fun"

**Result:** Bronze medal (first Olympic medal — massive excitement)

---

### **Before Second Race (200 Medley):**

**Challenge:** Rumors about Chinese swimmers ("unbeatable" strength/conditioning)

**Initial reaction:** Intimidated ("These women are amazing!")

**Mental reset:**
1. "She is just a person like me"
2. "She is just as nervous"
3. "We are all in it together"
4. Remembered her imagery
5. Remembered work ethic (thousands of hours in pool, daily double workouts)
6. Remembered her game plan

**Result:** "No more self-doubt, no more intimidation. I was ready to race."

**Coaching lesson:** Help swimmers reframe competitors as **equals**, not threats.

## Clear Your Mind: Leave Baggage Behind

**Janet Evans' principle:**
> "Your swimming workout will be a lot tougher if you bring the day's problems into the pool with you. I need to come to workouts with no excess baggage or preconceived notions about how I should perform."

**Application for race day:**
- School stress? Leave it.
- Friend drama? Leave it.
- Family issues? Leave it.

**Goal:** Open a new door. Fresh chapter. Celebrate the competition.

## Managing Pre-Race Nerves

### **The Relaxation Principle (Janet Evans)**

**Quote:**
> "No matter how pumped I was before a race, no matter how important the event, I always reminded myself that I had to be completely relaxed so that my breathing and mental imagery flowed. If I'm not relaxed, it sets off a negative chain of events."

**Why relaxation matters:**
- Muscle tension → shallow breathing → increased anxiety → poor performance
- Relaxation → deep breathing → calm focus → optimal performance

---

### **The Tension Cycle (Break It!)**

**Negative cycle:**
1. Muscle tension
2. Shallow breathing
3. Increased anxiety
4. More tension
5. Poor race

**How to break it:**
- **Practice deep, open breathing** (slow inhale through nose, long exhale through mouth)
- **Follow with tight, constrained breathing** (shallow, fast)
- **Repeat: tight → relaxed → tight → relaxed**
- **Goal:** Find YOUR rhythm in the water

**Janet Evans' advice:** "You have to take care of your own tension, find the right comfort level, and stay with it."

---

## Stress Busters: Summer Sanders' Approach

**Philosophy:** Keep life simple in training AND competition.

**What she did:**
- **Hung out with friends** before races
- **Laughed a lot** ("It releases a lot of tension")
- **Stayed normal** (no fancy rituals, just being herself)

**Result:** After first Olympic race, "the pressure was off" — easier to perform.

**Coaching tip:** Encourage swimmers to stay social, laugh, and be themselves. **Normalcy reduces anxiety.**

---

## The Balance Principle: Life Outside the Pool

**Nicole Haislett-Bacher's wisdom:**

**For developmental and college swimmers:**
- "You need friends"
- "You need to go out"
- "You have to party with some reasonable limits"
- "Have some fun. Don't get overfocused."

**BUT:** Know your boundaries and priorities.

**Quote:**
> "Practice is always the next day, so if you play too hard the night before, you will pay the price, and your coach will make you pay the price. Find the right balance between play and serious commitment."

## Pre-Race Rituals That Work

### **1. Consistent Pre-Race Checklist**

**Example routine (customize for your swimmers):**
- [ ] Arrive early (reduce rushing)
- [ ] Warm-up (physical + mental)
- [ ] Find quiet spot
- [ ] Breathing exercises (5 min)
- [ ] Visualization (3-5 min)
- [ ] Positive self-talk (1-2 min)
- [ ] Check equipment (cap, goggles, suit)
- [ ] Final pump-up or calm-down (athlete's choice)
- [ ] Race

---

### **2. The Music Strategy**

**What works:**
- **Pump-up music** for high-energy swimmers (rock, hip-hop, EDM)
- **Calming music** for anxious swimmers (classical, acoustic, ambient)

**Nicole Haislett-Bacher used headphones** to block distractions and enter her zone.

**Coaching tip:** Let swimmers control their pre-race music. It's personal.

---

### **3. The Touch-and-Feel Cue**

**Crissy Perham walked to the podium and touched the first-place spot.**

**Why it works:** Kinesthetic (physical touch) + visualization (seeing yourself win) = powerful mental anchor.

**Other kinesthetic cues:**
- Touch the blocks before race
- Feel the water with your hand
- Adjust goggles (familiar sensation)
- Shake out arms (muscle memory)

## Common Pre-Race Mental Mistakes

### **❌ Mistake 1: Overconfidence**

**Story:** Crissy Perham at 1992 Barcelona Olympics (200m final)

**What happened:**
- Leading the race
- Thought: "No way she can keep up with me"
- Lost concentration and focus
- Started visualizing **competitor's finish** (not her own!)
- Lost the gold medal

**Quote:**
> "I gave her the race. I wasn't swimming my race. I thought there was no way she could keep up with me. I still wake up in the middle of the night seeing myself finish. I should have had the gold medal."

**Lesson:** **Stay focused on YOUR race**. Don't assume victory. Execute the plan.

---

### **❌ Mistake 2: Getting Psyched Out by Competitors**

**Summer Sanders' experience:** Heard rumors about Chinese swimmers being "unbeatable."

**How she recovered:**
1. Recognized they're human ("just a person like me")
2. Remembered her training
3. Refocused on her game plan

**Coaching strategy:** **Reframe competitors** as equals, not threats.

---

### **❌ Mistake 3: Bringing Baggage to the Pool**

**Problem:** School stress, relationship drama, family issues

**Solution (Janet Evans):** "Leave the baggage behind. Open a new door. Fresh chapter."

**Practical exercise:**
- Before entering pool deck: **Mental release** ("I'm leaving [problem] outside")
- Take 3 deep breaths
- Say: "This is MY time. I'm here to swim FAST."

## Pre-Race Visualization Script (5 Minutes)

**Use this 30 minutes before race:**

**1. Find a quiet spot** (away from team noise)

**2. Close your eyes**

**3. Take 5 deep breaths** (slow inhale, long exhale)

**4. See yourself behind the blocks:**
- Feel your suit, cap, goggles
- Hear the starter's commands
- See the pool, lanes, competitors

**5. Visualize your race:**
- **Start:** Explosive dive, streamline, breakout
- **First 50:** Strong strokes, breathing rhythm, hitting your pace
- **Turn(s):** Fast flip, tight streamline, powerful push-off
- **Final 50:** Accelerate, full stroke extension, strong kick
- **Finish:** Drive to the wall, touch first, see your time

**6. Feel the emotions:**
- Confidence
- Strength
- Speed
- Joy

**7. Open your eyes**

**8. Say to yourself:** "I am READY."

## Coaching Implementation: 4-Week Pre-Race Prep Plan

### **Week 1: Establish Routines**
- Have each swimmer create their pre-race checklist
- Practice it at a low-stakes meet or time trial
- Debrief: What worked? What didn't?

### **Week 2: Breathing + Relaxation**
- Teach deep breathing exercises
- Practice tension cycle (tight → relaxed)
- Do 5-minute guided relaxation before main set

### **Week 3: Visualization Practice**
- Use the 5-minute script above
- Have swimmers visualize their best race
- Practice visualization 3x this week (not just race day)

### **Week 4: Full Integration**
- Combine routine + breathing + visualization
- Practice at meet
- Swimmers self-assess: "Did I feel prepared?"
