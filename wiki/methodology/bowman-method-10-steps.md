# The Bowman Method: 10 Steps to Excellence
**Source:** *The Golden Rules: 10 Steps to World-Class Excellence* by Bob Bowman (2016)

## Core Philosophy

> "Achieving excellence should not be drudgery. It should excite you; you're going after something you want to. Savor every moment."

The Method is **Bob Bowman's systematic approach** for taking someone with potential and transforming them into a world-class performer. Used with Michael Phelps for 20+ years, but applicable to anyone pursuing mastery.

**Not about medals:** "We were not chasing medals; medals were just the tangible rewards. We were chasing excellence."

---

## The 10 Rules (Steps)

### Rule 1: A Champion Sets a "Dream Big" Vision
**Stake your vision on where you want to go.**

**Key principle:** In your mind, program your internal viewfinder toward a performance, achievement, or scene you want to be part of in the future.

**Requirements:**
- **Fact-based:** Use data, experience, and gut instinct
- **Suspend belief:** Reach beyond what seems possible
- **Creative thinking:** Go somewhere comfortable, let your mind work
- **Written down:** Document it or tell someone trusted
- **Engaging:** Must excite you emotionally, give you goosebumps

**Bowman on Phelps (age 11, 1997):**
> "Michael has real potential. I can see him doing things no other swimmers can do. In 2000, he'll be a factor at Olympic Trials. By 2004, he'll definitely win medals. And it's only going to build from there."

**The meeting:** Debbie Phelps said "You're crazy, Bowman." But the vision provided the framework for everything that followed.

**Application:** The vision provides the picture frame for future success. Without it, no starting point.

---

### Rule 2: Adopt an "All-In!" Attitude, Not a "Get Out!" One
**Seek to achieve, not to medal.**

**Core distinction:** Don't make "win gold" the mission - another swimmer may simply be better. Set sights on **breaking records, nailing best time possible** - tangible, achievable, within your control.

**Michael Phelps on Mrs. Know-It-All:**
At a fundraiser before 2004 Athens Olympics, a team parent told Michael he shouldn't enter the 100m butterfly because "you'll have no shot of winning that at the Olympics."

**Result:** Michael won 100m fly gold in Athens in Olympic-record time. Post-race: "What's up now, Mrs. Know-it-all?"

**Lesson:** What makes Michael great is **he cannot stand to lose. Or be told he can't achieve something. Or be seen by others as lagging.** That's the All-In Attitude.

**Meadowbrook slogan:** "Attitude. Action. Achievement."

**Why attitude comes first:** Unless you have the inclination to be positive, inspired, a "what-more-can-I-do" participant, you won't completely enjoy success.

**Grandmother Helen example:**
- Age 55, last kid out of house
- Decided to pursue 30-year dream: get college degree
- Friends/family: "Why do you need that?"
- Maw: "Why not just try it?" approach
- **Result:** Got degree, became Bowman's hero

**Application:** Move forward with positive energy. Take the "Why not just try it?" approach.

### Rule 4: Short-Term Goals Lead to Long-Term Success
**The daily cookie-in-hand decision.**

**Hierarchy:**
1. **Long-term (8-10 years):** Forest-level vision
2. **Olympic cycle (4 years):** Benchmark goals  
3. **Annual (1 year):** Specific, plannable
4. **Immediate (right now):** ← **MOST IMPORTANT**

**Cookie example:**
- Everyone agrees: eating healthy is good
- Everyone can find a plan
- **Key decision:** What do you do when the cookie's in your hand?

> "That's where progress is made."

**Michael at age 13:**
- Listed 3 events with target times to **hundredth of second**
- 3 things needed to achieve each goal
- Posted on refrigerator (saw it daily)

**Results at year-end meet:**
- 200 Fly: Goal 2:04.68, Swam 2:04.68 (**EXACT**)
- 400 IM: Goal 4:31.86, Swam 4:31.81 (-0.05)
- 1500 Free: Goal 16:00.00, Swam 16:00.88 (+0.88)

**Bowman:** "If you have a very good eye for goal setting and you know how to turn that into daily training, you can predict your performance."

**Application:** Break vision into intermediate goals. Reset constantly. Focus on RIGHT NOW decisions.

### Rule 5: Live the Vision Every Day
**Your most critical performances take place away from the crowds.**

**Michael's 200m butterfly world record (age 15):**
- Didn't happen on race day alone
- Product of **thousands of mundane workouts**
- Twice daily: 7am before school, 3 hours after classes
- Off-the-beaten-path Baltimore pool
- Only focus: black lane line

**Swimming vs. other sports:**
- Not baseball/golf with sunlight and views
- You're wet, underwater, using raw muscle power
- Improvement comes in **milliseconds, not minutes**

**The breathing fix:**
Week after week at age 11, Bowman drilled: "Don't pick your head up until your hands are under your waist. Just set your chin on top of water and put it right back down."

Michael would get frustrated: "Oh, yeah!" *bang fist on water*

**Loop:** Prod → push back → nudge → practice. Repeat, rinse, repeat, rinse.

**Within 4 years:** World record.

**Bowman's promise:** "Michael, you now have it in the bank forever."

**Result:** 16 Olympic events swimming butterfly, 14 medals.

**Application:** Excellence is the product of everyday effort. Details matter. Routine + relevance = gold.

### Rule 6: A Team Approach Can Inspire Individual Success
**Collective energy elevates everyone.**

**North Baltimore Aquatic Club, September 2013:**
Assembled greatest swim team outside Olympics:
- Yannick Agnel (France): 2 Olympic golds
- Conor Dwyer: Olympic gold
- Oussama Mellouli: Olympic gold
- Lotte Friis (Denmark): Olympic bronze
- Jessica Long: 17 Paralympic medals (12 gold)
- Matt McLean, Tom Luchsinger, Cierra Runge, Gillian Ryan
- Allison Schmitt (joining later): 5 medals in 2012
- Chase Kalisz (joining later): Future American record holder
- **Michael Phelps:** Returning from retirement

**Why Baltimore, not sunny California?**
Athletes sought **Bowman's Method** - they valued the process over the setting.

**Tom Luchsinger ("Mr. Perfect"):**
- Struggled in meets, but **All-In Attitude**
- Always on time, picked up teammates when cars wouldn't start
- Remembered birthdays, sent tweets
- Brought positive energy daily

**Result:** NBAC team collectively sharpened him for Olympic Trials.

**Allison Schmitt on team energy:**
> "You can use other people's energies to help goose you up or get you through rough spots. This team is very good about pushing one another."

**Application:** Surround yourself with people pursuing excellence. Feed off collective energy.

### Rule 7: Stay Motivated Over the Long Haul
**Passion + tactics to combat burnout.**

**The grind is real:**
- Twice-daily practices, year-round
- Staring at black lane line for hours
- Millisecond improvements
- "Swimming isn't like baseball or golf. You're wet, underwater, using raw muscle power."

**Allison Schmitt's secret:**
> "Yes, there are days when you don't want to go to practice, but there are days when most people don't want to go to work. I get by with a fake-it-till-you-make-it attitude. Eventually, if you tell yourself enough that it will be fun, you'll trick yourself into being happy."

**Bowman's tactics to combat monotony:**

**1. Countdown app:**
- Days until Rio on iPhone
- "Pretty soon zero will be here. Let's get to work."

**2. Theme events:**
- Friends Friday: swimmers bring buddies (Michael invited Ravens players)
- Half-Way to Rio Roast
- Storytelling sessions for young swimmers

**3. John Harbaugh's playbook (Ravens coach):**
- Guest speakers from business/entertainment
- Players present (Marshal Yanda: "Embrace the Grind")
- Field trips (rookies to Gettysburg battlefields)
- Result: 2nd most NFL victories 2008-2014

**Bowman's approach:** "The more you sprinkle motivational practices into regular vision quest, the easier to keep passion charged."

**4. Find secondary passion:**
- Bowman: thoroughbred horses
- Provides balance, prevents overthinking swimming
- "Horses made me a better swim coach"

**Michael's outlets:**
- Golf, video games, card games
- "He can find joy simply sitting in a chair, eyes staring ahead, thinking. He's actually rather deep." - Debbie Phelps

**5. Immediate feedback, not meetings:**
- Walk pool deck every hour giving feedback
- Shows swimmers you care about their effort
- Don't wait for Monday staff meeting
- "If I can convey interest at moment's notice, they stay sharp"

**Dick Ebersol (NBC Sports president) story:**
After Michael's 8th gold in Beijing, in Ebersol's office watching Olympic tribute video:
> "Every time I see this I cry. And I've probably seen it hundreds of times!"

**Lesson:** After 40 years in TV sports, Dick still had **passion** for Olympics. That's what separates high achievers from dreamers.

**Application:** Passion must be sustained. Use tactics: countdowns, team events, secondary interests, immediate feedback.

### Rule 8: Adversity Will Make You Stronger
**Practice being uncomfortable.**

**Core principle:** Train in worst conditions → best performance in competition.

**Navy SEALs workout (2010):**
- Michael and team invited to SEAL training base
- 4 hours: hill runs, ocean submersions, obstacle course, ocean rowing
- SEAL constantly yelling
- Covered in sand all day (extra discomfort)
- Michael got concussion from oar

**Why?** Olympics = worst conditions (bad rooms, 8 people/suite, broken routines, media circus)

**Simulated pressure tactics:**
- 3-4 times/week: create uncomfortable situations
- Head-to-head races when exhausted
- Get up close: "This isn't good enough! Who do you think you are?"
- Stop practice: "We're starting over. This is beneath our standards."

**Message:** Not "you must do better" but "**You can do better. Let's do it.**"

**Broken goggles story (Australia World Cup):**
- Bowman intentionally stepped on Phelps' goggles before heat
- Goggles filled with water during race
- Michael **counted strokes** to find the wall
- Worked through annoyance

**Beijing 2008, 200m butterfly final:**
- Diving in for potential record-breaking 10th gold
- Goggles immediately fill with water
- Can't see competition or wall
- **Falls back on stroke-count method from practice**
- Wins race + world record

> "I was just hoping I was winning."

**London 2012, Day 1: Fourth place in 400 IM**
- First time since 2000 Michael swam Olympic event without medaling
- Embarrassed, angry
- Michael: "I didn't train! I played golf! I can't swim!"
- Bowman: "There's a whole long list of things you've done wrong, but we're going to start with breaststroke. I'm not letting you off the hook, but this is what we can do TODAY."

**Next week:** 4 more golds, 2 silvers.

**Winston Churchill quote Bowman loves:**
> "Success is the ability to go from one failure to another with no loss of enthusiasm."

**Jessica Long's visualization:**
When having bad day in practice:
> "I visualize myself at the Trials, people watching me take off my legs, this girl with no legs getting in the pool. I visualize saying to myself, 'You did it! You got here!'"

**Broken hand story (December 2007):**
- 9 months before Beijing
- Michael broke hand slipping on ice
- Initial reaction: "I've just given away 3 gold medals"
- Bowman: "Let's look at the facts. What CAN we do?"
- Doctor: 6 weeks in cast
- Bowman: "That doesn't work. What's plan B?"
- Surgery with pin: 10 days to heal
- Solution: 3 hours/day stationary bike, Day 7: plastic bag over hand kicking, Day 10: back to training

**Lesson:** "This perfect plan comes off the tracks. How are we going to deal with this problem and **how quickly?**"

**Tom Luchsinger coming out (2014):**
- Article: "King of the Double Life"
- Couldn't sleep, tortured by hiding sexuality
- Made deal: if didn't repeat championship, would come out
- Failed to make Worlds team
- Came out publicly

**Post-coming out:**
> "My friendships have gotten stronger. My smile is more genuine. I laugh a lot more. My body has time to recover because I'm actually easy-going. I have found qualities I like about myself. My good days far outnumber bad days."

**Bowman's lesson:** Tell people to **find someone to share troubling thoughts**. Don't just say it once - remind often. Sharing relieves stress.

**Application:** 
1. Practice under pressure regularly
2. Visualize good that can come from disappointment
3. "Cancellation should not be confused with failure" (Jon Stewart/David Letterman)
4. Reduce anxieties at all cost - find confidant
5. Comeback shouldn't be solo venture - seek help

### Rule 9: When the Time Comes, Perform with Confidence
**Don't forget the details. But don't think about them when performing.**

**Beijing 2008: 100m butterfly final vs. Milorad Cavic**

**Setup:**
- Day 8 of competition
- Michael had 6 golds, going for record-tying 7th
- Cavic (Serbia): fresh, rested (only 2 prior races)
- Cavic beat Michael in prelims, broke Olympic record
- Cavic quote: "It would be good for the sport if [Michael] lost. I'd love to be that guy who took it away from him."

**Race morning routine:**
- Bowman up 4:30am, McDonald's Egg McMuffin + coffee
- Michael slept in (routines, routines, routines)
- Walk to bus: **no strategy talk** (all done weeks before in Baltimore)
- Instead: Bowman shared Cavic's quote
- Michael: "He said WHAT?" - brushfire lit

**The plan (set in Baltimore):**
- Go out in 23 seconds first lap
- "If you go 23-anything, you win"
- Otherwise: normal routine (butterfly kick, breathing pattern, **slam the wall**)

**Practice of "slamming wall":**
Bowman to Phelps: "Michael, what's my job?"  
Phelps: "To get me in the ballpark."  
Bowman: "Michael, what's your job?"  
Phelps: "**To get my hands on the wall.**"

**The race:**
- After 50m: Cavic leading, Michael in 7th
- Michael's split: 24.00 (0.01 off plan - not good)
- Final 50m: Cavic slightly ahead
- Bowman in stands 15m from finish: gyrating, willing Michael forward
- At wall: Cavic appeared to win (glided in)
- Scoreboard: Michael 50.58 OR (Olympic Record)
- Official results: **1. M. Phelps 2. M. Cavic**
- **Margin of victory: 0.01 seconds**

**Post-race:**
Michael: "If I had glided, I would have been way too long. I took short, faster strokes to try to get my hand on the wall. I ended up making the right decision."

**Bowman's lesson:**
> "Details matter. They'll make the difference when you're called on to compete. By reviewing and reinforcing seemingly minor details daily, MP was ready when he had to perform. He didn't need to think about closing hard. He simply did it."

**Baltimore Sun headline:** "History by a hair"

**Application:** 
- Practice details until automatic
- Don't think when performing - execute
- Preparation happens in training, not on race day
- Confidence comes from demonstrated ability (thousands of wall slams)

## Key Themes Across All Rules

### 1. Process Over Outcome
> "The process is more important than the outcome because that's what is controllable."

- Nick Saban: "Don't look at the scoreboard, play the next play"
- In swimming: don't worry about place, execute the turn, breakout, finish

### 2. Daily Excellence
- Most critical performances happen **away from crowds**
- "Excellence is product of everyday effort"
- Olympic podium is just validation of 1,000+ training days

### 3. Demonstrated Ability = Confidence
> "Confidence comes from demonstrated ability. You can't dream it up."

**How it works:**
1. Do something successfully in practice
2. Next time you believe you can do it again
3. Repeat thousands of times
4. Confidence becomes automatic

### 4. Vision → Goals → Daily Actions
- Vision establishes the forest
- Goals find the trees
- Daily actions walk the path
- All three must align

### 5. Adversity Is Training
- Champions need things to work on
- Failure is part of process
- Learn from every setback
- Resilience is practiced, not innate

## Bowman's Coaching Identity

**GPS analogy:**
> "Think of me as GPS. Swimmers drive the car. I'm that annoying thing on dashboard saying 'turn right.' When swimmers go off course: 'Recalculating... recalculating...' The job: keep bringing them back to the goal."

**What he teaches beyond swimming:**
- Self-discipline
- Goal-setting mastery
- Handling pressure
- Dealing with adversity
- Growing as a person

**Yannick Agnel on why he trained with Bowman:**
> "Bob is definitely focused on making you grow up as a man and become more than just a swimmer."

---

## Application for Coaches

1. **Help athletes define dream** - emotional vision, not just times
2. **Build All-In Attitude** - positive energy, "what-more-can-I-do"
3. **Set specific goals** - from 10-year vision to right-now decisions
4. **Live vision daily** - routine + relevance = excellence
5. **Create team culture** - collective energy elevates all
6. **Combat burnout** - theme events, secondary passions, immediate feedback
7. **Practice adversity** - worst conditions = best performances
8. **Trust the details** - when performed correctly, confidence flows
9. **Focus on process** - outcomes will follow
10. **Remember:** Physical talent gets you to table. Mental/emotional characteristics make you champion.

## The Bottom Line

**Bowman's mission:**
> "To help people realize that the best moments in life come not from the achievement but from the everyday pursuit of excellence."

**Not just for Olympians:**
- Working mom with presentation + sick kid
- Commuting dad missing daughter's game
- High school senior with Stanford dreams
- Salesperson chasing promotion
- Anyone with vision they want to achieve

**The Method works when:**
- You have vision (Rule 1)
- You bring All-In Attitude (Rule 2)
- You set intermediate goals (Rule 4)
- You do daily work (Rule 5)
- You stay motivated (Rule 7)
- You handle adversity (Rule 8)
- You perform with confidence (Rule 9)

**Final word:**
> "Achieving excellence should not be drudgery. It should excite you. Savor every moment. The Method wants the process to be as satisfying as the reward is lasting."
