# Drill Progression Principles: The Guzman Method

**Source:** *The Swimming Drill Book* by Ruben J. Guzman  
**Context:** Systematic approach to teaching swimming through progressive drill sequences  
**Application:** Building training plans that move from simple → complex, isolated → integrated

---

## Overview: Why Progression Matters

Most coaches know **what drills** to use. The Guzman method teaches **in what order** to use them.

**Core insight:** Swimming is a **complex motor skill** requiring:
- Body position awareness
- Breathing control
- Propulsive mechanics
- Coordination/timing

**The mistake:** Teaching all components simultaneously → Cognitive overload → Poor execution

**The solution:** **Progressive skill building** — Master one component before adding the next.

---

## The Five-Stage Progression Framework

### Stage 1: Body Position & Buoyancy (Foundation)

**Goal:** Establish proper alignment and water confidence

**Key principle:** You can't stroke effectively if your body position is wrong. **Position first, propulsion second.**

**Drills (from Chapter 1):**
1. **Standing Streamline (Dry)** — Learn the shape on deck
2. **Cannonball Float** — Understand positive buoyancy
3. **Dead Swimmer's Float** — Relax and float face-down
4. **Streamline Float** — Hold streamline in water
5. **Pencil Float/Drop** — Vertical body control
6. **Sliding (front/back)** — Glide with minimal drag

**Progression timeline:**
- **Week 1:** Static floats and streamline holds (3-5 seconds)
- **Week 2:** Gliding (5+ yards off wall)
- **Week 3:** Streamline + kick (maintain position while kicking)

**Assessment:** Can swimmer hold streamline for 5 yards without body position breakdown?  
- **YES** → Move to Stage 2  
- **NO** → Repeat Stage 1 drills with feedback/video

---

### Stage 2: Breathing & Kicking (Independent Components)

**Goal:** Establish rhythmic breathing and leg propulsion separately

**Key principle:** Don't teach breathing **during** stroke learning. Teach it **before** as an independent skill.

**Breathing drills:**
- **Over and Under Breathing** — Exhale underwater, inhale above, repeat rhythmically
- **Bobbing** — Vertical breathing practice
- **Wall breathing** — Freestyle/backstroke breathing at wall (no arm stroke)

**Kicking drills:**
- **Flutter Kick Deck Drill (Dry)** — See the mechanics
- **Slow Flutter on Front (with board)** — Feel proper ankle flex
- **Streamline Front Flutter Kick** — Kick in optimal position
- **Vertical Flutter Kick** — Test kick effectiveness (must be hip-driven to stay afloat)

**Progression timeline:**
- **Week 1:** Breathing patterns independent of stroke
- **Week 2:** Kick mechanics (deck drill → board → streamline)
- **Week 3:** Kick assessment (vertical kick test)

**Assessment:** Can swimmer breathe rhythmically every 3-5 seconds? Can they kick 25 yards in streamline?  
- **YES** → Move to Stage 3  
- **NO** → Isolate weak component (breathing vs. kicking) and repeat

---

### Stage 3: Propulsive Mechanics (Arm Stroke Components)

**Goal:** Learn arm stroke mechanics **without** full stroke coordination

**Key principle:** Isolate arm path, catch, and recovery before adding breathing, kicking, and timing.

**Mechanic isolation drills (stroke-specific):**

#### Freestyle:
- **Single-arm freestyle** — One arm at a time (opposite arm extended or at side)
- **Catch-up drill** — Full stroke, but arms "catch up" at front (front quadrant emphasis)
- **Fist drill** — Closed fist = forces forearm catch awareness
- **Sculling** — Develop hand/forearm "feel"

#### Backstroke:
- **Single-arm backstroke**
- **Double-arm backstroke** — Both arms pull simultaneously (exaggerates timing)
- **Handshake drill** — High elbow recovery path

#### Breaststroke:
- **Breaststroke kick (isolated)** — 2-kick-1-pull drill
- **Breaststroke pull (isolated)** — Pull with flutter kick
- **Inverted breaststroke kick** — On back (teaches proper whip)

#### Butterfly:
- **Single-arm butterfly** — One arm fly, other arm extended
- **Dolphin kick (vertical)** — Core-driven body wave
- **3-stroke drill** — 3 fly, 3 free (breathing pattern)

**Progression timeline:**
- **Week 1:** Single-component drills (arms only, legs only)
- **Week 2:** Exaggerated drills (fist drill, catch-up)
- **Week 3:** Contrast drills (drill → full stroke alternation)

**Assessment:** Can swimmer execute arm stroke with proper mechanics at slow speed?  
- **YES** → Move to Stage 4  
- **NO** → Identify specific flaw (low elbow, late catch, etc.) and target with drill

---

### Stage 4: Timing & Coordination (Integration)

**Goal:** Synchronize arms, legs, and breathing into full stroke

**Key principle:** Once mechanics are solid, **timing drills** teach the rhythm of the stroke.

**Coordination drills:**

#### Freestyle:
- **6-3-6 drill** — 6 kicks on side, 3 strokes, rotate (rotation timing)
- **6-kick switch** — 6 kicks per arm stroke (deliberate timing)
- **Breathe-every-3** — Bilateral breathing pattern

#### Backstroke:
- **6-kick switch backstroke** — Same as freestyle, on back
- **Continuous arms backstroke** — Non-stop arms, focus on kick timing

#### Breaststroke:
- **1-kick-1-pull** — Standard timing
- **2-kick-1-pull** — Kick-dominant (teaches power)
- **1-kick-2-pull** — Pull-dominant (teaches glide)

#### Butterfly:
- **2-kick-1-pull** — Standard butterfly timing (kick on entry, kick on exit)
- **1-kick-1-pull** — Advanced timing variation

**Progression timeline:**
- **Week 1:** Exaggerated timing (6-3-6, 2-kick-1-pull)
- **Week 2:** Modified timing (approaching normal stroke)
- **Week 3:** Full stroke with timing focus

**Assessment:** Does the stroke look coordinated? Is breathing integrated smoothly?  
- **YES** → Move to Stage 5  
- **NO** → Return to Stage 3 (mechanics) or repeat Stage 4 drills

---

### Stage 5: Speed & Efficiency (Application)

**Goal:** Apply technique under race conditions (speed, fatigue, turns)

**Key principle:** Technique must hold up when:
- Swimming at race pace
- Fatigued (late in workout/race)
- Executing turns and underwater work

**Application drills:**
- **Descend sets** — Progressive speed increase (does technique break down?)
- **Stroke count** — Efficiency metric (fewer strokes = better technique)
- **Drill-to-fast contrast** — Drill 25, sprint 25 (transfer drill feel to speed)
- **Threshold pace with focus** — Long repeats at race pace (technique under fatigue)

**Progression timeline:**
- **Week 1:** Moderate pace with technique check-ins
- **Week 2:** Descend sets (controlled speed increase)
- **Week 3:** Race-pace repeats with stroke count tracking

**Assessment:** Can swimmer hold technique at race pace? Does stroke count stay consistent when tired?  
- **YES** → Skill mastered, maintain with periodic drill reinforcement  
- **NO** → Return to Stage 3-4 (technique breaks down under load)

## Practical Application: Sample 12-Week Progression

### Weeks 1-2: Foundation (Stage 1)

**Focus:** Body position and streamline

**Monday:**
```
Warm-up: 200 choice
Drill Set:
  4×50: 25 streamline float / 25 choice w/:15R
  100 sliding on front (push-off, glide, no kick)
  100 sliding on back
Main: 8×50 choice stroke (easy pace) w/:15R
Cool-down: 100 choice
```

**Wednesday:**
```
Warm-up: 200 choice
Body Position Circuit:
  2×50 pencil float practice
  2×50 streamline float practice
  2×50 sliding on front
  2×50 sliding on back
Main: 200 continuous easy swim (any stroke)
Cool-down: 100 choice
```

**Friday:**
```
Assessment Day:
  Streamline glide test (must hold 5+ yards)
  Dead swimmer's float (30 seconds continuous)
  200 continuous swim (any stroke)
```

**Outcome:** If passing assessment → Move to Weeks 3-4. If not → Repeat Weeks 1-2.

---

### Weeks 3-4: Breathing & Kicking (Stage 2)

**Focus:** Establish independent breathing rhythm and kick mechanics

**Monday:**
```
Warm-up: 300 choice (include 100 kick)
Breathing Drill Set:
  4×25 over-and-under breathing w/:20R
  4×25 wall breathing (freestyle) w/:20R
Kicking Drill Set:
  4×50 streamline flutter kick (front) w/:15R
  2×50 streamline back kick w/:15R
Main: 6×50 choice stroke w/:15R
Cool-down: 100 choice
```

**Wednesday:**
```
Warm-up: 200 choice
Kick Circuit:
  100 flutter kick deck drill (dry)
  2×50 slow flutter kick (with board) w/:15R
  2×50 streamline front flutter kick (no board) w/:15R
  50 vertical flutter kick (timed: how long can you stay afloat?)
Main: 8×50 freestyle w/:15R (breathe every 3)
Cool-down: 100 kick easy
```

**Friday:**
```
Assessment:
  Vertical flutter kick (60 seconds minimum)
  4×25 streamline kick w/:20R (maintain body position entire 25)
  200 freestyle with rhythmic breathing (every 3-5 strokes)
```

**Outcome:** If passing → Move to Weeks 5-7. If not → Repeat Weeks 3-4 with focus on weak area (breathing vs. kicking).

---

### Weeks 5-7: Arm Mechanics (Stage 3)

**Focus:** Develop catch, pull, and recovery mechanics

**Monday:**
```
Warm-up: 400 (200 swim, 200 kick)
Drill Set (Freestyle Focus):
  4×50 single-arm freestyle (25 left, 25 right) w/:15R
  4×50 catch-up drill w/:15R
  4×50 fist drill w/:15R
  4×50 finger-tip drag drill w/:15R
Main: 6×100 freestyle w/:20R (apply drill focus)
Cool-down: 200 choice
```

**Wednesday:**
```
Warm-up: 300 choice
Sculling + Catch Development:
  100 sculling (various positions)
  4×50: 25 fist drill / 25 freestyle w/:10R
  4×50: 25 catch-up / 25 freestyle w/:10R
Main: 8×50 freestyle descend 1-4, 5-8 w/:15R
Cool-down: 100 choice
```

**Friday:**
```
Stroke Mechanics Check:
  Video analysis: single-arm freestyle + full stroke
  Stroke count test: 4×25 freestyle (count strokes per 25)
  Main: 4×100 freestyle w/:20R (technique focus)
```

**Outcome:** Video shows proper catch and recovery → Move to Weeks 8-10. If not → Identify flaw and prescribe targeted drill.

---

### Weeks 8-10: Timing & Coordination (Stage 4)

**Focus:** Integrate arms, legs, breathing into full stroke

**Monday:**
```
Warm-up: 400 (200 swim, 200 kick)
Timing Drill Set:
  4×50 6-3-6 drill w/:15R
  4×50: 25 6-kick switch / 25 freestyle w/:10R
  4×50 freestyle bilateral breathing (every 3) w/:15R
Main: 6×100 freestyle w/:20R (smooth, coordinated stroke)
Cool-down: 200 choice
```

**Wednesday:**
```
Warm-up: 300 choice
Drill-to-Swim Contrast:
  4×75: 25 drill choice / 50 freestyle w/:15R
  2×150: 50 drill choice / 100 freestyle w/:20R
Main: 8×50 freestyle descend 1-4, 5-8 w/:15R
Cool-down: 100 choice
```

**Friday:**
```
Coordination Assessment:
  200 freestyle continuous (does it look smooth?)
  4×50 freestyle stroke count w/:15R (consistency check)
  Video review: full stroke coordination
```

**Outcome:** Stroke is coordinated and efficient → Move to Weeks 11-12. If not → Repeat Stage 4 drills.

---

### Weeks 11-12: Speed & Efficiency (Stage 5)

**Focus:** Maintain technique at race pace

**Monday:**
```
Warm-up: 600 (400 swim, 200 kick/drill)
Drill Maintenance:
  4×50 drill choice w/:10R (keep skills sharp)
Main Set:
  6×100 freestyle descend 1-3, 4-6 w/:20R
    (Goal: Negative split each set, hold technique)
Cool-down: 200 choice
```

**Wednesday:**
```
Warm-up: 400 choice
Race-Pace Set:
  8×50 freestyle at goal 100 pace w/:30R
  (Check stroke count each 50 — should be consistent)
Main: 2×200 freestyle at threshold pace w/:40R
Cool-down: 200 choice
```

**Friday:**
```
Time Trial:
  100 freestyle all-out (does technique hold?)
  Stroke count during time trial (compare to easy pace)
  Video review under race pace
```

**Outcome:** Technique holds at speed → Progression complete. If breakdown occurs → Return to Stage 3-4.

## Coaching Decision Tree: When to Progress vs. Repeat

### Decision Point 1: After Stage 1 (Body Position)
**Test:** Streamline glide 5+ yards off wall without position breakdown

**Pass:** → Move to Stage 2 (Breathing & Kicking)  
**Fail (hips drop):** → Repeat streamline float + sliding drills  
**Fail (shoulders too high):** → Emphasize chest press (push chest down slightly)  
**Fail (head up):** → Dead swimmer's float practice (face-down relaxation)

---

### Decision Point 2: After Stage 2 (Breathing & Kicking)
**Test:** Vertical flutter kick 60+ seconds + rhythmic breathing every 3 strokes for 200

**Pass:** → Move to Stage 3 (Arm Mechanics)  
**Fail (can't stay vertical):** → Kick from hips, not knees (repeat deck drill + vertical kick)  
**Fail (breath holding):** → Over-and-under breathing drill until rhythmic  
**Fail (both):** → Focus on weaker component first, then retest

---

### Decision Point 3: After Stage 3 (Arm Mechanics)
**Test:** Video analysis shows proper catch, high elbow recovery, full extension

**Pass:** → Move to Stage 4 (Timing)  
**Fail (low elbow catch):** → Fist drill + sculling  
**Fail (wide recovery):** → Finger-tip drag drill  
**Fail (short stroke):** → Catch-up drill + stroke count  
**Fail (multiple issues):** → Single-arm drill to isolate and fix one arm at a time

---

### Decision Point 4: After Stage 4 (Timing)
**Test:** Full stroke looks coordinated, breathing is smooth, no dead spots

**Pass:** → Move to Stage 5 (Speed)  
**Fail (kick-pull timing off):** → 6-kick switch or 6-3-6 drill  
**Fail (breathing disrupts stroke):** → 3-stroke breathing pattern drill  
**Fail (dead glide):** → Catch-up drill with emphasis on continuous motion

---

### Decision Point 5: After Stage 5 (Speed)
**Test:** Technique holds at race pace, stroke count consistent when fatigued

**Pass:** → Skill mastered, maintain with weekly drill work  
**Fail (technique breaks at speed):** → Slow down, repeat Stage 3-4 at higher technical load  
**Fail (stroke count increases when tired):** → Efficiency drills (stroke count, DPS focus)

## Common Progression Errors (and Fixes)

### Error 1: Skipping Stages
**What it looks like:** Teaching full stroke on Day 1 without body position foundation  
**Result:** Swimmer develops compensation patterns (head-up freestyle, low hips, etc.)  
**Fix:** Return to Stage 1, rebuild foundation

**Example:**
- **Swimmer:** Can complete 100 freestyle but has terrible body position  
- **Diagnosis:** Skipped Stage 1  
- **Prescription:** 2 weeks of streamline/sliding drills, then rebuild stroke from Stage 2

---

### Error 2: Moving Too Fast
**What it looks like:** Progressing to next stage after 1-2 sessions (not enough repetition)  
**Result:** Skill not ingrained, reverts to old habits under pressure  
**Fix:** Minimum 2-3 weeks per stage for beginners, 1-2 weeks for intermediates

**Rule of thumb:**
- **Beginners:** 3-4 weeks per stage
- **Intermediates:** 2-3 weeks per stage
- **Advanced:** 1-2 weeks per stage (review/refinement)

---

### Error 3: Progressing Despite Failed Assessment
**What it looks like:** "They're close enough, let's move on"  
**Result:** Weak foundation → Struggles in later stages  
**Fix:** Be strict with progression criteria. If they don't pass, repeat.

**Hard truth:** Spending extra time on Stage 1-2 **saves** time overall. Rushing creates long-term technical debt.

---

### Error 4: One-Size-Fits-All Progression
**What it looks like:** Entire team follows same progression regardless of skill level  
**Result:** Beginners overwhelmed, advanced swimmers bored  
**Fix:** Differentiate by skill level

**Tiered approach:**
- **Lane 1 (Beginners):** Stages 1-3 (6-12 weeks)
- **Lane 2 (Intermediate):** Stages 3-4 (4-8 weeks)
- **Lane 3 (Advanced):** Stage 5 + refinement (ongoing)

---

### Error 5: Abandoning Drills After Stage 5
**What it looks like:** "They know how to swim now, no more drills needed"  
**Result:** Technique gradually degrades over months/years  
**Fix:** Maintenance drilling — 10-15% of every workout should be drill work

**Maintenance schedule:**
- **Daily:** 200-400 drill work in warm-up
- **Weekly:** One full drill-focused session
- **Monthly:** Technique assessment (video + stroke count)

## Integration with Other Training Concepts

### Linking to Schneider's Core Drills
Schneider's 9 drills ([see Drill Library: Schneider](drill-library-schneider.md)) map to Guzman stages:

| Schneider Drill | Guzman Stage | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Streamline Float | Stage 1 | Body position foundation |
| 6-3-6 | Stage 4 | Timing and rotation |
| Catch-up | Stage 3 & 4 | Mechanics + front quadrant timing |
| Fist drill | Stage 3 | Forearm catch awareness |
| Finger-tip drag | Stage 3 | High elbow recovery |
| Single-arm | Stage 3 | Isolated mechanics |
| Stroke count | Stage 5 | Efficiency metric |
| Vertical kick | Stage 2 | Kick effectiveness test |
| Pull/Kick sets | Stage 2 & 3 | Component isolation |

**How to use both:**
- **Guzman:** Defines the progression **sequence**
- **Schneider:** Defines the specific **drills** at each stage

---

### Linking to Lucero's Drill Philosophy
Lucero ([see Drill Fundamentals: Lucero](drill-fundamentals-lucero.md)) asks **why** we drill.  
Guzman answers **when** and **in what order**.

**Combined approach:**
1. **Lucero:** Understand the purpose of drilling (awareness, exaggeration, isolation)
2. **Guzman:** Apply progressive sequence (body position → mechanics → timing → speed)
3. **Schneider:** Execute with specific drills

---

### Linking to Hannula's Whole vs. Part Practice
Hannula ([see Hannula Stroke Teaching Principles](hannula-stroke-teaching-principles.md)) argues for **whole-stroke** teaching in many cases.  
Guzman provides the **part-practice** (drill-based) alternative.

**When to use each:**
- **Guzman (part-practice):** When fundamental components are missing (beginners, major technique flaws)
- **Hannula (whole-stroke):** When components exist but need refinement (advanced swimmers, minor adjustments)

**Example:**
- **Beginner:** Use Guzman progression (Stages 1-5 over 12-16 weeks)
- **National-level swimmer:** Use Hannula whole-stroke approach with targeted drill refinement

## Key Coaching Takeaways

1. **Follow the progression order**  
   Body position → Breathing/Kicking → Mechanics → Timing → Speed  
   Skipping stages creates technical debt.

2. **Assess before progressing**  
   Each stage has clear pass/fail criteria. Don't move on until they pass.

3. **Be patient with Stage 1-2**  
   Foundation stages take the longest but save time overall.

4. **Differentiate by skill level**  
   Beginners need all 5 stages. Advanced swimmers may only need Stage 5 refinement.

5. **Maintenance is forever**  
   Even elite swimmers need ongoing drill work to maintain technique.

6. **Integrate with other frameworks**  
   Guzman provides sequence, Schneider provides drills, Lucero provides philosophy.

## Sample Workout: Progression-Based Practice

**Scenario:** Mixed-skill team (lanes by ability)

### Lane 1 (Beginners — Stage 1-2)
```
Warm-up: 200 choice
Drill Focus:
  4×50: 25 streamline float / 25 choice w/:20R
  100 sliding (front and back)
  4×25 over-and-under breathing w/:20R
Main: 6×50 choice stroke (easy pace, focus on breathing) w/:20R
Cool-down: 100 choice
TOTAL: 1100
```

---

### Lane 2 (Intermediate — Stage 3-4)
```
Warm-up: 400 (200 swim, 200 kick)
Drill Focus:
  4×50 single-arm freestyle w/:15R
  4×50 catch-up drill w/:15R
  4×50 6-3-6 drill w/:15R
Main: 8×100 freestyle w/:20R (apply drill techniques)
Cool-down: 200 choice
TOTAL: 2000
```

---

### Lane 3 (Advanced — Stage 5)
```
Warm-up: 600 (400 swim, 200 kick/drill)
Drill Maintenance:
  4×50 drill choice w/:10R (self-selected based on feel)
Main Set:
  8×100 freestyle descend 1-4, 5-8 w/:15R
  4×200 freestyle threshold pace w/:30R
Cool-down: 200 choice
TOTAL: 3400
```

**All lanes:** Same drill concepts, different stages of progression.
