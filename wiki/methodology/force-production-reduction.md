# Force Production and Force Reduction in Swimming

**Source:** Athletic Development: The Art & Science of Functional Sports Conditioning by Vern Gambetta  
**Application:** Understanding the eccentric-concentric cycle for better training and injury prevention

---

## The Performance Paradigm

All athletic movement involves three interrelated components:

```
┌─────────────────────────┐
│   FORCE REDUCTION       │ ← Most overlooked, most important
│   (Eccentric/Loading)   │
└────────────┬────────────┘
             │
             ▼
┌─────────────────────────┐
│   PROPRIOCEPTION        │ ← Body awareness, control
│   (Joint position)      │
└────────────┬────────────┘
             │
             ▼
┌─────────────────────────┐
│   FORCE PRODUCTION      │ ← Most visible, most measured
│   (Concentric/Unloading)│
└─────────────────────────┘
```

**Key insight:** You can't have quality force production without quality force reduction.

---

## Force Reduction: The Missing Link

### What Is Force Reduction?

**Other names:**
- Eccentric phase
- Loading phase
- Yielding phase
- Deceleration
- Shock absorption

**In swimming:**
- Hand entry and catch
- Leg recovery in all strokes
- Body rotation deceleration
- Turn wall contact
- Breakout deceleration

### Why It's Overlooked

1. **Hard to measure**
   - No "1RM eccentric"
   - Can't easily quantify
   - Less obvious than power output

2. **Not visually impressive**
   - Absorption looks passive
   - Explosion looks exciting
   - Coaches focus on what they SEE

3. **Uncomfortable to train**
   - Causes more soreness (DOMS)
   - Requires control (not just effort)
   - Feels "easier" but is actually harder

---

## Why Force Reduction Matters

### 1. Injury Prevention

> "Most injuries occur during the force-reduction phase. It is during this phase that gravity has its greatest impact."

**Common swimming injuries caused by poor force reduction:**
- **Shoulder impingement:** Poor catch entry (slamming hand down)
- **Hamstring strains:** Kicking recovery phase (poor eccentric control)
- **Knee pain (breaststroke):** Whip kick without eccentric quad/hamstring control
- **Lower back pain:** Poor core control during rotation

**The problem:** Gravity/water/momentum is trying to SLAM the body/limbs.  
**The solution:** Muscles must act as shock absorbers and springs.

### 2. Performance Enhancement

**A stretched muscle can exert MORE force** (stretch-shortening cycle)

**The science:**
- Eccentric muscle action stores elastic energy
- Like compressing a spring
- Released explosively in concentric phase

**Swimming application:**
- Better catch = more force on pull
- Controlled rotation = explosive hip drive
- Loaded turn = explosive push-off

### 3. Movement Quality

Force reduction = CONTROL.

**Without control:**
- Sloppy technique
- Wasted energy
- Inconsistent performance

**With control:**
- Smooth, flowing movement
- Efficient use of momentum
- Repeatable mechanics

## The Eccentric-Concentric Cycle

### Loading Phase (Force Reduction)

**Characteristics:**
- ⚡ Eccentric muscle contraction (lengthening under tension)
- ⚡ Storing elastic energy
- ⚡ Preparing for explosive action
- ⚡ MOST IMPORTANT for injury prevention

**Swimming examples:**
- **Catch entry:** Hand enters, arm extends, lats stretch (loading)
- **Breaststroke kick:** Legs recover, hamstrings/glutes stretch
- **Turn approach:** Body decelerates, legs absorb momentum
- **Streamline off wall:** Arms overhead, lats/core loaded

### Transition Phase (Proprioception)

**The split-second between eccentric and concentric**

- Neural feedback: "Where am I in space?"
- Body position awareness
- Balance and stability
- Decision point: How much force to produce?

**Swimming examples:**
- **Top of catch:** Transition from entry to pull
- **Wall contact:** Absorb, reorient, prepare to push
- **Rotation apex:** Switch from loading one side to unloading the other

### Unloading Phase (Force Production)

**Characteristics:**
- ⚡ Concentric muscle contraction (shortening)
- ⚡ Releasing stored energy
- ⚡ Acceleration
- ⚡ MOST VISIBLE (what everyone sees and measures)

**Swimming examples:**
- **Pull-through:** Lats/triceps contract, hand accelerates
- **Breaststroke kick:** Explosive hip extension
- **Wall push-off:** Legs drive body into streamline
- **Breakout:** Explosive first stroke

## Training Force Reduction

### Land-Based Dryland

#### 1. Eccentric-Emphasized Exercises

**Nordic hamstring curls:**
- Slow lower (3-5 seconds)
- Explosive return
- Builds eccentric hamstring strength → prevents strains

**Tempo push-ups:**
- Lower for 4 seconds
- Explode up in 1 second
- Ratio: 4:0:1:0 (down:pause:up:pause)

**Eccentric pull-ups:**
- Jump to top position
- Lower for 5 seconds
- Repeat
- Builds lat eccentric strength → better catch entry control

**Single-leg RDLs (slow eccentric):**
- Lower for 3 seconds (balance + control)
- Return in 1 second
- Mimics streamline position stability

#### 2. Plyometrics (Reactive Force Reduction)

**Depth jumps:**
- Step off box
- **Land softly** (force reduction)
- Immediately explode up (force production)
- Builds reactive strength

**Med ball catch-and-throw:**
- Partner throws med ball at chest
- **Absorb the impact** (force reduction)
- Immediately throw back (force production)
- Mimics turn wall contact

**Box drop to broad jump:**
- Step off box
- Land in squat
- Immediately broad jump forward
- Transfers to explosive push-offs

#### 3. Tempo Training

**What is tempo?**
- Numbers like 3:1:1:0 or 4:0:2:1
- Format: Eccentric : Pause : Concentric : Pause

**Why it works:**
- Forces CONTROL of eccentric phase
- Builds time under tension
- Reduces injury risk

**Example: Goblet squat 3:1:1:0**
- 3 seconds down
- 1 second pause at bottom
- 1 second up
- 0 pause at top, immediately next rep

## In-Water Application

### Drills That Emphasize Force Reduction

#### Catch-Up Freestyle
**Focus:** Control the entry and extension phase
- One arm dead-stop in front
- Other arm enters SOFTLY (not slapping)
- Feel the stretch in lats before pulling
- **Result:** Better loading phase awareness

#### Breaststroke Glide Drill
**Focus:** Control the leg recovery
- Full stroke
- 3-second glide in streamline
- Slow leg recovery (3 seconds)
- Explosive kick
- **Result:** Eccentric control + explosive concentric

#### Turn Approach Drills
**Focus:** Decelerate, then explode
- Sprint into wall
- Land feet on wall in perfect position
- Absorb momentum (don't bounce)
- Explosive push-off
- **Result:** Controlled force reduction → max force production

#### Underwater Pullouts (Pause at Top)
**Focus:** Load before the pull
- Push off wall
- Streamline with arms overhead
- 1-second pause (feel the stretch)
- Explosive pull
- **Result:** Awareness of loaded position

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Skipping the Eccentric Phase

**Example:** Swimmer slaps hand on water at entry  
**Problem:** No loading phase → less force on pull → wasted energy  
**Fix:** "Spear" the water, extend arm fully, FEEL the lat stretch before pulling

### ❌ Bouncing at the Bottom

**Example:** Goblet squat where athlete "bounces" out of the hole  
**Problem:** Using momentum, not muscle control → injury risk  
**Fix:** Pause at bottom (even 1 second), then drive up

### ❌ Fast Eccentric, Slow Concentric

**Example:** Pull-ups where athlete drops fast, grinds up slowly  
**Problem:** No eccentric control → shoulder stress  
**Fix:** Lower with control (2-3 sec), explode up (1 sec)

### ❌ Ignoring Eccentric in Injury Prevention

**Example:** Swimmer has chronic shoulder pain, coach prescribes MORE pulling sets  
**Problem:** Shoulder can't ABSORB force (eccentric deficit), so adding more production makes it worse  
**Fix:** Eccentric-focused shoulder work (band resisted entry position, slow lowering rows)

## Programming Guidelines

### Eccentric Training Frequency

**General strength phase:**
- 2-3x/week eccentric-emphasized exercises
- Examples: Tempo squats (3:0:1:0), Nordic curls, eccentric pull-ups

**Power phase:**
- 1-2x/week reactive (plyometric) work
- Examples: Depth jumps, med ball catches, box drop to broad jump

**Taper phase:**
- MAINTAIN with light loads
- Don't introduce new eccentric stressors
- Examples: Bodyweight tempo work, soft landings

### Recovery Considerations

**Eccentric training causes MORE soreness (DOMS)**
- Expect 24-48 hour soreness
- Don't program heavy eccentric work before big meets
- Allow 48-72 hours recovery after heavy eccentric sessions

**Progression:**
- Week 1-2: Tempo work (controlled eccentric)
- Week 3-4: Add load (weighted tempo)
- Week 5-6: Plyometrics (reactive)
- Week 7-8: Reduce volume, maintain intensity

## Force Reduction Checklist

### Land Training
✅ Include tempo exercises (3-4 second eccentrics)  
✅ Nordic hamstring curls 2x/week  
✅ Eccentric pull-ups or slow lowering rows  
✅ Plyometrics with emphasis on SOFT landings  
✅ Single-leg balance work (reactive stability)  

### Pool Training
✅ Teach "soft" hand entries (not slaps)  
✅ Catch-up drill for lat loading awareness  
✅ Turn drills: decelerate → pause → explode  
✅ Underwater pullout with pause at top  
✅ Breaststroke drills with slow recovery  

### Injury Prevention
✅ Assess eccentric strength (can they control a slow negative?)  
✅ Address imbalances (weak eccentric = injury waiting to happen)  
✅ Rehab should include eccentric work (not just concentric)

## The Nick Green Principle (Applied)

> "Our aim was that our boat would go half an inch faster per stroke than every stroke of our opposition. We focused on quality and efficiency of movement, particularly in technique."

**Translation for force reduction:**

**Poor force reduction = wasted energy:**
- Slapping hand entry = lost momentum
- Uncontrolled rotation = power leak
- Bouncing off turn wall = slower push-off

**Quality force reduction = free speed:**
- Smooth catch entry = more force on pull
- Controlled rotation = explosive hip drive
- Perfect turn wall contact = maximum push-off

**The margin:** Elite swimmers control force reduction so well that they extract 1-2% more efficiency PER STROKE.

Over 100 strokes in a race, that's the difference between gold and 5th place.

---

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Force reduction (eccentric) > Force production (concentric)** for injury prevention  
✅ **Most injuries occur during force reduction phase** (not force production)  
✅ **A loaded muscle produces MORE force** (stretch-shortening cycle)  
✅ **Control = efficiency** (smooth movement, no energy leaks)  
✅ **Train eccentrics deliberately:** tempo work, Nordic curls, controlled landings  
✅ **In-water application:** soft entries, controlled turns, loaded pullouts  
✅ **Recovery matters:** Eccentric work causes more soreness, plan accordingly
