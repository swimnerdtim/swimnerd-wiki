# Starts, Turns, and Finishes: Montgomery's Competition Essentials
**Source:** *Mastering Swimming* by Jim Montgomery & Mo Chambers, Chapter 8 (2009)  
**Context:** Comprehensive guide to maximizing pool performance at walls  
**Audience:** Competitive masters swimmers, workout swimmers improving turns, coaches

---

## The 30% Rule

> "In a 25-yard (or 25m) pool, approximately **one-third of a competitive swimmer's time** is spent moving from the flags to the wall and back to the flags."

**Therefore:** Swimmers who polish starts, turns, finishes gain significant advantage.

**Benefit for non-competitors:** More efficient turns = improved workout quality, faster swimming, better momentum maintenance.

---

## Core Philosophy: Maintaining Momentum

### Why Swimmers Are Different

**Unlike running, cycling, speed skating:**
- Other athletes strive for maximum speed in first moments
- Swimmers reach top speed in **hundredths of second** diving from starting block

**Main objectives of start:**
1. Transition dive speed into water
2. Carry momentum into swimming speed

**Elements affecting start quality:**
- Body position in air
- Entry into water
- Quality of underwater streamline
- Timing of initial kicks and first strokes

### The Push-Off Principle

**After turn:** Explosive leg drive + tight torpedolike streamline = **Faster than swimming on surface**

**Seamless transition requires:**
- Maintain momentum of push-off
- Hold underwater streamline
- Break out smoothly into stroke rhythm

---

## STARTING PROTOCOL (Universal)

**Consistency = Mental Freedom**

Same procedure age-group → masters → Olympics  
Knowing what to expect → Focus on race strategy

### The Sequence

**1. Short Whistles**
Deck referee signals swimmers to ready:
- Remove T-shirts, sweats
- Final adjustments to caps, goggles
- Give attention to referee
- May announce event and heat

**2. Long Whistle**
Signals step onto starting block:
- Place one or both feet at front of block
- **Option:** Position adjacent to block with feet at pool edge
- **Or:** Enter water feet-first (with referee approval), place one hand on edge

**Backstroke specific:**
- All swimmers enter water at first long whistle
- Second long whistle signals positioning at block

**3. "Take Your Mark"**
Starter takes control:
- Swimmers move into starting position
- Await starting signal

**4. Starting Signal**
- Whistle
- Gun
- Electronic beep (most common)

**Race begins.**

## FORWARD START (Freestyle, Breaststroke, Butterfly)

### Safety First!

**Requirements:**
- Pool specifically designed for competitive springboard diving
- Depth approved for competitive diving
- Minimum 15 meters unobstructed water
- Dive away from sidewalls and shallow areas
- Starting blocks secured with nonskid surface
- Direct supervision by coach or lifeguard
- 6 feet (2m) depth MINIMUM

**Warning:** "Diving into shallow water is dangerous and may result in permanent spinal cord injury or possible death."

### Learning Progression (Step-by-Step)

#### Step 1: Perfect the Streamline

**On pool deck or shallow water:**
1. Place one hand over other
2. **Two squeezes:**
   - Thumb of top hand around pinkie side of bottom hand
   - Ears squeezed between biceps
3. Align spine:
   - Move chin away from chest
   - Contract abdomen and buttocks
   - Feet together
4. Reach fingertips to sky
5. Have partner help with alignment
6. Raise shoulders toward ears (further streamline)

**Lie down on deck** with toes pointed to perfect position.

#### Step 2: Dive From Pool Deck

**Practice in deep water:**
- Maintain streamlined body position throughout dive
- Imagine whole body entering through **single hole** created by fingertips
- Visualize diving through hula hoop on water surface without touching sides

**Progression:**
- Move imaginary hula hoop farther as confidence grows
- Resist temptation to look up (increases frontal resistance, hinders momentum)
- If goggles leak/slip: Tighten them

**Focus:**
- Hands together
- Body streamlined
- Head and neck aligned with spine

#### Step 3: Standing Broad Jump (From Block)

**Position:**
- Feet almost shoulder-width apart
- Toes just over front edge

**Action:**
- Jump from blocks into water feet first
- Maximize distance using hip and leg muscles
- Swing arms to increase momentum
- Explode off blocks

#### Step 4: Swinging Dive

**Position:** Front of starting block (same as Step 3)

**Action:**
1. Imagine hula hoop on water surface in front
2. Swing arms up, forward, around (create momentum)
3. Dive from block
4. Pierce center of hoop with fingertips
5. Keep body tight and streamlined
6. Body follows fingertips through center of hoop

#### Step 5: Transition to Breakout

**Build on swinging dive:**
- Maintain underwater streamlined position until breakout
- **Breakout** = Transition from underwater phase to full-stroke swimming
- When done well: Carries momentum seamlessly into stroke rhythm

See specific breakout sections for stroke details.

#### Step 6: Take Your Mark (Track Start)

**Why track start?**
- Fast
- Suitable for limited flexibility
- More stability
- **Recommended for new swimmers**

**Alternative:** Grab start (both feet front, grab block inside or outside feet) - Experienced swimmers may prefer traditional method.

**Track Start Position (Preferred):**

**At long whistle:**
1. Step onto block
2. Position at front edge:
   - Feet almost shoulder-width apart
   - Toes over edge
   - Eyes focused down pool

**At "Take Your Mark":**
1. Move one foot back on block
2. Bend down
3. Grab underside of block with both hands
4. Keep elbows slightly bent, arms tensed
5. Pull back from block for balance
6. **Back leg:** Bend to at least 90°, lift heel slightly
7. **Front leg:** Bend slightly
8. Draw weight forward (shoulders and arms)
9. **Center of gravity:** Held over ball of front foot
10. Head down (don't rest chest on legs)

**Variations (Flexibility/Preference):**
- Back foot farther back, hips lower (limited flexibility)
- Hands free for relay-style start (some prefer)
- Weight neutral or leaning back over back foot (preference)

**Practice:** Hold position for at least 2 seconds. Experiment with foot and hand positioning.

#### Step 7: GO!

**At starting beep:**
1. Pull body forward slightly (using underside of block)
2. Shifts center of gravity over water
3. Feel yourself falling forward
4. Push with legs
5. Body unfolds over water
6. Assume streamlined position
7. Imagine whole body shooting through narrow tunnel of air
8. Into hole in center of hula hoop

**Key:** Become comfortable with ENTIRE sequence (commands → breakout)

**Practice approach:**
1. Review steps slowly
2. Become comfortable
3. Add power to leg drive
4. Visualize exploding from starting position

## BACKSTROKE START

### Similarities to Forward Start

**Same hallmarks:**
- Explosive leg drive
- Streamlined body position
- Movement through single hole in water
- Streamlined underwater kicking
- Breakout strokes

### Skills Progression

**Perform in 6+ feet (2m) depth**

**Skills 1-5:** Hold pool edge or gutter  
**Skills 6-7:** Use starting block handles

#### Skill 1: Ready Position

**Setup:**
1. In pool
2. Hold pool edge/gutter with both hands
3. Position feet on wall:
   - Shoulder-width apart
   - Just under water surface
   - Most prefer staggered feet (choose comfortable position)
4. Head and neck aligned with spine
5. Knees bent to 90° (sitting position)

#### Skill 2: L-Seats

**From ready position:**
1. Drive with legs
2. Keep arms at sides
3. Shoot up and over water
4. Form L shape with body in air
5. Land buttocks first in pool

**Focus:** Leg thrust that gets you up, out, over water

#### Skill 3: L-Seats With Leg Kick

**Repeat Skill 2 + Add:**
- Upward kick with both feet as you enter water

#### Skill 4: Streamline the Entry

**Repeat Skill 3 + Add arm motion:**

**As you drive legs and lift hips:**
- Throw arms back to sides OR over head
- Into streamlined position
- Hands enter water first (create single hole)
- Maintain streamlined position
- Whole body follows fingertips through hole

**If landing on back or dragging:** Focus on:
- Driving legs
- Arching back
- Thrusting legs up before entry

**Underwater phase:**
- Maintain streamline
- Feel yourself losing momentum
- Begin underwater dolphin kick OR flutter kick
- Surface for breakout
- **High-amplitude underwater dolphin** = "Double-leg kick"

**Breathing tip:** Blow water out of nose (keep nose clear on back underwater)

#### Skill 5: Take Your Mark

**Move to starting block when comfortable**

**Position:**
1. Hands on handle of starting block
2. Feet shoulder-width apart on pool wall
3. **USS and USMS rules:** Feet completely submerged
4. Stay relaxed and focused

**At "Take Your Mark":**
- Pull head closer to block
- Bring knees into 90° position
- Buttocks held away from wall

#### Skill 6: GO!

**At starting signal:**
1. Drive with legs
2. Lift hips and body up, out, over water
3. Throw arms back into streamline
4. **Ideal:** Body arched over water
5. Poised for clean entry through single hole created by fingertips

**Practice drill:** Place soft kickboard or pool noodle behind you, practice getting buttocks over board.

---

### Finding What Works

**Experiment with:**
- Foot positioning
- Hand positioning

**Some swimmers:** More success using pool edge/gutter than starting block

**Challenge:** Learning to arch body over water (most swimmers master with time invested in progression)

## RELAY START

### Why It's Exciting

**Most exciting portion of meet:**
- Competitors AND spectators
- Critical timing of relay exchanges
- Close races become more exciting
- Minimize exchange time

### Roles in Exchange

**Two swimmers, different responsibilities:**

**Outgoing swimmer:**
- Time movements to leave block exact moment incoming swimmer touches
- Gain momentum from arm swing
- Begin movement BEFORE incoming touch (unlike forward start)

**Incoming swimmer:**
- Maintain stroke tempo and rhythm until finish
- Any deceleration → Early/false start → Disqualification

### Position in Relay

**First swimmer:**
- Forward start (freestyle relay)
- In-water backstroke start (medley relay)

**Swimmers 2, 3, 4:**
- Relay start (faster than forward start)

### The Rhythm Factor

> "Rhythm and timing go hand in hand in successful relay exchanges."

**Key:** Outgoing swimmer synchronized movements to incoming swimmer's stroke tempo and speed.

**Problem:** Incoming swimmer deceleration → Early start → DQ

**Solution:** Practice makes timing.

### Skills Progression

**Focus:** Falling into rhythm of movements.

**Result:**
- More timely exchange
- Gain momentum and power off block

#### Skill 1: Get Into Rhythm (On Deck)

**Away from pool:**
1. Stand straight up
2. One foot forward, one back
3. Bring back foot forward
4. Swing arms forward, up, around
5. Use arm momentum to jump straight up
6. Jump uses both feet (standing broad jump feel)

**Maximize height:**
- Bend knees as you swing hands
- Lower body
- Feet little less than shoulder-width for takeoff
- **Don't** bend knees past 90°

**Sequence:** Step, swing, squat, jump  
**Repeat** until comfortable with timing and rhythm

#### Skill 2: Off the Blocks

**At front of block:**
1. Toes of one foot just over edge
2. Other foot back

**Diving sequence (same as deck):**
- Use arm swing momentum
- Move center of gravity forward over water
- Leave block
- Maintain arm swing momentum
- Throw arms forward
- Fingers pointing at entry spot

#### Skill 3: Streamline Your Entry

**Clean entry with minimum frontal resistance:**
- Streamline body
- Body follows fingertips through hole created in water

**Experiment:**
- Timing of knee bend
- Depth of knee bend
- Maximize power

**Keys:**
- Keep shoulders low
- Momentum moving forward
- Resist temptation to stand up

#### Skill 4: Time the Start

**Once comfortable with rhythm:**

**Synchronize with incoming swimmers:**
1. Stretch arms out in front
2. Follow incoming swimmer with fingertips
3. Allow body to drop lower as they move closer

**Timing by stroke:**
- **Freestyle/Backstroke:** Begin step-and-swing as incoming swimmer begins final stroke to wall
- **Butterfly/Breaststroke:** More difficult to time finish (swimmers more prone to mistiming)

**If uncertain about incoming swimmer's rhythm:** Conservative relay exchange recommended.

### Team Responsibility

**Relays = TRUE team events**

Every swimmer contributes to exchange success:
1. **Incoming swimmer:** Consistent stroke rhythm to finish
2. **Outgoing swimmer:** Study incoming rhythm, time perfectly
3. **Team:** Practice together when possible

**Reality for masters:** Can't always afford extended practice time → Choose more conservative exchanges.

## TURNS: The Four Phases

### Beyond Just the Wall

**Turn = More than what happens at wall**

**Four critical phases:**
1. **The approach**
2. **The turn itself**
3. **The push-off and streamline**
4. **The breakout**

**All four phases critical for maintaining momentum.**

### Learning Approach

**First:** Focus on technical aspects of each phase (slower speeds)  
**Then:** Increase turning speed

**Why:** Mastering mechanics at slower speeds → Individual phases blend together smoothly.

## FREESTYLE FLIP TURN

### Why Learn It?

**Benefits:**
- Raises workout quality
- Improves momentum to/from walls
- Less shoulder stress than open turn (touch-and-go)
- Faster overall

**With consistent practice:** Master the flip, improve speed and flow.

### Flip Turn Progression

**Become comfortable with each skill before next.**

#### Skill 1: Bubbles

**Essential:** Must expel air through nostrils during flip turn OR water goes up nose.

**Practice:** Get comfortable blowing bubbles out nose while underwater.

#### Skill 2: Somersault

**Purpose:** Comfortable with flipping part

**Remember:** Blow bubbles from nose as body flips

**How to:**
1. Lie on water surface, face down
2. Arms at sides, palms facing pool bottom
3. Tuck into ball:
   - Nose toward knees
   - Heels toward buttocks
4. Sweep arms over head in large, powerful circle
   - Like pushing tall stovepipe hat off head
5. Keep elbows in front of face (where you can see them)
6. Flip completely over
7. End: Lying on back, fingertips pointed same direction you started

**If trouble completing flips:**
- Probably rolling to side during somersault
- Prevents hips from going straight over shoulders
- **Fix:** Focus on aligning head, neck, spine

**Alternative:** Hold pull buoy or kickboard in each hand on surface during flip (gives body sensation of coming over top)

#### Skill 3: Approach to Midpool (NOT at wall yet)

**Adds approach strokes to flip**

**Process:**
1. Swim 4-5 strokes
2. Into head-lead position
   - Arms at sides
   - Palms facing down
3. Press chest slightly under water
4. Do the flip from Skill 2

**Common error:** Rushing into flip before both hands back at sides

**Must be:**
- Submerged position at flip start
- Arms at sides
- Palms down
- Chest pressed down slightly

#### Skill 4: Approach to Midpool + Streamline

**Also away from wall + Adds streamline**

**Process:**
1. Swim 4-5 strokes
2. Head-lead position (arms at sides, palms down)
3. Submerge
4. Flip
5. Move arms into streamline (after knocking off stovepipe hat)

**Finish:** Lying on back with arms in tight streamline

**Repeat several times:** Perfect streamlined finish
- Align head, neck, spine
- Engage core muscles

#### Skill 5: Add the Wall

**Goal:** Learn to judge when to initiate flip

**Target:** When coming through flip into streamline:
- Both feet firmly planted on wall
- Knees bent to 90°
- Poised for effective push-off

**Process:**
1. Start past backstroke flags
2. Swim toward wall
3. Use underwater markings on wall to judge distance
4. Move into head-lead on final stroke
5. Submerge
6. Flip into streamline
7. Plant feet on wall

**Learning curve:**
- Requires trial and error
- Consistent practice
- Swimmers make all kinds of mistakes:
  - Come up in next lane
  - Miss wall completely
  - Hit buttocks on wall

**With practice:** All swimmers can successfully master flip turn.

#### Skill 6: Approach, Flip, Streamline + PUSH-OFF

**Once comfortable judging distance:**

**Add the push-off:**
1. Approach wall
2. Submerge chest
3. Flip into streamline
4. Drive with legs
5. Rotate body to side

**Critical moment:** Between foot plant and push-off
- Slow to streamline → Lose momentum
- Body drops lower
- Resulting push-off weak and often too deep

**Think:** "Pool wall is hot stove"
- Quick touch-and-go
- Fast transition: flip → streamline → push-off

#### Skill 7: Complete Turn (Approach, Flip, Streamline, Push-off, BREAK OUT)

**Final step:** Rotation to abdomen

**Process:**
1. Approach wall
2. Submerge into flip
3. Transition to streamline
4. Push-off
5. Rotate to side
6. **Finally:** Rotate onto front

**Add kicks (as you rotate):**
- Deep dolphin OR freestyle kicks
- Maintain momentum from push-off
- Type and amount depend on:
  - Your strengths
  - Chosen distance

**Sprinters with strong dolphin:** Maximize underwater time (several dolphin kicks)  
**Distance swimmers with weak kicks:** Better to swim on surface

**As nearing surface:**
- Keep stroke long
- Maintain strong body line
- First two strokes WITHOUT breathing
- Settle into stroke rhythm

---

### Flip Turn Tips & Ideas

**Practice opportunities:** EVERY wall approach (flip instead of open turn)

**Gradual integration:**
- Start flipping only one end of pool
- Gradually build to flipping all turns

**Focus on phases separately:** Link together slowly into faster, more elegant turn.

**Additional tips:**

✅ **Maintain/increase speed approaching wall** (Don't slow down)  
✅ **Keep head down** (Don't take final breath or lift head)  
✅ **Eyes on underwater target**  
✅ **Don't roll to side approaching wall**  
✅ **Press chest down** to lift hips, spring heels toward wall  
✅ **"Rush hips to wall"** (engage abdominal muscles)  
✅ **Sweep hands toward head BEFORE feet hit wall** (elbows close together, hands into streamline early)  
✅ **Positioned to push off moment feet contact wall**  
✅ **Start kicking** as soon as rotated onto front (5-6 feet from wall)  
✅ **Maintain momentum** by kicking through breakout into swimming  

❌ **Don't bring hands/arms down or outside body line** (delays turn, must realign before push-off)  
❌ **If push-off too deep** (feel like submarine): Starting flip too close to wall, check for 90° knee bend at foot plant

## BACKSTROKE FLIP TURN

### Once You've Mastered Freestyle Flip

**Easy transition to backstroke turn**

**Difference:** Only in approach and breakout phases

**Skills progression:** Focuses mainly on these two phases + carrying stroke rhythm through flip and streamline.

### Skills Progression

#### Skill 1: Begin Approach Midpool (NOT at wall)

**Start in middle of pool:**
1. Swim backstroke
   - Left arm up
   - Right arm at side
2. Count hand hits (hand entries) as you go
3. On fifth stroke:
   - Move right arm across body over left shoulder
   - Roll onto front
   - Pull through into head-lead position of freestyle flip turn

**Practice:** Several times with BOTH right and left arms (comfortable crossing over and rolling to either side)

#### Skill 2: Know Your Finishing Stroke Count

**From backstroke flags to wall:**
- Count your strokes
- Begin swimming several strokes outside flags
- Reach normal stroke tempo when passing under flags
- Begin counting

#### Skill 3: Transition From Approach to Flip

**Process:**
1. Position outside flags
2. Swim toward wall
3. Count strokes as you pass under flags
4. Begin crossover **TWO strokes BEFORE wall**
   - Example: Stroke count = 5 strokes
   - Begin crossover and roll stroke after 3 strokes
5. Once on front and pulling through (arm that crossed over)
6. Begin flip
7. Plant feet on wall

**Check position:**
- 90° knee bend at foot plant
- Streamlined upper body
- Like freestyle flip turn

**Practice:** Stroking into wall and making flip while crossing over to BOTH left and right sides.

#### Skill 4: Transition From Streamline to Breakout

**Before combining with flip:**

**Practice alone:**
1. Push off center of wall
2. Extend arms and legs (tight streamlined position)
3. Remember: Exhale through nose
4. Choice of kicks:
   - Traditional backstroke kick OR
   - Dolphin kick
5. Maintain momentum of push-off
6. Begin first breakout stroke when head ~1 foot (3m) below surface
7. Follow with second stroke just as head about to break surface
8. Reach surface:
   - Keep kick steady
   - Maintain long body line
   - Settle into stroke rhythm

**Competition rule:** Head must break surface within 15 meters of wall (typically marked on lane lines)

**Experiment:** Find ideal amount of kicking

**Strong dolphin kickers:** 3+ underwater dolphin kicks before two breakout strokes  
**Weaker kicks:** Transition into backstroke kick more quickly

#### Skill 5: Complete Backstroke Turn (Approach, Flip, Streamline, Break Out)

**Adds breakout to complete turn**

**As in freestyle flip turn:**
- Focus on maintaining momentum into flip
- Exploding off wall
- Through underwater phase
- Breakout
- Back to swimming

**Resist temptation:**
❌ Drop chin  
❌ Lift head to look around  

**Why:** Breaking head alignment:
- Slows momentum
- Disrupts stroke count consistency during approach
- Increases resistance leaving wall

## OPEN TURNS (Breaststroke & Butterfly)

### Why Different?

**Rules requirement:**
- Breaststroke and butterfly: Must touch wall with BOTH hands simultaneously
- Cannot use traditional flip turns (feet touch first)
- Use open turn (nonflip turn)

**Same success factors:**
- Maintain stroke rhythm and momentum
- Reaching wall
- Throughout turn, streamline, breakout

### Skills Progression

**First steps:** Ready position (swimmer's position on wall preparing to push off)

**Important:** Leave wall from this position for EVERY repetition in workout
- Transitions smoothly into streamlined push-off
- Reinforces mechanics of good short-axis turns

#### Skill 1: Assume Ready Position

**Setup:**
1. Extend wall-side arm, hold pool edge/gutter
2. Extend other arm underwater toward opposite end (palm facing up)
3. Plant balls of feet on wall
4. Knees flexed but relaxed
5. Chin over wall-side shoulder
6. Look down arm at wall

**Visualize:** All body parts between two hands connected in single line:
- Hand on wall
- Elbow
- Chin
- Shoulder
- Elbow in water
- Other hand

#### Skill 2: Push Off and Streamline

**From ready position:**
1. Move hand on wall toward ear ("answering phone")
   - Think: "Call the police"
2. Body drops underwater
3. Slide hand past ear
4. Over head
5. Toward outstretched hand
6. Form streamlined position

**Then:**
- Drive with legs
- Push off wall

**Practice:** Transition from ready position → streamline → push-off until comfortable

**Challenge yourself:** See how much distance you gain moving from wall.

#### Skill 3: Approach

**Swim toward wall:**
- Maintain stroke rhythm and speed
- Getting close: Keep head in line with spine
- Extend arms, reach for wall
- **Ideal:** Body stretched out on water surface completing kick as hands touch wall

**With practice:** Judge distance several strokes away, adjust stroke length to achieve optimum body position at every wall (including finish).

#### Skill 4: Approach to Ready Position

**Process:**
1. Swim to wall
2. Touch with arms extended
3. Begin turn: Draw one arm back (leading with elbow) - "Elbow the robber"
4. Same time: Bring knees up toward chest
5. Position feet on wall (pointing either to side or diagonally up)

**Keys:**
- Maintain momentum and speed
- Make body small as you spin
- Keep center of gravity low
- Think: "Call the police"
- Move into ready position:
  - Turn palm up
  - Fully extend arm toward opposite end

**Practice:** Several times, FREEZE when reaching ready position.

#### Skill 5: Approach to Turn to Push Off

**Complete two-hand touch turn sequence:**

**Cues to connect pieces:**
1. Swim to wall
2. Touch with two hands
3. Pull knees up, elbow back ("Elbow the robber")
4. Move wall-side hand up ("Call the police")
5. Streamline
6. Push off

---

### BUTTERFLY BREAKOUTS

**From ready position:**
1. Push-off and streamline
2. Begin dolphin kick
3. Maintain tight streamline underwater
4. Build speed
5. Aggressive kick on breakout stroke (just before back breaks surface)
6. Keep head in line with spine on first stroke
7. Begin breathing in rhythm of stroke

**Competition rule:** Must break surface within 15 meters of wall.

---

### BREASTSTROKE BREAKOUTS (The Pull-Out)

**Competition allowance:** One pull, one breaststroke kick, one dolphin kick underwater after start and after each turn.

**Rule:** Head must break water surface before beginning sweep-in of next arm pull.

**Commonly called:** Breaststroke pull-out

**Purpose:** Smooth transition from wall into stroke rhythm.

#### The Pull-Out Sequence

**1. Push-off from ready position** (tight streamline)

**2. As momentum slowing:**
- Begin underwater arm pull (resembles butterfly pull more than ordinary breaststroke pull)
- Press/sweep arms and hands out (point slightly greater than shoulderwidth)
- Palms angled toward pool walls
- Bend elbows to 90°
- Open armpits
- Reach deep into water with fingertips
- Catch water with hands and arms

**3. From catch position:**
- Sweep hands in and down toward feet
- Maintain high-elbow position
- Straight body line

**4. After completing pull:**
- Ride out momentum in head-lead streamlined position

**5. When speed decreases:**
- Sneak arms up and over head (keep close to body)
- Lift heels to begin breaststroke kick

**6. Fire the kick:**
- Hands should be streamlined
- Body approaching surface
- **Resist:** Lifting head up for air (will drop hips, slow momentum)
- **Instead:** Keep head in line with spine, begin breathing with stroke rhythm

#### Adding the Dolphin Kick

**Once comfortable with traditional pull-out:**
- Add dolphin kick to sequence as completing underwater arm pull
- Adds underwater speed while in head-lead position

**Key to success:** Properly timing pull, arm recovery, kicks

**How:** Ride out momentum from each propulsive movement before beginning next → Reduce resistance, maintain better wall speed and momentum.

---

### Learning Curve

**Like flip turn:** Open turns with two-hand touch take time and practice.

**First:** Focus on mechanics  
**Gradually:** Add speed as comfortable

**Common bad habit:** One-hand-touch turns for breaststroke/butterfly during workouts
- Quicker, take less effort
- **BUT:** Not legal in competition → DQ
- **Recommendation:** Complete ALL turns legally in workouts → Every wall is opportunity to fine-tune

## INDIVIDUAL MEDLEY TRANSITIONAL TURNS

**Once mastered flip turns and open turns:** Easily progress to IM turns.

**IM order (always):** Butterfly → Backstroke → Breaststroke → Freestyle

**Requires 3 specific transitional turns.**

### Butterfly to Backstroke

**Process:**
1. Swim butterfly to wall
2. Finish with full stroke
3. Two-hand touch
4. Drive one elbow back ("Elbow the robber")
5. Draw knees up
6. Plant feet on wall (facing up)
7. Bring other arm over ("Call the police")
8. Push off on back (tight streamline)
9. Complete normal underwater-backstroke-kick sequence
10. Breakout

### Backstroke to Breaststroke

**Process:**
1. Approach wall swimming backstroke
2. Mark full stroke count from flags to finish
3. Reach wall: Touch with hand
4. Rotate slightly onto side
5. Pull knees up and around
6. Plant feet on wall
7. Move chin from one shoulder to other
8. **Result:** Ready position
9. Push off, streamline
10. Complete breaststroke pullout
11. Breakout stroke

### Breaststroke to Freestyle

**Essentially same as breaststroke-to-breaststroke turn**

**BUT:** Follow push-off and streamline with:
- Underwater kicking sequence for freestyle (not breaststroke pull-out)
- Breakout for freestyle

---

### The IM Challenge

**Most challenging:** Transitioning between stroke rhythms
- Rhythm of stroke approaching wall
- Rhythm of stroke departing from wall

**USMS competitions:**
- 100-, 200-, 400-yard/meter IM events
- Truly tests ability to make stroke transitions
- Minimize wall time (approach, at wall, streamline, breakout)

**Practice:** Mix transitional turns into workouts → Improve transitions, add variety.

## FINISHES

### Why Coaches Care

**Even for non-competitors:** Quality finishes benefit every repetition.

**Problems:**
- Decelerate into wall → Cheating self of full distance benefits
- Stop short → Prevent swimmers behind from finishing

### Great Finishes (Workouts & Races)

#### Single-Hand Touches (Freestyle & Backstroke)

✅ **Stretch out approaching wall** (head aligned, body streamlined, minimize resistance)  
✅ **Maintain momentum** by extending forward, keeping head down  
✅ **Don't breathe between flags and finish** (maintain stroke tempo and speed)  
✅ **Hit wall fully extended and rotated onto side** (extended arm tight against ear)  
✅ **Avoid finishing with half stroke**  
✅ **Practice leading with BOTH right and left arms** (either side feels comfortable in race)  
✅ **Backstroke:** Know stroke count from flags to wall  
✅ **Backstroke option:** Half-dolphin kick with final stroke (finish faster and slightly deeper - 8-10 inches [20-25cm] max)  

#### Two-Hand Touches (Breaststroke & Butterfly)

✅ **Prepare well in advance** (set up before reaching flags)  
✅ **Adjust strokes** so can carry momentum of final stroke to wall  
✅ **Finish fully extended**  
✅ **Keep head in line with spine** (maintain forward momentum)  
✅ **Drive fingertips into wall under water line**

## Walls as Opportunities

**Whether workout or competition:**

**Every approach:**
- Chance to gain speed with tighter streamline or more aggressive push-off
- Refine final strokes to wall
- Perfect amount of strokes needed to carry momentum into turn

**Every flip or spin:**
- Tidy up mechanics
- Reach ready position little more quickly

**Every finish:**
- Nail the wall for victory

**Remember:** Spend 30% of pool time approaching and departing from walls → Gain lot of distance by perfecting each phase of turns.

## Assessment Checklist

**Self-evaluate your starts, turns, finishes:**

**FREESTYLE FLIP TURN**
□ Comfortable exhaling through nose during flip  
□ Can complete somersault consistently  
□ Judging distance well (feet plant at 90°)  
□ Quick touch-and-go at wall  
□ Tight streamline off wall  
□ Smooth rotation to front  
□ Maintaining momentum through breakout  

**BACKSTROKE TURN**
□ Know stroke count from flags  
□ Comfortable crossing over to flip  
□ Feet planting correctly  
□ Streamlined push-off on back  
□ Effective underwater kicking  
□ Head breaks surface before 15m  

**OPEN TURNS (Breast/Fly)**
□ Two-hand touch every time  
□ Quick transition to ready position  
□ Explosive push-off  
□ Tight streamline underwater  
□ Proper stroke-specific breakout  
□ No one-hand touches in practice  

**STARTS**
□ Comfortable with starting protocol  
□ Streamline position perfected  
□ Explosive leg drive  
□ Clean entry (minimal splash)  
□ Maintaining momentum underwater  
□ Smooth transition to swimming  

**FINISHES**
□ Accelerate or maintain speed into wall  
□ No breathing last 4-5 strokes  
□ Fully extended at touch  
□ Hit wall under water line  
□ No half-stroke finishes
