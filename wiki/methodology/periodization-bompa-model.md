# Periodization: The Bompa Model for Swimming

**The Six-Phase System That Builds Championship Strength — From Foundation to Peak Performance**

---

## What Is Periodization?

**Periodization** = Structuring training into phases, each with specific objectives that build toward peak performance.

> "Peak performance occurs because you plan for it!"  
> — Tudor Bompa, *Periodization Training for Sports*

**Why swimmers need periodization:**
- **Non-periodized training** = strength gained in September is lost by NCAAs
- **Random strength workouts** = random results
- **No peak** = year-round mediocrity instead of championship performance
- **Injury risk** = jumping straight to heavy lifting without anatomical preparation

---

## The Six Phases of Strength Training

Bompa's periodization model uses **six phases** that build sequentially. For swimming, you'll use 3-5 of these phases depending on your event and training age.

### Phase 1: Anatomical Adaptation (AA)
**Goal:** Prehabilitation (prevent injury before it happens)

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks (early off-season)

**Training focus:**
- High volume (3-4x/week)
- Low loads (12-20 reps per set)
- General strength (all muscle groups)
- Strengthen tendons, ligaments, joints

**Swimming-specific application:**
- Build foundation for heavier phases
- Increase bone density and connective tissue strength
- Improve oxidative capacity of slow-twitch fibers
- **Example:** 3 sets × 15 reps lat pulldowns, 3 sets × 20 push-ups, core work

**Key insight:** You **cannot** skip this phase. Jumping straight to heavy weights = injury. AA prepares your body's infrastructure (tendons, ligaments) for the force demands ahead.

---

### Phase 2: Hypertrophy (H)
**Goal:** Increase muscle cross-sectional area (bigger muscles = more force potential)

**Duration:** 3-4 weeks (mid off-season)

**Training focus:**
- Moderate-high volume (3-4x/week)
- Moderate loads (8-12 reps to near-exhaustion)
- Bodybuilding-style sets with controlled tempo

**Swimming-specific application:**
- **Sprint swimmers (50-100):** Use this phase to build muscle size
- **Distance swimmers (800-1500):** Skip or shorten this phase (muscle mass = drag)
- **Mid-distance (200-400):** Use strategically if starting from low muscle mass

**Key difference from bodybuilding:** Athletes use **heavier loads** and **faster movement speed** than bodybuilders. Focus on **movement speed**, not just exhaustion.

**Example workout:**
- 4 sets × 10 reps bench press @ 70% 1RM
- 4 sets × 10 reps lat pulldowns @ 70% 1RM
- 3 sets × 12 reps medicine ball slams

**Warning:** Not all swimmers need hypertrophy. Bob Bowman rarely uses heavy hypertrophy for distance swimmers. More muscle = more oxygen demand + more drag.

---

### Phase 3: Maximum Strength (MS)
**Goal:** Develop absolute force capacity (the foundation of ALL power and muscular endurance)

**Duration:** 3-4 weeks (late off-season / early pre-season)

**Training focus:**
- Moderate-low volume (2-3x/week)
- Very high loads (3-6 reps @ 85-95% 1RM)
- Long rest (3-5 minutes between sets)
- **Neural adaptation** (not muscle growth)

**Swimming-specific application:**
- **ALL swimmers benefit** — even distance swimmers
- Builds "strength reserve" (the gap between max strength and race demands)
- Improves motor unit recruitment and synchronization

**Example workout:**
- 5 sets × 4 reps bench press @ 88% 1RM (rest 4 min)
- 5 sets × 5 reps lat pulldowns @ 85% 1RM (rest 4 min)
- 4 sets × 6 reps squats @ 85% 1RM (rest 3 min)

**Why it matters:** A swimmer with a **higher strength reserve** can maintain stroke power longer. Maximum strength is the engine — power and endurance are the transmission.

> **Study:** Cross-country skiers who did maximum strength training (without endurance training) improved **time to exhaustion** by increasing work economy (Hoff, Gran, Helgerud, 2002).

**Relative strength matters:** For swimmers, the ratio of strength to body weight is crucial. Gymnasts can't do an iron cross unless relative strength ≥ 1:1 (absolute strength equals body weight).

---

### Phase 4: Conversion (C)
**Goal:** Convert maximum strength into sport-specific abilities (power, power-endurance, or muscular endurance)

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks (pre-competition phase)

**Three conversion paths for swimmers:**

#### **Path 1: Power (Sprinters 50-100m)**
- **Method:** Explosive movements, ballistic lifts, plyometrics
- **Load:** 65-85% 1RM, move weight as fast as possible
- **Reps:** 6-12 per set
- **Rest:** 2-3 minutes
- **Example:** 6 sets × 8 explosive bench throws @ 70% 1RM

#### **Path 2: Power-Endurance (200-400m)**
- **Method:** Moderate loads, short rest, sustained speed
- **Load:** 60-75% 1RM
- **Reps:** 8-15 per set
- **Rest:** 1-2 minutes
- **Example:** 4 sets × 12 lat pulls @ 70% 1RM, 90s rest

#### **Path 3: Muscular Endurance (800-1500m)**
- **Method:** Light loads, high reps, short rest, circuit training
- **Load:** 40-60% 1RM
- **Reps:** 15-50 per set (yes, up to 400 reps total!)
- **Rest:** 30-60 seconds
- **Example:** Circuit — 3 rounds, 1 min rest:
  - Pull-ups: 12 reps
  - Med ball slam: 15 reps
  - Plank: 60s
  - Cable row: 15 reps @ 60% 1RM
  - Squat jumps: 10 reps

**Energy system match:** Muscular endurance training **must** match the aerobic/lactic demands of distance swimming. You're training the neuromuscular system to work in sync with the cardiovascular system.

---

### Phase 5: Competitive (CP)
**Goal:** Maintain strength gains during racing season

**Duration:** Variable (entire competition season)

**Training focus:**
- Low volume (1-2x/week)
- Moderate intensity (6-8 reps @ 70-80% 1RM)
- **Maintenance only** — just enough stimulus to keep gains

**Swimming-specific application:**
- **Biggest mistake:** Stopping strength training during taper/season
- **Reality:** Strength decays in 2-3 weeks without stimulus
- **Solution:** 1-2 short strength sessions per week during season

**Taper strength work:**
- Reduce volume 40-60% two weeks before major meet
- **Keep intensity high** (still lift 75-80% 1RM)
- Example: Instead of 4 sets × 8 reps, do 2 sets × 6 reps @ same weight

**Key principle:** Staying strong is easier than rebuilding strength mid-season.

---

### Phase 6: Transition (T)
**Goal:** Active recovery, regeneration, prevent overtraining

**Duration:** 2-4 weeks (post-season)

**Training focus:**
- Light cross-training (hiking, yoga, paddle boarding)
- Mental reset
- Address lingering injuries
- **No structured strength training**

**Swimming-specific application:**
- Take 2-4 weeks completely off after championship season
- Stay active but unstructured
- Come back fresh for next cycle

## Periodization for Different Swimming Events

### **Sprinters (50-100m)**
**Energy system:** Anaerobic alactic (ATP-CP) + anaerobic lactic  
**Strength focus:** Maximum strength → Power  
**Annual plan:**

| Phase | Duration | Strength Focus |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| AA | 6 weeks (Sep-Oct) | 3 sets × 15-20 reps, general strength |
| MS | 4 weeks (Nov) | 5 sets × 4-6 reps @ 85-95% 1RM |
| Power | 6 weeks (Dec-Jan) | Explosive lifts, plyometrics, 6-12 reps |
| Competitive | Feb-Mar | 2 sets × 6 reps @ 75% 1RM, 1-2x/week |

**Key exercises:** Bench press, lat pulldowns, medicine ball throws, reactive jumps (for starts/turns)

---

### **Mid-Distance (200-400m)**
**Energy system:** Anaerobic lactic + aerobic threshold  
**Strength focus:** Maximum strength → Power-Endurance  
**Annual plan:**

| Phase | Duration | Strength Focus |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| AA | 6 weeks (Sep-Oct) | 3 sets × 15-20 reps |
| MS | 3 weeks (Nov) | 4 sets × 5 reps @ 85% 1RM |
| Power-Endurance | 6 weeks (Dec-Jan) | 4 sets × 12 reps @ 70% 1RM, 90s rest |
| Competitive | Feb-Mar | 2 sets × 8 reps @ 70% 1RM, 2x/week |

**Key exercises:** Pull-ups, cable rows, medicine ball circuits, core endurance

---

### **Distance (800-1500m)**
**Energy system:** Aerobic (with anaerobic threshold)  
**Strength focus:** Maximum strength → Muscular Endurance (long)  
**Annual plan:**

| Phase | Duration | Strength Focus |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| AA | 6 weeks (Sep-Oct) | 3 sets × 20 reps, injury prevention |
| MS (light) | 3 weeks (Nov) | 3 sets × 6 reps @ 80% 1RM |
| Muscular Endurance | 8 weeks (Dec-Jan) | Circuits: 3 rounds × 15-30 reps, 60s rest |
| Competitive | Feb-Mar | 1x/week circuit maintenance |

**Key insight:** Distance swimmers still need maximum strength! It improves **work economy** (efficiency per stroke). But conversion phase focuses on **muscular endurance** (15-50 reps, short rest).

**Circuit example (Distance):**
- 3 rounds, 1 min rest:
  - Lat pulldowns: 20 reps @ 50% 1RM
  - Push-ups: 15 reps
  - Cable rows: 20 reps @ 50% 1RM
  - Plank: 90s
  - Leg press: 20 reps @ 60% 1RM

## Common Mistakes (AVOID THESE!)

### ❌ **Mistake 1: Skipping Anatomical Adaptation**
**Problem:** Jumping straight to heavy weights → tendon/ligament injury  
**Fix:** Always start with 4-6 weeks of AA (12-20 reps, light loads)

### ❌ **Mistake 2: Year-Round Heavy Lifting**
**Problem:** No periodization = no peak for championships  
**Fix:** Periodize loads (light → heavy → conversion → maintenance)

### ❌ **Mistake 3: Bodybuilding Methods for Swimmers**
**Problem:** Slow tempo (600ms per rep) doesn't transfer to explosive swimming (100-180ms)  
**Fix:** Use explosive lifts during power phase, focus on movement speed

### ❌ **Mistake 4: Same Rep Ranges Year-Round**
**Problem:** No variation = staleness, plateaus  
**Fix:** AA (12-20 reps) → MS (3-6 reps) → Conversion (6-50 reps depending on event)

### ❌ **Mistake 5: Stopping Strength Training During Season**
**Problem:** Strength decays in 2-3 weeks, performance drops by championships  
**Fix:** Maintain 1-2x/week strength sessions during competition phase

### ❌ **Mistake 6: Wrong Exercises (Olympic Lifts for Swimmers)**
**Problem:** Clean & jerk trains triple extension (legs), not lats/pecs/core (swimming prime movers)  
**Fix:** Choose exercises that mimic stroke mechanics (pull-ups, cable rows, bench press)

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Periodization works** — plan for peak performance, don't hope for it  
✅ **Six phases build sequentially** — AA → H → MS → Conversion → Competitive → Transition  
✅ **Maximum strength is the foundation** — even distance swimmers benefit  
✅ **Match strength type to event** — sprints = power, distance = muscular endurance  
✅ **Train prime movers** — lats, pecs, core, triceps (not quads/glutes like Olympic lifts)  
✅ **Phases build on each other** — you can't skip AA or MS  
✅ **Maintain strength in-season** — 1-2x/week prevents decay  
✅ **Taper strength work** — reduce volume 40-60% before major meets, keep intensity high
