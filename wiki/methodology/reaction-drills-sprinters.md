# Reaction Drills for Sprinters

**Source:** *Sprinting: A Coach's Challenge* by Samuel James Freas  
**Key Insight:** "Reaction drills are the secret weapon that gave LSU sprinters 0.1-0.3 second advantage off the blocks"  
**Application:** Daily neuromuscular training to improve fast-twitch fiber recruitment and reduce reaction time

---

## Why Reaction Drills Matter

**The Arkansas/LSU Discovery:**

When Freas took over at Arkansas with limited pool time (90 min/day), he HAD to find ways to improve sprinters on land. He knew from coaching track that **reaction time is trainable**.

**Results:**
- Arkansas/LSU sprinters consistently had **THE BEST reaction times** in the NCAA
- They were "beating everyone off the blocks" by age 2 of the program
- Relay exchanges were faster and cleaner than anyone else

**The secret:** **Daily reaction drills** targeting:
1. Visual reaction (eyes → brain → body)
2. Auditory reaction (ears → brain → body)
3. Proprioceptive reaction (feel → brain → body)

---

## The Science (Simplified)

**Reaction time = Stimulus → Brain → Muscle activation**

**Three components:**
1. **Sensory detection** (see/hear the stimulus)
2. **Central processing** (brain decides what to do)
3. **Motor response** (muscles fire)

**Can you train it?** YES.

**How:**
- Repetition (neural pathways strengthen with practice)
- Variability (train multiple stimulus types)
- Specificity (practice the EXACT movement you'll race)

**Freas philosophy:**
> "The feet cannot start moving soon enough off the wall or blocks. Practice REACTING, not thinking."

## Types of Reaction Drills

### 1. **Hand Reaction Drills** (Visual Stimulus)

**Purpose:** Train visual processing + hand/arm speed

**Basic drill:**
- Coach holds hand up
- Athlete in ready position (track start or grab start)
- Coach drops hand → Athlete GOES
- Focus: Minimize time between seeing hand drop and body movement

**Progressions:**
1. **Stationary reaction** (just upper body)
2. **Start position reaction** (full start position, GO on hand drop)
3. **Variable stimulus** (hand drop at random times—no predictability)
4. **Multi-stimulus** (coach drops hand OR yells "go" → athlete reacts to BOTH)

**Daily volume:** 10-15 reps

---

### 2. **Foot Reaction Drills** (Auditory Stimulus)

**Purpose:** Train auditory processing + leg/foot speed

**Basic drill:**
- Athlete in start position (on blocks or push-off position)
- Coach gives auditory signal: whistle, clap, beep, "GO"
- Athlete REACTS (drives forward, pushes off wall, etc.)

**Progressions:**
1. **Simple reaction** (single stimulus → GO)
2. **Choice reaction** (two different sounds → different movements)
3. **Delayed reaction** (stimulus → pause → GO on second stimulus)

**Key:** Make stimulus UNPREDICTABLE. If athlete anticipates, they're not reacting.

**Daily volume:** 10-15 reps

---

### 3. **Quick Feet Drills** (Ground-Based)

**Purpose:** Train leg speed, agility, fast-twitch recruitment

**Drills:**

**A. Ladder drills**
- Quick feet through agility ladder
- Patterns: one foot in each square, two feet in each square, lateral shuffle, etc.
- Goal: Speed + precision

**B. Line jumps**
- Jump side-to-side over a line
- 20-30 seconds max effort
- Count reps (track improvement)

**C. Box taps**
- Rapid foot taps on a low box (6-12" height)
- Alternate feet as fast as possible
- 20-30 seconds

**Daily volume:** 3-5 minutes total

---

### 4. **Quick Hands Drills** (Upper Body)

**Purpose:** Train arm speed, hand entry speed, stroke turnover

**Drills:**

**A. Shadowboxing**
- Stand in front of mirror
- Rapid punches (simulates freestyle arm turnover)
- Focus: Speed, not power
- 30-60 seconds

**B. Partner hand-slap drill**
- Partners face each other, hands up
- One tries to slap partner's hands
- Partner reacts and pulls hands away
- Switch roles
- 30 seconds each

**C. Wall push reactions**
- Hands on wall in push-up position
- Partner taps shoulder → Athlete pushes explosively away from wall
- Variation: Partner taps LEFT shoulder → push and rotate left, etc.

**Daily volume:** 3-5 minutes total

---

### 5. **Underwater Reaction Drills** (Pool-Specific)

**Purpose:** Train reaction in the actual environment (water)

**Drills:**

**A. Push-off reactions**
- Athlete at wall in push-off position
- Coach stands at side of pool with visual or auditory cue
- Cue → Athlete EXPLODES off wall
- Measure distance traveled (competition with teammates)

**B. Dive reactions**
- Athlete on blocks
- Random start signals (not just "take your mark... GO")
- Variation: Long hold, short hold, fake start (stay frozen)
- Builds mental discipline + reaction speed

**C. Relay exchange reactions**
- Incoming swimmer approaching wall
- Outgoing swimmer on blocks
- Practice "seeing" the touch point and timing the exchange
- Goal: Minimize exchange time (measure with stopwatch)

**Daily volume:** 5-10 reps (don't overdo—quality > quantity)

## Sample Daily Reaction Routine (15 minutes)

**Pre-workout (5 min):**
```
- 10 hand reaction drills (visual stimulus)
- 10 foot reaction drills (auditory stimulus)
- 2 min quick feet (ladder or line jumps)
```

**Mid-workout (5 min):**
```
- 5 push-off reactions from wall
- 5 relay exchange practices
```

**Post-workout (5 min):**
```
- 2 min quick hands (shadowboxing or partner drill)
- 10 dive reactions (on blocks)
- Flexibility (transition to cool-down)
```

**Total time:** 15 min  
**Total reps:** 40-50 reactions  
**Frequency:** EVERY DAY (including taper)

## Reaction Drill Equipment

**Minimal setup:**
- Whistle or clap (auditory stimulus)
- Your hand (visual stimulus)
- A line on the deck (for jumps)

**Enhanced setup:**
- **Starting device** (electronic beep, like a real start)
- **Agility ladder** (for quick feet patterns)
- **Low box** (6-12" for taps and jumps)
- **Medicine ball** (for reactive throws/catches)
- **Reaction ball** (irregular bounce for hand reactions)

**Freas philosophy:** Don't overthink it. Simple drills done DAILY beat complex drills done occasionally.

---

## Integration with Strength Training

**Freas approach:** Combine reaction drills with strength/power work

**Example:**

**Strength day (post-weights):**
```
After power cleans or box jumps:
- 10 explosive push-off reactions from wall
- 2 min quick feet
- 10 hand reactions

Why? Nervous system is already "fired up" from lifting.
Perfect time to reinforce fast-twitch recruitment.
```

**Circuit training day:**
```
Station 1: Box jumps × 10
Station 2: Quick feet ladder × 30 sec
Station 3: Hand reaction drill × 10
Station 4: Medicine ball slams × 10

Rotate through 3-4 rounds.
```

## Taper: Keep Reaction Drills Going

**Critical insight from Freas:**

Most coaches DROP dryland during taper.  
**BIG MISTAKE for sprinters.**

**Why:**
- Reaction speed is neuromuscular (not cardiovascular)
- It doesn't create fatigue the way weights or high yardage does
- Stopping reaction drills = losing reaction sharpness

**Freas taper protocol:**
- **Drop weights 10 days out**
- **Reduce pool volume**
- **KEEP reaction drills + flexibility**

**Daily taper routine:**
```
AM:
- 15 min flexibility
- 10 min reaction drills (hands, feet, quick movements)

PM:
- Pool workout (reduced volume)
- 5 min reaction drills (push-offs, dives)
- 10 min flexibility
```

**Result:** Sprinters arrive at championship meet with SHARP reactions and rested bodies.

## Measuring Progress

**Track these metrics:**

1. **Reaction time off blocks** (electronic timing if available)
   - Goal: <0.65 seconds (elite level)
   - Improvement: 0.05-0.10 sec over a season

2. **Quick feet drill counts** (how many line jumps in 30 sec)
   - Goal: 60+ (elite level)
   - Track weekly, compete with teammates

3. **Relay exchange time** (watch time vs. flat start time)
   - Goal: Outgoing swimmer is 0.2-0.4 sec FASTER than flat start time
   - Measures quality of exchange reaction

4. **Subjective feel** ("Do I feel faster off the wall?")
   - Athletes will KNOW when they're reacting faster
   - Trust the feel

## Age Group Application

**For 8-and-unders:**
- Make it a GAME
- "Red light, green light" (reaction to visual cue)
- "Freeze dance" (reaction to auditory cue)
- No pressure, just fun

**For 9-12:**
- Introduce basic reaction drills (5 min/practice)
- Quick feet games (ladder drills, line jumps)
- Celebrate fast reactions (positive reinforcement)

**For 13+:**
- Full Freas reaction protocol (15 min/day)
- Measure and track progress
- Competitive drills (teammate vs. teammate)

## Common Mistakes & Fixes

### ❌ Mistake: Anticipating Instead of Reacting

**Problem:** Athlete "guesses" when the signal will come and moves early

**Fix:**
- Randomize timing (long holds, short holds)
- Penalize false starts (do it again correctly)
- Use UNPREDICTABLE stimuli

### ❌ Mistake: Doing Reaction Drills Once a Week

**Problem:** Not enough frequency to build neural adaptation

**Fix:**
- DAILY reaction drills (even if just 5 minutes)
- Consistency > intensity

### ❌ Mistake: Only Training One Type of Reaction

**Problem:** Only practicing auditory (whistle) reactions, ignoring visual and proprioceptive

**Fix:**
- Train ALL THREE: visual (hand drop), auditory (whistle), proprioceptive (feel the movement)
- Vary stimuli daily

### ❌ Mistake: Skipping Reaction Drills During Taper

**Problem:** Reaction sharpness fades right before championship

**Fix:**
- KEEP reaction drills through taper
- They don't create fatigue
- They MAINTAIN neuromuscular sharpness

## The "Hammer Down" Connection

**Reaction drills feed into race mentality:**

When Freas says **"Hammer Down"** (full effort, race mode), sprinters who have trained reactions DAILY are able to:
- Explode off the blocks faster
- React to the turn faster
- Execute relay exchanges cleaner
- Finish with better timing

**It all connects:** Reaction → Power → Speed → Victory

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Reaction time IS trainable** (neuromuscular adaptation)  
✅ **Train DAILY** (5-15 min minimum)  
✅ **Train all three types:** visual, auditory, proprioceptive  
✅ **Integrate with strength work** (nervous system already activated)  
✅ **KEEP going through taper** (don't lose sharpness)  
✅ **Measure progress** (reaction time, quick feet counts, relay exchanges)  
✅ **Make it competitive** (teammates race each other in drills)  
✅ **Start young** (age-groupers can build lifelong habits)
