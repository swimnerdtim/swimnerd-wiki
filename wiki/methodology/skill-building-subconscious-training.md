# Skill-Building: The Subconscious Training System (Bassham)

**Source:** *With Winning in Mind* by Lanny Bassham  
**Focus:** How to build automated swimming skills faster and more efficiently  
**Key insight:** Quality × Quantity × Efficiency = Subconscious mastery

---

## The Skills Factory: How Champions Are Built

**Bassham's discovery:**  
Elite athletes aren't "talented" — they've built superior Subconscious skill banks through:
1. **Quality training** (learning from the best)
2. **Quantity of training** (consistent volume)
3. **Efficiency of training** (all 3 mental processes engaged simultaneously)

**What people call "talent" = Acquired skill + Passion + Persistence**

---

## The Three Factors of Skill Development

### 1. Quality (What You Learn)

**Questions to ask:**
- Is this information from a proven source?
- Is it current and sport-specific?
- Is it well-presented (book, video, coach, camp)?
- Are my instructors skilled at TEACHING (not just doing)?

**Swimming application:**
- Learning from NCAA champion coaches vs. YouTube random
- Attending elite training camps vs. local clinics only
- Working with stroke technique specialists vs. generalists

**Bassham's example:**
- Studied Olympic gold medalists for 2 years
- Interviewed champions about their mental techniques
- Built a SYSTEM from proven methods
- Result: Gold medal in 1976

**For coaches:**
- Continuously educate yourself (podcasts, certifications, conferences)
- Bring in guest coaches for specialized skills
- Film analysis from elite swimmers

---

### 2. Quantity (How Much You Train)

**Not just "hours in the pool" — actual LEARNING time**

**Common mistake:**
- Swimmer at practice for 2 hours
- Spends 30 minutes chatting
- Spends 20 minutes adjusting goggles
- Spends 10 minutes complaining
- **Actual training time: ~1 hour**

**Performance Journal fixes this:**
- Record ACTUAL work done (not just "showed up")
- Track: Sets completed, reps, intensity, focus level

**Bassham's quantity:**
- 5 hours/day, 5 days/week for 12 years before first Olympics
- Later discovered: "I could have done better on HALF the effort with Mental Management®"

**Swimming application:**
- 6-8 practices/week (elite)
- 2-3 hours/practice (focused work)
- Quality reps > junk yardage

---

### 3. Efficiency (Training All Three Mental Processes Simultaneously)

**Most valuable factor — least understood**

**Definition:** Training is EFFICIENT when you develop:
1. Conscious Mind (what you're thinking about)
2. Subconscious Mind (the physical skill)
3. Self-Image (your belief about yourself)

**ALL AT THE SAME TIME.**

---

## Efficient Training Formula

**Maximum efficiency = Thinking right + Doing right + Believing right (simultaneously)**

### ❌ Inefficient Training Example

**Swimmer doing 10×100 free:**
- **Physical:** Good technique (✅ Subconscious)
- **Mental:** Thinking "I hate this set" (❌ Conscious)
- **Belief:** "I always suck at the end" (❌ Self-Image)

**Result:** Physical skill improves slightly, but Conscious and Self-Image are being HURT.  
**Efficiency: 33%**

---

### ✅ Efficient Training Example

**Same swimmer, same set:**
- **Physical:** Good technique (✅ Subconscious)
- **Mental:** "Execute my stroke. Nail the walls." (✅ Conscious)
- **Belief:** After each rep: "That's like me!" (✅ Self-Image)

**Result:** All three mental processes grow together.  
**Efficiency: 100%**

**This is HOW you get better faster.**

## The 7 Training Guidelines

### Guideline 1: Catch Yourself Doing Something RIGHT

**Wrong approach:**
- "What am I doing wrong?"
- "Why do I keep missing that wall?"
- Focus on errors = Expert in failure

**Right approach:**
- "What did I do RIGHT on that rep?"
- "My breathing was perfect — what did I feel?"
- Focus on solutions = Expert in success

**Self-Image impact:**
- Every time you think about an error → imprint "it's like me to make that mistake"
- Every time you think about success → imprint "it's like me to nail it"

**Bassham's rule:**  
"I can't remember how I got my bad shots. I only remember my good ones."

**Practical drill:**

#### Success Analysis After Practice

**Coach asks:** "What did you do WELL today?"  
**Swimmer lists:** 3-5 specific things (turns, underwaters, tempo, finish)  
**Write in journal:** Success Analysis section

**Builds Self-Image daily.**

---

### Guideline 2: Train 4-5 Days Per Week (Minimum)

**Training frequency chart:**

| Days/Week | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| 1 day | **Worse than nothing** (skills deteriorate faster) |
| 2-3 days | Maintenance (stay where you are) |
| 4-6 days | **Growth** (move up the mastery curve) |
| 7+ days | Burnout risk (need rest for adaptation) |

**Why 1 day/week is worse than zero:**
- Body/mind start adapting to training
- Then 6 days off = adaptation reverses
- Net effect: slower than if you hadn't trained at all

**Swimming application:**
- **Elite:** 9-10 sessions/week (doubles)
- **Age group:** 6-8 sessions/week
- **Masters:** 4-5 sessions/week

**Key:** CONSISTENT frequency matters more than occasional hero sessions.

---

### Guideline 3: Wherever You Are, Be ALL There

**The focus rule:**
- At practice → 100% focused on swimming
- At home → leave practice at the pool
- At school → leave swimming at the pool

**Why it matters:**
- Bringing problems from one area into another = divided focus
- Divided focus = wasted training time
- Mental clutter = errors

**Bassham's discipline:**
- Before entering the range: "Leave everything else behind"
- After leaving the range: "That's done. Focus on what's next."

**For swimmers:**

#### Pre-Practice Mental Shift

**Ritual before entering the pool:**
1. Put phone away
2. Deep breath
3. Say (internally): "I'm here. Time to work."

**Signals to brain:** This is practice time. Focus mode ON.

#### Post-Practice Mental Shift

**Ritual after leaving the pool:**
1. Quick debrief (what went well)
2. Write in journal
3. Say: "Practice is over. Time for ______."

**Prevents:** Dwelling on bad reps, carrying negativity home.

---

### Guideline 4: Rehearse Match Day Often Within Training

**Treat training like competition (sometimes)**

**Bassham:** "Every day, imagine you're competing at the Olympics and winning."

**Why it works:**
- Self-Image can't tell real from imagined
- If you rehearse winning daily → Self-Image believes "it's like me to win"
- Competition day feels FAMILIAR (not scary)

**Swimming application:**

#### In-Practice Competition Simulation

**1-2 times/week:**
- **Announce:** "This is finals at NCAAs. This is your race."
- **Swimmers:** Full mental program (visualization, focus, routine)
- **Coaches:** Treat it like a meet (official start, cheering, times announced)

**Benefits:**
- Builds Self-Image of "I perform well under pressure"
- Practices mental routines in realistic conditions
- Competition feels like "just another day"

#### Nightly Visualization

**Before bed:**
- 5-10 minutes
- Visualize upcoming race
- See yourself executing perfectly
- **Feel the success**

**Bassham did this for 2 months before setting national record.**

---

### Guideline 5: When You're Swimming Well, Swim A LOT

**Jack Writer's rule:** "When I'm shooting well, I keep shooting."

**Why:**
- Great training days = golden imprinting opportunities
- Every good rep → "it's like me" imprint
- Momentum = self-reinforcing

**Swimming application:**

#### Positive Momentum Protocol

**Swimmer crushing a set?**
- ✅ Do 2-4 extra reps at the end
- ✅ "Let's ride this wave"

**Swimmer having a bad day?**
- ✅ STOP training
- ✅ "Don't practice losing"
- ✅ Go home, reset, come back tomorrow

**Why stopping matters:**
- Bad reps = negative imprints
- "Pushing through" on a terrible day = practicing failure
- Better to rest and return fresh

**Exception:** If it's a MENTAL issue (bad attitude), that's different. Work through that.

---

### Guideline 6: Train with People Better Than You

**Self-Image principle:** You rise to the standard you're around.

**Bassham's experience:**
- Trained with Jack Writer (World/Olympic Champion)
- Trained with Lones Wigger (World/Olympic Champion)
- Trained with Margaret Murdock (World Champion)
- **They never talked about failures in front of each other**
- "I don't want to give you an edge"

**Result:** Bassham became Olympic Champion.

**Swimming examples:**

#### Leon Marchand at ASU (Bob Bowman)

- Trains in same pool as NCAA champions
- Sees elite work DAILY
- His Self-Image: "This is normal"
- Result: 4 Olympic golds (Paris 2024)

#### Training Group Strategy

**For coaches:**
- Group swimmers by ABILITY + ATTITUDE
- Put developing swimmers with positive veterans
- AVOID: Grouping complainers together (negative imprints spread)

**For swimmers:**
- Seek out the best in your area
- Train with clubs/groups above your current level
- Absorb their standards, work ethic, mindset

**Exception:** Teaching OTHERS doesn't hurt you (if your Self-Image is strong)

**Bassham ran International Shooting School:**
- Taught hundreds of beginners
- Still shot at Olympic level
- Why? His Self-Image separated "who I am" from "who they are"

**For coaches:** Teaching strengthens YOUR mastery (as long as you don't absorb their errors).

---

### Guideline 7: Plan Your Year

**Annual training plan = 4 quarters:**

### Q1: Foundation (Evaluation + Planning)

**Focus:** Set goals, test new ideas, build base

**Activities:**
- Technique changes (stroke adjustments, starts, turns)
- Equipment trials (suits, paddles, fins)
- Mental skills training (Directive Affirmations, Mental Programs)
- Goal setting (write Directive Affirmations)

**Swimming example:**
- Sept–Nov (post-summer season)
- Base aerobic work
- Drill-heavy practices
- Low-pressure meets

---

### Q2: Conditioning (Hard Work)

**Focus:** Build strength, endurance, mental toughness

**Activities:**
- High-volume training
- Intense dryland
- Race-pace work
- Mental toughness sets (broken swims, hypoxic work)

**Swimming example:**
- Dec–Feb (winter training)
- Highest yardage of year
- Double-days
- Grind phase

---

### Q3: Competition (Peak Performance)

**Focus:** Taper, race, perform

**Activities:**
- Lower volume, higher intensity
- Mental preparation (visualization, affirmations, race strategy)
- Simulate meet conditions in practice
- Goal: Best times at championship meets

**Swimming example:**
- March–April (conference/NCAAs)
- OR July–Aug (Olympic Trials, Olympics)
- Taper protocol
- Race execution

---

### Q4: Recovery (Rest + Reflection)

**Focus:** Physical and mental recovery

**Activities:**
- Active rest (light swimming, other sports)
- Reflection (what worked? what didn't?)
- Goal review (did I hit my targets?)
- Plan Q1 for next year

**Swimming example:**
- May (post-NCAAs)
- OR Sept (post-summer season)
- 2-4 weeks off or very light

**Bassham:** "Do NOT skip this step. You NEED rest to reflect and reset."

## The Performance Journal System

**Why most swimmers don't keep one:**
1. Don't know HOW
2. Think it's a diary (recording mistakes)
3. Lazy

**Why champions DO keep one:**
1. "You can't manage what you don't measure"
2. Coaches can SEE your progress (or lack thereof)
3. Writing success = deeper imprint than thinking/talking

---

## What to Track in a Performance Journal

### 1. Competition Log

**Record:**
- Date
- Meet name
- Events swam
- Times
- Place
- Key learnings

**Example:**

```
3/15/26 | Big Ten Championships | 200 Free
Prelims: 1:34.52 (3rd seed)
Finals: 1:33.87 (2nd place, PR by 0.4)
Key learning: My 3rd 50 was fastest. New strength!
```

---

### 2. Equipment Log

**Record ONLY when you change something:**
- New suit (brand, model, date)
- Goggles
- Paddles, fins, snorkel
- Tempo trainer settings

**Why:**
- If times suddenly drop, you can check: "Did I change equipment?"

---

### 3. General Data (Daily)

**Record after EVERY practice:**
- Date
- Location (pool name if traveling)
- Duration (actual swim time, not including rest)
- What you accomplished (specific sets)

**Example:**

```
4/5/26 | Home pool | 2 hours
Main set: 20×100 free @ 1:15 (avg 1:05)
Underwater focus: 5 dolphin kicks every wall
Result: Best set of the week
```

---

### 4. Solution Analysis

**Record solutions to problems:**

**Format:**
- Problem: [Describe issue]
- Solution: [What worked]

**Example:**

```
Problem: Breathing into the wall on 200 fly (last 3 practices)
Solution: Coach said "eyes down 3 strokes before wall"
Tried it: WORKED. Smoother turns.
```

**OR if no solution yet:**

```
Problem: I'm looking for a solution to: Feeling heavy in warm-up every morning
Testing: Earlier bedtime, more hydration, lighter dinner
```

---

### 5. Success Analysis (MOST IMPORTANT)

**Write ONLY what you did WELL.**

**Format:**
- List 3-5 specific successes from practice

**Example:**

```
✅ Nailed every underwater (5 kicks, tight streamline)
✅ Tempo felt perfect on 100s
✅ Finished strong on last rep (usually fade)
✅ Mental focus was locked in the whole practice
```

**Why this matters:**
- Writing = deeper imprint than thinking
- You become what you write about
- Builds Self-Image daily

**Bassham:** "The primary benefit of the journal is building Self-Image through written imprints."

---

### 6. Daily Goal Statement

**Write ONE goal in first-person present tense** (as if already achieved):

**Examples:**

```
"I often swim under 1:45 in the 200 free."
"I am the most consistent swimmer on our team."
"I dominate my underwaters in every race."
```

**Why it works:**
- You write it EVERY DAY
- Two outcomes:
  1. You reach the goal
  2. OR you stop writing it (because you don't believe it)
- As long as you keep writing → you're moving toward it

**Cross-reference:** [Mental Management System — Directive Affirmation](./mental-management-system-bassham.md)

## Practical Journal Setup

### Format

**Option 1:** Physical notebook
- Pros: Handwriting = stronger imprint
- Cons: Can't search, harder to track trends

**Option 2:** Digital (Notes app, Google Doc)
- Pros: Searchable, graphs, easy to share with coach
- Cons: Weaker imprint

**Bassham recommends:** Physical journal in durable notebook

---

### Daily Routine (5 minutes)

**Right after practice:**

1. General Data (1 min)
   - Date, duration, what you did

2. Solution Analysis (1 min)
   - Any problems solved today?

3. Success Analysis (2 min)
   - List 3-5 things you did WELL

4. Goal Statement (1 min)
   - Write your current goal (same one daily for 21 days)

**Total time:** ~5 minutes  
**Benefit:** Massive Self-Image growth over weeks/months

## How Skills Move from Conscious → Subconscious

**The progression:**

### Stage 1: Conscious Incompetence

**"I don't know what I'm doing"**
- Beginner learning to swim
- Every movement requires thought
- Slow, choppy, frustrating

---

### Stage 2: Conscious Competence

**"I can do it if I think about it"**
- Learning freestyle breathing
- Must consciously remind yourself: "Rotate. Breathe. Exhale underwater."
- Works, but requires focus

---

### Stage 3: Subconscious Competence

**"I can do it without thinking"**
- Breathing is automated
- Body knows when to rotate
- Conscious mind free to focus on other things (pace, strategy)

**This is the goal.**

---

### How to speed up Conscious → Subconscious transfer:

**1. Repetition with CORRECT technique**
- 1,000 reps wrong = 1,000 bad imprints
- 1,000 reps right = Subconscious mastery

**2. Focus on FEELING, not thinking**
- "What does a good catch FEEL like?"
- "How does my body feel when my streamline is tight?"

**3. Reinforce success immediately**
- Good rep → "That's like me!"
- Imprints the skill faster

**4. Minimize conscious override during performance**
- Practice: Think about technique
- Competition: TRUST the skill

---

## The Danger of Conscious Override

**Definition:** Consciously trying to control something that should be Subconscious.

**Example in swimming:**

### Conscious Override in a Race

**100 free, final 25:**

❌ **Thinking:**
- "Kick harder!"
- "Pull faster!"
- "Is she ahead?"
- "Don't breathe!"

**Result:**
- Rhythm breaks
- Stroke falls apart
- Time is slower than practice

✅ **Trusting:**
- "Execute."
- "Flow."
- (Mostly silence — let Subconscious work)

**Result:**
- Smooth, powerful, fast

---

## Bassham's Olympic Example (Munich 1972)

**Problem:**
- Bassham was physically the best in the world
- But felt MASSIVE pressure
- Conscious override kicked in
- Tried TOO HARD
- Result: Silver medal (lost to teammate who stayed calm)

**Lesson learned:**
- "I had the skill. I didn't trust it."
- Spent next 4 years building mental system
- 1976: Trusted his Subconscious
- Result: GOLD medal

**Quote:** "Champions work hard in training and work easy in competition."

## Training for Subconscious Automation

### Drill: Silent Reps

**Goal:** Train the Subconscious to execute without Conscious interference

**Setup:**
- Pick a simple set (8×50 free @ 1:00)
- Rule: NO internal dialogue during the swim
- Just FEEL the stroke

**What swimmers learn:**
- Body knows what to do
- Conscious chatter = interference
- Trust = speed

**Progression:**
- Master in practice
- Apply in competition

---

### Drill: Mental Program Practice

**Goal:** Build a pre-performance routine that triggers Subconscious execution

**Setup:**
1. **Point of Initiation:** Pull goggles down
2. **Point of Direction:** Visualize perfect start, underwater, breakout
3. **Point of Focus:** One word ("GO" or "ATTACK")
4. **Action:** Dive (Subconscious takes over)

**Practice:**
- Run this routine before EVERY rep in practice
- Becomes automatic
- Competition day: Routine feels NORMAL

## Bottom Line for Coaches

**To build Subconscious skills FASTER:**

1. **Improve quality:** Learn from the best (coaches, camps, film study)
2. **Increase quantity:** Train 4-6 days/week consistently
3. **Maximize efficiency:** Train all 3 mental processes simultaneously
   - Conscious: Think about what you WANT
   - Subconscious: Execute correct technique
   - Self-Image: Reinforce success ("That's like me!")

**Tools to implement:**
- Performance Journal (daily success tracking)
- Mental Programs (pre-race routines)
- "Catch yourself doing something right" (focus on solutions)
- Train with better swimmers (raise the standard)

**Result:** Swimmers who perform on autopilot in big meets because their Subconscious has been trained to EXECUTE.
