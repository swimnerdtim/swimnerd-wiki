# Sprint Training: The Freas Method

**Source:** *Sprinting: A Coach's Challenge* by Samuel James Freas  
**Key Insight:** "Train a sprinter to sprint. If you do not remember anything else, remember this!"  
**Application:** A revolutionary approach to sprint training that prioritizes speed, reaction, and neuromuscular training over traditional distance-based cardiovascular work

---

## Core Philosophy

**Traditional coaching wisdom (1970s-90s):** Build an aerobic base first, then add speed work during taper.

**Freas's revolutionary approach:** Train sprinters fast ALL YEAR. Speed is a skill that must be practiced daily, not something you "add" at the end.

### The Arkansas/LSU Discovery

When Sam Freas took over at Arkansas, he had **90 minutes of pool time per day** (not the typical 3-4 hours). This constraint forced him to rethink everything:

- **Can't build a 500 freestyle base in 90 minutes**
- **Had to prioritize:** What actually makes sprinters fast?
- **Result:** Focused on strength, power, reaction, flexibility, and swimming FAST

**His sprinters dominated.** LSU sprinters consistently went faster at the SECOND championship meet of the season—something distance swimmers struggled with.

---

## The Five Components of Sprint Fitness

Freas adapted a **fitness-based approach** instead of pure yardage:

| Component | Sprint Application | Training Methods |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| **1. Cardiovascular Endurance** | Energy systems for 50-100 racing | Short, intense repeats (not 5000m sets) |
| **2. Muscular Endurance** | Sustain power through finish | Dryland circuits, tubing, body resistance |
| **3. Strength** | Explosive power off blocks/walls | Weights (power focus), plyometrics |
| **4. Flexibility** | Streamline, range of motion, injury prevention | Daily 15-30 min flexibility work |
| **5. Agility** | Quick reactions, fast-twitch recruitment | Reaction drills, quick feet/hands |

**Key:** All five components are trained DAILY in sprint programs. Distance swimmers emphasize 1-2.

---

## Freas' Ten Essentials of Sprinting

*From the book's appendix—the distilled wisdom:*

1. **Race every day in practice**
2. **Swim at 100% speed all year long, not just during taper**
3. **Swim superfast all year long, not just during taper**
4. **Always work dryland** to improve all five fitness components
5. **Work on starts, turns, and finishes almost every day** (at least 5× per week)
6. **Practice no-breath 50s and minimal-breath 100s** (better body position, better speed)
7. **Execute high kick** with heels 9-15 inches out of the water (better body position)
8. **Practice reaction drills every day**
9. **Change training if speed is lost** due to overtraining (cardiovascular OR neuromuscular)
10. **Be happy, don't worry; get plenty of sleep and eat healthily**

## Training Terminology

### Pool Training Types (Freas classification)

| Type | Effort | Interval | Purpose | Frequency |
|------|--------|----------|---------|-----------|
| **Recovery** | Easy, technique focus | Generous | Active rest, maintain feel | After hard days |
| **Fast** | 90-95% | Moderate | Race pace simulation | 2-3×/week |
| **Superfast** | 100%+ (overspeed) | Long (full recovery) | Neuromuscular recruitment | 2-3×/week |
| **"Hammer Down"** | Max effort, race mentality | Race conditions | Mental + physical peak | 1×/week + meets |

**Critical distinction:** "Fast" ≠ "Superfast"
- **Fast:** Controlled 90-95%, learning to swim at race pace repeatedly
- **Superfast:** ALL-OUT, recruiting every muscle fiber, often with aids (fins, paddles, downhill sets)

### Sample Superfast Set (from Freas)

```
300 yards SUPERFAST swimming
(broken into manageable chunks with FULL RECOVERY)

Example:
- 3 × 100 on 10:00 (yes, 10 minutes rest)
- Goal: Each 100 FASTER than race pace
- Use: Fins, paddles, or "overspeed" conditions

Why? Teaches the nervous system what FASTER feels like.
```

## The "Hammer Down" Mentality

**Origin:** Freas's signature phrase—from trucker slang for "floor it"

**What it means:**
- Maximum effort
- Race mentality (not practice mode)
- Celebrated in practice (not saved for meets)

**When to use:**
- Timed 25s/50s in practice
- End-of-practice "send-offs"
- Relay pickups
- Friday before meets

**Philosophy:** Sprinters need to practice RACING, not just swimming fast. "Hammer Down" is permission to go ALL OUT with full competitive intensity.

## Weekly Training Structure (In-Season)

**Freas approach:** Rotate training stress across systems to avoid burnout

### Sample Week (College Sprinter)

| Day | AM | PM | Focus |
|-----|----|----|-------|
| **Monday** | Weights (strength) + Dryland | Recovery swim (2000-2500) | Recover from weekend |
| **Tuesday** | Reaction drills + Flexibility | **SUPERFAST** (low volume, max effort) | Neuromuscular |
| **Wednesday** | Weights (power) | **Fast** (race pace sets) | Lactate tolerance |
| **Thursday** | Dryland circuits | Recovery + Starts/turns drill | Technical precision |
| **Friday** | Flexibility + Quick feet | **Hammer Down** (timed 25s/50s) | Race prep |
| **Saturday** | — | **Dual meet** or time trial | Competition |
| **Sunday** | Optional easy 1000-1500 | OFF | Rest |

**Total weekly yardage:** 12,000-18,000 (vs. 40,000-60,000 for distance)

**Key:** Quality > Quantity. Every swim is PURPOSE-DRIVEN.

## Taper Philosophy

### The Freas Taper Advantage

**Why Freas sprinters peaked twice:**

Traditional taper = **Acute neuromuscular shock**
- Swimmers do 50,000 yds/week cardiovascular → suddenly 15,000 yds/week speed
- Body confused: "What is this new stimulus?"
- **Result:** Short peak window (7-10 days)

Freas taper = **Fine-tuning an already-fast system**
- Swimmers do 15,000 yds/week speed → 8,000 yds/week superfast
- Body knows speed; just removing fatigue
- **Result:** Extended peak window (3-5 weeks possible)

### Taper by Somatotype

**Mesomorphs** (muscular, naturally powerful):
- **MORE REST** (lose strength slowly, gain fatigue quickly)
- Drop weights 10 days out
- Keep reaction drills + flexibility only

**Ectomorphs** (lean, wiry):
- **LESS REST** (lose strength quickly)
- Light weights until 5 days out
- Maintain more dryland volume

**Endomorphs** (heavier build):
- **MODERATE REST**
- Judge individually based on how they hold strength

## The Second Championship Meet Strategy

**Problem:** Most coaches think you can only taper once.

**Freas reality:** LSU sprinters ALWAYS swam faster at NCAAs (2nd meet) than SECs (1st meet).

### Between-Meet Plans

**1 week between meets:**
- Days 1-2: Easy (flexibility + reaction drills only)
- Day 3: **Fast** (reset neuromuscular system)
- Day 4: Easy + technical work
- Day 5-7: **Championship prep** (meet warm-up simulation)

**2 weeks between meets:**
- Days 1-3: Easy recovery
- Days 4-7: Light training cycle (weights return, moderate volume)
- Days 8-10: **Superfast** (re-peak nervous system)
- Days 11-14: Championship taper (same as first meet)

**3-5 weeks between meets:**
- Use FULL taper for first meet
- Take 5 days to return to normal training
- **Fine-tune for 2-3 weeks:** More reaction drills, more flexibility, LESS muscular work
- Re-taper for second meet

**Secret:** Sprinters' nervous systems can re-peak multiple times if they're trained fast year-round.

## Common Mistakes (What NOT to Do)

### ❌ Mistake #1: "Building a Base"
**Wrong:** "Sprinters need 5,000-10,000 yards/day of aerobic base-building"

**Right:** Sprinters need strength, power, and fast-twitch fiber recruitment. Aerobic base for a 50 freestyler is like marathon training for a 100m track sprinter.

### ❌ Mistake #2: Saving Speed for Taper
**Wrong:** "We'll add speed work in the last 3 weeks before championship"

**Right:** Speed is a SKILL. You can't learn a new skill in 3 weeks. Train fast ALL YEAR.

### ❌ Mistake #3: Ignoring Dryland
**Wrong:** "Pool time is limited—just swim"

**Right:** Dryland develops strength, power, flexibility, and reaction that the pool cannot provide. Freas teams did 45-60 min dryland DAILY.

### ❌ Mistake #4: Treating All Sprinters the Same
**Wrong:** "Everyone does the same workout"

**Right:** Mesomorphs need more rest. Ectomorphs need more strength work. Women lose strength faster than men. Masters athletes need different recovery. **Individualize.**

### ❌ Mistake #5: Over-Coaching Technique During Taper
**Wrong:** "Let me fix your stroke 3 days before championship"

**Right:** Taper is for confidence and feel. Make only minor, positive adjustments. Don't let sprinters overthink.

## Age Group Application

**For 10-and-unders:**
- **NO WEIGHTS**
- Dryland: Sit-ups, push-ups, jumping drills, basic gymnastics
- Pool: **GAMES + Short sprints** (not lap swimming)
- Homework: 10 sit-ups, 10 push-ups, 10 jumps before bed (increase by 1/day)

**For 11-14:**
- Introduce body-weight circuits
- Teach proper start/turn technique (practice 5×/week)
- Keep it FUN—sprinting should feel like racing, not work
- Limited yardage: 2000-3000/practice max

**For high school:**
- Full Freas program applicable
- Year-round weight training
- Water polo off-season (builds power + fun)
- Social aspect critical—peer recognition, team culture

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Train sprinters to sprint**—speed is a skill practiced daily  
✅ **Quality > Quantity**—12,000 purposeful yards > 50,000 garbage yards  
✅ **Five fitness components**—strength, power, endurance, flexibility, agility  
✅ **Reaction drills EVERY DAY**—fast-twitch recruitment is trainable  
✅ **Individualize by somatotype**—mesomorphs ≠ ectomorphs  
✅ **Celebrate speed in practice**—"Hammer Down" mentality  
✅ **You CAN peak twice**—if trained fast year-round  
✅ **Starts, turns, finishes = 30-50% of a 50 free**—practice them daily

## Sample "Hammer Down" Workout (Friday before meet)

**Time:** 60 minutes  
**Mindset:** RACE SIMULATION

```
Warm-up:
- 400 easy choice
- 6 × 50 (25 drill / 25 build) on 1:00
- 200 easy pull

Flexibility (10 min on deck)

Main Set:
- 4 × 25 from dive, TIMED, "Hammer Down"
  - Goal: Faster than race pace
  - Rest: Until you're ready to GO (no set interval)
  - Celebrate each one—coach goes NUTS

- 100 easy

- 2 × 50 from dive, TIMED, race simulation
  - Full effort, race mentality
  - Rest 5 min between

- 200 easy cool-down

Finish: Team cheer, visualization, confidence-building talk
```

**Total yardage:** ~1500  
**Total FAST yardage:** 200  
**Intensity:** Maximum  
**Outcome:** Ready to race

## Freasisms (Coaching Wisdom)

> **"Don't coach the greatness out of an athlete."**

> **"Never let a sprinter think he is working harder than another."** (Disguise individualization)

> **"Don't be afraid to have sprinters shave during the season if they're not swimming fast enough."**

> **"Championships are to be a celebration, not a 'fear-fest.'"**

> **"The more you allow sprinters to think, the worse off they will be and the slower they will swim."**

> **"If you have a group of sprinters, just prior to championship, isolate them from everyone else. Work on their individual needs and interests."**

> **"Sprinters have speed—make them feel good about it. Don't make them feel inadequate because sustaining speed is difficult."**
