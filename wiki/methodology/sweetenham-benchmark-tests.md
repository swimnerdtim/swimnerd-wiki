# Bill Sweetenham Benchmark Test Sets

**Practical Testing Protocols to Set Training Paces, Monitor Fitness, and Predict Performance**

---

## Source

*Championship Swim Training* by Bill Sweetenham & John Atkinson (2003)  
**Author credentials:** Australian National Team Head Coach, British Olympic Head Coach, Great Britain Performance Director

---

## Why Test?

**Sweetenham's philosophy:** Test sets provide objective data to:
1. Set individualized training paces
2. Monitor adaptation to training
3. Predict competition readiness
4. Identify strengths and weaknesses
5. Prevent overtraining

**Golden rule:** "What you expect and what you get are often very different." Testing reveals the truth.

---

## 1. The 7 × 200m Step Test

**Purpose:** Establish anaerobic threshold pace and set training repeat times

**Best for:** 200m and longer swimmers

### Protocol

**Warm-up:** Standard team warm-up

**Main set:**
1. **1 × 200m** at 400m pace (RI 30s, take HR)
2. **2 × 200m** at 400m pace (RI 30s, take HR after each)
3. **3 × 200m** at 400m pace (RI 30s, take HR after each)
4. **1 × 200m** at 200m pace (RI 30s, take HR)

**Recovery:** 400m easy, then swim-down

### Key Measurements
- Split times for each 200m
- Heart rate after each swim
- Stroke count (optional but valuable)

### Using the Results

**Average the 7 × 200m times**, excluding fastest and slowest.

**Example:**
- Total time for 5 middle swims: 11:20.00
- Average 200m: 2:16.00
- Average 100m: 1:08.00

**Apply correction factors to set training paces:**

| Distance | Correction | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **400m repeats** | None | 4:32.00 (1:08 × 4) |
| **200m repeats** | -2 seconds | 2:14.00 |
| **100m repeats** | -1.5 seconds | 1:06.50 |
| **50m repeats** | -1 second | 33.00 |

**Rest intervals:** 10-20 seconds for all anaerobic threshold work.

## 2. The 8 × 50m Efficiency Test

**Purpose:** Test efficiency at increasing speeds; design training sets based on stroke count & stroke rate

**Best for:** All swimmers (50m-1500m events)

### Protocol

**8 × 50m on 2:30, descending 1-8:**
- Swim 1: Slowest (PB + 15 seconds)
- Swim 8: Fastest (PB + 1 second or faster, max effort)

**Record for EACH swim:**
- Time
- Stroke count
- Stroke rate (strokes per minute)

### Graphing the Results

**Graph 1: Stroke Count vs. Velocity**
- X-axis: Time per 50m (velocity)
- Y-axis: Stroke count

**Graph 2: Stroke Rate vs. Velocity**
- X-axis: Time per 50m (velocity)
- Y-axis: Stroke rate (strokes per minute)

### Using the Results

**Example:** Swimmer's 8th swim = 32.07 seconds @ 20 strokes, 47.1 strokes/min

**Training set design:**
- **10 × 50m on 1:30 @ 32.0-32.5 seconds**
- Hold **18 stroke count**, maintain **42 stroke rate**

**Progression options:**
1. Faster leaving time (1:15 instead of 1:30)
2. Lower stroke count (16-17 instead of 18)
3. Slower stroke rate (same time with less effort)

### 8-Week Race Prep Using Efficiency Test

**Monday morning sessions leading into competition:**
- **8 weeks out:** 8 × 50m descending
- **7 weeks out:** 7 × 50m descending
- **6 weeks out:** 6 × 50m descending
- **5 weeks out:** 5 × 50m descending
- **4 weeks out:** 4 × 50m descending
- **3 weeks out:** 3 × 50m descending
- **2 weeks out:** 3 × 50m descending
- **1 week out:** 3 × 50m descending

**Keeps swimmers focused on efficiency + speed** without excessive volume.

## 3. Cold-Swim Test

**Purpose:** Confirm current racing speed; teach athletes to perform under difficult conditions

**Best for:** Fully committed swimmers with injury-free status

### Protocol

**No pool warm-up.** Comprehensive dryland warm-up only.

**Swim:**
- 1 × 100m or 200m **maximum effort**
- Training suit (not racing suit)
- Record splits, total time, stroke rate

### When to Use

**Conduct 10-12 cold swims per season:**
- Half when swimmer feels good
- Half when fatigued or under heavy training
- Unannounced (coach's discretion)
- Both morning and afternoon sessions

### Using the Results

**Compare to personal best:**
- Cold swim time = 61 seconds
- PB = 60 seconds
- Difference = **+1.66%**

### Mock Trials at 6%, 4%, 2% of PB

**Build confidence through progression:**
- PB = 60.00
- 6% trial = 63.60 (6 × 0.6 = 3.6)
- 4% trial = 62.40 (4 × 0.6 = 2.4)
- 2% trial = 61.20 (2 × 0.6 = 1.2)

**Goal:** Eventually perform cold swims at **3% of PB** with perfect race protocols (starts, turns, finishes).

### Applying to Midseason Meets

**Under work conditions:**
- Heats: 3% of PB
- Semifinals: 2% of PB
- Final: 1% of PB

**When rested:**
- Heats: 3% of goal time
- Semifinals: 2% of goal time
- Final: 1% of goal time

**Championship meets (rested):**
- Heats: 2% of goal time
- Semifinals: 1% of goal time
- Final: Goal time

## 4. High-Performance Endurance Test Progression

**Purpose:** Build aerobic capacity + anaerobic threshold endurance over 8-12 weeks

**Best for:** Distance and mid-distance swimmers

### Set Progression

**Set 1: 40 × 100m FS on 1:20**
- Heart rate: 30 BBM
- Must make cycle at any intensity before progressing

**Set 2: 30 × 100m FS on 1:30**
- Heart rate: 20-30 BBM
- Aim for faster times than Set 1

**Set 3: 24 × 100m FS**
- 18 × 100m on 1:45 (HR: 10-20 BBM)
- 6 × 100m on 2:00 (HR: 10-20 BBM)
- Record times, stroke counts, breathing patterns
- Calculate average (exclude fastest/slowest)

**Set 4: 18 × 100m FS on 2:00**
- Hold fastest average from Set 3
- Record heart rates, stroke counts
- Compare speed to heart rate

**Set 5: 2000m or 3000m Timed Swim**
- Even pace, as fast as sustainable
- ~30 minutes of work = close to anaerobic threshold

### Using 3000m Time Trial to Set Training Paces

**Example:**
- 3000m time: 36:00 (2,160 seconds)
- Average 100m: 1:12.00

**Apply correction factors:**

| Distance | Correction | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **400m repeats** | None | 4:48.00 |
| **200m repeats** | -2 seconds | 2:22.00 |
| **100m repeats** | -1.5 seconds | 1:10.50 |
| **50m repeats** | -1 second | 0:35.00 |

**Rest intervals:** 10-20 seconds.

### Using 2000m Time Trial to Set Training Paces

**Example:**
- 2000m time: 26:00 (1,560 seconds)
- Average 100m: 1:18.00

**Apply correction factors:**

| Distance | Correction | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **400m repeats** | None | 5:12.00 |
| **200m repeats** | -2 seconds | 2:34.00 |
| **100m repeats** | -1.5 seconds | 1:16.50 |
| **50m repeats** | -1 second | 0:38.00 |

## 5. Maximum Heart-Rate Test

**Purpose:** Determine true max HR for each swimmer (not age-based formula)

**Best for:** All swimmers

### Protocol (200m and Farther Swimmers)

1. 100m build to maximum (record HR)
2. 100m build to maximum (record HR)
3. 3 × 100m at PB + 4 seconds, RI 10s (record HR after each)
4. Add 50m swims if HR still rising (RI 10s)

**Stop when:** Heart rate stops rising or begins to drop.

### Protocol (50m and 100m Swimmers)

1. 100m build to maximum (record HR)
2. 100m at PB + 4 seconds, RI 10s (record HR)
3. 4 × 50m at race pace, RI 10s (record HR after each)

**Add extra 50m swims if required.**

### Key Insights

- Some swimmers require **>3 minutes of work** to reach max HR
- **Girls may have max HR 5-10 bpm lower than boys**
- Health status and recent training affect max HR
- **Never prescribe a fixed HR for all swimmers** — individualize based on BBM (beats below max)

**Test frequency:** Every 4-6 weeks

## 6. Individual Medley Pace Test Progression

**Purpose:** Develop IM-specific endurance and establish goal splits

**Best for:** IM swimmers

**Prerequisite:** Complete IM Strengths & Weaknesses Chart (see book p. 39)

### Test Structure (Conducted Over 4 Days or 4 Weeks)

**Day 1: Butterfly**
- 4 × 100m BF at PB + 8s, on 2:20
- 2 × 100m BF at PB + 12s, on 2:00
- 2 × 100m BF at PB + 4s, on 2:40
- 1 × 100m BF max effort (target: under PB + 4s), on 3:00
- 1 × 200m BK at goal split pace for 400 IM

**Day 2: Backstroke + Breaststroke**
- 6 × 200m BK descending by 4 seconds each (5:00 rest)
- Target 5th swim: goal BK split pace (e.g., 2:16.00 for 1:08 × 2)
- Target 6th swim: goal BR split pace (e.g., 2:22.00 for 1:11 × 2)

**Day 3: Breaststroke + Freestyle**
- 6 × 200m BR descending by 4 seconds each (5:00 rest)
- Target 5th swim: goal BR split pace
- Target 6th swim: goal FS split pace (e.g., 2:00.00 for 1:00 × 2)

**Day 4: Freestyle + IM**
- 6 × 200m FS descending by 4 seconds each (5:00 rest)
- Target 5th swim: goal FS split pace
- Target 6th swim: straight 200 IM at half of 400 IM goal pace

**Key:** Test at goal-oriented pace, NOT set heart rates.

## 7. Kick Test Set

**Purpose:** Test kicking ability; set goal-oriented kick training paces

**Best for:** All swimmers

### Protocol

- 1 × 800m kick (target: 12:00 or faster)
- 200m swim-down
- 1 × 400m kick (target: 6:00 or faster)
- 200m swim-down
- 1 × 200m kick (target: 3:00 or faster)
- 200m swim-down
- 1 × 100m kick (target: within 20s of PB 100m swim time)
- 200m swim-down
- 1 × 50m kick
- 150m swim-down
- 1 × 25m kick
- 75m swim-down

**Use kickboard for all strokes.** Breaststroke swimmers may alternate 50 FS/50 BR on 800m.

**Awards:** Swimmers completing 800m/400m/200m under target times (long course) earn certificates → "12-Minute Club," "6-Minute Club," "3-Minute Club."

### Alternative Method

- 800m kick as fast as possible (record time)
- 400m target = half of 800m time
- 200m target = half of 400m time (recorded)
- 100m target = half of 200m time (recorded)
- 50m target = half of 100m time (recorded)
- 25m target = half of 50m time (recorded)

## 8. Speed Tests (Starts, Turns, Finishes, Swimming)

**Purpose:** Improve all race segments; establish baseline speed values

### Starts: 6 × 20m
- From command start, timed from head passing 15m → 20m
- Record times, compare across weeks

### Turns: 6 × 25m
- Start at least 12.5m from wall
- Timed from head passing 7.5m before turn → 7.5m after turn
- Target: **0.6 seconds** for freestyle/backstroke
- Butterfly/breaststroke: time from hands touching wall → feet hitting wall

### Finishes: 6 × 20m
- Sprint from 20m out
- Timed from flags (5m) → wall touch

### Swimming Speed: 6 × 25m (Push Start)
- Record times for all 6 swims
- Exclude fastest and slowest
- Average the middle 4 swims
- **This becomes your baseline swimming speed to improve throughout season**

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Test to set training paces** — don't guess  
✅ **7 × 200m step test** → anaerobic threshold training speeds  
✅ **8 × 50m efficiency test** → stroke count, stroke rate, race-pace targets  
✅ **Cold swims** → confirm racing speed under pressure  
✅ **40 × 100m progression** → build aerobic/threshold endurance  
✅ **Max HR test every 4-6 weeks** → individualize training zones  
✅ **Record everything** → times, stroke counts, heart rates, splits  
✅ **Swimmers who test, progress** — objective data beats guesswork
