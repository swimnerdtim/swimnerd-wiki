# Bill Sweeteenth Tapering for Competition

**The 21-Day Race Preparation Model — Volume Reduction, Intensity Maintenance, and Recovery Protocols for Championship Performance**

---

## Source

*Championship Swim Training* by Bill Sweetenham & John Atkinson (2003)  
**Author credentials:** Australian National Team Head Coach, British Olympic Head Coach, Great Britain Performance Director

---

## Core Philosophy

**"If in doubt, leave it out."**

Sweetenham's taper model prioritizes:
1. **Volume reduction** (not intensity reduction)
2. **Full recovery between sessions** (no carried fatigue)
3. **Race-pace work maintained** (but not increased)
4. **Mental preparation** (race plans, visualization, meal plans)
5. **Sleep and rest** (non-negotiable)

**Warning:** "Increasing, decreasing, introducing, or withdrawing only **ONE thing at a time** from a training program."

**Never:** Decrease volume AND increase intensity simultaneously = recipe for disaster.

---

## The 21-Day Model (For 15+ Years, Post-Maturation)

### Modified Tapers by Age

- **15+ years (post-maturation):** 21-day taper (full model)
- **15 years and under (pre-maturation):** 15-day taper (last 15 days of model)
- **Age-group (below maturation):** 10-day taper (last 10 days of model)

**Adaptation weeks throughout season = taper rehearsals.** Every adaptation week should mirror the first week of taper.

---

## 5-4 Weeks Out: Setting the Stage

### Training Changes Begin

**5 weeks out:**
- **Race preparation starts** in both morning and afternoon sessions
- **No increase in race-pace training volume** (maintain 10-15% of total volume)
- **Broken swims at goal pace** begin (exact 50m splits, stroke rate, stroke count)
- **Reduce critical speed and anaerobic work** according to monthly mesocycle model

### What Increases
- **Starts, turns, finishes** practice with high demand + constant feedback
- **Relay takeover** practice
- **Race-specific broken swims** (short rest initially, building to long rest closer to meet)

### What Decreases
- Critical speed training
- Lactate-tolerance work
- Anaerobic volume

**Kick sets:** Maintain through taper for lower-body fitness, but reduce volume.

**High-Velocity Overloads (HVOs):** Do NOT increase volume, but alternate placement:
- After warm-up (speed enhancement)
- Before swim-down (race enhancement)

### Dryland Training
**From 5 weeks out:**
- **Decrease repetitions**, increase load slightly
- **Significant strength/power gains occur during taper**
- Reduce gym sessions per week

### Competition Meal Planning
- Review meal list as a team
- Practice selecting from buffet meals
- Swimmers should prepare own meals during this period

---

## 4 Weeks Out: The Last Hard Week (Optional)

**Some coaches include a "last hard endurance-anaerobic week" 4 weeks out.**

**Rationale:** Final chance to make aerobic gains; body adjusts through last 3 weeks.

**Caution:** Can be counterproductive if:
- Swimmer is young or heavily muscular
- Swimmer has less aerobic background
- Volume of anaerobic work isn't reduced

**Better approach for most:** Hold aerobic volume steady in 5th and 4th weeks, then reduce.

### Plyometrics & Squats
**5 weeks out → 3 weeks out:**
- Perform **4 sets × 4 jumps** (plyometrics) daily
- Decrease by **1 set each week** (3 weeks out = 3 sets)
- Replace leg exercises in gym with easy running instead

## 23-21 Days Out: Rest and Reset

### 2-Day Rest for Coach & Athlete

**22nd or 23rd day out:** Rest for 2 days so taper starts with **fresh athlete and coach**.

### Pre-Competition Checklist (Review or Prepare)
- ✅ Self-monitoring chart
- ✅ Competition checklists
- ✅ Vivid visualization skills (practice daily)
- ✅ Prerace warm-up (heats, semifinals, finals)
- ✅ Relaxation skills
- ✅ Prerace plan (written)
- ✅ Race plan (written)
- ✅ Competition meal plan (menu)
- ✅ Familiarization with competition pool

## 21-14 Days Out: Week 1 of Taper

### Training Volume
**Drop to 60% of peak weekly volume** (see figure 14.2 in book).

**Example:** Peak week = 75km → Week 1 of taper = 45km

### Training Intensity
**After the 21st day:** No training faster than race pace, except:
- Short HVOs (25m or less)
- Sprint-assisted work (sprinters only)
- 6-stroke max sprints (50m/100m swimmers)

**Race pace = second 50m of goal pace** for 100m or 200m events.

### Recovery Protocol
**Swimmers must fully recover from one practice before the next.**

**No carried fatigue** = key to successful taper.

### Weekly Breakdown (Week 1)

| Training Type | Number of Sets |
|---|---|
| **Critical speed** | 2 |
| **Anaerobic/race-pace** | 1 |
| **HVOs** | 40 total for week |

**Sets are now 50-60% of highest weekly training volume while maintaining intensity.**

**50m repeats at back end of critical speed sets** to maintain race endurance (instead of longer repeats at start).

**Warning:** Excessive anaerobic/lactate work can lead to overstress. Disregarding heart rates can lead to detraining.

---

## 14-7 Days Out: Week 2 of Taper

### Training Volume
**Drop to 50% of peak weekly volume.**

**Example:** Peak week = 75km → Week 2 of taper = 37.5km

### Training Focus
- **Broken and split swims at race pace** from 10 days out
- **Exact pace work** (heat, semifinal, final strategies)
- **Perfect race stroke rates** at goal pace

### Weekly Breakdown (Week 2)

| Training Type | Number of Sets |
|---|---|
| **Critical speed** | 1 (mainly 50m repeats) + 1 pace set |
| **Anaerobic/race-pace** | 1 |
| **HVOs** | 30 total for week |

**Sets become more race-specific** (more race pace, less threshold work).

### Quality Work Reduction
Sweetenham recommends **dropping quality work by 5% per week** in last 3 weeks:
- Week 1 of taper: 15% race pace
- Week 2 of taper: 10% race pace
- Week 3 of taper: 5% race pace

**For 400m, 800m, 1500m swimmers:** Add 5-7% to these percentages.

### 10-Day Simulation (Optional)
**10 days before meet:**
- **Morning:** Full-blooded max-effort swim (slightly shorter race distance or different stroke)
- **Afternoon:** Race-specific broken swim
- **Purpose:** Simulate race day (meal, warm-up, race plan, swim-down)

### Critical Warning
**Avoid anaerobic (lactate) training within 10 days of meet.**

**If in doubt, leave it out.**

---

## 7-0 Days Out: Week 3 of Taper (Final Week)

### Training Volume
**Minimal volume** — rest and recovery all-important.

### Weekly Breakdown (Week 3)

| Training Type | Number of Sets |
|---|---|
| **Critical speed** | 1 |
| **Anaerobic/race-pace** | 1 (single-effort pace swims) |
| **HVOs** | 20 total for week |

### 6 Days Out
**Single-effort race-pace swims** (not broken) at goal pace.

**Focus:** Stroke rate, breathing pattern, starts, turns, finishes.

### 2-3 Days Out
**Team meeting:**
- State expectations
- Provide meet information
- Define relay responsibilities

**Individual daily conversations** with each swimmer.

### 2 Days Out
**Help swimmers relax:**
- Go to a movie
- Buy a new CD
- Forget about the meet temporarily
- Reduce pressure

### Diet and Sleep
**No changes in:**
- Daily food intake
- Weight
- Skinfold measurements

**Sleep:** 8 hours per night + 2 hours rest during day.

**No late nights.**

## Carbohydrate Intake

**Sweetenham's position:**
- **Substantial carbohydrate intake throughout season** (not just taper)
- **Carbohydrate loading in last 7-10 days is NOT necessary**
- Overdoing carb loading → fluid retention in muscle → detrimental effect
- **Carb loading only necessary for events >20 minutes**

**During competition:** Take in carbohydrate fuel during swim-downs.

---

## Warm-Up and Swim-Down

### Warm-Up Guidelines
- **Less rested = longer warm-up needed**
- **Sprinters generally need longer warm-up** than distance swimmers
- **Objectives:**
  - Prevent injury
  - Increase blood circulation
  - Slight rise in heart rate + core temp
  - Mental preparation
- **Don't overstress legs** with excessive starts/turns in warm-up (large muscle groups)

### Swim-Down Protocol
**Minimum 1000m**, with considerable amount at 50-60 BBM (beats below max).

**Add 4-5 short bursts of 10-15m during second 500m** to assist recovery.

**Take in carbohydrate fuel during swim-down.**

### Individual Checking Speed (ICS) Test
**Use ICS test at end of swim-down** to assess recovery status:
- 100m at PB + 15s (fly/back/free) or PB + 20s (breast)
- Record time, stroke count, stroke rate, breathing pattern, heart rate
- Compare to training values
- **Only when ALL values match training = full recovery**

## Heats, Semifinals, Finals Preparation

### The Challenge
**Reintroduction of heats/semis/finals** to international swimming means:
- **Winner = best prepared, not always fastest**
- **Most medal winners went progressively faster** through rounds
- **Swimmers who failed often couldn't improve with each performance**

### Training Progression (Over 3 Workouts)

**Workout 1: Heat**
- 200m within 3% of next age-group national standard

**Workout 2: Semifinal**
- 200m within 2% of next age-group national standard
- Broken at 100m until HR recovers to 30 BBM

**Workout 3: Final**
- 200m within 1% of next age-group national standard
- Broken at 50m until HR recovers to 30 BBM
- **Measure time lapsed in HR recovery**
- **30-40 minutes active recovery**
- **Then:** Max-effort 100m or 200m aiming within 3% of PB

**Purpose:** Prepare athletes to perform at high level through heats/semis/finals. Only 50m specialists might avoid this skill.

### The Three-Rock Visualization
**Swimmers find 3 small, flat river rocks:**
- Print heat, semifinal, and final goal times in permanent marker
- Carry rocks in pocket
- Tip rocks from pocket several times a day
- Select rock nearest the edge for a challenge swim
- **Visualize goals constantly**

## Dual-Peaking Situations

**For a second meet within 15-18 days:**
- **25-30% of time = aerobic work**
- **Repeat last 6 days of training from major meet**

**Example:** If second meet is 9 days away:
- 3 days aerobic training
- Repeat last 6 days of taper

**Recommendation:** 6 days after first meet, conduct **time trial** (simulates extended championship meet).

**During 6-day recovery:**
- Train once per day
- Soft aerobic work with fast "spiking" HVOs
- Establishes "getting back up after meets" mentality

## Resting for Midseason Meets

**Hardest-working athletes often experience less-than-desired results** with rest at midseason meets.

**Why?**
- Differential between work and rest causes backward adjustment in fitness
- Physical adaptations take time to solidify
- Initial rest can cause temporary performance dip

**Options for midseason meets:**
1. **Perform fast in solid training** (2-3 days rest maximum)
2. **Take full week of adaptation** (preferred for hard-working athletes)

**If no option:** Choose one:
- Keep meters constant, decrease intensity (preferred)
- Keep intensity constant, decrease meters

**Never penalize better/harder-working athletes** with forced rest.

## Male vs. Female Swimmers

**Female swimmers:**
- Taper over **longer period** than males
- Greater muscle mass in legs = more stroke-specific stress
- May require **slightly less rest** during taper to maintain feel

**Male sprinters:**
- Require **longer tapers**
- More dryland work
- More resistance training at speed

**Breaststroke swimmers:**
- Longer taper due to **greater leg muscle stress**

---

## Flat Spots and "Taper Blues"

**"Flat spots" will occur** — temporary adaptation response.

**Duration:** Rarely longer than 4-6 days.

**Why they happen:**
- Lack of mental stimulation/challenge
- Overemphasis on aerobic work only
- Insufficient intensity control (using heart rate)

**Solution:**
- Maintain race-pace work (don't eliminate it)
- Use heart rate to control intensity (not just repeat times)
- Keep mental challenge in workouts

**If flat spot occurs:** Understand it's temporary; use heart rate to guide recovery.

---

## Coach's Responsibility

**"It is the coach's responsibility to provide the best opportunities and conditions for the swimmers, while retaining the confidence of the swimmers to trust that their program will enable them to be at their best in the worst conditions."**

**Do not be afraid** to judge your program on competition performance of:
- Most talented athletes
- **AND** least talented athletes

**Both should show improvement.**

---

## Recording Variations

**Both swimmer and coach should record:**
- All variations during race-prep phase
- Sleep patterns
- Diet changes
- Heart rates
- Stroke counts
- Split times
- Mood/energy levels

**Use detailed notes for next competition.**

**"Many coaches will not remember meet conditions that influenced the athlete 12 months prior."**

---

## The Bottom Line

**Sleep + Rest > Pool Work**

"Reducing training in the pool and gym is pointless if the athlete subsequently increases other activities or has less quality rest during race-pace training periods."

**Quality rest = quality performance.**

## Key Takeaways

✅ **21-day taper** for post-maturation swimmers (15 days for pre-maturation, 10 days for age-group)  
✅ **Reduce volume 60% → 50% → minimal** over 3 weeks  
✅ **Maintain intensity** — no training faster than race pace after day 21  
✅ **Full recovery between sessions** — no carried fatigue  
✅ **Drop quality work 5% per week** (15% → 10% → 5%)  
✅ **Avoid lactate training within 10 days** of competition  
✅ **If in doubt, leave it out** — rest > extra work  
✅ **Practice heats/semis/finals** progressions in training  
✅ **Sleep 8 hours + 2 hours rest daily** — non-negotiable  
✅ **Record everything** — next taper will be better
