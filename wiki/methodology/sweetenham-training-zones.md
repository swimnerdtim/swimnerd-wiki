# Bill Sweetenham Training Zones & Systems

**Five Training Zones for Championship Swimming — How to Distribute Volume, Individualize Pace, and Monitor Heart Rate**

---

## Source

*Championship Swim Training* by Bill Sweetenham & John Atkinson (2003)  
**Author credentials:** Australian National Team Head Coach, British Olympic Head Coach, Great Britain Performance Director

---

## The Five Training Zones

Sweetenham's system classifies ALL swimming training into **five training zones** based on intensity and swimming velocity:

### Zone 1: Aerobic (A1, A2, A3)
- **Lowest intensity, highest volume**
- Heart rate: 30-40 beats below maximum (BBM)
- Should be the largest block of training for all swimmers

### Zone 2: Anaerobic Threshold
- **Moderate intensity, sustained pace**
- Heart rate: 20-30 BBM
- Second-largest training block for most swimmers

### Zone 3: High-Performance Endurance
- **Critical speed, lactate removal, MVO₂**
- Heart rate: 10-20 BBM
- Race-specific endurance work

### Zone 4: Anaerobic (Race Pace)
- **Lactic acid accumulating work**
- 400m pace or faster
- Smaller volume, high intensity

### Zone 5: Sprint
- **Maximum velocity**
- Shortest volume, highest intensity
- Alactic power development

## Training Volume Distribution by Swimmer Type

Sweetenham provides clear **percentage targets** for each zone:

| Swimmer Type | Zone 1 (Aerobic) | Zones 2-3 (Threshold/HPE) | Zones 4-5 (Race Pace/Sprint) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Age-group & youth** | 70% | 20% | 10% |
| **Male sprinters** | 80% | 10% | 10% |
| **Reverse periodization sprinters** | 50% | 20% | 30% |
| **Female endurance swimmers** | 60% | 30% | 10% |
| **Senior swimmers (post-maturation)** | 70% | 20% | 10% |
| **Masters** | 85% | 10% | 5% |
| **Triathletes** | 65% | 25% | 10% |

**Key insight:** Zone 1 dominates weekly volume for nearly all swimmer types except reverse-periodization sprinters.

## Individualizing Training Zones

**Critical principle:** "What you expect and what you get are often very different."

Sweetenham emphasizes **two methods** for individualizing pace:

### Method 1: Heart Rate Below Maximum

**Don't prescribe a fixed heart rate** (e.g., "everyone swims at 160 bpm"). This ignores individual differences.

**Example:**
- Swimmer A: Max HR = 201 → Zone 1 = 161 bpm (40 BBM)
- Swimmer B: Max HR = 181 → Zone 1 = 141 bpm (40 BBM)

Both swimmers train at **their own appropriate zone 1 intensity**, even though heart rates differ by 20 beats.

**Wrong way:** Prescribing 160 bpm for both swimmers would put Swimmer B in Zone 2 (overtraining), while Swimmer A would be in Zone 1.

### Method 2: Personal Best + Constant

**Set training repeat times based on each swimmer's PB**, not the team's fastest swimmer.

**Example:** 10 × 100m freestyle on 1:30
- Swimmer A (PB 1:00) → Target 1:10-1:12 (PB + 10-12 seconds)
- Swimmer B (PB 1:10) → Target 1:20-1:22 (PB + 10-12 seconds)

Both swimmers work at **the same relative intensity** for their fitness level.

## Monitoring Training Intensity

**"Monitor training in all zones — what you expect and what you get are often very different."**

### Why Monitoring Matters

If swimmers perform **Zone 1 work at excessive intensity**:
- They become too fatigued for quality work in Zones 2-3
- Chronic overtraining develops
- Performance stagnates

**Solution:** Track heart rates, stroke counts, and split times for every training set.

## Race-Pace Training (Zone 4)

**Definition:** Any training at **400m pace or faster**

### Guidelines
- Should be **10-15% of total weekly volume** for most swimmers
- **Distance swimmers (400m, 800m, 1500m):** Increase to 15-20% of total volume
- **Sprinters:** Can reach 30% in reverse-periodization programs

**Warning:** Increasing race-pace volume without reducing aerobic volume = recipe for disaster.

---

## High-Velocity Overloads (HVOs)

**Short, max-effort swims** to develop top-end speed:
- **Duration:** 10-25m swims
- **Frequency:** 400-600m daily for sprinters
- **Placement:** Alternate between:
  - **After warm-up** (speed enhancement)
  - **Before swim-down** (race enhancement)

**Variations:**
- Swim, kick, pull, drill
- With paddles/fins (holding race stroke rate)
- Tethered sprints (resistance & assistance work)

---

## Golden Rule: One Change at a Time

**Sweetenham's warning:**

> "Increase, decrease, introduce, or withdraw only **ONE thing at a time** from a training program."

**Never:**
- Decrease volume AND increase intensity simultaneously
- Add new training types without reducing something else

## Training for Male vs. Female Swimmers

### Female Swimmers
- **Taper over a longer period** than males
- Reason: Greater muscle mass in legs = more stress in stroke-specific work
- May require slightly less rest during taper to maintain feel

### Male Sprinters
- **Longer tapers** (reverse periodization)
- More dryland work
- More resistance training at speed

---

## Practical Application: Weekly Training Blocks

Sweetenham recommends a **monthly mesocycle** structure:

| Week | Critical Speed Sets | Anaerobic/Race-Pace Sets | HVOs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | 3 | 1 | 40 |
| Week 2 | 2 | 2 | 50 |
| Week 3 | 1 | 3 | 60 |
| Week 4 (adaptation/taper rehearsal) | 1 | 1 | 30 |

**Adaptation weeks** = built-in recovery + taper rehearsal.

## Heart Rate Testing

### Maximum Heart-Rate Test
**Purpose:** Establish each swimmer's true max HR (not age-based formula)

**Protocol (200m+ swimmers):**
1. 100m build to maximum
2. 100m build to maximum
3. 3 × 100m at PB + 4 seconds (RI 10s, take HR after each)
4. 2-3 × 50m if required (RI 10s, take HR after each)

**When to stop:** Heart rate stops rising or begins to drop.

**Frequency:** Test every 4-6 weeks.

**Key insight:** Maximum heart rate varies by:
- Age
- Sex (females 5-10 bpm lower than males)
- Genetic profile
- Training load in days before test

---

## Why This System Works

1. **Individualized:** Swimmers train at appropriate intensities for their fitness
2. **Monitored:** Heart rate, stroke count, split times tracked consistently
3. **Progressive:** Volume and intensity manipulated separately
4. **Specific:** Training zones match energy system demands of events
5. **Recoverable:** Adaptation weeks prevent overtraining

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Five zones** cover all swimming training (aerobic → sprint)  
✅ **Zone 1 dominates** weekly volume (60-85% for most swimmers)  
✅ **Individualize pace** using heart rate below maximum OR PB + constant  
✅ **Monitor religiously** — expectations ≠ reality without tracking  
✅ **One change at a time** — never decrease volume AND increase intensity  
✅ **HVOs daily** — short speed work maintains top-end velocity  
✅ **Test max HR** every 4-6 weeks to keep zones accurate
