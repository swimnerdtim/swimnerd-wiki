# Swimming for Health & Longevity: Why It's the Best Exercise

**Source:** *The Complete Book of Swimming* by Dr. Phillip Whitten (1994), Chapters 1-5, 12  
**Context:** Scientific + anecdotal evidence for swimming's profound health benefits  
**Audience:** Coaches motivating reluctant swimmers, adults considering taking up the sport

---

## The Central Claim: Swimming is Universally Recognized as the Best All-Around Exercise

**Whitten's thesis (1994):**
> "Virtually all experts now recognize that swimming is the best and most complete form of exercise there is."

**Why:**
1. Same **aerobic benefits** as running
2. Works **all muscles** of the body (not just legs)
3. **Zero impact** on joints/connective tissue → rare injuries
4. **Psychological benefits:** self-esteem, stress relief, enhanced sex life
5. **Accessible to all ages:** 8 to 108

**Supported by:** The New York Times, Los Angeles Times, medical consensus (1990s forward)

## Case Study 1: Paul Bragg—"Rest is Rust"

**Profile:**
- Age when Whitten met him: 98
- Background: Frail, sickly child; contracted polio at age 14
- Prognosis: Lifetime as invalid

**His solution:** "Constant activity and exercise—swimming, tennis, running, and lots of sunshine."

**Achievements:**
- Wrestler on TWO U.S. Olympic teams
- Practicing osteopath
- Expert on nutrition
- Author of 100+ books on health
- **Oldest person ever to swim competitively** (at age 98)

**Daily routine at 98:**
- Swam in Waikiki every day
- Jogged up to 5 miles, multiple times per week
- Ate clean diet

**Motto:** "Rest is rust."

**Outcome:** Became a living legend on Honolulu's Waikiki Beach.

## Case Study 2: Nellie Brown—"Unsinkable Nellie"

**Profile:**
- Age: 87 (when interviewed)
- Occupation: Retired first-grade teacher
- Background: Childhood polio left one leg 2 inches shorter than the other

**The crisis (age 68, year 1961):**
> "I could barely walk anymore. I was afraid I'd have to spend the rest of my life in a wheelchair. For the first time in my life, I really felt old. I felt foolish and... just plain useless. It was frustrating and depressing."

**Doctor's prescription:** Swimming as therapy for her legs.

**Her reaction:** "I wanted to learn. I had no intention of being an invalid. I've got too much living to do!"

**Age she learned to swim:** 68 years old.

**Outcome at age 87:**
- Swimming **1 mile per day**
- Teaching swimming classes for Alexandria's mentally retarded and handicapped children
- In demand for media appearances (Good Morning America, local newspapers)
- Named Alexandria's Woman of the Year
- Looked "at least 20 years younger"

**Her assessment:**
> "Swimming gives me the exercise I need to keep going. It was easy to learn to swim, even at an advanced age."

**Lesson:** You CAN teach an old dog new tricks.

## The Sex Life Connection: Whitten's Research Study

**Study design:** Survey of Masters swimmers (competitive adult swimmers)

**Finding 1: Frequency of Sexual Activity**

**Question:** "How does your current level of sexual activity compare to when you were in your 30s?"

| Age Group | Same or More Frequent | Less Frequent |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------|
| 40s       | 92%                  | 8%            |
| 50s       | 88%                  | 12%           |
| 60s       | 86%                  | 14%           |

**Translation:** Nearly 90% of swimmers in their 40s-60s report having sex AS OFTEN or MORE OFTEN than they did at age 30.

**Finding 2: Physical Attractiveness**

**Self-ratings:**
- 80% rated themselves in top two categories ("very attractive" or "attractive")
- 20% rated themselves "average"
- **0% rated themselves below average**

**Partner ratings:**
- Partners rated the swimmers **even more attractive** than swimmers rated themselves

**Finding 3: Age Perception**

**Swimmers viewed themselves as:**
- **Younger** than their chronological age
- **More attractive** than others their age

**Partners agreed.**

### Why Swimming Enhances Sex Life

**Three hypotheses:**

#### 1. Testosterone Hypothesis (Weak Evidence)
- Moderate exercise increases testosterone in men AND women
- But: Testosterone elevation is short-lived after exercise
- Unclear link between testosterone level and sexual activity in humans (unlike other species)

#### 2. Growth Hormone Hypothesis (Promising)
- Human growth hormone (hGH) secreted during vigorous exercise + deep sleep
- hGH produces physiological changes associated with youth:
  - Increased muscle mass
  - Decreased body fat
  - Increased skin elasticity
- Younger people have sex more often
- **Hypothesis:** Increased sexual activity is byproduct of increased hGH production

#### 3. Psychological Factors (Strong Evidence)

**Dr. Helen Singer Kaplan (Human Sexuality Program, NY Hospital-Cornell):**
> "Chronic stress can be devastating to sexual functioning, turning lovemaking from a pleasurable sharing of intimacy into an impossible act."

**Stress & depression → increased cortisol → suppressed sex hormones → decreased libido**

**Swimming combats this:**
- Powerful anti-depressant (releases endorphins)
- Relieves stress
- Decreases cortisol
- Restores normal sex hormone production
- Improves neurochemical balance

**Dr. Shirley Zussman (Association for Male Sexual Dysfunction):**
> "The way you view yourself and the way you appear in your mate's eyes is very important. If you feel your partner sees you as desirable, you're more able to free yourself and enjoy the sexual experience."

**Body image matters:**
- Swimming improves body composition (less fat, more muscle)
- Swimmers see attractive, fit people at pool
- Receive positive feedback about their own appearance
- Increased self-esteem → sexual confidence

**Dr. Linda De Villers:**
> "Sexual confidence and satisfaction are highly correlated with a fit body."

**Example:** Many women avoid female-superior position during sex because "He can see my fat stomach." Swimming transforms that insecurity.

#### 4. Physiological + Psychological Interaction

**Stress constricts peripheral arteries → prevents blood flow to genitals → inhibits arousal**

**Swimming:**
- Opens blood vessels
- Improves circulation
- Enhances genital blood flow

**Obesity → excess fat converts testosterone to estrogen (in men) → decreased desire, erectile dysfunction, breast enlargement**

**Swimming:**
- Reduces body fat
- Restores testosterone balance
- Revitalizes libido

## The Anti-Aging Effect: Physiological Mechanisms

### Swimming Slows Aging Through:

1. **Cardiovascular Protection**
   - Strengthens heart muscle
   - Lowers resting heart rate
   - Reduces blood pressure
   - Improves circulation
   - Decreases heart disease + stroke risk

2. **Metabolic Benefits**
   - Improves insulin sensitivity → reduces diabetes risk
   - Increases metabolic rate
   - Burns fat efficiently
   - Maintains healthy weight

3. **Musculoskeletal Preservation**
   - Maintains muscle mass (fights sarcopenia)
   - Preserves bone density (especially important for women post-menopause)
   - Keeps joints flexible
   - Zero impact → protects connective tissue

4. **Hormonal Optimization**
   - Increases hGH production
   - Balances cortisol (stress hormone)
   - Optimizes sex hormones
   - Improves mood-regulating neurotransmitters

5. **Immune System Support**
   - Reduces chronic inflammation
   - Strengthens immune response
   - May reduce cancer risk (preliminary evidence)

6. **Cognitive Function**
   - Increases blood flow to brain
   - Promotes neurogenesis (new brain cell growth)
   - Improves memory + mental clarity
   - Reduces dementia risk

- Age: 32
- Event: 400 IM (hardest swimming event) at U.S. National Masters Championships
- Condition: **6 months pregnant**
- Result: Broke world record by 6 seconds

**Case: Leslie Craven**
- Age: 32

- Timing: **2 days before giving birth** to 8-pound baby boy
- Returned to pool: 3 days post-birth
- Quote: "I tried to swim every day of pregnancy. It felt wonderful—relaxing and soothing."

**Case: Kimberly Fagan (Tulsa, OK)**
- Problem: Chronic back pain since early teens
- Doctor's Rx: Swim several days/week during pregnancy
- Result: "Within a week the backaches just disappeared. I felt better when I was pregnant than I did before."

**Dr. Desider Rothe (Clinical Professor, OB-GYN, NY Hospital-Cornell):**
> "Not only is swimming the best all-around exercise there is but it is especially well-suited to the needs of pregnant women."

**Dr. Jane Katz (*Swimming Through Your Pregnancy*):**
> "Swimming helps you enjoy your pregnancy and prepare for labor and delivery by gently toning and strengthening your body. Your labor and delivery will be easier, and your recovery and return to fitness and figure will be quicker."

### Melpomene Institute Study (Minneapolis)

**Compared three groups of pregnant women:**
1. Swimmers
2. Runners
3. Sedentary (no exercise)

**Results:**

| Benefit | Swimmers | Runners | Sedentary |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Easier deliveries | ✓ | ✓ | ✗ |
| Less postpartum depression | ✓ | ✓ | ✗ |
| Faster return to normal weight | ✓ | ✓ | ✗ |
| **Negative outcomes (spontaneous abortions + stillbirths)** | **1/4 as many** | Higher | Higher |

**Critical finding:** Swimmers had **75% fewer** pregnancy losses than runners or sedentary women.

### Breast Cancer Protection

**The epidemic:**
- 1 in 9 American women will develop breast cancer (1994 data)
- Up from 1 in 20 two decades prior
- 200,000 new cases/year
- 46,000 deaths/year (1993)

**Suspected causes:**
- Sedentary lifestyle
- High-fat diet
- Chronic stress

**Swimming as prevention:**
- All three factors are modifiable
- Regular exercise (like swimming) significantly reduces risk
- Balanced diet + stress reduction compound the effect

**Note:** Whitten's book predates many large-scale studies, but subsequent research has confirmed exercise's protective effect against breast cancer.

## Why Swimming Beats Other Exercises

### vs. Running/Jogging:
- **Impact:** Swimming = zero; running = high (joint/connective tissue damage)
- **Injury rate:** Swimming = rare; running = common (shin splints, knee problems, stress fractures)
- **Muscles worked:** Swimming = full body; running = primarily legs
- **Aging accessibility:** Swimming = lifelong; running = many quit due to injury

### vs. Tennis/Racquetball:
- **Muscle balance:** Swimming = both sides equally; racquet sports = one-sided (creates imbalances)
- **Injury risk:** Swimming = low; racquet sports = moderate (rotator cuff, tennis elbow)
- **Cardio consistency:** Swimming = continuous; racquet sports = intermittent

### vs. Aerobics:
- **Impact:** Swimming = zero; aerobics = moderate to high
- **Injury risk:** Swimming = rare; aerobics = shin splints, knee problems
- **Cooling:** Swimming = water cools body; aerobics = overheating common
- **Skill progression:** Swimming = infinite technique refinement; aerobics = plateau quickly

### vs. Cycling:
- **Full-body engagement:** Swimming = yes; cycling = primarily legs
- **Bone density:** Swimming = maintains (weight-bearing in water); cycling = doesn't build bone
- **Weather dependence:** Swimming = indoor option always available; cycling = weather-dependent
- **Injury risk:** Swimming = low; cycling = road hazards, overuse injuries

**The unique advantage:** Swimming combines ALL the benefits with NONE of the downsides.

## Coaching Application: Motivating Adults to Start Swimming

### Common Objections + Responses:

**"I'm too old to learn."**
- Nellie Brown learned at 68, competed successfully
- Many Masters swimmers start in 40s, 50s, 60s
- Technique improvement continues for life (Abe Olanoff at 88)

**"I'm too out of shape."**
- Water supports body weight (near-zero impact)
- Progress comes FAST (Roberta Kresch example)
- Start with 1 lap, build gradually

**"I don't have time."**
- 30 minutes, 3x/week = significant health benefits
- More time-efficient than driving to tennis courts, waiting for court time
- Can swim any time pool is open

**"I'm embarrassed about my body."**
- Everyone in the pool is focused on their own workout
- Body transformation happens quickly (weeks, not months)
- Swimmers are supportive community

**"I tried exercise before and quit."**
- Swimming has built-in variety (4 strokes, equipment, interval sets)
- Social aspect (Masters teams) increases adherence
- Low injury rate = no forced breaks

### The Sales Pitch:

**"Swimming is like taking a daily vacation."** —Judy Collins

**Three-month transformation promise:**
1. **Month 1:** Learn basics, build endurance, reduce stress
2. **Month 2:** Visible body changes, increased energy, better sleep
3. **Month 3:** Confidence in water, social connections, looking forward to workouts

**The compound effect:**
- Physical: Stronger, leaner, more energetic
- Mental: Less stress, better mood, sharper thinking
- Social: New friends, team camaraderie
- Sexual: Increased desire + confidence
- Longevity: Adding years to life, life to years
