# Taper Design Guidelines

**How to Build an Optimal Taper — Volume, Intensity, Frequency, and Duration Protocols**

---

## The Four Taper Variables

A successful taper requires manipulating **four key training variables**:

1. **Training Volume** (total meters per week)
2. **Training Intensity** (speed/effort per set)
3. **Training Frequency** (sessions per week)
4. **Taper Duration** (total days of reduced load)

**The golden rule:** **Reduce volume aggressively. Maintain or increase intensity. Keep frequency high (or slightly reduce). Duration = 2-3 weeks.**

---

## Variable 1: Training Volume (Total Meters)

### Recommended Reduction

**Research consensus:** Reduce training volume by **41% to 60%** during taper for optimal performance gains.

**More aggressive reductions (60-90%) also work** but may risk psychological discomfort ("Am I doing enough?").

**Meta-analysis findings:**
- **<20% reduction:** Insufficient recovery, minimal performance gain
- **20-40% reduction:** Moderate improvement (1-2%)
- **41-60% reduction:** **Optimal improvement (2-4%)**
- **60-90% reduction:** Large improvement (2-6%) but higher risk of perceived "undertraining"
- **>90% reduction:** Risk of detraining (especially if taper is long)

**Key insight:** Coaches often under-taper (reduce volume too little). **Be bold.** Cut volume by HALF or more.

---

### Example Volume Reductions (Swimming)

**Pre-taper training load:** 50,000m/week (10,000m/day × 5 days)

| Taper Type | Week 1 Volume | Week 2 Volume | Total Reduction |
|------------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Conservative** | 35,000m (30% ↓) | 30,000m (40% ↓) | 40% |
| **Moderate (recommended)** | 30,000m (40% ↓) | 25,000m (50% ↓) | 50% |
| **Aggressive** | 25,000m (50% ↓) | 20,000m (60% ↓) | 60% |
| **Very Aggressive** | 20,000m (60% ↓) | 15,000m (70% ↓) | 70% |

**Recommendation for most swimmers:** Start with **50% reduction** (25,000m/week). Adjust based on individual response in smaller meets.

---

### How to Reduce Volume

**Two primary methods:**

#### Method 1: Reduce Reps Per Set
**Example:**
- **Pre-taper:** 10 × 200m @ race pace, rest 2:00
- **Taper:** 5 × 200m @ race pace, rest 2:00 (same intensity, half the reps)

#### Method 2: Reduce Sets Per Session
**Example:**
- **Pre-taper:** Main set = 3 sets of 5 × 100m (1,500m total)
- **Taper:** Main set = 2 sets of 5 × 100m (1,000m total)

**DO NOT:**
- ❌ Shorten individual reps (e.g., 100s → 50s) if your race is 200m+
- ❌ Reduce intensity to cut volume (defeats the purpose)

**Key principle:** **Quality over quantity.** Fewer reps, same speed = optimal taper.

## Variable 2: Training Intensity (Speed/Effort)

### Recommended Level

**Non-negotiable rule:** **Maintain intensity at ≥80-90% of pre-taper levels.**

**Research finding:** Reducing intensity during taper = **rapid detraining**.

- **Houmard et al. (1990):** Reducing intensity to <70% during taper resulted in **VO₂ max decline** and **zero performance improvement** in runners.
- **Mujika et al. (2002):** Swimmers who maintained intensity during taper improved **2.2%**; those who reduced intensity showed **no improvement**.

**Translation for coaches:**
- If pre-taper race-pace set = 10 × 100m @ 1:05 (race pace)
- During taper = 5 × 100m @ 1:05 **(same pace, fewer reps)**

**DO NOT slow down the intervals to "save energy."** The taper is not rest — it's **sharpening**.

---

### Intensity Distribution During Taper

**Recommended weekly intensity breakdown:**

| Intensity Zone | Pre-Taper (%) | Taper Week 1 (%) | Taper Week 2 (%) |
|----------------|---------------|------------------|------------------|
| **Race Pace (Zone 2-3)** | 15-20% | 20-25% | 25-30% |
| **Threshold (Zone 4)** | 25-30% | 20-25% | 15-20% |
| **Aerobic (Zone 5)** | 50-60% | 50-55% | 45-50% |
| **Recovery (Zone 6)** | 5-10% | 5-10% | 5-10% |

**Key insight:** **Increase proportion of race-pace work** as taper progresses. Reduce threshold volume but maintain some to stay "sharp."

---

### Example Taper Week Intensity Plan

**Week 1 of Taper (200m Swimmer):**

**Monday:**
- Warm-up: 1,000m easy
- **Main set:** 5 × 200m @ race pace (Zone 2), rest 3:00
- Cool-down: 500m easy
- **Total:** 4,000m (50% of pre-taper 8,000m session)

**Wednesday:**
- Warm-up: 800m
- **Main set:** 8 × 100m @ threshold pace (Zone 4), rest 1:30
- **Sprint set:** 4 × 25m all-out (Zone 1), rest 2:00
- Cool-down: 400m
- **Total:** 3,200m

**Friday:**
- Warm-up: 1,000m
- **Main set:** 3 × 300m @ aerobic pace (Zone 5), rest 2:00
- **Race-pace set:** 4 × 50m @ race pace, rest 1:00
- Cool-down: 500m
- **Total:** 3,500m

**Total weekly volume:** ~21,000m (58% reduction from 50,000m)  
**Intensity:** Race pace maintained, threshold slightly reduced, aerobic volume cut

## Variable 3: Training Frequency (Sessions Per Week)

### Recommended Frequency

**Research finding:** **Maintain training frequency** or reduce by **≤20%**.

**Why?**
- Frequency ≥3 sessions/week maintains physiological adaptations
- Dropping to 1-2 sessions/week = rapid detraining (VO₂ max declines, muscle glycogen oxidative capacity drops)

**Example:**
- **Pre-taper:** 10 sessions/week (2x/day, 5 days)
- **Taper Week 1:** 8 sessions/week (2x/day 3 days, 1x/day 2 days) — **20% reduction**
- **Taper Week 2:** 7 sessions/week (2x/day 2 days, 1x/day 3 days) — **30% reduction**

**DO NOT:**
- ❌ Drop to 3 sessions/week or less (unless taper is very short, e.g., 7 days)
- ❌ Skip training entirely for 4-5 days before the meet ("I'll save energy")

**Key principle:** **Stay in the pool.** Frequency maintains adaptations. Volume reduction provides recovery.

---

### When to Reduce Frequency More Aggressively

**Scenario 1: Overtrained athlete**
- If baseline fatigue is very high (chronic overreaching), frequency can drop to 4-5 sessions/week during taper.

**Scenario 2: Masters swimmers (35+ years old)**
- Older athletes may need longer recovery between sessions. Frequency can drop to 5-6 sessions/week.

**Scenario 3: Short taper (7-10 days)**
- Maintain frequency high (8-10 sessions/week) to prevent detraining during brief taper.

---

## Variable 4: Taper Duration (Total Days)

### Recommended Duration

**Research consensus:** **8 to 21 days** (1-3 weeks) is optimal for most swimmers.

**More specific guidelines by event:**

| Event Distance | Optimal Taper Duration | Rationale |
|----------------|------------------------|-----------|
| **50m Sprint** | 7-14 days | Short events = less fatigue accumulation, shorter taper needed |
| **100m Sprint** | 10-14 days | Moderate taper to recover neuromuscular power |
| **200m Mid-Distance** | 14-21 days | Longer taper to fully replenish glycogen, clear lactate system |
| **400m Distance** | 14-21 days | Maximum glycogen supercompensation needed |
| **800m-1500m Distance** | 14-21 days | Aerobic base maintained, glycogen maximized |

**Key insight:** **Sprinters need shorter tapers** (7-14 days). **Distance swimmers need longer tapers** (14-21 days).

---

### What Happens at Different Taper Lengths?

**Too Short (<7 days):**
- Incomplete recovery
- Muscle glycogen not fully restored
- Neuromuscular fatigue persists
- Performance gain: **0.5-1.5%** (minimal)

**Optimal (8-21 days):**
- Full glycogen supercompensation
- Neuromuscular power peaks
- Hormonal balance restored
- Performance gain: **2-4%** (maximal)

**Too Long (>21 days):**
- Detraining begins (VO₂ max declines)
- Muscle atrophy starts
- Aerobic capacity drops
- Performance gain: **0-2%** (declining)

**Exception:** Some elite swimmers use **4-week tapers** (e.g., Bob Bowman for Michael Phelps at 2008 Olympics). But this requires **very careful** intensity maintenance and is NOT recommended for most athletes.

---

### Example Taper Timelines

**Scenario 1: Conference Championships (2-week taper)**

| Days Before Meet | Phase | Volume Reduction | Intensity | Focus |
|------------------|-------|------------------|-----------|-------|
| **14-10** | Early Taper | 30-40% ↓ | 90% maintained | Remove fatigue, maintain sharpness |
| **9-5** | Mid Taper | 50-60% ↓ | 95% maintained | Glycogen loading, race-pace work |
| **4-1** | Final Taper | 60-70% ↓ | 100% maintained | Speed maintenance, mental prep |

**Scenario 2: Olympic Trials (3-week taper)**

| Days Before Meet | Phase | Volume Reduction | Intensity | Focus |
|------------------|-------|------------------|-----------|-------|
| **21-15** | Early Taper | 30-40% ↓ | 85-90% | Initial recovery, threshold work |
| **14-8** | Mid Taper | 50-60% ↓ | 90-95% | Glycogen supercompensation begins |
| **7-1** | Final Taper | 70-80% ↓ | 100% | Peak sharpness, starts/turns, mental rehearsal |

## Environmental Factors and Tapering

### Jet Lag

**Problem:** Crossing multiple time zones disrupts circadian rhythm, impairs sleep, reduces performance.

**Taper adjustment:**
- Arrive **3-7 days early** to acclimate (1 day per time zone crossed)
- **Extend taper by 2-3 days** if traveling >6 time zones
- Prioritize sleep hygiene during taper (dark room, consistent bedtime)

**Example:**
- Normal taper: 14 days
- Travel to Europe from USA (6-hour time difference): **17-day taper** (arrive 5 days early)

---

### Heat Acclimatization

**Problem:** Competing in hot/humid environments without acclimatization = dehydration, hyperthermia, performance decline.

**Taper adjustment:**
- Train in heat for **7-14 days before taper** (30-60 min/day in hot conditions)
- During taper, **reduce heat exposure** but maintain some (2-3 sessions/week, 20-30 min)
- Hydrate aggressively (monitor urine color, weigh before/after sessions)

**Example:**
- Competing in outdoor 50m pool in summer heat
- Pre-taper: 10 days of heat training (60 min/day)
- Taper: 2-3 heat sessions/week (20 min easy swimming in warmest part of day)

---

### Altitude Training

**Problem:** Training at altitude improves red blood cell production (EPO effect) but requires recovery time at sea level to realize benefits.

**Taper adjustment:**
- **"Live high, train low" approach:** Live at altitude (2,000-2,500m), train at sea level
- If training at altitude, **descend to sea level 14-21 days before competition** (EPO effect peaks 2-3 weeks after descent)
- Combine altitude descent with taper (volume reduction begins upon return to sea level)

**Example:**
- Altitude camp: 3 weeks at 2,200m
- Descend to sea level: 21 days before competition
- Begin taper immediately upon descent (coincide recovery from altitude with taper recovery)

## Psychological Aspects of Tapering

### Common Athlete Concerns

**"I feel sluggish during the first week of taper. Am I losing fitness?"**

**Answer:** NO. This is **glycogen supercompensation** (muscles storing extra fuel + water). You're actually getting STRONGER. The "heavy legs" feeling disappears by week 2.

**"I have so much energy, I want to swim more. Can I add extra sets?"**

**Answer:** NO. **Trust the taper.** Excess energy = sign that recovery is working. Adding volume now = sabotaging the taper.

**"My stroke feels off. Should I do more technique work?"**

**Answer:** **Maintain normal technique volume** (don't increase). Reduced volume may make stroke feel different, but performance will peak on race day. Focus on **quality** (perfect technique on fewer reps).

---

### Psychological Skills During Taper

**Recommended mental training strategies:**

1. **Visualization:** 10-15 min/day imagining perfect race execution (start, turns, pacing, finish)
2. **Goal setting:** Review race goals daily (process goals, not just outcome goals)
3. **Positive self-talk:** Replace "I'm not doing enough" with "I'm recovering to peak"
4. **Sleep hygiene:** 8-9 hours/night, consistent bedtime, dark room, no screens 1 hour before bed
5. **Journaling:** Track mood, sleep quality, energy levels daily (helps identify optimal taper length for future)

**Key insight:** **Mental training replaces physical volume.** The taper is when psychological preparation intensifies.

## Taper Design Checklist

Use this checklist to design your taper:

### Step 1: Determine Taper Duration
- [ ] Event distance identified (50m vs. 1500m)
- [ ] Taper length set (sprinters: 7-14 days; distance: 14-21 days)
- [ ] Environmental factors considered (jet lag, heat, altitude)

### Step 2: Calculate Volume Reduction
- [ ] Pre-taper weekly volume measured (e.g., 50,000m/week)
- [ ] Target reduction determined (41-60% for optimal results)
- [ ] Weekly progression planned (exponential decay preferred)

### Step 3: Set Intensity Protocol
- [ ] Race-pace work maintained or increased (≥90% of pre-taper speed)
- [ ] Threshold work slightly reduced but not eliminated
- [ ] Aerobic work reduced proportionally with volume
- [ ] Sprint work maintained (starts, turns, breakouts)

### Step 4: Plan Training Frequency
- [ ] Pre-taper frequency documented (e.g., 10 sessions/week)
- [ ] Taper frequency set (≥80% of pre-taper, or 8+ sessions/week)
- [ ] Session distribution planned (e.g., 2x/day → 1x/day on some days)

### Step 5: Monitor and Adjust
- [ ] Daily logs kept (volume, intensity, mood, sleep, energy)
- [ ] Mid-taper time trial scheduled (optional, to assess progress)
- [ ] Individual response tracked (compare to previous tapers)

## Sample 2-Week Exponential Taper (200m Swimmer)

**Pre-taper weekly load:** 50,000m (10,000m/day × 5 days, 10 sessions/week)  
**Target reduction:** 50% (25,000m/week by end of taper)  
**Taper type:** Exponential (fast decay)

### Week 1 (Days 14-8 Before Meet)

| Day | AM Session | PM Session | Daily Volume | Notes |
|-----|------------|------------|--------------|-------|
| **Mon** | 3,500m (aerobic base, drills) | 3,500m (race pace: 5×200m @ 2:10, rest 3:00) | 7,000m | 30% reduction |
| **Tue** | 3,000m (easy, technique) | 3,000m (threshold: 8×100m @ 1:05, rest 1:30) | 6,000m | 40% reduction |
| **Wed** | 3,500m (kick, pull, swim) | 3,000m (race pace: 4×200m @ 2:10, rest 3:00) | 6,500m | 35% reduction |
| **Thu** | 2,500m (easy recovery) | 3,000m (mixed: 6×100m race pace, 4×50m sprint) | 5,500m | 45% reduction |
| **Fri** | 3,000m (aerobic, drills) | OFF | 3,000m | 70% reduction (active recovery day) |

**Week 1 Total:** 28,000m (44% reduction from 50,000m)  
**Frequency:** 9 sessions (10% reduction from 10 sessions)

---

### Week 2 (Days 7-1 Before Meet)

| Day | AM Session | PM Session | Daily Volume | Notes |
|-----|------------|------------|--------------|-------|
| **Mon** | 2,500m (easy, technique) | 2,500m (race pace: 4×200m @ 2:09, rest 4:00) | 5,000m | 50% reduction |
| **Tue** | 2,000m (warm-up, drills) | 2,500m (speed: 6×50m race pace, 4×25m sprint) | 4,500m | 55% reduction |
| **Wed** | 2,500m (easy, kick, pull) | 2,000m (race pace: 3×200m @ 2:09, rest 5:00) | 4,500m | 55% reduction |
| **Thu** | 2,000m (easy recovery) | 2,000m (starts, turns, breakouts) | 4,000m | 60% reduction |
| **Fri** | 1,500m (easy warm-up, 2×100m race pace) | OFF | 1,500m | 85% reduction (pre-race sharpening) |
| **Sat** | 1,000m (activation: easy swim, 4×50m build) | **RACE** | 1,000m | Race day warm-up |

**Week 2 Total:** 20,500m (59% reduction from 50,000m)  
**Frequency:** 8 sessions (20% reduction from 10 sessions)

**Total Taper Volume (2 weeks):** 48,500m (51% reduction from 100,000m over 2 weeks)

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Volume: Reduce 41-60%** — Cut meters in HALF or more (coaches under-taper too often)  
✅ **Intensity: Maintain ≥90%** — Speed stays HIGH; never slow down to "save energy"  
✅ **Frequency: Reduce ≤20%** — Stay in the pool; frequency maintains adaptations  
✅ **Duration: 8-21 days** — Sprinters shorter (7-14), distance longer (14-21)  
✅ **Exponential taper is best** — Front-load recovery, prevent detraining  
✅ **Adjust for environment** — Jet lag, heat, altitude require extended tapers  
✅ **Mental training intensifies** — Visualization, goal review, sleep hygiene are critical  
✅ **Trust the process** — Sluggishness in Week 1 is normal (glycogen loading); energy peaks in Week 2
