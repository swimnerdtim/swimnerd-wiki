# Taper & Peaking Strategies for Swimming

**Taper is the controlled reduction of training load to maximize performance at a target competition.**

When done right, taper can improve performance **2-6%**. At elite levels, that's the difference between 4th and gold.

---

## The Science of Taper

### What Happens During Taper

**Physiological adaptations:**
1. **Glycogen supercompensation** (muscle fuel stores increase)
2. **Muscle repair** (micro-damage from training heals)
3. **Hormonal rebalancing** (testosterone ↑, cortisol ↓)
4. **Neuromuscular freshness** (nervous system recovers)
5. **Blood volume normalization**
6. **Immune function improvement**

**Psychological benefits:**
- Reduced fatigue/staleness
- Increased motivation
- Improved focus
- Confidence from feeling fresh + fast

### Key Taper Variables

**1. Duration** (how long)  
**2. Volume reduction** (how much less)  
**3. Intensity** (how hard)  
**4. Frequency** (how often)

## Bob Bowman's Taper Philosophy

### The Core Principle

> "The process of success is more important than any outcome. I can't control how fast they're going to swim. I can't control the conditions in the pool. But what I can control is how we prepare for it and how we prepare for it every day for the last 6 months." — Bob Bowman

### Texas NCAA 2025 Taper

**Setup phase (SECs - 3 weeks before NCAAs):**
- Top 4 scorers (Kós, Hobson, Maurer) were **unshaved**
- Swam well but not fully rested
- Created **room to improve** for NCAAs

**Championship taper:**
- Full rest for top swimmers
- Kós went from losing every event at SECs → dominant at NCAAs
- 200 back: 1:34-low (Tuner's American Record broken by over a second)

### ASU 2024 Taper

**Bob gave more rest than usual for:**
1. Fall invite (needed to see where they were)
2. Mid-season dual meets (building confidence)

**Reason:** "I was trying to coach them mentally as well as physically."

**Result:** Team exceeded seeded times, won NCAA championship.

### The "Going to the Well" Debate

**Ray Looze (Indiana coach):** "You can only go to the well so many times."

**Bob's counter:**
> "I don't see the point in competing so far away from where you're going to be. What's the point of swimming a 500 fifteen seconds slower than your best time? Maybe 5 or 10 seconds is good. But Michael was always relatively close to his speed and we trained 80,000 meters a week. When we went to a meet, we didn't just keep that going till the day we got there. It seems stupid to do that. Why would we even go? Just keep practicing."

## Duration: How Long to Taper

### Research Guidelines

**Sprint events (50-100):** 1-2 weeks  
**Middle distance (200-400):** 2-3 weeks  
**Distance (800-1500):** 3-4 weeks

### Elite Practice

**Most common:** 2-3 weeks for championship meets

**Bob Bowman:** Typically 2.5-3 weeks for major championships

**Caeleb Dressel (Tokyo 2021):** 3-week taper, prioritized **sleep + recovery**

---

## Volume Reduction

### Research-Based Guidelines

**Week 1:** Reduce volume to **60-70%** of peak training  
**Week 2:** Reduce to **40-50%**  
**Week 3:** Reduce to **20-30%** (championship week)

### What to Reduce

**Cut first:**
- Aerobic base sets (long steady swimming)
- High-volume kicking
- Dryland volume (not intensity)

**Maintain:**
- Speed work
- Race-pace efforts
- Starts and turns
- Technical work

---

## Intensity: The Most Critical Variable

### The Golden Rule

**Reduce volume significantly. Maintain intensity.**

Intensity is what preserves speed and power. Volume creates fatigue.

### How to Maintain Intensity

**Keep:**
- Sprint sets (25s, 50s all-out)
- Race-pace work (goal pace for target events)
- Broken swims at race pace or faster
- Power work (explosive starts, underwaters)

**Example taper week:**
```
Monday AM: 3000m (50% volume) - includes 8x50 sprint @ 3:00
Monday PM: Dryland (30 min power focus)

Tuesday AM: 2500m - includes race-pace 200s
Tuesday PM: 2000m - speed + turns

Wednesday AM: OFF
Wednesday PM: 2500m - race simulation (prelims + finals)

Thursday: 2000m easy + starts

Friday: 1500m activation (easy + a few fast 25s)

Saturday: RACE
```

---

## Frequency: How Often to Train

### Research-Based

**Don't reduce training frequency** until final 3-5 days.

Reducing frequency too early can lead to detraining.

**Maintain:**
- Same number of sessions per week
- Just reduce duration and volume of each

**Final week:**
- Can drop to 1x/day or 5-6 sessions total
- Championship morning: 1000-1500m activation swim

## Psychological Taper

### Common Athlete Concerns

1. **"I feel slow/heavy"** (Days 3-7 of taper)
2. **"I'm losing fitness"**
3. **"Everyone else looks fast"**
4. **"I should be doing more"**

### Coaching Responses (Bob Bowman Style)

**Trust the process:**
> "We've done this hundreds of times. Your body is adapting. You'll feel it in 3-4 days."

**Focus on process goals, not outcome:**
- Don't think about winning/losing
- Think about: dive, breathe on second stroke, 1.2 tempo, head lower than hips into turn

**Rehearse success:**
- Visualization daily
- Watch videos of best swims
- Mental practice of race scenarios

---

## The "Reverse Taper" Phenomenon

**Days 3-7:** Many swimmers feel **worse** before feeling better.

**Why:**
- Body is rebalancing
- Nervous system adjusting
- Glycogen storage causes temporary heaviness
- Psychological: used to feeling tired = "normal"

**Solution:** Trust it. By days 8-10, the magic happens.

## Race Week Specifics

### 7 Days Out

- **Last hard workout** (race-pace work, but not all-out)
- After this: all speed + activation work

### 5 Days Out

- Suited swim (50-70% effort) to feel speed
- Broken swims at race pace or slightly faster

### 3 Days Out

- Short race-pace efforts (not full distance)
- Starts + turns + underwaters
- Total volume: <2000m

### 2 Days Out

- Easy 1500-2000m
- A few fast 25s to keep neuromuscular system firing
- Drills, technique work

### 1 Day Out

- **Championship morning:** 1000-1500m
- Easy swimming
- 4-6 fast 15s or 25s (not all-out, just sharp)
- Feel the water, wake up the nervous system

### Race Day Morning (Prelims)

- 1200-1800m warm-up
- Progressive: easy → moderate → race-pace 25s
- Specific to your event (fly/back/breast/free)

## Nutrition During Taper

### Carbohydrate Loading

**Days 3-1 before race:**
- Increase carb intake to **8-10g per kg bodyweight**
- Focus on easily digestible carbs (rice, pasta, bread, fruit)
- Reduce fiber/fat slightly to avoid GI issues

### Hydration

- Increase water intake
- Monitor urine color (pale yellow = hydrated)
- **Avoid alcohol** (dehydrates, disrupts sleep)

### Race Day

- **3-4 hours before:** High-carb meal (familiar foods)
- **1 hour before:** Banana, energy bar, or small snack
- **15 min before:** Sip water, maybe energy gel

## Common Taper Mistakes

### 1. Reducing Intensity Too Much

**Problem:** Swimmer gets slow because they never swim fast.

**Fix:** Maintain speed work throughout taper.

### 2. Not Reducing Volume Enough

**Problem:** Still too much fatigue, can't recover fully.

**Fix:** Be aggressive with volume cuts. It feels scary, but it works.

### 3. Adding New Workouts/Techniques

**Problem:** Body doesn't know what to do. Confusion, not adaptation.

**Fix:** Taper is NOT the time to experiment. Do what you know.

### 4. Over-Resting

**Problem:** Swimmer loses feel for water, gets sluggish.

**Fix:** Stay active. Reduce volume, but keep training.

### 5. Ignoring Sleep

**Problem:** All the taper in the world won't help if you're not sleeping.

**Fix:** Prioritize 8-9 hours/night during taper.

## Hubert Kós Case Study (Texas 2025)

### The Setup

**SECs (3 weeks before NCAAs):**
- Kós lost every event (50 back, 100 back, 200 back)
- Unshaved, not fully rested

**Bob's assessment:** "I really blew it with him last year at ASU. This year we got it right."

### The Taper

**Week before NCAAs:**
- Suited 150 backstroke: **1:10.0**
- Exactly what he'd split in the race

**Kós after that 150:** "Man, I could go like 1:33 or something."

**Bob:** "Yeah, feel free. Go ahead. Do it. Please do."

### The Result

**200 backstroke at NCAAs: 1:34-low**

- Broke Turer's American Record by over a second
- Kós was "mad he didn't go 1:33" (the goal they'd talked about)

**Bob:** "He's capable of that for sure."

**Outcome:** SEC Swimmer of the Year, but All-SEC Second Team (because you have to WIN an event to make first team - he lost every event at SECs).

## Michael Phelps: The 0.01-Second Finish

**2008 Beijing 100 Fly:** Beat Milorad Čavić by 0.01 seconds.

**Not a taper decision.** A **12-year ingrained habit.**

Every repeat in practice = chance to practice a perfect finish.

Bob yelling for 12 years: "Do it over. That finish was terrible. Make sure you're on a straight arm."

**Phelps:** Fingers fully extended, horizontal, driving straight into wall.

**Čavić:** Tired, floated up, touched vertical with flat hand.

> "I bought a 37-acre horse farm and built a 5,000-square-foot house off of that 1/100th of a second. I promise you it mattered." — Bob Bowman

## Individual Differences

### Some Swimmers Need More Rest

**Leon Marchand:** Needs long breaks, time off. After 2024 Paris: 6 weeks off.

**Training philosophy:** Always have **a little bit in reserve**. Never push to complete exhaustion.

### Some Swimmers Need Less Rest

**Michael Phelps:** Could go to the edge of exhaustion, be asked to do more, and be fine the next day.

**Training philosophy:** Give everything every day. When you don't have anymore, keep giving.

### How to Know

**Trial and error over seasons.**

Track:
- How they feel at different taper lengths
- Performance outcomes
- Athlete feedback

**Adjust accordingly.**

## The Mental Game of Taper

### Visualization Protocols

**Bob Bowman's system (used with Phelps):**

1. **Progressive relaxation** (get in relaxed state)
2. **Visualize race from two perspectives:**
   - First-person (in the water, feeling it)
   - Third-person (watching yourself from stands)
3. **Make it vivid:**
   - Smell the chlorine
   - See the venue
   - Feel the blocks under your feet
   - Hear the crowd
4. **Visualize multiple scenarios:**
   - Perfect race
   - Adversity (goggles leak, bad start, etc.)
   - How you'll respond to adversity

**Result:** By race day, you've swum it **300+ times** in your head.

### Process vs. Outcome Focus

**Outcome thoughts (create anxiety):**
- "I have to win"
- "What if I don't make finals?"
- "Everyone's watching"

**Process thoughts (create calm focus):**
- "Dive, breathe on second stroke, 1.2 tempo"
- "Head lower than hips into turn"
- "Drive fingers into wall, straight arm"

**Use process thoughts to control activation level.**

## Post-Taper: What's Next?

### After the Big Meet

**Option 1: Another meet in 2-4 weeks**
- Take 3-5 days very easy
- Rebuild volume gradually
- Mini-taper (1 week) before next meet

**Option 2: Off-season**
- Take 1-2 weeks completely off
- Active recovery (other sports, light swimming)
- Mental reset

**Option 3: Long course transition** (post-NCAAs → Olympic Trials)
- Bob Bowman: "Start long course immediately after short course. They're already in shape. We just roll right into it."
- Texas 2025: Top swimmers took **4 days off**, then back into full training

---

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Reduce volume aggressively (to 20-30% by championship week)**  
✅ **Maintain intensity (speed, race-pace, power)**  
✅ **Don't reduce frequency until final 3-5 days**  
✅ **Trust the process** (days 3-7 feel weird, then magic)  
✅ **Prioritize sleep** (8-9 hours/night)  
✅ **Visualize daily** (300+ mental reps before race day)  
✅ **Focus on process, not outcome**  
✅ **Individualize** (what works for Phelps won't work for Marchand)
