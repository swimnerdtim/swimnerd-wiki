# Taper Strategies

## Overview
Tapering is the systematic reduction of training load to optimize performance for competition. Championship-level tapers typically last 2-3 weeks.

## Key Principles

### Volume Reduction
- Reduce training volume by 40-60%
- Maintain intensity at race pace or faster
- Keep frequency of sessions relatively high

### Physiological Adaptations
- Glycogen supercompensation
- Muscle repair and recovery
- Nervous system freshening
- Immune system recovery

## Bob Bowman's Approach

Bob Bowman (Michael Phelps' coach) emphasizes:
- **"Feel" over metrics** - Listen to athlete feedback
- **Individualization** - Each swimmer tapers differently
- **Race simulation** - Include race-pace efforts
- **Mental preparation** - Taper is psychological as much as physical

### Phelps' Olympic Taper (Beijing 2008)
- 3-week taper
- Volume reduced to 30-40% of peak training
- Daily race-pace 50s and 100s
- Focus on underwater work and turns
- Maintained morning + afternoon structure

## Common Taper Mistakes

1. **Over-resting** - Too much reduction leads to loss of feel
2. **Panic training** - Adding volume last minute
3. **Ignoring technique** - Taper is time to refine details
4. **Poor nutrition** - Need to maintain quality fuel

## Taper Timeline

### Week 1 (3 weeks out)
- Reduce volume to 70% of peak
- Maintain all strokes
- Include broken swims at race pace

### Week 2 (2 weeks out)
- Reduce to 50% volume
- Increase race-pace work
- Dial in starts and turns

### Week 3 (Race week)
- Reduce to 30-40% volume
- Short, sharp race-pace efforts
- Visualize races
- Prioritize sleep and nutrition
