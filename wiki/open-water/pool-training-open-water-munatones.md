# Pool Training for Open Water Success

**Source:** *Open Water Swimming* by Steven Munatones (2011)  
**Context:** Pool-based training to prepare for open water competition  
**Audience:** Pool coaches training athletes for 5K, 10K, and marathon open water events

---

## Philosophy: Pool Is Your Laboratory

**The reality:**
- Most open water training happens IN THE POOL
- Open water conditions are unpredictable
- Pool allows controlled practice of race-specific skills
- Technique refinement requires repetition in stable conditions

**Key principle:** *Prepare for the worst in the pool, hope for the best in the race.*

---

## Essential Open Water Skills to Train in Pool

### 1. Bilateral Breathing (Non-Negotiable)

**Why it matters:**
- Single-side breathing = dangerous blind spot in open water
- Swimmers drift toward their breathing side without bilateral ability
- Can't see competitors, boats, hazards on blind side
- Essential for navigation and safety

**How to teach:**

**Progression:**
1. **Week 1-2:** 3-stroke breathing (every 3rd stroke)
   - All warm-up and cool-down sets
   - Low intensity only
   
2. **Week 3-4:** Add to moderate-intensity sets
   - 5 × 400 @ 3-stroke breathing
   - Focus on comfort, not speed
   
3. **Week 5+:** Make it mandatory for all freestyle
   - 5-stroke or 7-stroke patterns for advanced swimmers
   - Variable patterns: 3-5-3-5 or 3-3-5-5

**Drill: Blind Side Test**
- Swimmer breathes right side only for 50 yards
- Coach stands on left side holding up fingers/colors
- Swimmer reports what they saw → proves blind spot
- Repeat with bilateral breathing → problem solved

**Pool sets:**
```
Set 1: Bilateral Basics
- 10 × 100 free @ :15 rest
- Odd 100s: Breathe left only
- Even 100s: Breathe right only
- Goal: Equal speed/comfort both sides

Set 2: 3-5-7 Pyramid
- 400 free @ 3-stroke breathing
- 400 free @ 5-stroke breathing  
- 400 free @ 7-stroke breathing
- 400 free @ 5-stroke breathing
- 400 free @ 3-stroke breathing

Set 3: Race Simulation
- 8 × 200 @ :20 rest
- Breathe every 3rd stroke
- Last 50 of each 200: breathe every stroke (fatigue simulation)
```

### 2. Sighting & Head-Up Freestyle

**The skill:** Lifting head to navigate without losing speed

**Two techniques:**

**A. Crocodile Eyes (minimal disruption)**
- Eyes just above waterline
- Maintain stroke rhythm
- Breathe to side after sighting
- Best for calm water

**B. Water Polo Style (maximum visibility)**
- Head and chest lift out of water
- Spot target, return to streamline
- More disruption but necessary in rough water

**How to teach:**

**Week 1: Introduce the skill**
- Place cones/objects on deck
- 4 × 50: sight every 6 strokes, identify cone colors
- Focus on smooth integration, not speed

**Week 2-3: Build frequency**
- 6 × 100: sight every 6 strokes
- Time penalty should be <2 seconds per 100

**Week 4+: Race-specific**
- 10 × 200: sight first 3 strokes, then every 10 strokes
- Simulates start chaos + settling into rhythm

**Pool sets:**
```
Set 1: Sighting Basics
- 12 × 50 @ :10 rest
- Sight every 6 strokes
- Identify object coach holds up at far end
- Goal: No missed objects

Set 2: Sighting Intervals  
- 10 × 100 free @ 1:30
- Sight every 6 strokes (head-up freestyle)
- Record time
- Goal: Within 2 seconds of normal 100 pace

Set 3: Progressive Sighting
- 6 × 300 @ :30 rest
- First 100: sight every 10 strokes
- Second 100: sight every 6 strokes
- Third 100: sight every 4 strokes
- Simulates approaching turn buoy

Set 4: Combo Drill
- 16 × 75 @ 1:15
- 25 head-up free (sight continuously)
- 50 normal free
- Focus on quick recovery after sighting
```

### 3. Pack Swimming Simulation

**The skill:** Swimming in close proximity to others, handling contact

**Why it matters:**
- Open water races = constant contact, especially first 200-400 yards
- Athletes panic if not prepared
- Needs desensitization through practice

**How to teach:**

**Drill 1: Three-Wide Swimming**
- 3 swimmers per lane (normally 1-2)
- Intentional proximity
- Swim full practice in this formation
- Teaches spatial awareness, comfort with contact

**Drill 2: Corkscrew Practice**
- Swim down lane, dive under lane line at wall
- Surface in adjacent lane
- Simulates swimming over competitors in pack
- Return lap: reverse the move

**Drill 3: Controlled Chaos**
- 4-6 swimmers in one lane
- 4 × 200 free @ :30 rest
- Swimmers intentionally change positions mid-set
- Practice surging past, falling back, moving up

**Pool sets:**
```
Set 1: Pack Introduction
- 8 × 100 @ 1:30
- 3-4 swimmers per lane
- Start together, finish together
- Intentional contact/proximity

Set 2: Position Changes
- 6 × 200 @ :20 rest
- 4 swimmers per lane
- Every 50: one swimmer surges to front
- Rotate who surges
- Practice responding to attacks

Set 3: Mass Start Simulation
- All swimmers start in shallow end
- On whistle: race to far end (400)
- Full contact allowed (goggles required)
- Debrief: What worked? What scared you?

Set 4: Turn Buoy Chaos
- Place kickboard in deep end as "buoy"
- 10 × 75: 
  - Swim 50 to buoy
  - 3-4 swimmers turn simultaneously
  - Sprint 25 back
- Teach tight turns with contact
```

---

### 4. Drafting Practice

**The skill:** Swimming 6-12 inches behind another swimmer to save energy

**Energy savings:** 10-20% when done correctly

**How to teach:**

**Drill 1: Follow the Feet**
- Pairs of swimmers
- Lead swimmer swims normal pace
- Following swimmer maintains 6-12 inch gap
- Swap roles every 200

**Drill 2: Drafting Intervals**
- 6 × 400 @ :30 rest
- Pairs: swap lead every 100
- Following swimmer: feel the easier effort
- Record HR or perceived exertion

**Drill 3: Break the Draft**
- Lead swimmer surges randomly during 400
- Following swimmer must respond or lose draft
- Teaches race-realistic drafting

**Pool sets:**
```
Set 1: Drafting Basics (Pairs)
- 8 × 200 @ :20 rest
- Swap lead every 100
- Following swimmer: 6-12 inches from feet
- Focus on maintaining gap

Set 2: Drafting Endurance
- 3 × 1000 @ 1:00 rest
- Swimmer 1 leads 500, Swimmer 2 leads 500
- No gap between swimmers
- Compare HR: lead vs. draft

Set 3: Race Simulation
- 10 × 300 @ :30 rest
- First 200: draft
- Last 100: swing wide and sprint past
- Practice the race-finishing move

Set 4: Three-Person Rotation
- 3 swimmers per lane
- 6 × 500 @ :45 rest
- Rotate lead every 100 (1-2-3-1-2-3...)
- Teaches pack dynamics
```

### 5. Feeding Practice (Marathon Distance)

**The skill:** Taking nutrition without stopping or losing position

**Goal:** Complete feed in <5 seconds

**Four steps:**
1. **Seek and spot:** Sight feeder 50-75 yards out
2. **Reach and roll:** Grab bottle with one hand, roll to side
3. **Gulp and go:** Consume in 3-5 seconds while slow-swimming
4. **Toss and turn:** Discard, resume race pace immediately

**How to teach:**

**Week 1: Static practice**
- Swimmers at wall
- Coach hands bottle
- Practice: reach, drink, toss, push off
- Goal: <5 seconds wall-to-wall

**Week 2-3: Moving practice**
- Set up feeding station at wall
- 10 × 100: 
  - Swim 75
  - Feed at wall (don't stop)
  - Finish 25
- Time the feed: <5 seconds

**Week 4+: Race simulation**
- 3000-5000 continuous swim
- Feed every 500-1000 yards
- Practice with race-day nutrition

**Pool sets:**
```
Set 1: Feeding Basics
- 10 × 100 @ 1:30
- Swim 75, feed at wall, finish 25
- Coach times the feed
- Goal: <5 seconds from touch to full-speed swimming

Set 2: Continuous Feeding
- 3000 straight swim
- Feed every 500 yards
- Practice: gel, sports drink, water
- No stopping allowed

Set 3: Race-Day Rehearsal
- 5000-10000 continuous
- Feed at prescribed intervals (same as race plan)
- Use exact race-day nutrition
- Debrief: What worked? Adjust plan.
```

## Distance-Specific Training Plans

### Short Distance (1-5K) Pool Training

**Focus:**
- Speed endurance
- High turnover
- Race-pace tolerance
- Repeated surges

**Weekly structure:**
- 3-4 pool sessions
- 1-2 open water sessions
- 1 long continuous swim (3000-5000)

**Sample week:**

**Monday: Speed + Sighting**
```
800 warm-up
8 × 100 @ 1:20 (sighting every 6 strokes)
16 × 50 @ :50 (fast, race pace)
6 × 200 @ :20 rest (bilateral breathing, 3-stroke)
400 cool-down
Total: 5000
```

**Wednesday: Threshold + Pack**
```
1000 warm-up
5 × 400 @ :30 rest (3-4 per lane, pack simulation)
10 × 100 @ 1:30 (descend 1-5, repeat)
8 × 50 @ 1:00 (sprint, race-start simulation)
400 cool-down
Total: 5400
```

**Friday: Race Simulation**
```
800 warm-up
2 × (1000 @ 2:00 rest + 5 × 100 @ 1:30)
  - 1000: race pace, sight every 10 strokes
  - 100s: faster than race pace
Main set simulates 5K race effort
400 cool-down
Total: 5200
```

**Saturday: Open Water**
- 3-5K continuous swim
- Practice race starts, turns, sighting
- Variable pace (race simulation)

---

### Middle Distance (5-25K) Pool Training

**Focus:**
- Aerobic endurance
- Feeding practice
- Sustained pace
- Mental toughness

**Weekly structure:**
- 3-4 pool sessions
- 1-2 open water sessions (longer)
- 1 ultra-long swim (8000-12000)

**Sample week:**

**Monday: Aerobic Base + Sighting**
```
1000 warm-up
3 × 1500 @ 1:00 rest (sight every 8-10 strokes)
6 × 200 @ :20 rest (bilateral, 3-stroke)
12 × 100 @ 1:30 (drafting practice, pairs)
500 cool-down
Total: 7500
```

**Wednesday: Threshold + Feeding**
```
1000 warm-up
5000 continuous (feed every 1000 yards)
  - Practice race-day nutrition
  - No stopping for feeds
8 × 100 @ :15 rest (flush set)
500 cool-down
Total: 7200
```

**Friday: Pack Simulation**
```
800 warm-up
10 × 300 @ :30 rest (3-4 per lane)
  - First 200: draft
  - Last 100: surge past
20 × 50 @ :45 (odd: fast, even: easy)
400 cool-down
Total: 5200
```

**Sunday: Long Continuous**
```
10000 straight swim
Feed every 2000 yards
Practice race-day routine
Goal: negative split (faster second half)
```

### Marathon Distance (25K+) Pool Training

**Focus:**
- Ultra-endurance
- Mental fortitude
- Feeding mastery
- Pace discipline

**Weekly structure:**
- 2-3 pool sessions (high volume)
- 2 open water sessions (very long)
- 1 ultra swim (15000-20000)

**Sample week:**

**Tuesday: Continuous Volume**
```
1000 warm-up
12000 continuous (feed every 2000)
  - Negative split mandatory
  - Practice feeding with coach
  - Bilateral breathing entire swim
1000 cool-down
Total: 14000
```

**Thursday: Threshold + Skills**
```
1000 warm-up
6 × 1000 @ 1:00 rest (sight every 10 strokes)
10 × 200 @ :20 rest (3-4 per lane, pack)
20 × 100 @ 1:30 (bilateral, alternating sides each 100)
800 cool-down
Total: 9000
```

**Saturday: Open Water**
- 15-25K continuous swim
- Full race simulation
- Feeding every 30-45 minutes
- Coach follows in kayak/boat

## Stroke Technique Modifications for Open Water

### 1. Arm Turnover

**Pool swimming:** Often longer, more pronounced glide  
**Open water:** Shorter, quicker turnover

**Why:**
- Waves disrupt glide phase
- Need continuous propulsion against elements
- Higher turnover = better balance in rough water

**How to train:**
```
Set: Stroke Rate Ladders
- 5 × 400 @ :30 rest
- 400 #1: 16-18 strokes per 25
- 400 #2: 18-20 strokes per 25
- 400 #3: 20-22 strokes per 25
- 400 #4: 22-24 strokes per 25
- 400 #5: Back to 16-18 (control)

Goal: Maintain speed despite higher stroke count
```

### 2. Body Rotation

**Pool swimming:** Maximal rotation (60-90 degrees)  
**Open water:** Moderate rotation (45-60 degrees)

**Why:**
- More stable in rough water
- Easier to sight without disrupting stroke
- Less susceptible to being rolled by waves

**How to train:**
- Flatter body position during race-pace sets
- Stronger core engagement
- Practice in wave pool if available

### 3. Kick

**Pool swimming:** Often minimal (2-beat, energy conservation)  
**Open water:** Variable, situation-dependent

**When to kick more:**
- Fighting current
- Rough water (stability)
- Sprinting to finish
- Breaking free from pack

**When to kick less:**
- Drafting
- Ultra-long swims (energy conservation)
- Swimming with current

**How to train:**
```
Set: Variable Kick Intensity
- 8 × 400 @ :30 rest
- 400 #1: Minimal kick (2-beat)
- 400 #2: Moderate kick (4-beat)
- 400 #3: Strong kick (6-beat)
- 400 #4: Sprint kick (continuous flutter)
- Repeat 2x

Goal: Learn to change kick intensity mid-swim without losing rhythm
```

## Core Strength for Open Water

**Why it matters:**
- Body position in rough water
- Stroke efficiency without walls to push off
- Balance and stability

**Pool sets with core focus:**

```
Set 1: No-Wall Push-Offs
- 10 × 100 @ 1:30
- Do NOT push off walls
- Flip turn, immediately resume stroking
- Teaches core-driven turns

Set 2: Vertical Kicking
- 5 × 1:00 vertical kick (hands out of water)
- :30 rest between
- Builds core and kick strength

Set 3: Single-Arm Freestyle
- 12 × 50 @ :15 rest
- Odd: Left arm only (right at side)
- Even: Right arm only (left at side)
- Forces core rotation and stability
```

## Mental Training in Pool

### 1. Boredom Tolerance

**The challenge:** Open water = monotony, no black line, no walls

**How to train:**
- Long continuous swims (no intervals)
- Minimal rest between sets
- Practice 60+ minutes without stopping

```
Set: Mental Endurance
- 5000 continuous swim
- No stopping, no grabbing wall
- Count strokes, sing songs, visualize race
- Goal: Stay present and focused
```

### 2. Isolation Practice

**The challenge:** Open water = alone with your thoughts

**How to train:**
- Solo lane during busy practice (if possible)
- Swim with eyes closed (short distances, with lifeguard)
- Meditation/visualization during warm-down

### 3. Discomfort Tolerance

**The challenge:** Cold water, fatigue, contact, navigation stress

**How to train:**
- Cold-water exposure (gradually decrease pool temp if possible)
- Hypoxic sets (controlled oxygen deprivation)
- Pack swimming (intentional contact)

```
Set: Discomfort Adaptation
- 10 × 200 @ :15 rest (short rest = discomfort)
- 3-4 swimmers per lane (contact)
- Bilateral breathing only (hypoxic if needed)
- No goggles for last 2 × 200 (sensory deprivation)
```

## Open Water Simulation Sets

### Set 1: Full Race Rehearsal
```
**For 5K race:**
1000 warm-up (bilateral breathing)
200 easy + 6 × 50 sprint @ 1:00 (mass start simulation)
2 × (1500 @ 2:00 rest + 10 × 50 @ :50)
  - 1500: race pace, sight every 8-10 strokes
  - 50s: surge pace (simulates pack moves)
500 cool-down
Total: 6200
```

### Set 2: Marathon Feeding Practice
```
**For 25K+ event:**
15000 continuous
Feed every 2500 yards (matches race frequency)
Use exact race-day nutrition
Coach times every feed (<5 seconds)
Negative split (second 7500 faster than first)
Practice race-day wetsuit if applicable
```

### Set 3: Rough Water Adaptation
```
**Simulating adverse conditions:**
1000 warm-up
5 × 400 @ :30 rest (sight every 4-6 strokes, high stroke rate)
10 × 200 @ :20 rest (3-wide swimming, intentional contact)
8 × 100 @ 1:20 (head-up freestyle entire 100)
20 × 50 @ :45 (odd: sprint, even: recovery — simulates surges)
500 cool-down
Total: 6500
```

## Key Takeaways for Coaches

✅ **Bilateral breathing is mandatory** — start day one  
✅ **Sighting should cost <2 sec/100** — drill it until automatic  
✅ **Pack swimming must be practiced** — athletes panic if unprepared  
✅ **Drafting saves 10-20% energy** — teach it systematically  
✅ **Feeding takes practice** — <5 seconds or lose position  
✅ **Long continuous swims build mental toughness** — no shortcuts  
✅ **Pool is your laboratory** — control variables, perfect execution  

---

## Periodization: 12-Week Open Water Prep

**Weeks 1-4: Base Building**
- Focus: Bilateral breathing, sighting introduction, aerobic volume
- Volume: 20,000-30,000 yards/week
- Open water: 1x/week, skill focus

**Weeks 5-8: Race-Specific**
- Focus: Pack simulation, drafting, race-pace sets, feeding practice
- Volume: 25,000-35,000 yards/week
- Open water: 2x/week, one long (8-12K)

**Weeks 9-11: Peak**
- Focus: Full race simulations, feeding mastery, mental prep
- Volume: 30,000-40,000 yards/week
- Open water: 2x/week, one race-distance

**Week 12: Taper**
- Focus: Sharpening, rest, mental visualization
- Volume: 15,000-20,000 yards/week
- Open water: 1x light swim, course preview
