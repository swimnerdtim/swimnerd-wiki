- Don't overcook the first 50 — controlled aggression wins

## Universal Championship Racing Principles

### 1. **Start and Turn Excellence**
- Reaction times for champions: 0.60-0.77 (all within NCAA legal range)
- Underwater work separates top-3 from rest of field
- Every turn must be race-critical

### 2. **Pacing Discipline**
- **Sprints (50-100):** Controlled first half, minimize fade
- **Mid-distance (200s):** Even splits or slight negative split
- **Distance (500-1650):** Metronomic pacing, variance <0.5 sec per 100y

### 3. **Finishing Speed**
- Champions finish strong or maintain speed
- Last 25/50/100 should match or beat middle splits
- Finishing ability is trained, not innate

### 4. **Margins of Victory**
- 50 Free: 0.62 seconds (dominant)
- 100 Free: 1.30 seconds (dominant)
- 100 Back: 0.01 seconds (razor-thin)
- 200 IM: 0.47 seconds (tactical)
- 500 Free: 2.32 seconds (dominant)

**Insight:** Sprint and distance events allow for dominant performances. Mid-distance and stroke races (especially backstroke) are incredibly tight.
