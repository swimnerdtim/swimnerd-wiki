## 💡 Distance & IM Training Insights

### 500 Free: Build Back-Half Speed

**Traditional mistake:** Aerobic base only  
**Modern approach:** Train to close fast when tired

**Training sets that simulate championship pacing:**
- 10 x 100 on 1:30 holding race pace (24.5-25.5 per 100)
- 5 x 100 on 2:00 descending 1-5 (finish at 23.5-24.0)
- **Key:** Last one must be fastest when most fatigued

### 200 IM: Breaststroke Under Duress

**The science:** Breast comes after fly and back (pre-fatigued)

**Training protocol:**
- 200 fly/back/breast/free (simulate fatigue curve)
- 4 x 50 breast on 1:00 after 100 fly + 100 back
- **Goal:** Hold race-pace breast (28-29 sec) when gassed

### 400 IM: Stroke Endurance Hierarchy

**What fails first at NCAAs:**
1. **Breaststroke** (technique breaks down)
2. **Backstroke** (tempo slips)
3. **Butterfly** (usually held - it's first)
4. **Freestyle** (mental - you're just done)

**Training focus:**
- **Breast:** Maintain stroke length when lactic
- **Back:** Tempo work (maintain 30-32 SPM under fatigue)
- **Free:** Mental toughness - "fastest 100 of your life" simulation

## 🎯 Actionable Race Tactics

### 500 Free: The 3-Phase Race

**Phase 1 (100-300):** Establish rhythm, slight positive split allowed  
**Phase 2 (400-700):** THE RACE - hold the standard, don't panic at 25.5  
**Phase 3 (800-900):** Empty the tank - this is where you win or lose

### 200 IM: The Breaststroke Gambit

**Option A - Conservative (most common):**
- Solid fly (20.5-21.5)
- Steady back (24.5-25.5)
- **ATTACK breast** (28.5-29.5)
- Survive free (24-25)

**Option B - Aggressive (Kos/Lasco):**
- Fast fly (20.0-20.5)
- Strong back (24.0-24.5)
- **ELITE breast** (sub-29.0)
- Close fast (sub-24.5)

### 400 IM: The Endurance Chess Match

**Winning strategy (Maurer model):**
1. Controlled fly (don't go sub-48)
2. Even backstroke (all 50s within 1 second)
3. Survive breaststroke (don't go over 32/50)
4. **Bet the house on freestyle** (close sub-25 per 50)
