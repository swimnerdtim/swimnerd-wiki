# The Big 4 of Starts and Turns (Freas Method)

**Source:** *Sprinting: A Coach's Challenge* by Samuel James Freas  
**Key Insight:** "The Big 4" are non-negotiable biomechanical principles discovered through underwater observation and experimentation at Arkansas/LSU  
**Impact:** Freas's relay teams won championships; individual sprinters beat opponents by body lengths off starts/turns

---

## The Origin Story: Siong Ang vs. Neil Brooks

**The mystery:** Neil Brooks (Australian Olympian, 6'6", 240 lbs, superior vertical jump) kept LOSING to Siong Ang (University of Houston, shorter, less powerful) in 50-yard SCY races—but dominated in LCM.

**Freas went underwater to watch.** Here's what he saw:

### What Neil Did WRONG:
- Feet dropped deep out of streamline on start
- Hyperextended back on turn push-off (feet went deeper, more drag)
- "Pop-up" breakout (head jerked up, broke streamline early)
- Rotating finish (less accuracy, slower touch)

### What Siong Did RIGHT:
- **Perfectly flat streamline** (like an arrow)
- Toes pointed toward bottom on push-off (flatter trajectory)
- Head stayed DOWN through breakout (stayed in streamline)
- Fingertip touch on finish (head down, watching fingers)

**Result:** Siong made up 18+ inches on EVERY start and turn. In a 50-yard race (one start + one turn), that's the race.

## The Big 4 Principles

### 1. **Flatter = Faster**

**The flatter a body is in relationship to the surface of the water, the faster and farther it will travel on a turn or push-off.**

**Why:**
- Less surface area creating drag
- Streamline hydrodynamics optimized
- Avoiding bow/stern wave interference

**How to achieve:**
- Push off with toes pointing DOWN (not straight back)
- Keep hips UP (don't let them sag)
- Entire body in ONE PLANE (no arching, no piking)

**Test it:**
- Push off at different angles (flat vs. angled)
- Measure distance traveled to same point (e.g., flags)
- **Flat will win every time**

---

### 2. **Streamline Longer = Go Farther**

**The longer the head stays in a streamline position, the greater the speed and distance achieved on a dive or turn. Keep the head in line for at least four strokes.**

**Common mistake (1970s-80s coaching):**
> "After a streamline glide, pop your head up to break through a hole in the water."

**Freas reality:**
- "Pop-up" breaks streamline = instant drag
- Head staying down = body stays flat = less resistance

**The rule:**
- **First stroke:** Head LOCKED in line with spine, bottom arm pulls, opposite arm stays forward
- **Second stroke:** Head STILL in line, body breaks surface (no "pop")
- **Third stroke:** Head STILL in line
- **Fourth stroke:** NOW you can adjust head position
- **Fifth stroke or later:** First breath (if needed)

**Drill:**
```
12.5-yard underwater breakout drill:
- Push off wall in perfect streamline
- Dolphin kick (feet start IMMEDIATELY—see #3)
- First stroke: Pull with bottom arm, keep head DOWN
- Surface smoothly through the water (not UP out of it)
- Count strokes before first breath
- Goal: 4+ strokes before breathing
```

---

### 3. **Kick Immediately**

**The feet cannot start kicking soon enough off the wall, and rarely too soon off the start.**

**Traditional coaching error:**
> "Glide in streamline, THEN start kicking."

**Freas discovery:**
- Feet drop during "glide"
- Kicking creates lift + propulsion
- **Start kicking THE INSTANT feet leave the wall** (turn) or body enters water (start)

**Technique:**

**On turns:**
- Feet hit wall → toes IMMEDIATELY start dolphin kick (or flutter)
- No pause, no glide
- Kick keeps body FLAT (prevents feet from dropping)

**On starts:**
- Body enters water → feet start kicking while still submerged
- Kicking begins BEFORE breakout stroke
- Helps body stay flat and moving forward

**Common question:** "Can you kick TOO soon off a start?"

**Freas answer:** Rarely. The only risk is kicking so hard you break your streamline by arching. Otherwise, kick ASAP.

---

### 4. **Finish Head-Down**

**On the finish, when the body rotates to elongate the reach, a problem with quality and accuracy is created. Keep your head down while watching the fingertips touch the wall.**

**Traditional coaching (wrong):**
> "Rotate to your back on the last stroke to stretch for a fingertip touch."

**Problems with rotation:**
1. **Accuracy suffers** (fingertips miss the pad)
2. **Speed suffers** (rotation creates drag)
3. **Timing suffers** (harder to judge distance)

**Freas method:**
- **Last stroke = FULL STROKE** (not a half-stroke lunge)
- **Head stays DOWN** (eyes watching fingertips)
- **Fingertips drive FORWARD into the wall** (not reaching up/across)
- **Palm follows** (fingertips → full hand)

**Drill:**
```
Finish drill (practice 5×/week):
- 15-yard sprint from flags
- Focus: Eyes on fingertips the entire time
- Touch: Fingertips first, then palm
- NO head lift, NO rotation
- Repeat until automatic
```

## Starts: Technique Breakdown

### Starting Position

**Options:**
1. **Grab start** (both feet forward)
2. **Track start** (one foot back)

**Freas philosophy:** No "best" method. Experiment and measure.

**How to choose:**
1. Have athlete try both
2. Time to 15 yards (dive + underwater + breakout)
3. Measure distance traveled underwater
4. Use whichever is FASTER + FARTHER for that individual

### The Entry

**Goal:** Enter through a HOLE in the water

**Technique:**
- Slight hip flexion (creates streamline angle)
- Toes plantar flexed (pointed, but LOOSE—not rigid)
- Body enters in perfect horizontal line
- No lateral shift (hips don't twist sideways)

**Common mistake:** "Pike" entry (hips pike, feet drop deep)

**Fix:** Drive forward MORE than down. Think "long and flat" not "deep dive."

### The Glide/Breakout (Applying Big 4)

**Instant feet start kicking** (#3)
→ Body stays FLAT (#1)  
→ Head stays IN LINE for 4 strokes (#2)  
→ Bottom arm pulls while opposite arm stays extended (#2)  
→ Body breaks surface smoothly (no "pop-up")

**Visual cue for swimmers:**
> "Be an arrow. Arrows don't pop up—they fly through the target."

## Turns: Technique Breakdown

### Freestyle Flip Turn (Freas Method)

**Old method (WRONG):**
- Flip to back
- Push off on back in streamline
- Rotate to stomach underwater
- "Rotating objects travel better through water" (WRONG for swimming)

**Freas method (RIGHT):**
- Flip AND twist SIMULTANEOUSLY (gyrating action)
- Push off on SIDE (almost on stomach)
- Stay FLAT during push-off
- Feet land ENTIRELY on wall (not just toes)

### The Push-Off Depth

**This is the SECRET.**

**Rule:** The depth of your TOES on the wall = the depth of your body underwater.

**Example:**
- Want to be 18" underwater? → Toes must land 18" below surface
- Want to be 12" underwater? → Toes must land 12" below surface

**Why this matters:**

At the 50-yard turn, you're negotiating BOW/STERN WAVES from your own speed. If you push off too shallow (above the wave), you ADD 0.2-0.4 seconds. If you push off through the wave, you're clean.

**Optimal depths (Freas testing):**
- **150+ lbs:** 18-24" underwater
- **100-150 lbs:** 12-18" underwater
- **Under 100 lbs:** 9-12" underwater

(Adjust based on leg strength and push-off power)

### Turn Technique Checklist

1. ✅ Never breathe into the turn (last breath at least 3 strokes out)
2. ✅ Drive kick going INTO the turn (don't glide in)
3. ✅ Head depth on approach = Foot depth on wall (control this!)
4. ✅ Somersault + twist simultaneously (gyrate, don't rotate)
5. ✅ Toes point DOWN (creates flat push-off angle)
6. ✅ ENTIRE FOOT lands on wall (not just toes—you can't jump from your toes)
7. ✅ Streamline is PERFECT (hands locked, head squeezed)
8. ✅ Feet kick IMMEDIATELY off the wall
9. ✅ Head stays down for 4 strokes

**Practice turns in EVERY workout.** Time them. Make them a science.

## Relay Starts (Freas Innovation)

### Historical Context

**1960s-70s:** Conventional "windup" relay starts (both feet on block, arms swing back then forward)

**Mid-1980s:**
- **Cal Benz (Nebraska):** Two-step relay start
- **Sam Freas (LSU):** One-step relay start (1986)

**Freas testing:** Step relay starts traveled **8-12 inches farther** in the same time as conventional starts.

**1987:** Freas experimented with **RUNNING relay starts** (legal at the time). NCAA Rules Committee panicked and asked him not to use them at NCAAs. He complied (but regrets it).

### Step Relay Start Technique

**Setup:**
- One foot ON THE EDGE of the block (front foot)
- Other foot BACK (ready to step)
- Arms extended forward, eyes/fingertips forming imaginary line to incoming swimmer's touch point

**Execution:**
1. **Wind-up:** Arms swing back as incoming swimmer approaches
2. **Step + Swing:** Back foot steps forward AS arms swing forward
3. **Timing:** Body fully extended over water, only toes touching block, at the INSTANT of touch
4. **Entry:** Same as regular start (hole in the water, flat trajectory)

**Key:** The step adds FORWARD MOMENTUM (like a running start). More distance = faster relay exchange.

**Practice:**
- Teach on land first (jump, don't dive—just learn timing)
- Then transfer to blocks
- Time relay exchanges (should be FASTER than individual starts)

## Depth and Wave Negotiation

### The 50-Yard Turn Problem

**Scenario:** You're sprinting a 50 free. At the turn, you've created:
- Bow wave (in front of your head)
- Stern wave (behind your feet)

**If you push off SHALLOW:** You surface INTO your bow wave = extra drag = +0.2-0.4 seconds

**If you push off at PROPER DEPTH:** You push THROUGH the wave zone and surface in clean water = fast turn

**How to train this:**

```
Turn depth drill:
- Sprint 25, focus on FAST turn
- Push off at different depths (12", 18", 24")
- Measure time to flags
- Find YOUR optimal depth
- Practice hitting that depth EVERY TIME
```

## Dryland Training for Starts/Turns

### Streamline Practice (Dry Land)

**Purpose:** Muscle memory for perfect streamline position

**Exercises:**

1. **Wall streamline holds**
   - Stand against wall
   - Arms overhead, locked, biceps squeezing ears
   - Hips, shoulders, head flat against wall
   - Hold 30-60 sec, repeat 5×

2. **Partner streamline checks**
   - Partner tries to "break" your streamline by pulling arms apart
   - Resist
   - Builds strength in streamline position

3. **Streamline walks**
   - Hold streamline position
   - Walk forward on toes
   - Focus: Head locked between biceps, core tight
   - 25-50 yards

### Reaction Drills (for starts)

See: [Reaction Drills for Sprinters](../methodology/reaction-drills-sprinters.md)

**Quick version:**
- Hand reaction drills (visual stimulus → GO)
- Foot reaction drills (auditory stimulus → GO)
- Practice 5-10 minutes EVERY DAY

### Plyometric Training (for explosive push-offs)

- Box jumps (develop explosive leg power)
- Depth jumps (develop reactive strength)
- Horizontal bounds (develop forward power)

**Goal:** Stronger push-off = farther glide = faster start/turn

## Practice Prescription

**How often to practice starts/turns:**

Freas rule: **At least 5 workouts per week**

**Sample weekly integration:**

| Day | Start/Turn Work |
|-----|-----------------|
| Monday | 5 timed turns (from sprint into wall) |
| Tuesday | 10 reaction starts (from blocks, to 15 yards) |
| Wednesday | Relay start practice (5 exchanges per swimmer) |
| Thursday | 3 × 25 from dive, TIMED (full start-to-finish) |
| Friday | "Hammer Down" 50 (timed, race start + race turn) |

**Total time:** 10-15 minutes per workout

**Total impact:** 30-50% of a 50 free time

## Meet Warm-Up: Timed Sprint Check

**Freas secret:** Check starts/turns on meet day, but DON'T tell swimmers their times.

**Why not tell them?**
> "The more you allow sprinters to think, the worse off they will be."

**What to do instead:**
- Time the 25 from dive (to feet at wall)
- Use it as an INDEX (are they where they should be?)
- Give VERBAL FEEDBACK only:
  - "Great!"
  - "Awesome!"
  - "Let's do another"
  - "A little adjustment on your breakout..."

**If something's wrong:**
- Don't say "You were slow"
- Say: "You don't LOOK right. Calm down, let your stroke carry you. Let's do another."
- Make small adjustments (breakout, rhythm, effort level)

**Never, ever:**
- "You're 9.9—you should be 9.5" (creates panic)
- "That's slow" (creates doubt)
- Give exact splits (creates overthinking)

**Trust the FEEL.** Sprinters know when they're right.

## Common Mistakes & Fixes

### ❌ Mistake: Popping Head Up on Breakout

**Fix:**
- Drill: Underwater breakouts (focus on keeping head down)
- Cue: "Be an arrow—arrows don't pop up"
- Video feedback (underwater camera)

### ❌ Mistake: Feet Drop Deep on Start

**Fix:**
- Adjust entry angle (more forward, less down)
- Strengthen core (plank holds, streamline walks)
- Kick sooner/harder (feet start immediately)

### ❌ Mistake: Shallow Turn Push-Off

**Fix:**
- Practice depth control (toes deeper = body deeper)
- Measure depth with coach underwater
- Mark wall at target depth (visual cue during practice)

### ❌ Mistake: Rotating Finish

**Fix:**
- Drill: 15-yard finish sprints (eyes on fingertips)
- Cue: "Drive INTO the wall, not UP to it"
- Practice timing last stroke (full stroke, not lunge)

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Flatter = Faster** (push off with toes DOWN, keep body FLAT)  
✅ **Streamline longer = Go farther** (head down for 4 strokes minimum)  
✅ **Kick immediately** (feet start the instant they leave the wall/enter water)  
✅ **Finish head-down** (eyes watch fingertips touch the wall)  
✅ **Practice 5×/week minimum** (starts, turns, finishes = 30-50% of race)  
✅ **Depth control matters** (know YOUR optimal push-off depth)  
✅ **Measure and individualize** (what works for one athlete may not work for another)  
✅ **Don't overthink on meet day** (trust the training, trust the feel)
