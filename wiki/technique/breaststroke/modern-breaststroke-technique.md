# Modern Breaststroke Technique: The Heart-Shape Pull

**Source:** *Championship Swimming* by Tracey McFarlane Mirande (1988 Olympic Breaststroke Champion)  
**Context:** Complete modern breaststroke mechanics — arms, timing, recovery  
**Audience:** Coaches teaching breaststroke, swimmers learning/refining technique

---

## What Changed: Modern vs. Old-School Breaststroke

### Pre-1988: Head Above Water
- Not allowed to put head underwater
- **Much more drag**
- Less efficient

### Post-1988: Head Can Submerge
- **Significantly reduced drag**
- More streamlined
- Faster stroke

**Even Tracey (1988 Olympian) had to learn new techniques:** "Breaststroke has changed since I swam it in the Olympics in 1988."

---

## Fundamental Difference: Breaststroke vs. Freestyle/Backstroke

### Freestyle/Backstroke: Side-to-Side Rotation
- Long, streamlined stroke
- Never flat on stomach
- Rotating eliminates "snowplow" position

### Breaststroke/Butterfly: FLAT (Snowplow Position)
- **Back to the position we eliminated in freestyle**
- Flat on stomach in water
- Must work to overcome drag
- Must "trick" the water into streamlining past you

**Hardest to be streamlined** in breaststroke — but modern technique (head underwater) makes it much easier.

---

## Breaststroke Mechanics Overview

### Two-Motion Propulsion
**Legs push + Arms pull** working in sequence (not simultaneously)

**Timing:**
1. Arms start **just before** legs
2. Legs finish **just after** arms at end of each stroke

**Why some struggle:**
- Different kick motion (not flutter kick)
- Timing coordination required
- Not natural for great freestylers/backstrokers/butterflyers

**Individual medley reality:** Many swimmers struggle through breaststroke portion.

---

## Arm Mechanics: The Heart-Shape Pull

### Core Principle: Arms in Front of You

**99% of arm stroke should be visible in front of you while swimming.**

**Rule:** Hands should NEVER be farther down than your shoulders.

### What NOT to Do

❌ **Old-school mistake:** Pulling past shoulder area  
❌ **Chicken-wing elbows:** Dropping elbows down next to waist/rib cage  
❌ **Pulling too far back:** Once arms are even with shoulders, additional motion creates ZERO power and adds drag  

**Why it fails:** Not creating strength through water + adding too much drag to bring arms back to top of stroke.

---

## Teaching the Heart-Shape Pull

### Setup (3 Options for Learning)

**Option 1: Lane Line (Best)**
- Stand in waist-deep water
- Reach over lane line
- **Don't lean on it** — just reach over
- Prevents pulling elbows too far back

**Option 2: Kickboard**
- Place kickboard at 90° angle under armpits
- Works for smaller swimmers
- May not work for large adults

**Option 3: Pool Noodle**
- Put Styrofoam noodle under arms
- Acts like lane line

**Option 4: No Equipment**
- Bend over in water at waist
- Less effective but workable

**Why use aids?** Makes it impossible to bring elbows back too far; forces focus on "sweeping" water vs. "pulling" water.

## The 11-Step Heart-Shape Sequence

### Top of the Heart (Steps 1-3)

**Step 1: Streamlined Start**
- Arms extended in front, just under surface
- Backs of hands together, pinkies up
- Streamlined position

**Step 2: Look Down**
- Look at pool bottom (slightly in front)
- See your arm motion
- Palms face outward, arms straight

**Step 3: Press Out**
- Separate hands
- Press outward and UP slightly with entire arm
- Thumbs angled down, pinkies up
- Push out toward side of pool
- **Drawing top of heart** with straight arms

### Sides of the Heart (Steps 4-5)

**Step 4: Bend Elbows (6 Inches Wide)**
- When hands/arms are ~6 inches wider than shoulders (less for kids)
- Bend elbows slightly
- Turn hands like freestyle catch

**Step 5: Pull Down (Draw Sides of Heart)**
- Pull down for ~12 inches
- Almost like freestyle motion but out at side
- **Drawing sides of heart**

### Bottom of the Heart (Steps 6-7)

**Step 6: Make the Point**
- Rotate hands and elbows
- Palms face each other

**Step 7: Push Together**
- Push palms and arms together
- End ~24 inches under chin (depends on arm length)
- Almost directly under chin
- **Limber swimmers:** May touch elbows together (don't force it)

**Result:** This heart shape is where you get POWER.
- Powerful enough to lift shoulders/head almost out of water
- **This is correct** — don't fight the motion
- Lifts you up and forward

---

## The Recovery: Minimizing Drag (Steps 8-11)

### Purpose of Recovery
**Get hands back to top of heart for next stroke** while minimizing drag.

**Your head goes underwater for this part.**

### The 4-Step Fast Recovery

**Step 8: Fold & Touch**
- As soon as arms/palms touch at bottom of heart
- Keep hands and (if possible) forearms together
- Fold elbows to at least 90°

**Step 9: Push Out & Up**
- Push hands out and up toward surface
- **Streamlined position** (like freestyle)
- Ready to start next stroke

**Step 10: Rotate Hands**
- Pinkies up
- Begin next heart-shape pull

**Step 11: Repeat**
- Top of heart → sides → bottom → recovery

---

## Why Recovery Speed Matters

### The Critical Truth

> "As soon as you have pushed your palms together, **you are done moving water**. You want to get to the start of your next stroke as quickly as you can."

**Fastest way:**
1. Fast fold of elbows
2. Fast push-out of arms into streamlined shape

### Common Mistake: Ignoring Recovery

**Many swimmers forget to focus on recovery** — but it's EXTREMELY important.

**If done wrong:** Slows down rest of stroke.

**Goal:** Bring arms/hands forward and get into streamlined position ASAP.

---

## How to "Fool" the Water

### Breaststroke's Fundamental Challenge
**You're flat** → More drag than freestyle/backstroke

### Two Ways to Minimize Drag

**1. Modern Technique:** Head underwater during recovery  
**2. Fast Recovery:** Palms together → streamlined position → launches you forward with minimum drag

**Result:** Water "thinks" you're less flat than you are.

## Natural Swimmers vs. Those Who Struggle

### Who Finds Breaststroke Natural?
- Tracey McFarlane Mirande: "It comes naturally to me."
- Some people's bodies/flexibility suit breaststroke

### Who Struggles?
- Great freestylers/backstrokers/butterflyers
- Runners and bikers (often have trouble with kick motion)
- People with limited hip flexibility

**Why:** Different kick motion + timing coordination required.

## Teaching Progression

### Separate, Then Combine

**Lesson structure:**
1. Arm movement (heart-shape pull)
2. Breathing (separate lesson)
3. Kick (separate lesson)
4. Rhythm drill (combining all parts)
5. Turn & underwater pullout

**Why separate?** Easier to learn each component, then feel the rhythm of entire stroke.

**Drills:** Help you feel each part of stroke before combining.

## Key Coaching Cues

### For Arm Position
✅ "Draw a heart shape under the water"  
✅ "Keep your hands where you can see them"  
✅ "Stop when your hands reach your shoulders"  
✅ "Never go past your shoulders"  

### For Power
✅ "Feel your body lift up and forward"  
✅ "Let the stroke lift you — don't fight it"  
✅ "If you're standing, you should feel like taking a step forward"  

### For Recovery
✅ "Fold, push, shoot — FAST"  
✅ "Get done moving water? Get streamlined ASAP"  
✅ "Palms together → streamline → next stroke"  
✅ "Do the recovery as rapidly as you can"

## Common Faults & Fixes

### Fault #1: Pulling Past Shoulders
**Problem:** Arms go too far back, past shoulder line  
**Why it's wrong:** Creates zero power, adds drag  
**Fix:** Use lane line or kickboard to physically prevent pulling back  

### Fault #2: Chicken-Wing Elbows
**Problem:** Elbows drop to waist/rib cage  
**Why it's wrong:** No strength through water  
**Fix:** Think "wide elbows" — keep elbows higher than hands during pull  

### Fault #3: Slow Recovery
**Problem:** Taking too long to get back to streamlined position  
**Why it's wrong:** Adds drag, slows stroke rate  
**Fix:** "Fold-push-shoot" as fast as possible  

### Fault #4: Hands Separate Too Wide
**Problem:** Pushing hands wider than 6 inches past shoulders  
**Why it's wrong:** Inefficient, adds drag  
**Fix:** "6 inches wider than shoulders, then BEND and pull"

## Practice Protocol

### Solo Practice (Over Lane Line)
1. Stand in waist-deep water
2. Reach over lane line
3. Execute heart-shape pull
4. Focus on seeing 99% of stroke in front of you
5. Feel shoulders/head lift up
6. Practice recovery speed

### Partner Practice (Eyes on Technique)
- Swimmer performs stroke
- Partner watches for:
  - Hands going past shoulders? ❌
  - Elbows dropping to ribs? ❌
  - Slow recovery? ❌
  - Head lifting during power phase? ✅

## Actionable Takeaways for Coaches

✅ **Teach heart-shape first** — use lane line or noodle to prevent over-pulling  
✅ **99% visible rule** — hands should never go past shoulders  
✅ **Power comes from heart shape** — recovery is about minimizing drag  
✅ **Speed the recovery** — fold, push, shoot FAST  
✅ **Separate before combining** — arms → breathing → kick → timing  
✅ **Modern technique uses head underwater** — reduces drag significantly  
✅ **Flat position is CORRECT for breast/fly** — opposite of free/back
