## Freestyle Problems & Solutions

### Body Position Issues

**Problem: Legs sink / "uphill" swimming**
- **Downhill Float** — Look at bottom, straight spine, lean forward on chest
- **Log Roll** — Maintain core tension while rolling through positions
- **Twelve Kick Switch** — Hold "downhill" float while moving, side to side

**Problem: Can't maintain streamline**
- **Twelve Kick Switch** — Identify longest freestyle position
- **Catch Up** — Feel fully extended glide position

---

### Kick Problems

**Problem: Ineffective kick / no propulsion**
- **Toe Point Drill** — Point toes, rotate feet inward (centralized splash)
- **Vertical Kicking Sequence** — Engage large muscles (quads/hamstrings/glutes)
- **Single Leg Kicking** — Develop full leg kick, keep foot connected

**Problem: Rigid, tense kick**
- **Floppy Foot Drill** — Let water bend foot like a flag (fish-like propulsion)

**Problem: Wrong muscles engaged**
- **Vertical Kicking** — Kick from hip (not knee), fairly straight legs

**Problem: Kick too deep or too shallow**
- **Toe Point Drill** — 12-15 inches depth, tops & bottoms of feet push water

---

### Arm Stroke Problems

**Problem: Short stroke / not reaching**
- **Catch Up** — Wait for hands to meet in front (maximize length)
- **Horizontal Rope Climbing** — Move body past anchored hand

**Problem: No acceleration through stroke**
- **Pull/Push Freestyle** — PULL (entry → shoulder), PUSH (shoulder → hip)

**Problem: Wrong hand pitch / slipping through water**
- **All Thumbs Drill** — Contrast thumb-first (wrong) vs. fingertip-first (right)
- **Sculling** — Develop feel for water, learn to hold water with hand

**Problem: Dropped elbow / only using hand**
- **Fist Freestyle** — Force forearm propulsion, keep elbows high
- **Sculling** — Hold water with forearm, not just hand

**Problem: Can't feel the water**
- **Sculling** — Press against water (figure-eight pattern), three positions

---

### Recovery Problems

**Problem: Tense, rigid recovery**
- **Floppy Hand** — Completely relax hand/wrist, dangle loosely

**Problem: Low elbow recovery / shoulder pain**
- **Shark Fin** — Elbow higher than hand (lead with elbow)

**Problem: Misaligned recovery / wide swing**
- **Shark Fin** — Elbow travels on track from shoulder to entry point

---

### Breathing Problems

**Problem: Head lift when breathing**
- **Weightless Arm** — Roll to side (not lift), weightless arm feeling
- **Temple Press** — Press temple into water, rotate body (not head)
- **Dead Arm Freestyle** — Breathe with one arm extended (proves rotation works)

**Problem: Breathing disrupts rhythm**
- **Inhale Arm / Exhale Arm** — Coordinate breathing with arm entry
- **Bilateral Breathing** — Breathe every 3rd stroke (symmetrical rhythm)

**Problem: Can't get enough air**
- **Temple Press** — Create bow wave, one-goggle rule
- **Weightless Arm** — Roll enough that top arm is weightless/out of water

**Problem: Asymmetrical stroke**
- **Bilateral Breathing** — Develops even hip roll, symmetrical stroke

---

### Leverage Problems

**Problem: Arm-driven (not core-driven)**
- **Three Stroke Switch** — Hip rotation drives arms (not vice versa)
- **Hip Skating** — Push off each hip like skating blade
- **Pendulum Effect** — Rhythmic weight shift hip to hip

**Problem: No power from core**
- **Three Stroke Switch** — Feel leverage as hips drive stroke
- **Hip Skating** — Push-off from hip initiates stroke, arm follows

**Problem: Disconnected hips & arms**
- **Pendulum Effect** — Shift weight to hip as same-side arm enters

---

### Coordination Problems

**Problem: Kick doesn't match arm stroke**
- **Freestyle with Dolphin** — One kick per arm, downbeat matches hand entry
- **Heads Up Freestyle** — One kick matches each arm entry

**Problem: All elements work individually but not together**
- **Heads Up Freestyle** — Forces coordination (grab water, continuous stroke, match kick)
- **Horizontal Rope Climbing** — Efficient forward motion (advance past anchored hand)
- **Bilateral Breathing** — Symmetrical roll, kick, arm stroke

**Problem: Understanding mechanics but not feeling efficiency**
- **Horizontal Rope Climbing** — Experience efficient forward motion (essence of swimming)

## Backstroke Quick Reference

### Body Position
**Problem: Body too flat or rounded**
- **Float on Spine** — Straight spine (not rounded or arched), balance on spine
- **Water-Line Drill** — Chest, hips, thighs at surface (head position key)
- **Twelve Kick Switch Backstroke** — Maintain balance while switching sides

### Kick
**Problem: Knees breaking surface**
- **No Knees Streamline Kick** — Straight legs from hip, toes pointed
- **Boiling Water Drill** — Toes just breaking surface (not knees)

**Problem: Wrong foot position**
- **Pigeontoed Kicking** — Knees/feet rotate inward (like freestyle)

**Problem: Disrupted rhythm**
- **Quarter Roll with Cup on Forehead** — Maintain head stability during kick

### Arm Stroke
**Problem: Short stroke**
- **One Arm Pull/Push** — Full extension, accelerate to hip
- **Up and Over** — High recovery, full reach on entry

**Problem: Can't feel the water**
- **Fist Backstroke** — Force forearm engagement
- **Corkscrew** — Gradual hand pitch change through stroke

### Recovery
**Problem: Low, flat recovery**
- **Clock Arms** — Straight arm recovery over "12 o'clock"
- **Two-step Recovery** — High elbow, then hand follows
- **Locked Elbow Drill** — Exaggerate straight arm recovery

**Problem: Recovery crosses centerline**
- **Dog-ears** — Thumbs brush ears on recovery (alignment)

### Breathing
**Problem: Irregular breathing / holding breath**
- **Rhythmic Breathing Sequence** — Inhale 1 stroke, exhale 2-3 strokes
- **The Breathing Pocket** — Find comfortable head position for breathing

### Leverage
**Problem: No core engagement**
- **Three Stroke Switch Backstroke** — Hip rotation drives stroke
- **One Arm Rope Climb** — Leverage from core, not just arm
- **Opposition Freeze Frame** — Feel hip/shoulder rotation together

**Problem: Hips too flat**
- **Armpit Lift** — Lift armpit out of water each stroke (forces rotation)

### Coordination
**Problem: Arms & hips not connected**
- **Roll, Pull, Roll, Push** — Coordinate hip rotation to pull & push
- **Pinkie Lead** — Hand entry triggers hip rotation

**Problem: Asymmetrical stroke**
- **Backstroke Balance Drill with Cup** — Maintain balance both sides
- **Opposition Overlap** — Both hands in front together (symmetry checkpoint)

## Breaststroke Quick Reference

### Body Position
**Problem: Hips too low**
- **Streamline** — Perfect streamline position (base of all breaststroke)
- **Rocking Drill** — Chest down-hips up, chest up-hips down (pendulum)

### Kick
**Problem: Wrong foot position**
- **Duck Feet** — Flex feet, rotate outward (heel-width, toe-width)
- **Breaststroke Kick on Your Back** — See and feel correct foot position

**Problem: Weak kick**
- **Vertical Breaststroke Kick** — Keep head above water with kick alone
- **Heads Up Breaststroke Kick** — Moving forward with kick only

### Arm Stroke
**Problem: Too wide or too narrow**
- **3D Breaststroke Arms** — Out-back-in (not just out and in)
- **Breaststroke Sculling** — Feel water with hands (outward & inward press)

**Problem: No acceleration**
- **Hand Speed Drill** — Slow to fast (like snapping rubber band)
- **Half-stroke Breaststroke** — Short stroke forces quick turnover

**Problem: Poor hand pitch**
- **Corners Drill** — Rotate hands 90° at widest point
- **Breaststroke with Fists** — Force forearm use, hand pitch awareness

### Recovery
**Problem: Hands too wide during recovery**
- **Growing Your Recovery** — Start narrow, gradually wider
- **Shoot to Streamline** — Hands shoot forward to streamline (not wide)

**Problem: Shoulders stay high**
- **Fold and Shrug** — Fold elbows tight, shrug shoulders forward

### Breathing
**Problem: Head lift too high**
- **Inhale at the High Point** — Grab breath at peak (don't force higher)
- **Eyes on the Water** — Look down at water (not forward)

**Problem: Head position disrupts stroke**
- **Tennis Ball Drill** — Hold tennis ball under chin (prevents excessive lift)

### Leverage
**Problem: All arm, no body movement**
- **Breaststroke with Dolphin** — Feel forward body surge
- **Breaststroke Alternating Dolphin & Breaststroke Kick** — Compare propulsion

**Problem: Wrong timing**
- **Stroke Up to Breathe, Kick Down to Glide** — Stroke=lift, kick=glide

### Coordination
**Problem: Arms & legs work together (illegal/inefficient)**
- **No Stars** — No starfish position (arms & legs extended simultaneously)
- **Stroke, Breathe, Kick, Glide Mantra** — Say it while swimming

**Problem: Not using glide**
- **Glide Length/Glide Speed** — How far can you glide? How fast?
- **Thread the Needle** — Streamline through hand target each stroke

## Butterfly Quick Reference

### Body Position
**Problem: Hips too flat / no undulation**
- **Taking a Bow** — Chest press initiates body wave
- **Weight Shifting** — Chest down-hips up, chest up-hips down

### Kick
**Problem: Kick originates from knee (not hip)**
- **Ribbon Writing** — Hips lead the wave, feet follow
- **Deep to Shallow Dolphin** — Kick deep in front, shallow in back

**Problem: No power from kick**
- **Dolphin Dives** — Kick yourself out of water
- **Vertical Dolphin** — Keep head above water with kick alone

**Problem: Tight, rigid kick**
- **Back Dolphin** — Relaxed, fluid motion on back

### Arm Stroke
**Problem: No acceleration**
- **Pitch to Press** — Change hand pitch to press back at shoulders

**Problem: Wrong hand path**
- **Sweeping Question Marks** — Trace "?" with each hand
- **The Round Off** — Wide entry, trace hourglass pattern

**Problem: Weak stroke**
- **One-arm Butterfly** — Develop strength, feel leverage
- **Left Arm, Right Arm, Both Arms** — Compare single vs. double

### Recovery
**Problem: Recovery too low / hands drag**
- **Pinkies-up** — Exit pinkie-first, swing pinkies-up over water
- **The Flop** — Let hands flop over (relaxed recovery)

**Problem: No reach on entry**
- **Reaching to a "Y"** — Extend arms wide in front (not narrow)

### Breathing
**Problem: Head lift too high**
- **Breathing Timing Drill** — Chin touches water on inhale
- **Eyes on the Water Butterfly** — Look down (not forward)

**Problem: Wrong breathing timing**
- **Flying Dolphin Dives** — Breathe at surface, not diving down

**Problem: Breathing disrupts stroke**
- **Hammer and Nail** — Arms drive breathing action (not head lift)

### Leverage
**Problem: No connection arms-to-core**
- **Advanced One-arm Butterfly** — Non-stroking arm at side (forces core use)
- **Chest Balance** — Balance on chest during hand entry (leverage point)

**Problem: Too much kick (wastes energy)**
- **No Kick Butterfly** — Can you swim fly with no kick? (test coordination)

**Problem: Excessive glide**
- **No Pause Fly** — Continuous motion (hands exit as they enter)

### Coordination
**Problem: All elements separate (not unified)**
- **Coordination Checkpoint** — 2 kicks per stroke (small-BIG)
- **Quiet Butterfly** — Minimal splash (efficiency test)

**Problem: Breathing disrupts rhythm**
- **Hammer and Nail** — Arms power the breath (integrated movement)

## Drill Selection Strategy

### By Session Goal

**Warm-Up (Establish fundamentals)**
- Body position drills (Float, Roll, Switch)
- Light kick drills (Vertical, Toe Point)
- Basic arm drills (Pull/Push, Catch Up)

**Main Set (Address specific flaw)**
- Part practice drills for identified problem
- Alternate drill & swim

**Cool-Down (Integrate & reinforce)**
- Coordination drills (Heads Up, Bilateral)
- Full-stroke focus with drill awareness

**Taper (Quality, low volume)**
- Favorite drills at easy pace
- Focus on feel, not effort

### By Swimmer Level

**Novice (Learning fundamentals)**
- Body position: Downhill Float, Log Roll
- Kick: Toe Point, Vertical Kicking
- Arms: Pull/Push, Catch Up
- Breathing: Temple Press, Weightless Arm

**Intermediate (Refining technique)**
- All novice drills plus:
- Fist drills (Freestyle/Backstroke/Breaststroke)
- Leverage drills (Three Stroke Switch, Hip Skating)
- Contrast drills (All Thumbs)

**Advanced (Fine-tuning & racing)**
- X drills (exaggeration for specific points)
- Complex coordination drills
- Speed-specific drills
- Race-pace with drill focus

### By Time Available

**5 minutes**
- 1-2 drills, 2-3 reps each
- Focus on highest-priority problem

**10 minutes**
- 3-4 drills, 3-4 reps each
- Progression sequence (e.g., body position → kick → arms)

**15-20 minutes**
- 5-6 drills, multiple reps
- Complete technique breakdown & rebuild
- Include coordination drills at end

## Universal Drill Principles

✅ **Know the purpose** — Empty practice doesn't improve technique  
✅ **Start slow** — Speed comes after correct form  
✅ **Focus on THE point** — Ignore aspects not being targeted  
✅ **Perfect each step** — Skipping steps reinforces wrong technique  
✅ **Alternate drill & swim** — Transfer new technique to full stroke  
✅ **Use progressions** — Build complex skills from simple ones  
✅ **Contrast wrong vs. right** — Helps choose better technique  
✅ **Exaggerate to feel** — X drills make point more obvious  
✅ **Short distances first** — When tired, revert to old habits  
✅ **Regular practice** — Include drills in EVERY session

## Problem Diagnosis Flowchart

**Not moving forward efficiently?**
1. Check body position (downhill float?)
2. Check kick (connected to water?)
3. Check arm stroke (acceleration? hand pitch?)
4. Check coordination (all parts working together?)

**Moving forward but exhausting quickly?**
1. Check for tension (relaxed recovery? floppy feet?)
2. Check for wasted motion (streamlined? aligned?)
3. Check breathing (rhythmic? within line of stroke?)

**Good technique but no power?**
1. Check leverage (core-driven? hip rotation?)
2. Check hand/foot pitch (holding water?)
3. Check timing (kick matches arms?)

**Inconsistent performance?**
1. Check symmetry (bilateral drills)
2. Check fatigue resistance (hold technique when tired?)
3. Check muscle recruitment (engaging large muscles?)

## Drill Integration Guidelines

### How Many Drills Per Session?
- **Warm-up:** 3-5 drills (5-10 minutes)
- **Main set:** 2-3 drills targeted at specific flaws
- **Cool-down:** 1-2 coordination drills

### How Many Reps?
- **Learning new drill:** 3-5 reps (get comfortable)
- **Refining technique:** 4-8 reps (build muscle memory)
- **Maintenance:** 2-3 reps (keep skills sharp)

### Drill vs. Swim Ratio
- **Novice:** 50% drill, 50% swim
- **Intermediate:** 30% drill, 70% swim
- **Advanced:** 20% drill, 80% swim
- **Taper:** 10-15% drill, 85-90% swim (high quality, low volume)

### When to Progress?
**Move to next drill when:**
- Can perform current drill correctly 3x in a row
- Can explain the purpose
- Can feel the intended effect
- Notice improvement in full stroke
