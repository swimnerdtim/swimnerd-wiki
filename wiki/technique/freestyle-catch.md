# Freestyle Catch Technique

## Overview
The catch is the initial phase of the freestyle pull where the hand engages the water and sets up for propulsion.

## Key Components

### Hand Entry
- Enter thumb-side down, fingers first
- Entry point: Between head and shoulder line
- Minimal splash (entering a "mail slot")

### Early Vertical Forearm (EVF)
- Elbow stays high near surface
- Forearm drops vertical quickly
- Hand faces backward (not down)
- Creates large "paddle" surface

## Bob Bowman's Teaching Points

From training Phelps and Leon Marchand:

> "The catch isn't about pulling water - it's about anchoring your hand in place and pulling your body over it."

### Drill Progression
1. **Sculling drills** - Feel for water pressure
2. **Catch-up stroke** - Isolate each arm
3. **Fist drill** - Feel with forearm, not just hand
4. **Single-arm freestyle** - Perfect one side at a time

## Common Mistakes

1. **Reaching too far** - Overextending wastes energy
2. **Pulling down** - Hand should go back, not down
3. **Dropping elbow** - Loses EVF position
4. **Rushing the catch** - Need patience to set it up

## Championship-Level Details

### Michael Phelps' Catch
- Extremely high elbow
- Hand enters slightly wider than shoulder
- Immediate rotation to EVF position
- Feels for "grip" on water before pulling

### Katie Ledecky's Catch
- Straighter arm entry
- Deeper catch position
- Longer glide phase
- Emphasizes continuous motion

## Training Focus

**Volume phase:** Focus on technique, not power
**Taper phase:** Add resistance (paddles, bands) to feel the catch under race conditions
