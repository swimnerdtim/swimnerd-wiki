# Freestyle: Anatomy & Mechanics

**Muscle recruitment patterns, kinetic chain, and biomechanical foundations of efficient freestyle.**

---

## Prime Movers (Propulsive Phase)

### **Upper Body Pull**

**1. Pectoralis Major (Clavicular Portion)** — Initiates the catch
- **Function:** Begins hand entry → catch phase
- **Action:** Shoulder flexion, medial rotation
- **Key point:** First muscle engaged as hand enters water

**2. Latissimus Dorsi** — Primary propulsive muscle
- **Function:** Pulls arm through water (catch → finish)
- **Action:** Shoulder extension, adduction, medial rotation  
- **Power output:** Majority of underwater force generation
- **Training focus:** Pull-ups, lat pulldowns, cable rows

**3. Wrist Flexors** — Maintain paddle position
- **Function:** Hold wrist in slight flexion throughout pull
- **Key point:** Wrist stability = effective hand paddle

**4. Biceps Brachii & Brachialis** — Elbow flexion (catch phase)
- **Function:** Transition from straight arm (hand entry) → ~30° elbow flexion (mid-pull)
- **Action:** High elbow catch position
- **Avoid:** Excessive flexion (>45°) = reduced leverage

**5. Triceps Brachii** — Elbow extension (finish phase)
- **Function:** Extend elbow during final push (hips → thigh)
- **Action:** Complete propulsive phase
- **Note:** Extension amount varies by swimmer (some finish early, some push all the way back)

## Recovery Phase Muscles

### **Arm Recovery**

**1. Deltoid (Anterior/Middle/Posterior)** — Lifts arm out of water
- **Function:** Shoulder flexion + abduction
- **Action:** Hand exits near hip → overhead position

**2. Rotator Cuff (Supraspinatus, Infraspinatus, Teres Minor, Subscapularis)**
- **Function:** Stabilize shoulder joint during recovery
- **Action:** Prevent impingement, control arm path
- **Injury prevention:** Strengthening rotator cuff = shoulder health

---

## Stabilizers (Critical for Efficient Mechanics)

### **Scapular (Shoulder Blade) Stabilizers**

**Muscles:**
- Pectoralis minor
- Rhomboids
- Levator scapula
- Middle & lower trapezius
- Serratus anterior

**Function:**
- **Anchor the shoulder blade** — Creates stable base for arm propulsion
- **Enable upward rotation** — Allows elongated reach at hand entry
- **Reposition during recovery** — Works with deltoid/rotator cuff

**Why it matters:**
> "All propulsive forces generated by the arm and hand rely on the scapula's having a firm base of support."  
> — Ian McLeod, *Swimming Anatomy*

**Training implication:** Weak scapular stabilizers = power leak = slower swimming

**Dryland exercises:**
- Scapular push-ups
- Band pull-aparts
- Prone Y-T-W raises
- Serratus punches

---

### **Core Stabilizers**

**Muscles:**
- Transversus abdominis
- Rectus abdominis  
- Internal oblique
- External oblique
- Erector spinae

**Function:**
- **Link upper and lower extremities** — Transfer power from arms → legs
- **Enable body roll** — Coordinate rotation during freestyle
- **Maintain streamline** — Reduce drag

**Swimming-specific role:**
- **Body roll coordination:** Core rotates torso, allowing high elbow recovery + reduced frontal drag
- **Power transfer:** Strong core = efficient kinetic chain

**Dryland exercises:**
- Planks (front/side)
- Dead bugs
- Pallof press
- Russian twists
- Streamline holds on physioball

## Kicking Mechanics (Propulsive + Recovery)

### **Downbeat (Propulsive Phase)**

**1. Iliopsoas** — Initiates hip flexion
- **Action:** Starts downward kick from hips

**2. Rectus Femoris** — Hip flexion + knee extension
- **Action:** Drives knee extension following hip flexion

**3. Quadriceps (Vastus Lateralis, Intermedius, Medialis)**
- **Action:** Forceful knee extension
- **Power output:** Main contributors to downbeat propulsion

### **Upbeat (Recovery Phase)**

**1. Gluteal Muscles (Maximus & Medius)** — Hip extension
- **Action:** Initiate upward kick

**2. Hamstrings (Biceps Femoris, Semitendinosus, Semimembranosus)**
- **Action:** Hip extension (assist glutes)

### **Plantarflexion (Throughout Entire Kick)**

**Gastrocnemius & Soleus**
- **Action:** Maintain pointed foot position
- **Mechanism:** Active muscle contraction + water pressure during downbeat

## The Kinetic Chain: Linking It All Together

**Concept:** The body is a chain of linked segments. Movement in one segment affects all others.

**In freestyle:**
1. **Arms generate power** (lats + pecs)
2. **Core transfers power** (obliques + transversus abdominis)
3. **Legs maintain balance** (kick coordination with arm strokes)

**If one link is weak:**
- Power transfer is lost
- Movement becomes uncoordinated  
- Injury risk increases

**Example:**
- Weak core → Cannot coordinate body roll → Over-rotation or flat swimming
- Weak scapular stabilizers → Shoulder instability → Rotator cuff injury

**Training takeaway:** Build the foundation (core + stabilizers) BEFORE maxing out power muscles (lats/pecs).

---

## Reciprocal Arm Movement

**Freestyle is reciprocal** — While one arm pulls, the other recovers.

**Coordination:**
- **Right arm:** Propulsive phase (underwater)
- **Left arm:** Recovery phase (above water)
- **Body roll:** Coordinates transition between arms

**Common error:** Poor timing = both arms in recovery simultaneously = dead spot in propulsion

**Fix:** Drills emphasizing catch-up timing, body roll drills

## Common Muscle Imbalances in Swimmers

**Problem:** Swimming overdevelops certain muscles, creating imbalances.

### **Upper Body**

**Overdeveloped:**
- Latissimus dorsi
- Pectoralis major

**Underdeveloped:**
- Scapular stabilizers (middle/lower trapezius, rhomboids)
- Rotator cuff

**Consequences:**
- Rounded shoulders (kyphotic posture)
- Shoulder impingement
- Rotator cuff injuries

**Fix:**
- Strengthen middle/lower traps (prone Y-raises, band pull-aparts)
- Strengthen rotator cuff (external rotation exercises)
- Stretch pecs/lats (doorway stretch, foam rolling)

---

### **Lower Body**

**Overdeveloped:**
- Quadriceps
- Hip flexors (iliopsoas, rectus femoris)

**Underdeveloped:**
- Hamstrings
- Gluteal muscles

**Consequences:**
- Anterior pelvic tilt
- Lower back pain
- Knee injuries

**Fix:**
- Strengthen glutes (bridges, clamshells, squats)
- Strengthen hamstrings (Nordic curls, Romanian deadlifts)
- Stretch hip flexors

## Dryland Training Principles for Freestyle

### **1. Transference**

**Direct transference** = Exercise mimics stroke movement
- Example: Cable pulls (simulates underwater pull)

**Indirect transference** = Exercise targets same muscle groups
- Example: Lat pulldowns (targets lats, primary mover in freestyle)

### **2. Isolation**

**Goal:** Strengthen weak/underdeveloped muscles
- Scapular stabilizers (prevent shoulder injury)
- Rotator cuff (shoulder health)
- Hamstrings/glutes (correct imbalances)

### **3. Exercise Order**

**Proper sequence:**
1. **Warm-up** (10 min) — Dynamic stretching, light aerobics
2. **Core stabilization** — Planks, dead bugs
3. **Multi-joint exercises** — Pull-ups, push-ups, squats
4. **Isolation exercises** — Bicep curls, tricep extensions
5. **Additional core work** — Russian twists, side planks
6. **Static stretching** — Cool-down

**Why this order?**
- Don't fatigue small muscles (biceps) before big movements (pull-ups)
- Analogy: Don't do exhaustive kick set before main freestyle set

### **4. Pushing vs. Pulling Balance**

**Pushing exercises:** Bench press, push-ups (pecs + triceps)  
**Pulling exercises:** Pull-ups, rows (lats + biceps)

**Why alternate?**
- One muscle group recovers while the other works
- Corrects imbalances (swimmers are pull-dominant)

## Sets, Reps, and Training Goals

### **Muscular Endurance (Distance Swimmers)**
- **Reps:** 15-20
- **Sets:** 2-3
- **Rest:** 30-60 seconds
- **Goal:** Sustain effort over long duration

### **Strength (Sprint Swimmers)**
- **Reps:** 5-8
- **Sets:** 4-5
- **Rest:** 2-3 minutes
- **Goal:** Maximum force production

### **Power (All Sprinters)**
- **Reps:** 6-10 (explosive)
- **Sets:** 3-4
- **Rest:** 2-3 minutes
- **Goal:** Speed + strength combined

**Rule of thumb:** Last 2-3 reps of final set should feel challenging (muscle fatigue indicator).

## Periodization for Freestyle Dryland

**Concept:** Break season into phases with different training goals.

| Phase | Pool Focus | Dryland Focus | Reps | Sets | Rest |
|-------|-----------|---------------|------|------|------|
| **Early season** | Aerobic base | Anatomical adaptation (injury prevention) | 12-20 | 2-3 | 1-2 min |
| **Mid-season** | Lactate tolerance | Strength building | 5-8 | 4-5 | 2-3 min |
| **Pre-competition** | Race pace | Power conversion | 6-10 | 3-4 | 2-3 min |
| **Competition** | Taper + racing | Maintenance | 6-8 | 2 | 2 min |

## Dryland Exercises for Freestyle (Examples)

### **Prime Movers**
- **Lats:** Pull-ups, lat pulldowns, cable rows
- **Pecs:** Push-ups, bench press, cable flys
- **Triceps:** Dumbbell kickbacks, tricep dips

### **Stabilizers**
- **Scapular:** Scapular push-ups, prone Y-T-W, band pull-aparts
- **Rotator cuff:** External rotation (bands/dumbbells), Cuban press
- **Core:** Planks, dead bugs, Pallof press, streamline holds

### **Kicking Power**
- **Quads:** Squats, lunges, leg press
- **Glutes:** Bridges, clamshells, Romanian deadlifts
- **Hamstrings:** Nordic curls, leg curls

## Key Takeaways

✅ **Lats + pecs = prime movers** — Focus dryland on these  
✅ **Scapular stabilizers = power transfer** — Weak stabilizers = shoulder injury  
✅ **Core links upper/lower body** — Strong core = efficient body roll  
✅ **Correct imbalances** — Strengthen middle/lower traps, rotator cuff, glutes, hamstrings  
✅ **Periodize dryland** — Match dryland goals to pool training phase  
✅ **Exercise order matters** — Multi-joint → isolation  
✅ **Reciprocal arms** — One pulls while other recovers
