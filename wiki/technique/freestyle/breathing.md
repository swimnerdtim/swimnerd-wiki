# Freestyle Breathing Technique

*Source: Bob Bowman - "What Swimmers Miss in Freestyle" (2024)*

## Core Principle: Head Moves WITH Body

> "In proper freestyle swimming, the head moves with the body to take the breath. It never moves independently." - Bob Bowman

### What NOT to Do

❌ **Lifting the chin** to take a breath  
❌ **Looking forward** during breath  
❌ **Looking to the side of the pool** during breath  
❌ **Over-rotating** the head  
❌ **"Checking the weather"** (looking up at ceiling)  

### What TO Do

✅ **Rotate head in line with body**  
✅ **Breathe in natural rotation of the body**  
✅ **One goggle in the water** during breath  
✅ **Head and body move together as one unit**  

## The Bow Wave Technique

### What is the Bow Wave?

When you swim, your head creates a bow wave (like a boat moving through water). This creates a small trough/pocket of air right next to your head.

**Proper breathing**: Take your breath INSIDE the bow wave
- One goggle stays IN the water
- About half your mouth shows above water
- You're actually breathing inside the pocket created by the bow wave

### Watch Michael Phelps

**What you'll see**:
- One goggle out of the water
- One goggle IN the water
- About half his mouth visible
- Breathing inside the bow wave
- Minimal head rotation
- Perfect alignment with body rotation

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Over-Rotation (Katie Ledecky Example)

**Problem**: Both goggles visible during breath
- Indicates over-rotation
- Head moving independently of body
- Creates drag and misalignment

**Fix**: Work on showing only one goggle during breath

### 2. "Checking the Weather"

**Problem**: Looking up toward ceiling when breathing
- Face is fully visible
- Over-rotating from the head
- NOT moving in unison with body

**Fix**: Keep rotation minimal, one goggle in water

### 3. Independent Head Movement

**Problem**: Head moving separately from body rotation

**Fix**: Lock head position to body, rotate as one unit

## Breathing Discipline

### Chase Kalisz Double-Breath Story

**The Problem**:
- Chase had habit of breathing into walls (double breath)
- Did it frequently in training
- Would carry into races

**The Intervention**:
- Bob: "Nuclear reaction" approach (admitted he went too far)
- Made Chase restart entire practice if he saw double breath
- Message: "Nobody's going to double breathe and win a gold medal"
- "If you want to get there, you have to do it"

**The Result**: 
- Chase never did another double breath
- Bowman: "I've never seen him do one since"

**Lesson**: Sometimes one decisive intervention on a bad habit is more effective than constant nagging

## Technical Checkpoints

### Self-Assessment Questions

1. **Can you see your whole face on video during breath?**
   - If yes → you're over-rotating

2. **Are both goggles visible when breathing?**
   - If yes → minimize rotation

3. **Does your head lift or look forward?**
   - If yes → work on lateral rotation only

4. **Can you feel the bow wave pocket?**
   - If no → you may be lifting too high

### Practice Drills (Implied)

**During warm-up** (30% of practice - Bowman's structure):
- Focus on gradual technique reinforcement
- Breathing drills with one goggle focus
- Body rotation drills
- Head position awareness

**During main set** (50% of practice):
- Maintain technique under fatigue
- Don't let breathing mechanics break down
- Every breath is practice for perfect technique

## Integration with Body Rotation

### The Complete Picture

**Freestyle breathing is NOT**:
- A separate action from swimming
- An interruption to your stroke
- Something your head does independently

**Freestyle breathing IS**:
- Part of natural body rotation
- Integrated into the stroke cycle
- Head locked to body, rotating together
- Minimal movement for maximum efficiency

### Timing

**When to breathe**: During natural body rotation
- As your body rolls to breathe side
- Head rotates WITH that roll
- Return rotation = head returns WITH body

## For Different Swimmer Levels

### Beginners
- Focus first on: keeping one goggle in water
- Drill: Side-kick with one arm extended, practice bow wave breathing
- Don't worry about speed, establish pattern

### Intermediate
- Refine: Minimizing rotation
- Video analysis: Check if both goggles showing
- Increase speed while maintaining technique

### Advanced
- Perfect: Bow wave breathing at race pace
- Integrate: Breathing every 2, 3, 5 strokes without technique breakdown
- Fatigue test: Maintain perfect breathing when exhausted

## Mental Cues

**"One goggle in the water"** - Primary focus cue

**"Head and body together"** - Unity cue

**"Breathe in the bow wave"** - Efficiency cue

**"No checking the weather"** - Correction cue

## Common Races & Breathing Patterns

**Sprint (50-100m)**:
- Minimal breathing or specific pattern
- Technique even more critical (less breathing = each breath must be perfect)

**Mid-Distance (200m)**:
- Consistent pattern (often every 2 or 3)
- Maintain technique through fatigue

**Distance (400m+)**:
- Regular breathing essential
- Technique must not break down over time
- Katie Ledecky: work on minimizing over-rotation even when tired

## Coaching Points (from Bowman's Approach)

### For Coaches

1. **Video is essential** - athletes often can't feel over-rotation
2. **Establish non-negotiables** - some habits need decisive intervention
3. **Practice = performance** - if they breathe wrong in practice, they'll breathe wrong in races
4. **Reinforce in warm-up** - 30% of practice is technique reinforcement time

### For Self-Coached Swimmers

1. **Film yourself regularly**
2. **Count goggles** - should only see one
3. **Feel the bow wave** - are you breathing in the pocket or above it?
4. **Check alignment** - is head rotation matching body rotation?

## The Bottom Line

**Proper freestyle breathing**:
- Head moves WITH body (never independently)
- One goggle in the water
- Breathe inside the bow wave
- Minimal rotation for maximum efficiency

**Impact**:
- Reduced drag
- Better body position
- More efficient energy use
- Faster swimming

**Remember**: Every breath in practice is practice for the perfect breath in your race. Make every rep count.
