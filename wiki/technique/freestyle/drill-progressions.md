# Freestyle: Drill Progressions

**Build perfect freestyle technique step-by-step with proven drills targeting body position, kick, arm stroke, recovery, breathing, leverage, and coordination.**

---

## Why Drills Matter

> "Without intervention, stroke flaws may be repeated over and over again, becoming habit. This can slow progress, cause frustration with the sport, and, in some instances, eventually lead to injury."  
> — Blythe Lucero, *The 100 Best Swimming Drills*

**Drills are NOT just mindless repetition.** Each drill has a specific purpose:
- **Isolate** one aspect of the stroke
- **Practice** correct technique repeatedly
- **Relearn** ingrained poor patterns
- **Build** from simple → complex

**Approach swimming as both ART and SCIENCE:**
- **Art** = "Feel" the water (fluid, graceful, powerful movement)
- **Science** = "Understand" physics (propulsion, drag, leverage)

---

## Four Types of Swimming Drills

### 1. **Progression-Based Drills**
Build the stroke one piece at a time (simple → complex).

**Example:** Kick → Kick + Catch → Kick + Catch + Pull → Full Stroke

---

### 2. **Part Practice Drills**
Isolate a specific stroke flaw and correct it through repetition.

**Example:** Catch-up drill (fixes overreaching, improves hand entry)

---

### 3. **Contrast Drills**
Perform the skill incorrectly, then immediately correct it to feel the difference.

**Example:** Fist freestyle (closed fist = poor feel) → Open hand (good feel)

**Why it works:** Contrasting wrong vs. right makes correct technique obvious.

---

### 4. **X Drills (Exaggeration Drills)**
Use exaggeration to emphasize a particular point of technique.

**Example:** Shark Fin drill (exaggerated high elbow recovery) → Normal recovery feels easier

---

## The Foundation: Economic Swimming

**Economic Swimming** = Managing energy to swim with less effort.

**Three pillars:**
1. **Minimize drag** — Streamlined body position
2. **Develop feel for the water** — Efficient catch/pull
3. **Access potential power** — Core-driven propulsion

**Progression to Efficient Swimming:**
- **Economic swimming** (less effort) + **Conditioning** (strength/endurance) = **Efficient swimming** (speed over time)

## BODY POSITION DRILLS

**Goal:** Master horizontal balance (floating), reduce drag, swim "downhill."

### **Drill 1: Downhill Float**

**Purpose:** Feel proper body alignment and weight distribution.

**How to do it:**
1. Push off the wall in streamline (arms extended overhead, hands stacked)
2. Hold streamline position, focusing on pressing chest/head down slightly
3. Feel hips/legs rise to the surface (you float "downhill")
4. Hold for 5-10 seconds, then take a stroke

**What to feel:**
- Chest/head press down → Hips rise
- Water line at mid-forehead (not top of head)
- Core engaged (like a plank)

**Common mistakes:**
- Head too high → Hips sink
- Loose core → Body breaks at hips

**Feedback:** If legs sink, press chest down more aggressively.

---

### **Drill 2: Log Roll**

**Purpose:** Learn to shift weight from side to side (body roll foundation).

**How to do it:**
1. Float on back in streamline
2. Roll to one side (90° rotation) — pause
3. Roll to stomach (180° rotation) — pause
4. Roll to opposite side (270° rotation) — pause
5. Return to back (360° rotation)
6. Repeat, rotating the opposite direction

**What to feel:**
- Core controls rotation (not arms/legs)
- Smooth, continuous roll (no jerky movements)

**Why it matters:** Body roll is essential for efficient freestyle breathing and recovery.

---

### **Drill 3: Twelve Kick Switch**

**Purpose:** Practice weight shift with kick (dynamic body position).

**How to do it:**
1. Push off in streamline on one side (right arm extended, left arm at side)
2. Kick 12 times (flutter kick)
3. Switch arms (left arm extends, right arm to side)
4. Kick 12 times
5. Repeat

**What to feel:**
- Weight shifts from one side to the other
- Bottom arm presses down (like pressing on a table) to initiate rotation
- Top arm relaxed during switch

**Progression:** Reduce kick count (12 → 6 → 3) as balance improves.

---

## KICK DRILLS

**Goal:** Productive + sustainable kick (forward momentum, rhythm, balance).

### **Drill 4: Toe Point Drill**

**Purpose:** Develop relaxed, flexible ankles (key to effective kick).

**How to do it:**
1. Sit on pool deck, legs extended
2. Point toes away from body (plantarflexion)
3. Flex toes back toward body (dorsiflexion)
4. Repeat 10-15 times
5. In water: Kick on back, focus on pointed toes throughout kick

**What to feel:**
- Toes pointed = streamlined kick
- Ankles relaxed (not stiff)

**Why it matters:** Flexed ankles = parachute drag. Pointed toes = propulsion.

---

### **Drill 5: Floppy Foot Drill**

**Purpose:** Eliminate rigid ankles (common in non-swimmers/runners).

**How to do it:**
1. Hold onto wall, face down
2. Kick with completely relaxed feet (imagine feet are floppy fins)
3. Allow water pressure to create toe-pointed position naturally

**Contrast:** Kick with stiff ankles (feel drag) → Kick with relaxed ankles (feel propulsion).

---

### **Drill 6: Vertical Kicking Sequence**

**Purpose:** Build kick power without relying on buoyancy.

**How to do it:**
1. Deep water, vertical position (no wall)
2. Kick to keep head above water (hands at sides)
3. **Level 1:** Keep chin above water (30 seconds)
4. **Level 2:** Keep chest above water (20 seconds)
5. **Level 3:** Hands above water (10 seconds)

**What to feel:**
- Small, fast kicks (not big, slow kicks)
- Kick from hips (not knees)

**Why it works:** Removes flotation assistance — forces powerful kick.

---

### **Drill 7: Single Leg Kicking**

**Purpose:** Isolate each leg to identify/correct imbalances.

**How to do it:**
1. Kick on side, bottom arm extended
2. Kick with bottom leg only (top leg relaxed)
3. Repeat 25m
4. Switch sides, kick with opposite leg

**Feedback:** If one leg is weaker, do extra reps on that side.

---

## ARM STROKE DRILLS

**Goal:** Maximize length, straightest path forward, feel the water.

### **Drill 8: Pull/Push Freestyle**

**Purpose:** Separate catch (pull) from finish (push) to feel each phase.

**How to do it:**
1. **Pull phase:** Catch → pull to chest (pause when hand reaches ribs)
2. **Push phase:** Push from chest → finish at thigh (accelerate through push)
3. Repeat, emphasizing distinction between pull and push

**What to feel:**
- Pull = anchoring hand in water, moving body past hand
- Push = acceleration, elbow extends

---

### **Drill 9: Catch-Up**

**Purpose:** Improve hand entry, lengthen stroke, prevent overreaching.

**How to do it:**
1. One arm extended (right arm forward)
2. Pull with left arm
3. **Touch hands** at front before starting next stroke (right arm pulls)
4. Alternate arms, "catching up" each time

**What to feel:**
- Full extension before starting next stroke
- Glide/elongation between strokes

**Variation:** Pause 3 seconds with hands together (increases difficulty).

---

### **Drill 10: All Thumbs Drill**

**Purpose:** Ensure proper hand entry angle (thumb down = shoulder injury risk).

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle, focus on hand entry
2. **Correct entry:** Fingertips enter first (hand angled slightly downward)
3. **Incorrect entry (identify):** Thumb enters first (hand flat/supinated)

**Fix:** Rotate hand so little finger enters slightly higher than thumb.

**Why it matters:** Thumb-first entry = shoulder impingement over time.

---

### **Drill 11: Sculling**

**Purpose:** Develop "feel" for the water (hands as anchors).

**How to do it:**
1. Float on stomach, arms extended forward
2. **Outward scull:** Press hands out to sides (palms face out)
3. **Inward scull:** Press hands together (palms face in)
4. Repeat continuous figure-8 motion
5. Move forward using only sculling (no kick)

**What to feel:**
- Constant hand pressure against water
- Hands never "slip" through water

**Variations:**
- Scull at chest level (mid-pull position)
- Scull at hips (finish position)

---

### **Drill 12: Fist Freestyle** (Contrast Drill)

**Purpose:** Emphasize importance of hand feel (contrast poor vs. good feel).

**How to do it:**
1. Swim 25m freestyle with **closed fists** (no hand paddles)
   - Feel: Very little propulsion, body slides backward
2. Swim 25m freestyle with **open hands**
   - Feel: Immediate increase in propulsion

**Takeaway:** Open hands = better feel for water = more propulsion.

---

## RECOVERY DRILLS

**Goal:** Relaxed, aligned recovery that balances the stroke.

### **Drill 13: Floppy Hand**

**Purpose:** Eliminate tension during recovery.

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle, focus on recovery arm
2. Let hand dangle loosely from wrist (like a wet noodle)
3. Wrist/fingers completely relaxed

**What to feel:**
- No tension in hand/wrist
- Recovery arm rests while underwater arm works

---

### **Drill 14: Shark Fin** (X Drill)

**Purpose:** Exaggerate high elbow recovery to feel proper alignment.

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle
2. During recovery, drag fingertips along water surface
3. Elbow points UP (like a shark fin)

**What to feel:**
- Elbow leads recovery (not hand)
- Hand stays relaxed below elbow

**Progression:** Once you feel high elbow recovery, reduce exaggeration to normal stroke.

---

## BREATHING DRILLS

**Goal:** Rhythmic breathing, inhale/exhale within the line of the stroke.

### **Drill 15: Inhale Arm / Exhale Arm**

**Purpose:** Coordinate breathing with arm strokes.

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle, breathe to right side
2. **Inhale** when right arm recovers (air comes in as arm swings forward)
3. **Exhale** when right arm pulls (air goes out as arm pushes water back)
4. Repeat every stroke cycle

**What to feel:**
- Inhale = quick, explosive
- Exhale = gradual, continuous (bubbles throughout pull)

**Progression:** Breathe every 3 strokes (bilateral breathing).

---

### **Drill 16: The Weightless Arm**

**Purpose:** Use breathing to create a "weightless" recovery arm.

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle
2. As you rotate to breathe, feel bottom arm (extended forward) press down
3. This downward press makes the recovering arm feel weightless

**What to feel:**
- One arm presses down → Opposite arm lifts easily

**Why it works:** Leverage = using one arm to balance the other.

---

### **Drill 17: Temple Press**

**Purpose:** Minimize head movement during breathing (reduce drag).

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle
2. As you breathe, press temple (side of head) into extended arm
3. Head rotates with body (not independently)

**What to feel:**
- Head stays aligned with spine
- One goggle stays in water (one-goggle rule)

**Common mistake:** Lifting head forward to breathe = hips sink.

---

### **Drill 18: Dead Arm Freestyle**

**Purpose:** Practice breathing without disrupting stroke rhythm.

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle, breathe to one side
2. Keep breathing-side arm "dead" (extended forward, no pull)
3. Pull only with opposite arm
4. Breathe every stroke

**What to feel:**
- Breathing doesn't interrupt body position
- Can breathe while maintaining streamline

---

## LEVERAGE DRILLS

**Goal:** Core-driven power (hips/shoulders generate propulsion, not just arms).

### **Drill 19: Three Stroke Switch Freestyle**

**Purpose:** Feel hip-driven propulsion.

**How to do it:**
1. Start on right side (right arm extended, left arm at side)
2. Take 3 strokes
3. Pause on left side (left arm extended, right arm at side)
4. Take 3 strokes
5. Repeat

**What to feel:**
- Hips drive rotation (not arms)
- Core generates power for arm stroke

---

### **Drill 20: The Pendulum Effect**

**Purpose:** Use body roll to create momentum (like a pendulum swinging).

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle
2. Exaggerate body roll (rotate 45° each side)
3. Feel momentum carry you from one side to the other

**What to feel:**
- Body roll creates rhythm
- Less arm effort needed when using momentum

---

### **Drill 21: Hip Skating**

**Purpose:** Feel core-driven propulsion (like skating on your side).

**How to do it:**
1. Kick on side (bottom arm extended)
2. Take one stroke, rotate to opposite side
3. Pause (like gliding on ice)
4. Repeat

**What to feel:**
- Hips lead rotation
- "Skating" from side to side

---

## COORDINATION DRILLS

**Goal:** Connect all stroke elements into fluid, powerful forward motion.

### **Drill 22: Heads Up Freestyle**

**Purpose:** Develop strong kick + balanced body position.

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle with head above water (eyes forward)
2. Keep hips at surface (requires strong kick + core engagement)

**What to feel:**
- Kick power needed to maintain position
- Core stabilization

**Why it works:** Removes buoyancy assist from head-down position — forces better technique.

---

### **Drill 23: Horizontal Rope Climbing**

**Purpose:** Feel "pulling yourself past your hand" (not pushing water back).

**How to do it:**
1. Imagine a rope extending forward from each hand
2. Swim freestyle, focus on pulling your body forward along the rope
3. Hand stays anchored in same spot (you move past it)

**What to feel:**
- Body moves forward, hand stays still
- Like climbing a horizontal ladder

---

### **Drill 24: Freestyle with Dolphin**

**Purpose:** Integrate core power (dolphin kick) into freestyle.

**How to do it:**
1. Swim freestyle with dolphin kick (instead of flutter kick)
2. Feel core generate power

**What to feel:**
- Undulation from chest → hips → feet
- Core-driven propulsion

---

### **Drill 25: Bilateral Breathing**

**Purpose:** Balance stroke, prevent over-rotation to one side.

**How to do it:**
1. Breathe every 3 strokes (alternating sides)
2. Or breathe every 5 strokes (advanced)

**What to feel:**
- Balanced body roll (both sides)
- Even stroke on left and right

**Long-term benefit:** Prevents muscle imbalances, improves sighting (open water).

---

## Drill Progression Summary

**Build from simple → complex:**

1. **Body Position** → Float, balance, streamline
2. **Kick** → Productive, sustainable propulsion
3. **Arm Stroke** → Length, path, feel
4. **Recovery** → Relaxed, aligned
5. **Breathing** → Rhythmic, in-line
6. **Leverage** → Core-driven power
7. **Coordination** → Connect all elements

**Training approach:**
- **Identify** stroke flaw (coach feedback)
- **Choose** drill targeting that flaw
- **Practice** repeatedly (over multiple sessions)
- **Reintegrate** into full stroke (coordination drills)

## How to Use Drills in Practice

### **Option 1: Stroke Progression (Start to Finish)**
Work through drills in order, one section at a time.

**Example session:**
1. **Warm-up:** 400m easy freestyle
2. **Body Position:** Downhill Float (4×25m)
3. **Kick:** Vertical Kicking (3×1min)
4. **Arm Stroke:** Catch-Up (6×50m)
5. **Recovery:** Shark Fin (4×25m)
6. **Breathing:** Temple Press (4×50m)
7. **Main Set:** 10×100m freestyle (applying all techniques)

---

### **Option 2: Targeted Correction (Fix Specific Problem)**
Focus on drills addressing a specific flaw.

**Example: Fix low elbow recovery**
1. **Warm-up:** 200m easy
2. **Drill:** Shark Fin (high elbow exaggeration) — 8×25m
3. **Drill:** Floppy Hand (relaxed recovery) — 4×50m
4. **Drill:** Three Stroke Switch (body roll integration) — 6×50m
5. **Application:** Freestyle with focus on high elbow recovery — 6×100m

---

### **Option 3: Drill Sandwich (Warm-up, Drill, Swim, Repeat)**

**Structure:**
- 50m drill
- 50m swim (apply drill technique)
- Repeat

**Example:**
- 50m Catch-Up
- 50m Freestyle (focus on hand entry)
- Repeat 8 times

✅ **Get feedback** — Ask coach to watch and correct  
✅ **Be patient** — Change takes time
