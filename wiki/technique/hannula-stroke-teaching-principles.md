# Hannula's Stroke Teaching Principles

**Source:** *Coaching Swimming Successfully* by Dick Hannula (2003)

## Foundation: Measure to Change

> "If you are going to change something, then you need to measure it." —Unknown

**Application:** When you can measure a skill or performance, you can effect change.

## Speed Formula

**Swimming Speed = Stroke Length + Stroke Rate**

### Stroke Metrics (All Measurable)

| Metric | Definition | How to Measure |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **Stroke Length** | Distance body travels per arm cycle | Pool length ÷ stroke count |
| **Stroke Frequency** | Arm cycles per minute at current rate | Stopwatch with stroke rate function |
| **Stroke Count** | Strokes completed per lap/length | Count each arm entry |

## Teaching Sequence (Howard Firby Method)

### Priority Order
1. **Technique** — correct fundamentals first
2. **Training** — build work capacity
3. **Train to compete** — race-specific preparation  
4. **Train to win** — championship mentality

> "Fundamentals must be learned before training can take place." —Bill Sweetenham

## Optimizing Distance Per Stroke

### Step 1: Maximize (Initial Learning)
- Goal: fewest strokes possible per length
- Teaches swimmers to REACH and extend
- **Warning:** Can lead to dropped elbows if overemphasized

### Step 2: Optimize (Race Application)

**Minimum Number Drill** (from Nort Thornton):

1. Swim 50 yards, count strokes, record time
2. Calculate **Total Number** = Time + Stroke Count
3. Example: 28.5 seconds + 31 strokes = **59.5**
4. Test variations — best efficiency = lowest total number

**Race Pace Integration** (Bill Sweetenham):

For 200-yard event:
- Personal best ÷ 4 = race pace per 50
- Find optimal stroke count at that pace
- Example: If 32 strokes for first 50, maintain 32 for each additional 50
- To improve: swim faster pace while maintaining stroke count

### Step 3: Add Tempo
- Once stroke length optimized, increase rate
- Monitor: as tempo increases, stroke length may drop slightly
- Goal: minimal drop-off while increasing turnover

## Resistance Before Propulsion

**Priority:** Reduce resistance FIRST (requires less energy than increasing propulsion)

### Resistance Reduction Strategies

1. **Streamlined body position**
   - Tapered body type ideal (shoulders → feet)
   - Higher hips in water = less drag
   - Neutral head position (eyes down 90°)

2. **Body alignment**
   - "Skewered on a broom handle" cue
   - Straight line: head → spine → hips
   - Minimize lateral movement

3. **Rotate to cut smaller hole**
   - Flat swimming = maximum resistance
   - Side-to-side rotation = streamlined
   - Head/shoulders create hole, body follows through

### Torpedo Position (Essential Skill)
- Arms hyperextended overhead
- Hands locked (one over other)
- Head down, arms pressed against ears
- Back of head and spine aligned ("platform position")
- **Application:** All starts, turns, underwater kicks

## Drills: 100% Correct or Wrong

> "Drills are only effective when precisely taught, monitored closely, and done correctly. 100% correct is always the goal."

### Drill Effectiveness Checklist
✅ Explained clearly
✅ Demonstrated by competent swimmer  
✅ Monitored constantly by coach
✅ Executed with perfect technique
✅ Corrected immediately when wrong

**No compromises** — sloppy drills teach bad habits.

## Feel for the Water

### Sculling Progression

**Positions to teach:**
1. Front extended (breaststroke position)
2. Midstroke (windshield wiper)
3. Finish position
4. Back positions (arms overhead or at sides)

**Hand Paddles for Feel:**
- "Groover" and "Gripper" paddles with grooves
- Variable sizes and hole patterns
- Han's Paddles (reverse for fingertip pointing)
- Swimming with fists, then opening hand

## 23 Coaching Guidelines (Howard Firby)

1. Begin with standard techniques
2. Get timing/general movement correct first
3. Develop skill first, speed later
4. Speak to EVERY swimmer EVERY session
5. Address swimmers by NAME
6. Use common words, avoid academic jargon
7. Sequence details — one movement at a time
8. Be concise, use word pictures ("arm like swordfish's sword")
9. Be specific (use your hand to demonstrate)
10. Start with what they know, build from there
11. **Compliment THEN correct**
12. Ask for feedback — have swimmer teach back
13. Pick demonstrators carefully (can be just above learners)
14. Be tolerant and patient
15. Reward improvement
16. Persist — some need more reminding
17. Avoid being "picky" — don't constantly interrupt
18. Avoid distractions (swimmers face you, backs to distractions)
19. Coach at swimmers' eye level (crouch, sit, or in water)
20. Use visual aids (video is game-changer)
21. Use buddy coaching (swimmers coach each other)
22. Make major changes early in season
23. Use stroke drills and progressions

## The One Minute Manager Method

Applied to swimming (from Blanchard & Lorber):

### 1. Tell Them What They Did Right
- Positive mental pictures
- Feel good = learn better
- Reinforcement tool

### 2. Keep It Short & Simple (KISS)
- One focus point at a time
- Concise commands

### 3. Never Reprimand Learners
- Persistent teaching, patient approach
- Treat as learners acquiring advanced skills

### 4. Watch Them Perform
- Show you care by watching
- Makes swimmers aware of need to perfect

### 5. Praise or Redirect
- Praise progress
- If not ready, repeat teaching sequence

**Teaching Loop:**
1. Explain → 2. Demonstrate → 3. Attempt → 4. Observe → 5. Praise OR Start Over

## Transition Drills (Progressive Teaching)

Combine drills in logical sequence:

**Example Crawl Progression:**
- Lateral streamlined kick → 
- One-arm extended swim → 
- Stretch-up swims → 
- Full stroke

**Example Kick/Swim Combo:**
- 9 lateral kicks, 9 strokes
- 7 lateral kicks, 7 strokes  
- 5 lateral kicks, 5 strokes
- 3 lateral kicks, 3 strokes

## Takeaways for Coaches

✅ **MEASURE everything** — stroke count, length, rate, times
✅ **Optimize before maximize** — race-specific efficiency matters more
✅ **Reduce resistance first** — easier than increasing propulsion
✅ **Demand perfect drills** — 100% correct or don't do them
✅ **Compliment then correct** — positive reinforcement first
✅ **Speak to every swimmer every practice** — by name
✅ **Teach fundamentals BEFORE hard training** — technique unlocks speed
