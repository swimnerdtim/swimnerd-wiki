# Phelps 100 Fly Finish Technique: The Half-Stroke

**Source:** *No Limits: The Will to Succeed* by Michael Phelps with Alan Abrahamson (2008)

## The Most Important Lesson in Swimming

**Bob Bowman to 12-year-old Phelps:**

> "Michael, you know what my job is?"  
> "It's to get you in the ballpark."  
> "You know what your job is?"  
> "**It's to get your freaking hand on the wall.**"

---

## Why Finish Matters

### Matt Biondi - Seoul 1988, 100m Fly

**The Setup:**
- Biondi first at turn
- Still leading with 10 meters to go
- Got caught between strokes
- Opted to **glide** instead of taking extra half-stroke
- Problem: He was farther from wall than he thought

**Result:**
- At 99 meters: **First place**
- At 100 meters: **Second place**
- Lost to Anthony Nesty by **0.01 seconds**

**Biondi afterward:**
> "One one-hundredth of a second. What if I had grown my fingernails longer?"

---

## The Anthony Nesty Lesson (2002)

**After Phelps' first major 100 fly victory:**

Anthony Nesty approached Phelps and said:
> "That's how I beat Matt Biondi in the 100 fly that day. **It was the touch.**"

**Phelps:**
> "That day was the first time I truly understood how important the finish of the 100 fly could be."

## Beijing 2008: The Half-Stroke That Won Gold

### 100m Butterfly Final vs. Milorad Cavic

**The Setup:**
- Phelps going for 7th gold (to tie Mark Spitz)
- Cavic turned first (23.42), Phelps 7th at turn (24.04)
- Phelps charging back final 50m
- Both swimmers hurtling toward wall together

**The Critical Decision (last stroke):**

**Cavic:** Opted to **glide in**  
**Phelps:** **Chopped his last stroke** - short and fast, a half-stroke

**Phelps afterward:**
> "I still can't fully explain why. Maybe it was experience. Absolutely competitive will. There wasn't time to form a complete thought. It was an impulse. I knew I had to do something. **Gliding was not going to win gold.**"

**Result:**
- Phelps: **50.58** (Olympic Record)
- Cavic: **50.59**
- **Margin: 0.01 seconds**

---

## Technical Breakdown: Why the Half-Stroke Won

### 1. Phelps' Final Stroke Mechanics

**What he did RIGHT:**
- **Short, fast stroke** (not full extension)
- **Head down** (Cavic's came up)
- **Feet straight** (Cavic's came up)
- **Horizontal body position** (minimal vertical movement)

**Swimming fast = horizontal proposition**
- Vertical movements slow you down
- Straight, horizontal line = fastest

---

### 2. The Omega Timing Pad

**Pressure required:** 6.5 pounds (3 kilograms)

**How it works:**
- Anything less: Pad thinks it's waves, won't respond
- 3kg or more: Clock stops

**Gliding in:**
- Less force at touch
- Risk of grazing pad without triggering

**Driving in:**
- More force at touch
- Guaranteed trigger

---

### 3. Long Finish vs. Short Finish

**Long finish (Cavic):**
- Full stroke extension
- Glide to wall
- Body decelerates
- Risk of being "too long"

**Short finish (Phelps):**
- Half-stroke adjustment
- Quick, choppy movement
- Maintained momentum
- "Turned a long finish into a short finish"

## Rome 2009: The 49.82

### World Championships 100m Fly Final

**The Setup:**
- Cavic set world record in semifinals: **50.01**
- Phelps' plan: Stay at Cavic's hip at turn, close hard

**The Turn:**
- Cavic: **22.69** (fastest ever)
- Phelps: **23.36** (his fastest ever)
- Gap: Only 0.67 seconds (better than Beijing)

**The Finish:**
- Phelps ran Cavic down with 10m to go
- This time: **No doubt at wall**

**Result:**
- Phelps: **49.82** (first man under 50 seconds)
- Cavic: **49.95**
- Back half: Phelps 26.46, Cavic 27.26

**Phelps' emotion:**
> Smacked water, "bring it" motion to crowd, hand up: number one, popped front of LZR suit.

## Application for Coaches

### 1. Drill the Finish Every Practice

**Bob Bowman's rule:**
> "I would not tolerate lazy finishes in practices, ever."

**Practice finish like you'll race:**
- Full speed into wall
- No lazy touches
- No gliding
- **Slam the wall**

---

### 2. Teach the Half-Stroke Adjustment

**When to use:**
- Caught between strokes at wall
- Not enough room for full stroke
- Need to maintain momentum

**How to execute:**
- **Quick, choppy motion**
- Short burst from shoulders
- Drive hand forward (not down)
- Maximum force at touch

**Practice drill:**
- Sprint sets ending at various distances (12.5m, 13m, 13.5m)
- Forces swimmers to adjust final stroke
- Builds adaptability

---

### 3. Body Position at Finish

**Coaching cues:**
- "Head down into wall"
- "Feet stay straight"
- "Horizontal = fast, vertical = slow"
- "Drive, don't glide"

**Video review:**
- Film finishes from side angle
- Show swimmers head/feet positioning
- Compare winners vs. losers

---

### 4. The Mental Aspect

**Demonstrated ability = confidence**

**Bowman:**
> "Confidence comes from demonstrated ability. You can't dream it up."

**How it works:**
1. Do something successfully in practice
2. Next time you believe you can do it again
3. Repeat thousands of times
4. Confidence becomes automatic

**In Beijing:**
Phelps didn't **think** about closing hard. He **simply did it** because he'd done it thousands of times before.

---

### 5. The "Ballpark" Philosophy

**Coach's job:** Get swimmer in position to win  
**Swimmer's job:** **Finish**

**In 100 fly:**
- Coach handles strategy, pacing, stroke work
- Swimmer handles execution, especially **the touch**

**Bowman:**
> "Details matter. They'll make the difference when you're called on to compete. By reviewing and reinforcing seemingly minor details daily, MP was ready when he had to perform."

## The Athens 2004 Template

### 100m Fly Final vs. Ian Crocker

**The Setup:**
- Crocker led at turn, world-record pace
- Phelps in **5th place**, 0.77 seconds back
- At 20m from finish, Phelps at Crocker's shoulder
- At 15m, Bob stopped watching (thought it was over)

**The Finish:**
- Three swimmers churned toward wall
- Serdinov bobbed too high
- Crocker caught in midstroke
- **Phelps reached at full extension, end of stroke**

**Result:**
- Serdinov: **51.36**
- Crocker: **51.29**
- Phelps: **51.25**
- **Margin over Crocker: 0.04 seconds**

**Baltimore Sun headline:** "History by a hair"

## Coaching Takeaways

### The 100 Fly Paradox

**Phelps' style:**
- Easy first 50
- Hammer second 50
- **Always fighting at the end**

**This made finish technique CRITICAL:**
- Can't rely on big lead
- Must execute under pressure
- Every hundredth counts

---

### Practice Points

**Daily reminders:**
1. "Get your hand on the wall"
2. No gliding in practice
3. Drive to the finish
4. Short, fast, aggressive

**Race-specific work:**
- Simulate being behind at turn
- Practice closing gap under fatigue
- Drill various finishing scenarios
- Build automatic response

---

## The Bottom Line

**Three finishes, three different outcomes:**

1. **Matt Biondi (1988):** Glided → Lost by 0.01
2. **Phelps Beijing (2008):** Half-stroke → Won by 0.01
3. **Phelps Rome (2009):** Drove through → Won by 0.13, broke 50 seconds

**The lesson:**
> "It's not just about being fast. It's about **finishing fast**. The last stroke matters as much as the first."

**Bowman's mantra:**
> "You swim aggressively all the way to the wall."
