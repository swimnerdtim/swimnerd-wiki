# Stroke Biomechanics: Muscle Recruitment Patterns

**Source:** *Swimming Anatomy* by Ian McLeod  
**Category:** Stroke Technique | Biomechanics | Muscle Function  
**For:** Coaches, swimmers, strength coaches designing swim-specific dryland programs

## Overview

Understanding which muscles power each phase of a stroke helps coaches:
- Design targeted dryland programs
- Identify stroke flaws (weak link in the kinetic chain)
- Prevent injuries by balancing muscle development
- Explain technique cues with anatomical clarity

**Key Concept: The Kinetic Chain**
> "Think of the body as a long chain and each body segment as a link in the chain. Because all segments are linked together, movement in one segment affects all the other segments. If a link in the chain is weak, a loss of power transfer can occur, bodily movements can become uncoordinated, and the risk of injury can increase."

## Freestyle

### Propulsive Phase (Underwater Pull)

#### Hand Entry → Catch
- **Pectoralis major** (clavicular/upper portion) — initiates the catch
- **Latissimus dorsi** — quickly joins to assist pec major
- **Wrist flexors** — hold wrist in slight flexion throughout pull

#### Mid-Pull (Maximum Force Generation)
- **Pectoralis major + Latissimus dorsi** — generate majority of propulsive force
- **Biceps brachii + Brachialis** — flex elbow from full extension → ~30° flexion
- Elbow held at ~30° flexion during power phase

#### Finish (Hip to Recovery)
- **Triceps brachii** — extends elbow, driving hand backward and upward
- Total extension depends on stroke mechanics (many coaches now teach early recovery before full extension)

### Recovery Phase
- **Deltoid** — lifts arm out of water
- **Rotator cuff** (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis) — repositions arm overhead
- **Shoulder blade stabilizers** — provide firm base for arm movement

### Stabilizers (Active Throughout)
**Shoulder Blade Stabilizers:**
- Pectoralis minor
- Rhomboids
- Levator scapula
- Middle & lower trapezius
- Serratus anterior

**Core Stabilizers:**
- Transversus abdominis
- Rectus abdominis
- Internal & external obliques
- Erector spinae

**Role:** Link upper and lower extremity movements; coordinate body roll

### Kick

#### Downbeat (Propulsive)
- **Iliopsoas + Rectus femoris** — initiate hip flexion
- **Quadriceps** (vastus lateralis, intermedius, medialis) — extend knee forcefully
- **Gastrocnemius + Soleus** — maintain plantarflexed foot position

#### Upbeat (Recovery)
- **Gluteus maximus + medius** — extend hip
- **Hamstrings** (biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus) — assist hip extension

## Butterfly

**Key difference from freestyle:** Arms move in unison (not reciprocal)

### Propulsive Phase
**Identical recruitment to freestyle:**
- Pectoralis major + Latissimus dorsi (primary movers)
- Wrist flexors (maintain neutral/slight flexion)
- Biceps brachii + Brachialis (flex elbow to ~40° at mid-pull)
- **Triceps brachii** — MORE forceful extension than freestyle during finish

### Recovery Phase
- **Deltoid + Rotator cuff** — lift arms out and reposition overhead
- **DIFFERENT from freestyle:** No body roll to aid recovery
- **Undulating torso movement** compensates — driven by paraspinal muscles + core

### Undulation Mechanics
1. **Paraspinal muscles** (multiple groups, lower back → skull) — arch back
2. Arms swing through recovery while back is arched
3. **Abdominal muscles** — contract to prepare upper body for hand entry
4. This creates the signature "wave" motion

### Kick
**Same muscles as freestyle kick, but legs move in unison**

**Dolphin kick (starts/turns):** Recruits larger muscle groups
- Hip flexors + extensors
- Knee extensors
- **Core stabilizers + paraspinals** — create undulating movement

## Backstroke

### Propulsive Phase

#### Hand Entry → Catch
- **Rotation at shoulder** — little finger enters first
- **Extension at elbow** — arm enters fully extended
- **Latissimus dorsi** — dominates the pull (larger role than in freestyle)
- **Pectoralis major** — smaller contribution than in freestyle

#### Mid-Pull
- **Wrist flexors** — maintain neutral to slightly extended wrist
- **Biceps brachii + Brachialis** — flex elbow to ~45° (sometimes up to 90°)

#### Finish
- **Greater emphasis on triceps brachii** during finish (similar to butterfly)
- Powerful elbow extension drives propulsion

### Recovery
- **Deltoid + Rotator cuff** — lift arm out and reposition overhead
- **Stabilizers** function similarly to freestyle (body roll aids recovery)

### Kick
**Directional difference only** (body position is supine):
- Upbeat = propulsive phase (analogous to freestyle downbeat)
- Downbeat = recovery phase
- Muscle recruitment patterns identical to freestyle

**Dolphin kick (starts/turns):** Same recruitment as butterfly

## Breaststroke

### Arm Pull

#### Propulsive Phase (Outsweep → Insweep)
**First half (outsweep):**
- **Pectoralis major** (clavicular) — initiates movement
- **Latissimus dorsi** — joins immediately

**Second half (insweep):**
- **Pectoralis major + Latissimus dorsi** — forceful contraction pulls arms to midline
- Forces directed: FORWARD (propulsion) + UPWARD (lift head/shoulders out of water)
- **Paraspinal muscles** — assist upward lift

#### Recovery Phase
- **Flexion & rotation at elbow** brings hands to midline under chest
- **Triceps brachii** — extends elbow to return to elongated position
- **Pectoralis major, anterior deltoid, biceps brachii (long head)** — flex shoulder joint

### Stabilizers
- **Shoulder blade stabilizers** — create firm base (even without body roll)
- **Core stabilizers** — link upper and lower extremity movements

### Kick

#### Propulsive Phase (Outsweep → Insweep)
**Starting position:** Feet hip-width apart, knees/hips flexed

**Outsweep:**
- **Outward rotation** at hip, knee, ankle — turn feet out
- **Gluteal muscles + Hamstrings** — extend hip
- **Rectus femoris + Quadriceps** — extend knee

**Insweep:**
- Knees/hips still not fully extended at transition
- Muscle groups continue action into insweep
- **Adductor muscles** (inner thigh) — rapidly adduct legs together (power phase!)
- **Gastrocnemius + Soleus** — point foot/ankle to minimize drag during finish

#### Recovery Phase
- **Rectus femoris + Iliopsoas** — flex hip
- **Hamstrings** — flex knee

## Common Muscle Imbalances in Swimmers

### Upper Body
❌ **Overdeveloped:** Latissimus dorsi, Pectoralis major  
❌ **Underdeveloped:** Scapular stabilizers (middle/lower trapezius, rhomboids)

**Result:** Rounded-shoulder posture, increased injury risk

### Lower Body
❌ **Overdeveloped:** Quadriceps, Hip flexors  
❌ **Underdeveloped:** Hamstrings, Gluteal muscles

**Result:** Postural imbalances, knee/hip injury risk

### Solution
Design dryland programs that:
1. Strengthen weak stabilizers (scapular, core)
2. Balance antagonist muscle groups (push/pull ratio)
3. Address flexibility in overdeveloped areas

## Elbow Movement Patterns Across Strokes

| Stroke | Catch Phase | Mid-Pull | Finish |
|--------|-------------|----------|---------|
| **Freestyle** | Extension → 30° flexion | Biceps + Brachialis hold position | Triceps extends (varies by mechanics) |
| **Butterfly** | Extension → 40° flexion | Biceps + Brachialis active | **Forceful** triceps extension |
| **Backstroke** | Extension → 45-90° flexion | Greater flexion than freestyle | **Forceful** triceps extension |
| **Breaststroke** | Near full extension (triceps maintains) | Biceps + Brachialis flex during insweep | Triceps extends during recovery |

**Dryland Implication:** Elbow extensors (triceps) are MORE active during swimming than flexors → Target 2:1 ratio (extensors:flexors) in strength program

## Practical Applications for Coaches

### Stroke Diagnosis
**Problem:** Swimmer collapses elbow during catch  
**Diagnosis:** Weak biceps brachii/brachialis OR poor lat/pec major recruitment  
**Fix:** Dryland pulling exercises + tempo work at catch phase

**Problem:** Weak finish, short stroke  
**Diagnosis:** Weak triceps brachii  
**Fix:** Triceps pushdowns, kickbacks, emphasis on full extension in drills

### Dryland Exercise Selection
**To improve freestyle catch:**
- Lat pull-downs (latissimus dorsi)
- Cable pulls (pectoralis major)
- Elbow flexion exercises (biceps/brachialis)

**To improve butterfly undulation:**
- Superman holds (paraspinal muscles)
- Physioball crunches (abdominals)
- Core stabilization exercises

**To prevent shoulder injury:**
- Scapular push-ups (serratus anterior)
- T-Y-A exercises (scapular stabilizers)
- Rotator cuff internal/external rotation with tubing

### Kick Development
**Weak freestyle kick:**
- Strengthen hip flexors (iliopsoas, rectus femoris)
- Strengthen quadriceps (knee extension power)
- Plantarflexion exercises (gastrocnemius/soleus)

**Weak breaststroke kick:**
- Adductor strengthening (power phase!)
- Hip flexibility for outsweep positioning
- Ankle flexibility + plantarflexion strength
