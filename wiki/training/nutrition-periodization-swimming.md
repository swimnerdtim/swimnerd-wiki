# Nutrition Periodization for Swimmers
**Source:** *Nutrition Periodization for Endurance Athletes* by Bob Seebohar, MS, RD, CSCS

## Overview

**Core Philosophy:** Your nutrition should support your training phases — not the other way around.

**Old school thinking:** "Carb-load the night before the meet."  
**New school thinking:** Adjust macronutrient ratios throughout the season based on training volume, intensity, and goals.

Just as you periodize training (base → build → taper → competition), you should periodize nutrition.

---

## The Three Cornerstones of Endurance Performance

All three must align:

1. **Training** - What you do in the pool/gym
2. **Recovery** - Sleep, rest days, regeneration
3. **Nutrition** - Fuel to support #1 and #2

**Critical insight:** Nutrition isn't just race week. It's year-round, phase-specific fueling.

---

## Macronutrient Needs by Training Phase

### Base/Preparation Phase (12-16 weeks)

**Training characteristics:**
- High volume, low-moderate intensity
- 6-10 workouts/week
- Focus: aerobic capacity, endurance, technique
- Long aerobic sets, threshold work

**Nutrition priorities:**

| Macronutrient | Amount | Purpose |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| **Carbohydrate** | 6-8 g/kg body weight/day | Fuel high training volume |
| **Protein** | 1.4-1.6 g/kg body weight/day | Muscle repair, adaptation |
| **Fat** | 0.8-1.0 g/kg body weight/day | Hormone production, recovery |

**Why high carbs?** High-volume training = high glycogen depletion. Inadequate carbs = poor recovery, stagnation, overtraining risk.

**Example (70 kg / 154 lb swimmer):**
- Carbs: 420-560g/day (1,680-2,240 calories)
- Protein: 98-112g/day (392-448 calories)
- Fat: 56-70g/day (504-630 calories)
- **Total: ~2,600-3,300 cal/day** (adjust based on individual needs)

---

### Build/Specific Preparation Phase (4-8 weeks)

**Training characteristics:**
- Moderate-high volume, increasing intensity
- More race-pace work, lactate tolerance sets
- Higher quality, slightly lower quantity

**Nutrition priorities:**

| Macronutrient | Amount | Purpose |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| **Carbohydrate** | 7-9 g/kg body weight/day | Support higher intensity |
| **Protein** | 1.5-1.7 g/kg body weight/day | Repair from intense efforts |
| **Fat** | 0.8-1.0 g/kg body weight/day | Maintain hormonal health |

**Why MORE carbs despite lower volume?** Intensity = greater glycogen depletion per workout. Race-pace work demands more muscle glycogen than aerobic swimming.

---

### Taper/Peak Phase (1-3 weeks before key meet)

**Training characteristics:**
- Sharply reduced volume (40-60% of peak)
- Maintain intensity via short, sharp efforts
- Focus: rest, glycogen supercompensation

**Nutrition priorities:**

| Macronutrient | Amount | Purpose |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| **Carbohydrate** | 8-10 g/kg body weight/day | Maximize glycogen stores |
| **Protein** | 1.2-1.4 g/kg body weight/day | Lower needs due to less damage |
| **Fat** | 0.6-0.8 g/kg body weight/day | Reduce to make room for carbs |

**Critical taper nutrition mistake:** Swimmers reduce training volume but keep eating like it's base phase → gain weight, feel sluggish.

**Right approach:**
- **Days 7-4 before meet:** 8 g/kg carbs (moderate loading)
- **Days 3-1 before meet:** 10 g/kg carbs (aggressive loading)
- Reduce fat slightly to avoid excess calories
- Keep protein moderate

**70 kg swimmer pre-meet example:**
- Carbs: 700g/day (2,800 cal) — **60-65% of total intake**
- Protein: 84-98g/day (336-392 cal)
- Fat: 42-56g/day (378-504 cal)
- **Total: ~3,500-3,700 cal/day for 2-3 days before racing**

---

### Competition Phase (Meet Week)

**Day-of-race nutrition:**

**Pre-race (2-4 hours before):**
- 1-4 g carbs/kg body weight
- Low fat, low fiber (avoid GI distress)
- Moderate protein
- Examples: Oatmeal + banana, bagel + honey, rice + small chicken breast

**1 hour before:**
- Light carb snack if needed: sports drink, applesauce, pretzels
- Avoid heavy food

**30 min before warm-up:**
- 8-16 oz sports drink OR water

**Between prelims/finals (if <4 hours apart):**
- Immediately post-swim: 0.5-1.0 g carbs/kg (simple, fast-absorbing)
- Examples: Sports drink, banana, rice cakes, white bread + jam
- Small protein: Chocolate milk, protein shake, deli turkey
- **Goal:** Rapid glycogen replenishment for finals

**Post-competition recovery:**
- Within 30 minutes: 1.0-1.2 g carbs/kg + 0.3 g protein/kg
- Example (70kg): 70-84g carbs + 21g protein
  - Chocolate milk (500ml): ~60g carbs, 20g protein ✅
  - Banana + protein shake
  - Rice bowl + grilled chicken

---

### Transition/Recovery Phase (2-4 weeks post-season)

**Training characteristics:**
- Low volume, low intensity, cross-training
- Active recovery, technique focus
- Mental break

**Nutrition priorities:**

| Macronutrient | Amount | Purpose |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| **Carbohydrate** | 5-6 g/kg body weight/day | Match reduced training |
| **Protein** | 1.2-1.4 g/kg body weight/day | Maintain muscle mass |
| **Fat** | 0.8-1.0 g/kg body weight/day | Normal healthy intake |

**Why reduce carbs?** Training volume drops 60-80%. Eating base-phase carbs = unwanted weight gain.

**Critical:** This is NOT "off-season eating whatever." Maintain structure, just adjust portions.

## Daily Timing: When to Eat What

### Before Morning Practice

**Problem:** Swimmers often skip breakfast → poor performance, muscle breakdown.

**Solution:**
- **60-90 min before:** Small carb-based meal
  - Oatmeal + banana
  - Toast + honey
  - Rice cakes + peanut butter
- **30 min before:** Light snack if needed
  - Sports drink
  - Applesauce pouch

### Before Afternoon/Evening Practice

**3-4 hours before:**
- Full balanced meal: carbs + protein + small fat
- Rice + chicken + veggies
- Pasta + lean meat + salad

**1-2 hours before:**
- Light carb snack: banana, toast, granola bar

**30 min before:**
- Sports drink or water

### During Practice

**Sessions <60 minutes:**
- Water only (500-750ml/hour)

**Sessions 60-90+ minutes:**
- Sports drink with 6-8% carbohydrate solution
- Target: 30-60g carbs/hour for sessions >75 min
- Sip 150-250ml every 15-20 minutes

### Immediately After Practice (The 30-Minute Window)

**Why it matters:** First 30-60 minutes = glycogen synthesis happens 3x faster than later.

**Target:**
- 1.0-1.2 g carbs/kg body weight
- 0.3-0.5 g protein/kg body weight
- 3:1 or 4:1 carb-to-protein ratio ideal

**Practical examples (70kg swimmer):**
- 70-84g carbs + 21-35g protein
- **Option 1:** 500ml chocolate milk (60g carbs, 20g protein) + banana
- **Option 2:** Protein shake (30g protein) + 2 rice cakes with jam
- **Option 3:** Bagel (50g carbs) + 2 tbsp peanut butter + apple

**Don't skip this.** Even if not hungry, liquid recovery (smoothie, shake, chocolate milk) works.

### 2-4 Hours Post-Training

Full balanced meal:
- Carbs: rice, pasta, potatoes, bread
- Protein: chicken, fish, lean beef, tofu
- Fat: olive oil, nuts, avocado
- Vegetables

## Hydration Guidelines for Swimmers

**Common mistake:** "I'm in water, I don't need to hydrate."  
**Reality:** Swimmers sweat 0.5-1.5 L/hour in warm pools. Dehydration = performance drop.

### Daily Hydration (Non-Training)

**Goal:** Straw-colored urine all day.

**Baseline:**
- 30-40 ml water/kg body weight/day
- 70kg swimmer: 2.1-2.8 L/day minimum

**Check:** Morning urine should be light yellow. Dark = dehydrated.

### Pre-Training Hydration

**2-3 hours before:**
- 400-600ml water or sports drink

**10-20 min before:**
- 200-300ml water

**Purpose:** Start practice euhydrated (well-hydrated).

### During Training Hydration

**General rule:**
- 150-250ml every 15-20 minutes
- 600-1000ml per hour depending on intensity/temp

**How to know:**
- Weigh yourself before/after practice (naked)
- For every 1 lb (0.45kg) lost → drink 16-24 oz (500-750ml)

**Sports drink vs water:**
- <60 min session: Water fine
- >60 min OR high intensity: Sports drink (replenishes sodium, carbs)

### Post-Training Hydration

**Rule:** Drink 150% of fluid lost via sweat.

**Example:**
- Lost 1 kg (2.2 lbs) during practice
- Drink 1.5 L (50 oz) over next 2-4 hours

**Why 150%?** You continue losing fluid via urine. Overcompensate slightly.

**Best choice:**
- Sports drink for first 500ml (sodium + carbs)
- Water for remaining

## Special Considerations for Swimmers

### Two-A-Days (Morning + Afternoon Practice)

**Challenge:** Limited recovery time between sessions.

**Nutrition strategy:**

**Immediately after AM practice:**
- Aggressive carb + protein (1.2 g/kg carbs, 0.4 g/kg protein)
- Liquid preferred if solid food unappealing

**Mid-morning snack (2-3 hours post-AM):**
- Balanced: carbs + protein
- Greek yogurt + granola + berries
- Turkey sandwich

**Lunch (if 4+ hours before PM):**
- Full meal: carbs + protein + fat + veggies

**Pre-PM snack (1-2 hours before):**
- Light carbs: banana, rice cakes, sports drink

**Post-PM practice:**
- Full recovery nutrition again

**Key:** You MUST refuel between sessions or PM practice quality tanks.

---

### Multi-Day Meets (Prelims/Finals)

**Friday night:**
- High-carb dinner: pasta, rice, potatoes
- Moderate protein
- Low fat (easier digestion)

**Saturday morning (Day 1):**
- 3-4 hours pre-warm-up: Carb-based breakfast
  - Oatmeal + banana + toast
  - Pancakes + syrup + small eggs
- Bring meet snacks: bananas, rice cakes, pretzels, sports drink

**Between prelims/finals (<4 hours):**
- Immediately post-prelims: Simple carbs (banana, sports drink, white bread + jam)
- 2 hours before finals: Light carb + small protein snack

**Saturday night (between Day 1/Day 2):**
- High-carb dinner again
- Restock glycogen for Sunday

**Sunday (Day 2):**
- Repeat Saturday protocol

**Mistake to avoid:** Eating heavy, greasy food at meets (pizza, burgers). Slows digestion, impairs performance.

---

### Weight Management During Season

**Problem:** Swimmers want to lose weight mid-season.

**Reality:** Active weight loss during heavy training = performance decline.

**Right approach:**

**Best time to lose weight:**
1. **Off-season/transition phase** (lowest training load)
2. **Early base phase** (if needed, lose slowly: 0.5 lb/week max)

**Never cut weight during:**
- Build phase
- Taper
- Competition phase

**Why?** Energy deficit + high training = inadequate recovery, loss of muscle mass, poor performance, injury risk.

**If already at healthy weight:** Focus on body composition via strength training + adequate protein, NOT calorie restriction.

## Practical Meal Planning Template

### Base Phase Daily Nutrition (70kg swimmer, 2x/day training)

**Breakfast (pre-AM practice):**
- Oatmeal (1 cup dry) + banana + honey
- OR toast (2 slices) + peanut butter + apple

**Post-AM practice:**
- Chocolate milk (500ml) + bagel
- OR protein smoothie (banana, protein powder, berries, milk)

**Mid-morning snack:**
- Greek yogurt + granola + berries

**Lunch:**
- Rice bowl (2 cups rice) + grilled chicken (150g) + stir-fry veggies + olive oil

**Pre-PM practice snack:**
- Banana + rice cakes + sports drink

**Post-PM practice:**
- Protein shake + fruit

**Dinner:**
- Pasta (2 cups) + lean meat/fish (150g) + marinara + salad + bread

**Evening snack (if needed):**
- Toast + jam OR fruit

**Macros:**
- Carbs: ~500g (7 g/kg)
- Protein: ~110g (1.6 g/kg)
- Fat: ~60g (0.9 g/kg)
- **Total: ~3,100 calories**

---

### Taper Phase Daily Nutrition (70kg swimmer, 1x/day light training)

**Reduce portions slightly but increase carb %:**

**Breakfast:**
- Oatmeal (1 cup) + banana + honey

**Snack:**
- Sports drink + pretzels

**Lunch:**
- Large rice bowl (2.5 cups rice) + chicken (100g) + veggies

**Pre-practice:**
- Banana + sports drink

**Post-practice:**
- Chocolate milk + bagel + jam

**Dinner:**
- Large pasta serving (2 cups) + small meat (100g) + bread + salad (light dressing)

**Evening:**
- Rice cakes + jam

**Macros:**
- Carbs: ~600g (8-9 g/kg) — **higher %**
- Protein: ~90g (1.3 g/kg) — **slightly lower**
- Fat: ~50g (0.7 g/kg) — **lower**
- **Total: ~3,200 calories** (similar total, but re-proportioned toward carbs)

## Key Takeaways for Coaches

✅ **Nutrition should change with training phases** — base, build, taper, competition, transition all have different macronutrient needs.

✅ **High-volume base ≠ highest carb needs** — intensity matters more than volume for glycogen depletion.

✅ **The 30-minute post-training window is real** — glycogen synthesis is 3x faster immediately after training.

✅ **Taper nutrition is tricky** — must increase carb % while reducing total calories (don't gain weight during taper).

✅ **Hydration matters in the pool** — swimmers sweat despite being in water. Monitor and enforce hydration.

✅ **Between-session nutrition makes or breaks two-a-days** — aggressive refueling after AM practice is non-negotiable.

✅ **Meet-day nutrition should be rehearsed in training** — don't experiment race day.
