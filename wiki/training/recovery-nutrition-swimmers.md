# Recovery Nutrition for Swimmers
**Source:** *Nutrition Periodization for Endurance Athletes* by Bob Seebohar, MS, RD, CSCS

## Overview

**Recovery nutrition is WHERE adaptation happens.**

Training breaks you down. Food + rest builds you back up — stronger, faster, more resilient.

**The 30-60 minute post-training window is when your body is MOST receptive to refueling.** Miss it repeatedly = slower recovery, reduced adaptation, increased injury/illness risk.

---

## The Science: Why Timing Matters

### Glycogen Synthesis Rates

**Immediately post-exercise (0-30 min):**
- Glycogen synthesis: **~7-10% per hour**
- Muscles are insulin-sensitive (glucose uptake 3x faster)
- Glucose transporters (GLUT4) still elevated from exercise

**2+ hours post-exercise:**
- Glycogen synthesis: **~2-5% per hour**
- Insulin sensitivity decreases
- Requires 24+ hours to fully restore glycogen if you don't eat strategically

**Practical consequence:**

**Scenario A: Eat within 30 min**
- Glycogen 90% restored in 6-8 hours
- Ready for next hard session

**Scenario B: Wait 2-3 hours to eat**
- Glycogen 60-70% restored in 24 hours
- Next session = running on fumes, poor quality

**Bottom line:** Eat SOON after training. The clock starts when you finish your last lap.

## The 30-Minute Rule

### What to Eat

**Target macros:**
- **Carbs:** 1.0-1.2 g/kg body weight
- **Protein:** 0.3-0.5 g/kg body weight
- **Ratio:** 3:1 or 4:1 carbs-to-protein (optimal)

**Why this ratio?**
- Carbs → refill glycogen
- Protein → repair muscle damage, enhance glycogen storage (when combined with carbs)
- Together → better than either alone

### Practical Examples (70 kg / 154 lb swimmer)

**Target:** 70-84g carbs + 21-35g protein

#### Option 1: Chocolate Milk (Easiest)
- 500ml (16 oz) low-fat chocolate milk
  - Carbs: ~60g
  - Protein: ~20g
- **+ 1 banana** (adds 25g carbs)
- **Total:** 85g carbs, 20g protein ✅

**Why chocolate milk?** Liquid = fast absorption, already mixed macros, convenient, tastes good (compliance).

#### Option 2: Protein Shake + Fruit
- Whey protein shake (1 scoop in water or milk)
  - Protein: 25-30g
  - Carbs (if with milk): 12g
- **+ 1 banana** (25g carbs)
- **+ handful of berries** (15g carbs)
- **+ honey (1 tbsp)** (17g carbs)
- **Total:** ~70g carbs, 30g protein ✅

#### Option 3: Solid Food (If Tolerated)
- Bagel (50g carbs) + 2 tbsp peanut butter (8g protein, some fat)
- **+ Greek yogurt (small)** (15g protein, 10g carbs)
- **Total:** 60g carbs, 23g protein ✅

#### Option 4: Rice Cakes + Turkey
- 3-4 rice cakes (30g carbs) + jam (15g carbs)
- **+ 3-4 slices deli turkey** (20g protein)
- **+ sports drink (250ml)** (15g carbs)
- **Total:** 60g carbs, 20g protein ✅

#### Option 5: Emergency/On-the-Go
- Sports drink (500ml): 30g carbs
- **+ Protein bar** (20g protein, 30g carbs)
- **Total:** 60g carbs, 20g protein ✅

---

### What if I'm Not Hungry?

**Reality check:** Appetite is suppressed 30-60 min post-hard training (especially after high-intensity sets). This is NORMAL.

**Solution:** Drink your recovery nutrition.

**Liquid options:**
- Chocolate milk
- Protein smoothie (blend protein powder + banana + berries + milk)
- Meal replacement shake
- Sports drink + protein shake

**Pro tip:** Make recovery shakes in advance. Bring to practice in a cooler. Drink in locker room before showering.

## Two-Hour Window: Second Meal

The 30-minute window isn't the ONLY meal. It's the FIRST recovery meal.

**Follow-up meal (2-3 hours post-training):**

**Target:**
- Full, balanced meal
- Carbs: 1.5-2.0 g/kg
- Protein: 0.4-0.5 g/kg
- Healthy fats
- Vegetables

**Examples (70 kg swimmer):**

### Example 1: Rice Bowl
- 2 cups cooked rice (90g carbs)
- 150g grilled chicken (40g protein)
- Stir-fried vegetables (fiber, vitamins)
- 1 tbsp olive oil (healthy fat)

### Example 2: Pasta Meal
- 2 cups cooked pasta (80g carbs)
- Marinara sauce (10g carbs)
- Lean ground turkey (150g, 35g protein)
- Side salad with olive oil dressing
- Garlic bread (1 slice, 15g carbs)

### Example 3: Breakfast-for-Lunch (Post-AM Practice)
- 3 eggs (18g protein, some fat)
- 2 slices toast (30g carbs)
- 1 cup oatmeal (50g carbs)
- Banana (25g carbs)
- Glass of orange juice (25g carbs)

**Total for 2-meal recovery:**
- Immediate (30 min): 70-84g carbs, 21-35g protein
- Follow-up (2-3 hours): 100-130g carbs, 30-40g protein
- **Combined:** Glycogen fully restocked, muscle repair underway

## Special Case: Two-A-Days

**Problem:** Only 4-6 hours between AM and PM practice.

**Challenge:** Not enough time for full glycogen restoration via normal eating.

**Solution:** AGGRESSIVE post-AM nutrition + strategic pre-PM fueling.

### Post-AM Practice (Within 30 minutes)

**Target:** 1.2-1.5 g carbs/kg (HIGHER than single session)
- 70kg swimmer: 84-105g carbs
- Protein: 0.4-0.5 g/kg (28-35g)

**Why more?** Compressed timeline. Must jumpstart glycogen synthesis immediately.

**Best options:**
- Large chocolate milk (750ml) + bagel + banana
  - Carbs: 120g, Protein: 30g ✅
- Smoothie: 2 bananas + 2 scoops protein + milk + honey + berries
  - Carbs: 100g, Protein: 50g ✅

### Mid-Morning Snack (2 hours post-AM, 2-3 hours pre-PM)

**Target:**
- Carbs: 1.0 g/kg
- Protein: 0.3 g/kg

**Example:**
- Greek yogurt (large, 300g): 30g carbs, 25g protein
- Granola (1/2 cup): 30g carbs
- Berries: 15g carbs
- **Total:** 75g carbs, 25g protein

### Pre-PM Practice (1 hour before)

**Target:** Light, easily digestible carbs
- Banana + sports drink
- Rice cakes + jam
- Applesauce pouch

**Avoid:** Heavy food, protein, fat (causes GI distress during warm-up)

### Post-PM Practice

**Repeat full recovery protocol:**
- Immediate: 1.0-1.2 g carbs/kg + 0.3-0.5 g protein/kg
- Follow-up meal (dinner): Full balanced meal

**Total daily intake on two-a-day:**
- Carbs: 8-10 g/kg (560-700g for 70kg swimmer)
- Protein: 1.6-2.0 g/kg (112-140g)
- **Calories:** 3,500-4,000+ depending on volume/intensity

**Critical mistake:** Skipping post-AM recovery or eating poorly between sessions → PM practice quality tanks, adaptation suffers.

## Between Prelims and Finals

**Scenario:** Saturday morning prelims (8 AM). Finals at 6 PM. 10 hours between.

**Immediate post-prelims (within 30 min):**
- Simple, fast-absorbing carbs
- Minimal fat, minimal fiber
- Light protein

**Why simple carbs?** Fast gastric emptying. You might have another race in 8-10 hours. Don't want heavy food sitting in stomach.

**Examples:**
- Sports drink (500ml) + white bread (2 slices) with jam
- Rice cakes (4) + honey + banana
- Pretzels + sports drink + small turkey sandwich (white bread)

**Target:** 1.0 g carbs/kg, 0.2-0.3 g protein/kg

### 4-6 Hours Pre-Finals (Midday Meal)

**Now you can eat more substantial:**
- Rice or pasta (familiar, well-tolerated carbs)
- Lean protein (chicken, turkey, fish)
- Small amount fat (olive oil, avocado)
- Avoid: Heavy sauces, fried food, excessive fiber

**Example:**
- Grilled chicken (100g)
- White rice (1.5 cups)
- Steamed veggies (small portion)
- White bread roll
- Sports drink

### 2-3 Hours Pre-Finals

**Light carb snack:**
- Banana
- Rice cakes + jam
- Sports drink

### 1 Hour Pre-Finals

**Just liquid:**
- Sports drink
- Water
- Maybe small applesauce pouch if nervous stomach

**Goal:** Start finals warm-up with topped-off glycogen, empty stomach (no GI distress), hydrated.

## Multi-Day Meet Nutrition

### Friday Night (Night Before Day 1)

**Dinner:**
- High-carb, moderate protein, low fat
- Familiar foods (no experiments)

**Examples:**
- Pasta + marinara + grilled chicken + bread
- Rice bowl + teriyaki chicken + steamed veggies
- Baked potato + lean steak + salad (light dressing)

**Avoid:**
- Heavy, greasy food (pizza, burgers, fries)
- Unfamiliar cuisine
- Excess fiber (beans, raw veggies, whole grains)
- Excess fat (slows digestion)

**Why low fat?** Fat delays gastric emptying. Want to wake up Saturday with digested food, not bloated.

### Saturday Morning (Day 1)

**3-4 hours before warm-up:**
- Carb-based breakfast
- Moderate protein
- Low fat, low fiber

**Examples:**
- Oatmeal + banana + honey + toast
- Pancakes + syrup + scrambled eggs (2)
- Bagel + jam + Greek yogurt + apple

**1-2 hours before:**
- Light carb snack if needed: banana, sports drink

### Saturday Between Races

**Post-each-swim:**
- Sip sports drink (don't chug)
- Small snack if >1 hour to next race: banana, rice cake

**Between sessions (prelims → finals):**
- Follow prelims/finals protocol above

### Saturday Night (Between Day 1 and Day 2)

**Dinner:**
- Repeat Friday night approach
- High-carb, moderate protein, low fat
- **Add:** Extra hydration (you've been racing all day)

**Restock glycogen for Day 2:**
- Rice, pasta, potatoes
- Lean protein
- Bread
- Fruit

### Sunday (Day 2)

**Repeat Saturday morning protocol.**

**Key:** By Day 2, glycogen stores can be depleted if you raced multiple events Saturday. Aggressive carb intake Saturday night is CRITICAL.

## Hydration and Recovery

**Recovery isn't just food — it's FLUID too.**

### Post-Training Hydration Target

**Rule:** Drink 150% of sweat losses.

**How to measure:**
1. Weigh yourself naked before practice
2. Weigh yourself naked after practice (towel dry first)
3. Weight loss (in kg or lbs) = sweat loss
4. Multiply by 1.5 to get rehydration target

**Example:**
- Pre-practice: 70.0 kg
- Post-practice: 68.5 kg
- Loss: 1.5 kg (1.5 liters sweat)
- **Drink:** 2.25 liters over next 4 hours

**Why 150%?** You continue losing fluid via urine after training. Overcompensate.

### Best Recovery Fluids

**First 500-750ml:**
- **Sports drink** (replaces sodium + carbs)
- Or chocolate milk (carbs + protein + sodium)

**Remaining:**
- Water
- Coconut water
- Diluted juice

**Avoid (immediately post-training):**
- Soda (empty calories, no sodium)
- Energy drinks (caffeine can delay rehydration)
- Alcohol (diuretic, impairs glycogen synthesis)

### Urine Color Check

**Goal:** Light yellow ("straw-colored") within 4-6 hours post-training.

**Dark yellow/amber?** Still dehydrated. Drink more.

**Clear?** Overhydrated (usually fine, but excessive water without electrolytes → hyponatremia risk).

## Sleep and Recovery

**Nutrition can't compensate for poor sleep.**

**Growth hormone release (muscle repair, adaptation):** Peaks during deep sleep.

**Glycogen synthesis:** Continues overnight (slower than daytime, but still happening).

**Immune function:** Sleep-deprived athletes get sick more often.

### Sleep Targets for Swimmers

**Age group (14-18 years):** 8-10 hours/night  
**College/Elite (18-25 years):** 7-9 hours/night  
**Masters (25+ years):** 7-8 hours/night

**During heavy training blocks:** Add 30-60 min/night

**During taper:** Some swimmers need MORE sleep (body finally recovering from accumulated fatigue).

### Pre-Sleep Nutrition

**Goal:** Sustain muscle protein synthesis overnight.

**Ideal pre-bed snack (30-60 min before sleep):**
- Slow-digesting protein (casein or Greek yogurt)
- Small amount carbs

**Examples:**
- Greek yogurt (200g) + berries
- Cottage cheese + fruit
- Casein protein shake
- Glass of milk + small bowl of cereal

**Why slow protein?** Casein digests over 6-8 hours, providing amino acids overnight (reduces muscle breakdown during sleep).

**Avoid:**
- Heavy, fatty meals (impairs sleep quality)
- Excess liquid (wakes you up to pee)
- Caffeine (obviously)

## Supplements for Recovery

**Disclaimer:** Whole foods first. Supplements are SUPPLEMENTARY.

### Evidence-Based Recovery Supplements

#### 1. Whey Protein

**Benefit:** Fast-digesting, complete amino acid profile, convenient.

**When:** Immediately post-training (if solid food not tolerable).

**Dose:** 20-30g per serving

**Best practice:** Mix with carb source (banana, milk, juice) for better glycogen restoration.

#### 2. Creatine Monohydrate

**Benefit:** Improves sprint performance, aids muscle recovery, supports repeated high-intensity efforts.

**Dose:** 3-5g/day (maintenance)

**When:** Post-training OR anytime (timing less critical than daily consistency)

**Note:** Requires ~1 week "loading" for new users (20g/day split into 4 doses for 5 days, then 3-5g/day maintenance).

#### 3. Tart Cherry Juice

**Benefit:** Reduces muscle soreness, anti-inflammatory, improves sleep quality (natural melatonin).

**Dose:** 8-12 oz twice/day (morning + evening)

**When:** During heavy training blocks or post-hard sessions.

**Evidence:** Moderate. Some studies show reduced soreness, faster recovery.

#### 4. Omega-3 Fatty Acids (Fish Oil)

**Benefit:** Anti-inflammatory, supports joint health, may reduce muscle soreness.

**Dose:** 2-3g EPA+DHA per day

**When:** Daily (not timing-specific)

**Best source:** Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines) 2-3x/week OR supplement.

#### 5. Electrolyte Supplements

**Benefit:** Replaces sodium, potassium, magnesium lost via sweat.

**When:** Post-training (especially in hot pools or during two-a-days).

**Best practice:** Sports drink or electrolyte tablet dissolved in water.

---

### What NOT to Waste Money On

❌ **BCAAs (branched-chain amino acids):**  
- Whey protein already contains BCAAs. Redundant if eating adequate protein.

❌ **Glutamine:**  
- Theoretical benefit, but studies show no performance improvement in well-fed athletes.

❌ **Most "recovery formulas":**  
- Overpriced. Chocolate milk is cheaper and equally effective.

❌ **Excessive antioxidant supplements (Vitamin C, E mega-doses):**  
- May BLUNT training adaptations by reducing oxidative stress signals needed for mitochondrial biogenesis.
- Get antioxidants from food (berries, veggies), not pills.

## Practical Recovery Checklist for Coaches

**Teach swimmers to do this after EVERY hard session:**

✅ **Within 30 minutes:**
- Drink recovery shake, chocolate milk, or eat carb+protein snack
- Target: 1.0-1.2 g carbs/kg + 0.3-0.5 g protein/kg

✅ **Within 2 hours:**
- Full balanced meal (carbs + protein + fat + veggies)

✅ **Hydration:**
- Drink 150% of sweat losses over 4 hours
- Check urine color (goal: light yellow)

✅ **Evening:**
- Pre-bed snack (slow protein + small carbs)
- Target 8-10 hours sleep

✅ **Next day:**
- Morning urine light yellow? ✅ Recovered
- Dark yellow? Dehydrated, drink more today

**If swimmers skip recovery nutrition consistently:**
- Performance plateaus
- Get sick more often
- Increased injury risk
- Chronic fatigue

**Recovery is NOT optional. It's where the magic happens.**
