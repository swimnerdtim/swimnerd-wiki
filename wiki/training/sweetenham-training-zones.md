# Bill Sweetenham's Five Training Zones
**Source:** *Championship Swim Training* by Bill Sweetenham & John Atkinson (2003)

## Philosophy

> "What you expect and what you get are often very different."

Sweetenham's training zone system provides a framework for individualizing training intensity. The critical insight: **monitor everything**, because swimmers often train at the wrong intensity without realizing it.

**The danger:** If swimmers do Zone 1 work at excessive intensity, it affects their ability to perform in Zones 2-3. Training zones must be enforced, not suggested.

---

## The Five Training Zones

### Overview Table

| Zone | Name | Intensity | Primary Purpose |
|------|------|-----------|----------------|
| **Zone 1** | Aerobic (A1, A2, A3) | Lowest | Base building, recovery, volume |
| **Zone 2** | Anaerobic Threshold | Moderate-high | Lactate clearance, tempo |
| **Zone 3** | High-Performance Endurance | High sustained | Critical speed, MVO₂ |
| **Zone 4** | Anaerobic (Race Pace) | Very high | Lactate tolerance, race specificity |
| **Zone 5** | Sprint | Maximum | Speed development, power |

## Training Volume Distribution

### Percentage of Total Training by Swimmer Type

| Swimmer Type | Zone 1 | Zones 2+3 | Zones 4+5 |
|--------------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Age-group & youth** | 70% | 20% | 10% |
| **Male sprinters** | 80% | 10% | 10% |
| **Reverse periodization sprinters** | 50% | 20% | 30% |
| **Female endurance** | 60% | 30% | 10% |
| **Senior swimmers** | 70% | 20% | 10% |
| **Masters** | 85% | 10% | 5% |
| **Triathletes** | 65% | 25% | 10% |

**Key insight:** Most swimmers do the MOST work in Zone 1, moderate work in Zones 2-3, and the LEAST work in Zones 4-5.

## Zone 1: Aerobic Training

### Three Types of Aerobic Work

**A1 - Recovery Work (Low Intensity)**
- Heart rate: 50-70 beats below max
- Pace: PB 200m ÷ 2 + 20 seconds
- Repeat distances: 200m-1500m
- Rest intervals: 5-20 seconds
- **Purpose:** Complement anaerobic/sprint work
- **Note:** NOT recommended for most females (unless heavily built sprinters)

**A2 - Aerobic Maintenance**
- Heart rate: 40-50 beats below max
- Pace: PB 200m ÷ 2 + 15-20 seconds
- Repeat distances: 200m-1500m
- Rest intervals: 10-20 seconds
- **Purpose:** Maintain aerobic capacity
- **More important than A1 for high-recovering athletes (females)**

**A3 - Aerobic Development**
- Heart rate: 30-40 beats below max
- Pace: PB 200m ÷ 2 + 10-15 seconds
- Repeat distances: 50m-400m
- Rest intervals: 10-20 seconds
- **Purpose:** Increase aerobic capacity
- **May overlap with anaerobic threshold for some swimmers**

### Example Calculation (Zone 1 Paces)

**Swimmer with 2:00.00 PB for 200m free:**
- Half PB = 1:00.00 per 100m
- **A1 pace:** 1:00 + 20 sec = **1:20.00** per 100m
- **A2 pace:** 1:00 + 15-20 sec = **1:15.00-1:20.00** per 100m
- **A3 pace:** 1:00 + 10-15 sec = **1:10.00-1:15.00** per 100m

## Zone 2: Anaerobic Threshold

**Definition:** The point where lactate accumulation begins to rise sharply

### Zone 2 Specifications

- **Heart rate:** 20-30 beats below max
- **Pace:** PB 200m ÷ 2 + 7-10 seconds
- **Repeat distances:** 50m-400m
- **Rest intervals:**
  - 50m: 10 seconds
  - 100-200m: 10-20 seconds
  - 200-400m: 10-20 seconds

### Recommended Set Lengths

- **Sprinters:** 2000-3000m
- **Middle distance:** 3000-4500m
- **Distance swimmers:** 5000m+

### The 3000m Test

**Purpose:** Gauge anaerobic threshold training paces

**How to use:**
1. Swim timed 3000m
2. Calculate average pace per 100m
3. Use this pace for Zone 2 training sets

**For age-group/sprint swimmers:** Use 2000m test instead

---

## Zone 3: High-Performance Endurance

**Critical principle:** Don't go too hard too early. If you reach max HR early in the set, you're working at the wrong intensity.

### Three Types of Zone 3 Work

#### 1. Critical Speed (CS)

**Specifications:**
- Heart rate: 10-20 beats below max
- Duration: 30 minutes of swimming effort
- Repeat distances: 50m-200m
- Work:rest ratio: 1.5:1

**Sample set:**
```
24 × 100m free on 1:45 holding 1:05
= 26 minutes work + 5:20 rest = ~30 min work time
```

**How to establish CS pace:**
1. Perform 7 × 200m step test (see test sets)
2. Extrapolate HR vs. velocity curve to max HR
3. That point = your critical speed

**Key insight:** 
- 400m race pace = CS + 3-4% faster
- 200m race pace = CS + 8% faster

**The final 100m test:**
- Last 100m of set should be FASTER than all other 100s
- If you can't do this → you worked too hard throughout set

**Coaching adjustments:**
- If swimmer hits max HR early → reduce pace
- If technique breaks down → reduce pace or increase rest
- May need to change from 100s to 75s or 75s to 50s

#### 2. Lactate Removal

**Purpose:** Train the body to remove lactate while continuing to work

**When to use:**
- After building high lactate through CS set
- Post-competition in swim-down pool

**Method:**
1. Complete race at competition
2. Go directly to swim-down pool
3. Perform lactate removal set at 10-20 BPM below max (M) or 10-15 BPM below max (F)

**Recommended distances:**
- 50m/100m swimmers: 800m set
- 200m/400m swimmers: 1000m set
- 800m/1500m swimmers: 1200m set

**Alternative:** Start training set with max effort 100m, then immediately go into CS set

#### 3. MVO₂ (Maximum Oxygen Uptake)

**Specifications:**
- Heart rate: 10 beats below max → max
- Swim distances: 300m-500m
- Work:rest ratio: 1:1

**Key insight:** Most swimmers don't reach MVO₂ until the last 100m of a 300-500m swim

**Alternative method:**
```
3 × 100m near max speed on very short rest (10 sec)
+ 200m swim-down
Repeat 3-5 times
```

**Pace:** PB 200m ÷ 2 + 4-7 seconds

## Zone 4: Anaerobic (Race Pace)

**Definition:** Lactate training conducted as race-pace specific work

**Critical principle:** Any set at 400m pace or faster = anaerobic/quality work

### Three Types of Lactate Training

#### 1. Lactate Production (Early Season)

**Method:** Introduce race-pace work with splits and broken swims

**Sample set:**
```
10 × 50m on 2:30-3:00
Holding 2nd 50m of 100m goal pace

Goal time: 1:00.00 (29.0/31.0)
Target: 31.0 or faster each repeat
```

#### 2. Lactate Tolerance (Mid-Season)

**More intense than production → fewer repeats needed**

**Sample sets:**
```
3 sets of 3 × 100m at 2nd 100m split of 200m goal
on 3:00 with 200m swim-down between sets

OR

8 × 100m on 4:00-6:00 holding 2nd 100m split
6 × 100m on 5:00 holding 2nd 100m split
```

**Best distances:** 50m-200m

**Recommended lengths:**
- 100m swimmer: 600-1200m
- 200m swimmer: 600-1600m
- 400m swimmer: 600-2000m

#### 3. Peak Lactate (Late Season)

**Method:** Produce peak lactate on EACH swim (not cumulative)

**Sample set:**
```
4 × 100m on 15:00 with active recovery between
```

**Can use broken swims:**
```
3 × 200m as 4 × 50m on 60 seconds
(dive start 1st 50, push start remaining)
```

**Best distances:** 100m-400m (straight or broken)

## Zone 5: Sprint

**Definition:** Maximum velocity training (NOT maximum effort - that's Zone 4)

**Key distinction:** 
- Zone 4 = maximum EFFORT (but not max speed due to fatigue)
- Zone 5 = maximum SPEED (with full recovery)

### Zone 5 Specifications

- **Repeat distances:** 10m-25m
- **Recovery:** Full (not "sufficient" - FULL)
- **Purpose:** High-velocity overloads (HVO)

**If rest is inadequate on 25m repeats → you're doing Zone 4 lactate production**

### Sample Set

```
12 × 25m as:
- 15m maximum speed sprint
- 10m distance per stroke (DPS)
On 1:30 rest
```

### Calculating Speed Training Pace

**For 100m swimmers:**
- 100m PB - 5 seconds = speed base
- Example: 60.00 - 5 = 55.00

**Then calculate by distance:**
- 25m target: 55 ÷ 4 = **13.75 sec**
- 20m target: 55 ÷ 5 = **11.00 sec**
- 15m target: 55 ÷ 100 × 15 = **8.25 sec**
- 12.5m target: 55 ÷ 100 × 12.5 = **6.87 sec**
- 10m target: 55 ÷ 100 × 10 = **5.50 sec**

**For 200m swimmers:**
- 200m PB - 10 seconds = speed base
- Example: 2:10.00 (130 sec) - 10 = 120.00

**Then:**
- 25m target: 120 ÷ 8 = **15.00 sec**
- 20m target: 120 ÷ 10 = **12.00 sec**

## Individualizing Training Zones

### Method 1: Heart Rate Below Maximum

**Two swimmers, both doing Zone 1:**
- Swimmer A: Max HR 201 → works at 161 BPM (40 below max)
- Swimmer B: Max HR 181 → works at 141 BPM (40 below max)

**Both swimming at their own level** ✅

**Wrong way:**
- Both told to train at 160 BPM
- Swimmer A: 41 below max = Zone 1 ✅
- Swimmer B: 21 below max = Zone 2 ❌ (overtraining risk)

**The rule:** Set heart rate targets as BEATS BELOW MAX, not absolute BPM

### Method 2: Personal Best + Constant

**Formula:** (PB 200m ÷ 2) + constant = training pace

**Example: 2:16.00 PB for 200m back**
- Half PB = 1:08.00 per 100m

**Training paces:**
- A1: 1:08 + 20 sec = **1:28.00**
- A2: 1:08 + 15-20 sec = **1:23.00-1:28.00**
- A3: 1:08 + 10-15 sec = **1:18.00-1:23.00**
- Zone 2: 1:08 + 7-10 sec = **1:15.00-1:18.00**
- Zone 3: 1:08 + 4-7 sec = **1:12.00-1:15.00**

## Aerobic Base & Endurance Swimming

### The Gradual Buildup Approach

**Key principle:** Increase ONE variable at a time

**8-Week Progression Example:**

**Week 1:** 6 sessions × 4000m = 24km
- 38 sec/50m pace (pull/swim)
- 50 sec/50m pace (kick)
- 50-sec cycle (swim/pull), 60-sec cycle (kick)

**Week 2:** 6 sessions × 4000m = 24km
- **37 sec/50m pace** (pull/swim) ← intensity increase
- **48 sec/50m pace** (kick) ← intensity increase
- Same cycles

**Week 3:** 7 sessions × 4000m = 28km ← volume increase
- Same paces as Week 2

**Week 4:** 7 sessions × 4000m = 28km
- **36 sec/50m pace** (pull/swim) ← intensity increase
- **46 sec/50m pace** (kick) ← intensity increase

**Week 5:** 8 sessions × 4000m = 32km ← volume increase
- Same paces as Week 4

**Week 6:** 8 sessions × 4000m = 32km
- Same paces, **cycles decrease** ← intensity increase
- 45-sec cycle (swim/pull), 55-sec cycle (kick)

**Week 7:** 9 sessions × 4000m = 36km ← volume increase

**Week 8:** 9 sessions × 4500m = 40.5km ← volume increase

**The rule:** Volume one week, intensity the next. Never both at once.

## Monitoring & Adjustment

### Signs You're Training at Wrong Intensity

**Zone 1 too hard:**
- Can't complete next day's Zone 2/3 work
- Chronic fatigue
- HR creeping up on "easy" sets

**Zone 3 too hard:**
- Reaching max HR early in set
- Technique breakdown
- Can't swim final 100m faster than earlier 100s

**Zone 4 insufficient:**
- Not hitting race splits in training
- Poor race performances despite high training volume

### The 3×200m Snapshot Test

**Purpose:** Check current fitness against previous 7×200m step test

**Method:**
1. In warm-up, swim 3×200m at 3rd, 4th, 5th pace from 7×200m test
2. Plot on laminated HR vs. velocity curve
3. Compare to previous test

**Interpretation:**
- **On the curve or shifted right** → fitness good, proceed with planned set
- **Shifted left** → fatigue present, adjust training paces down

## Practical Application

### Sample Training Week (Senior Swimmer)

| Day | AM Session | PM Session |
|-----|-----------|------------|
| Mon | Zone 1 (A2) - 5000m | Zone 3 (CS) - 3000m |
| Tue | Zone 1 (A3) - 4000m | Zone 4 (Lactate tolerance) - 1600m |
| Wed | Zone 2 (Threshold) - 4500m | Zone 1 (A2) - 4000m |
| Thu | Zone 1 (A1) - 5000m | Zone 5 (Sprint) - 600m + Zone 3 (MVO₂) - 1500m |
| Fri | Zone 2 (Threshold) - 4000m | Zone 4 (Peak lactate) - 1200m |
| Sat | Zone 3 (Lactate removal) - 3000m | OFF |
| Sun | OFF or Zone 1 (A1) - 6000m | OFF |

**Total:** ~70% Zone 1, ~20% Zones 2-3, ~10% Zones 4-5

## The Bottom Line

> "Monitor training in all training zones, because what you expect and what you get are often very different."

The zone system only works if:
1. ✅ Paces are individualized
2. ✅ Heart rates are monitored
3. ✅ Coaches enforce the zones
4. ✅ Volume distribution is respected
5. ✅ ONE variable changes at a time

**Without discipline:** Swimmers do Zone 1 at Zone 2 intensity, compromise recovery, and burn out.

**With discipline:** Each zone serves its purpose, adaptations occur, performance improves.
