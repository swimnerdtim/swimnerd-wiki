# Swimming Golf & Stroke Length Games: The Ultimate Training Tool

**Source:** *Triathlon Swimming Made Easy* by Terry Laughlin (Total Immersion)  
**Context:** Structured way to find your most economical stroke length and tempo  
**Audience:** All coaches & swimmers — measurable, fun, immediately actionable

---

## What is Swimming Golf?

**The game:** Count your strokes for a fixed distance (50s work best) and add your time in seconds. The total is your "golf score."

**Example:**
- 32 strokes + 50 seconds = **Golf score of 82**

**Goal:** Lower your score by finding smarter combinations of stroke length (SL) and stroke rate (SR), not by muscling it.

**Why it works:**
- **Immediate feedback** — you know instantly if a change helped
- **Forces efficiency** — can't cheat by just going harder
- **Fun** — competitive, gamified, addictive
- **Self-coaching** — swimmers learn to feel what works

---

## The Two Basic Versions

### Version 1: Same Time, Fewer Strokes

**How to play:**
- Swim 50 in a chosen time (e.g., 50 seconds)
- Count strokes (e.g., 32 strokes)
- Golf score = 82
- **Next rep:** Same time (50 sec), but subtract 1 stroke (31 strokes)
- New score = 81

**Example progression:**
1. 32 strokes + :50 = **82**
2. 31 strokes + :50 = **81**
3. 30 strokes + :50 = **80**
4. 29 strokes + :50 = **79** ← Can't go lower without slowing down

**The puzzle:**
- Can't just glide longer off the wall (slows you down)
- Must find MORE from each stroke
- Teaches maximum efficiency at a given speed

**What swimmers learn:**
- How to generate more distance per stroke
- Where wasted motion lives
- Patient, powerful pulls vs. rushed, weak ones

---

### Version 2: Same Strokes, Faster Time

**How to play:**
- Choose a stroke count (e.g., 30 strokes per 50)
- Swim at that count, note your time (e.g., :45)
- Golf score = 75
- **Next rep:** Same count (30 strokes), faster time (:44)
- New score = 74

**Example progression:**
1. 30 strokes + :45 = **75**
2. 30 strokes + :44 = **74**
3. 30 strokes + :43 = **73**
4. 30 strokes + :42 = **72** ← Can't go faster without adding strokes

**The puzzle:**
- Maintain exact same stroke length
- Increase stroke rate without getting choppy
- Teaches **tempo control** while preserving efficiency

**What swimmers learn:**
- How to "turn over" faster without shortening stroke
- The difference between fast arms and fast swimming
- How much speed comes from tempo alone

**Cross-reference:** See [Counsilman's Interval Training](../training/interval-training-practical-guide.md) for similar tempo work.

## Sample Swimming Golf Sets

### Beginner Set (Find Your Baseline)

**Warm-up:** 200-400 easy

**Main set:** 4 × 50 Swimming Golf
- Rest: 30-60 seconds between (or until ready)
- Goal: Find your natural stroke count and time
- Record all four scores
- **Best score = your current "par"**

**Example results:**
- 50 #1: 34 strokes + :52 = **86**
- 50 #2: 33 strokes + :51 = **84**
- 50 #3: 32 strokes + :50 = **82** ← Best score
- 50 #4: 33 strokes + :51 = **84**

**Now you know:** Current par = 82, optimal count = 32 strokes, sustainable speed = :50

---

### Intermediate Set (Lower Your Score)

**Warm-up:** 400 drill/swim

**Main set:** 3 rounds of 3 × 50 Golf
- **Round 1:** Swim all three at your "par" score
- **Round 2:** Try Version 1 (same time, fewer strokes)
- **Round 3:** Try Version 2 (same strokes, faster time)
- Rest: 20-30 sec between reps, 1-2 min between rounds

**Learning goal:** Which version feels easier? Which produces better score?

**Common discovery:** Most swimmers find Version 1 easier and more sustainable for long-distance swimming.

---

### Advanced Set (Tiger Woods in the Water)

**Warm-up:** 600 mixed drill/swim/golf

**Main set:** "How many ways can you score 80?"

If your best score is 77, target a score of 80 and see how many different stroke count/time combinations achieve it:

1. 30 strokes + :50 = **80**
2. 31 strokes + :49 = **80**
3. 32 strokes + :48 = **80**
4. 33 strokes + :47 = **80**
5. 34 strokes + :46 = **80**

**Rest:** As needed (this is HARD)

**Debrief:** Which combination felt easiest? That's your "sweet spot" for this speed.

**What this teaches:**
- Massive range of SL/SR options for any speed
- Some combinations are WAY more efficient than others
- Your body's "favorite gear" for a given pace

**Cross-reference:** [Gears: Easiest Way to Swim Faster](../methodology/sweetenham-training-zones.md) for similar concept.

## Adding Complexity: Factor in Effort

**Next level:** Measure Rate of Perceived Exertion (RPE) alongside golf score.

**Scale:**
- **RPE 1-2** = Super easy, could do 20+ reps
- **RPE 3-4** = Sustainable, could do 10-15 reps
- **RPE 5-6** = Challenging, could do 5-8 reps
- **RPE 7-8** = Hard, could do 2-3 reps
- **RPE 9-10** = All-out, 1 rep only

**Example:**
- 32 strokes + :37 = **69** @ RPE 5 ← Not sustainable
- 32 strokes + :37 = **69** @ RPE 3 ← Race-ready!

**Goal:** Lower your RPE at a given golf score, not just the score itself.

**Why this matters:** 
- A score of 69 @ RPE 5 = you can't hold it in a race
- A score of 69 @ RPE 3 = you can swim that way for 1500m+

**Cross-reference:** [Mental Preparation](../training/mental-preparation-schubert.md) for RPE in competition context.

## Golf Variations for Different Training Goals

### 1. FistGloves® Golf (Technique Focus)

**Setup:**
- Baseline: 3 × 50 Golf (record scores)
- Main: 6-9 × 50 Golf with FistGloves®
- Test: 3 × 50 Golf without gloves

**Question:** Does ungloved score improve after "educating" hands with gloves?

**What it tests:** Hand/forearm feel, early vertical forearm, catch quality.

---

### 2. Different Strokes Golf (Long-Axis Combo)

**Setup:** 3-4 rounds of:
- 3 × 50 backstroke Golf
- 3 × 50 (25 back + 25 free) Golf
- 3 × 50 freestyle Golf

**Compare:** Did backstroke warm-up improve freestyle score?

**Why this works:** Backstroke reinforces rotation, balance, patient catch — all transfer to freestyle.

**Cross-reference:** [Coupling Motions](../technique/coupling-motions.md) for stroke timing connections.

---

### 3. Silent Golf (Drag Reduction)

**Setup:** 4-6 × 50 Golf
- Goal: Swim as quietly as possible
- Bonus: Subtract 2 points from score if you can't hear yourself

**What it teaches:** Less splash = less drag = more speed for same effort.

**Cross-reference:** [Slippery Swimming](total-immersion-for-triathletes.md#slippery-swimming-the-smarter-way-to-speed).

---

### 4. Super Slow Golf (Economy Focus)

**Setup:** 4-6 × 50 Golf
- Swim at 60-70% effort (SLOW)
- Aim for lowest stroke count possible
- Accept slower time

**Example:**
- 26 strokes + :58 = **84** ← Lower count than usual

**What it teaches:** Your "economy gear" — the stroke length you can sustain forever.

**Best for:** Distance swimmers, triathletes, recovery days.

---

### 5. Descending Golf (Race Simulation)

**Setup:** 5 × 50 Golf, descending (get faster each rep)
- Rep 1: Easy (RPE 2)
- Rep 2: Steady (RPE 3)
- Rep 3: Moderate (RPE 4)
- Rep 4: Brisk (RPE 5)
- Rep 5: Fast (RPE 6-7)

**Track:** How much does stroke count increase as you speed up?

**Goal:** Minimize stroke count increase while maximizing speed increase.

**Example (good swimmer):**
- Rep 1: 30 strokes + :50 = **80** @ RPE 2
- Rep 5: 32 strokes + :42 = **74** @ RPE 7

**Only added 2 strokes but dropped 8 seconds** — excellent gear control!

**Cross-reference:** [Gears](total-immersion-for-triathletes.md#fourth-gear-lance-armstrong-in-the-water) for race-pace shifting.

## Using Golf Scores to Build Training Sets

**Once you know your golf scores, you can transform ANY generic set:**

### Example: Coach prescribes "5 × 100"

**Option 1:** Swim all 5 at your best golf score (×2 for 100s)
- Target: 64 strokes + 1:40 time = 224 "par"

**Option 2:** Version 1 approach — same time, descending strokes
- 100 #1: 66 strokes + 1:40 = 226
- 100 #2: 65 strokes + 1:40 = 225
- 100 #3: 64 strokes + 1:40 = 224
- 100 #4: 63 strokes + 1:40 = 223
- 100 #5: 62 strokes + 1:40 = 222

**Option 3:** Version 2 approach — same strokes, descending time
- 100 #1: 64 strokes + 1:45 = 229
- 100 #2: 64 strokes + 1:43 = 227
- 100 #3: 64 strokes + 1:41 = 225
- 100 #4: 64 strokes + 1:39 = 223
- 100 #5: 64 strokes + 1:37 = 221

**Result:** Transformed boring "5 × 100" into rich learning experience with immediate feedback.

## Swimming Golf for Distance Events

**Challenge:** Golf scores measure 50s. How do you apply to 200+?

**Answer:** Golf average per 50.

**Example for 200:**
- Total strokes: 128
- Total time: 3:20 (200 seconds)
- Golf score: **328**
- **Per-50 average: 82** (328 ÷ 4)

**Training goal:** Maintain your 50-yard golf "par" over longer distances.

**Progressive challenge:**
1. Can you hold par for 4 × 50?
2. Can you hold par for 2 × 100?
3. Can you hold par for 1 × 200?
4. Can you hold par for 4 × 100?
5. Can you hold par for 1 × 400?

**When stroke count climbs, you know:**
- Fatigue is setting in
- Technique is breaking down
- Time to rest or slow down

**Cross-reference:** [Distance Training](total-immersion-for-triathletes.md#distance-training-for-triathletes) for building long-swim capacity.

## Troubleshooting Swimming Golf

### "My score won't improve!"

**Possible causes:**
1. **Not enough rest** — take 30-60 sec between reps initially
2. **Trying too hard** — aim for RPE 4-5, not all-out
3. **Adding strokes to go faster** — defeats the purpose; practice Version 1 more
4. **Losing streamline** — check balance, head position, kick

**Fix:** Go back to basics. Do 200-400 of your best drill, then try again.

---

### "I get a great score, but I'm exhausted."

**Problem:** Low golf score at high RPE (e.g., score 70 @ RPE 7).

**Solution:** Practice achieving same score at lower effort:
- Swim Version 1 (fewer strokes, same time) at RPE 3-4
- Focus on eliminating wasted motion
- Use Silent Swimming as focal point

**Goal:** Get score of 70 @ RPE 3 before trying to lower score further.

---

### "My stroke count jumps all over."

**Problem:** Inconsistent technique.

**Solution:** 
- Establish a reliable "N" (normal count) first
- Practice 4-6 × 25 hitting exact same count every time
- Then graduate to 50s
- Count strokes EVERY LENGTH until it becomes automatic

**Cross-reference:** [Interval Training Basics](../training/interval-training-basics.md) for consistency work.

## For Coaches: How to Introduce Golf

### Week 1: Establish Par

**Monday:** Explain concept, do 4 × 50 Golf, find best score
**Wednesday:** Repeat 4 × 50 Golf, aim to beat Monday's score
**Friday:** 6 × 50 Golf, track all scores

**Homework:** Record scores in log book, identify patterns.

---

### Week 2: Learn the Versions

**Monday:** 3 rounds of 3 × 50 (Version 1 — same time, fewer strokes)
**Wednesday:** 3 rounds of 3 × 50 (Version 2 — same strokes, faster)
**Friday:** Mixed round (both versions)

---

### Week 3: Add Complexity

**Monday:** Golf with RPE tracking
**Wednesday:** FistGloves® Golf
**Friday:** Silent Golf or Super Slow Golf

---

### Week 4: Race Simulation

**Monday:** Descending Golf (5 × 50, get faster)
**Wednesday:** Distance Golf (4 × 100, maintain per-50 average)
**Friday:** Personal best attempt (best 3 out of 5)

**Result after 4 weeks:** Swimmers have rich understanding of their stroke, optimal counts, and efficiency patterns.

## Why Swimming Golf Works

**Psychological:**
- Immediate feedback = motivation
- Gamification = fun
- Clear goal = focus

**Technical:**
- Forces awareness of stroke length
- Reveals efficiency (or lack thereof)
- Can't fake it by just going harder

**Training:**
- Works for all speeds
- Works for all distances
- Adapts to any set design

**Quote from Terry Laughlin:**
> "Your improving golf score will provide an unerring measure of how well you're using SL to create speed."

---

## Key Takeaways for Coaches

1. **Golf scores = stroke efficiency metric** — better than pace alone
2. **Version 1 (fewer strokes) = best for distance swimmers** — builds sustainable economy
3. **Version 2 (faster tempo) = best for sprinters** — teaches speed without sacrificing length
4. **Add RPE for complete picture** — score + effort = true efficiency
5. **Use golf to transform generic sets** — turns "10 × 50" into rich learning experience

**Success indicator:** Swimmer improves golf score by 10-15% in 4-8 weeks, with LOWER RPE.
