# Masters Workouts: Progressive Speed & Descending Sets

*Source: Bayside/Great Neck Masters (2023-2024), 85 workouts parsed*

## Workout #3: Pyramid Negative Split (2300m SCM)

**Date**: 10/04/23 | **Location**: Bayside | **Focus**: Pacing control, negative splits

**Structure**:
- **Warmup**: 300 choice / 6x50 IM @ 1:00 (600m)
- **Main Set** (1600m):
  - 100 last 25 FAST @ 1:45 (rest 2:00)
  - 200 last 50 FAST @ 3:30 (rest 4:00)
  - 300 last 75 FAST @ 5:15 (rest 6:00)
  - 400 last 100 FAST @ 7:00 (rest 8:00)
  - **Then descend back down**:
  - 300 last 75 FAST @ 5:15 (rest 6:00)
  - 200 last 50 FAST @ 3:30 (rest 4:00)
  - 100 last 25 FAST @ 1:45 (rest 2:00)
- **Cool**: 100 EZ

**Key Features**:
- Teaches pacing control through fatigue
- Progressively longer distances with negative split focus
- Symmetrical pyramid structure
- Each repeat: coast first portion, crush final portion
- Intervals provide full recovery (almost 2:1 work:rest)

**Use When**:
- Teaching race pacing
- Building aerobic base with speed elements
- Developing negative split discipline
- Mid-season when fitness is solid

**Coaching Notes**:
- Watch for athletes crushing early portions (defeats purpose)
- "Last X meters FAST" means controlled swim, explosive finish
- Rest ratios allow full recovery = quality over volume
- Can modify distances/rest for different abilities

## Workout #9: Progressive Density 40x50 (2500m SCM)

**Date**: 10/25/23 | **Location**: Bayside | **Focus**: Speed endurance, progressive density

**Structure**:
- **Warmup**: 400 choice
- **Main Set**: 40x50 @ 1:00 (2000m)
  - **First 16**: Every 4th is FAST (4 fast out of 16)
  - **Next 12**: Every 3rd is FAST (4 fast out of 12)
  - **Next 8**: Every other is FAST (4 fast out of 8)
  - **Final 4**: ALL FAST (4 fast out of 4)
  - **Total FAST 50s**: 16 out of 40
- **Cool**: 100 EZ

**Key Features**:
- Progressive density of speed work
- Mental game: "only X more fast ones"
- Constant interval (1:00) = increasing fatigue
- Final block = pure grit (all 4 fast when exhausted)

**Use When**:
- Building speed endurance
- Mental toughness training
- Race simulation (holding speed when tired)
- Late season preparation

**Coaching Notes**:
- "FAST" means race pace effort, not all-out sprint
- Recovery 50s should be truly easy (don't let athletes cruise fast)
- Final 4 = test of will and conditioning
- Can adjust interval based on ability (:50/:55/1:00/1:05)

**Variations**:
- Use different strokes for fast 50s
- Make every 4th a different stroke
- Add fins for more speed
- Increase total to 50 or reduce to 30

## Workout #8: Ascending Ladder (2500m SCM)

**Date**: 10/23/23 | **Location**: Bayside | **Focus**: Aerobic threshold, pacing

**Structure**:
- **Warmup**: 300 choice
- **Pre-set**: 8x75 @ 1:30 (25 Drill/Drill/Build) (600m)
- **Main Set**: 2 rounds through:
  - 50 @ 1:00
  - 100 @ 2:00
  - 150 @ 3:00
  - 200 @ 4:00
  - 250 @ 5:00
  - **All FAST, All Choice** (1200m total)
- **Cool**: 100 EZ

**Key Features**:
- Ascending distances with proportional rest
- Constant pace challenge (can you hold your 50 pace for 250?)
- Choice of stroke = find what works when tired
- Two rounds = volume + pacing practice

**Use When**:
- Building aerobic capacity
- Threshold training
- Stroke versatility
- Testing pace sustainability

**Coaching Notes**:
- "All FAST" = threshold pace, sustainable but challenging
- Watch splits: should be relatively consistent per 50
- Stroke choice often changes round 1 → round 2 (fatigue)
- Can modify: all free, or assign stroke order

## Workout #11: Threshold Intervals - Hold Best (2450m SCM)

**Date**: 10/30/23 | **Location**: Bayside | **Focus**: Threshold pace, lactate tolerance

**Structure**:
- **Warmup**: 400 ROIM / 8x25 drill variations (600m)
- **Pre-set**: 3 rounds of 25 Scull / 50 DP-Tarzan / 75 Free @ intervals (450m)
- **Main Set**: 4 rounds of:
  - 3x100 Freestyle **Hold the Fastest Interval Possible**
  - Interval options: 1:15 / 1:20 / 1:25 / 1:30 / 1:35 / 1:40 / 1:45 / 1:50
  - **Goal**: Pick interval, hold all 3 at threshold pace
  - (1200m main)
- **Cool**: 100 EZ

**Key Features**:
- Self-selected interval = athlete ownership
- Must hold all 3 at same pace (no dying)
- 4 rounds = 12 total threshold 100s
- Challenge: Can you go faster interval in later rounds?

**Use When**:
- Threshold development
- Teaching pace judgment
- Building aerobic power
- Self-awareness training

**Coaching Notes**:
- First round: Find sustainable interval
- Rounds 2-3: Try to maintain or improve
- Round 4: All-out effort to hold fastest interval
- Track best interval round to round for progress

**Variations**:
- Assign specific interval for whole team
- Make it 4x100 instead of 3x100
- Add stroke requirement
- Incorporate negative split requirement

## Workout #14: Fast/Easy Contrast (2200m SCM)

**Date**: 11/08/23 | **Location**: Bayside | **Focus**: Speed contrast, recovery management

**Structure**:
- **Warmup**: 200 Swim / 200 Kick / 200 IM Drill (600m)
- **Pre-set**: 4x150 @ 3:00 (25 Build FAST / 50 Flutter Kick / 75 Free) (600m)
- **Main Set**: 3 rounds of:
  - 3x50 (FAST / EZ / FAST) @ 1:30
  - 6x25 (EZ / FAST) @ :35 / :30 / :25 (progressively tighter)
  - (900m main)
- **Cool**: 100 EZ

**Key Features**:
- Contrast training: fast then easy in same set
- Progressive interval tightening on 25s
- Teaches body to recover while still moving
- Quality speed work with built-in recovery

**Use When**:
- Speed development
- Recovery swimming skills
- Pace contrast training
- Peak season sharpening

**Coaching Notes**:
- "FAST" = sprint effort, not threshold
- "EZ" = truly easy, shake out lactic acid
- Interval tightening on 25s = less rest, same speed
- Final 25s @ :25 = minimal rest, test of recovery

## Common Themes in Progressive Sets

### Structure Patterns

1. **Pyramid** (up then down)
2. **Ladder** (ascending or descending only)
3. **Density** (increasing frequency of fast)
4. **Contrast** (fast/easy alternating)
5. **Volume** (progressive distance)

### Interval Strategies

- **Proportional rest**: Distance doubles, rest doubles (100@2:00, 200@4:00)
- **Constant interval**: Same rest regardless of distance
- **Tightening interval**: Progressively less rest
- **Self-selected**: Athlete chooses sustainable interval

### Speed Definitions

- **FAST** = Race pace threshold effort (sustainable)
- **MAX/BEST** = All-out sprint effort
- **EZ** = True recovery pace
- **Aerobic** = Sustainable training pace below threshold

### Progression Methods

**By Volume**:
- 100 → 200 → 300 → 400 (ascending)
- 400 → 300 → 200 → 100 (descending)
- 100 → 200 → 300 → 200 → 100 (pyramid)

**By Density**:
- Every 4th fast → every 3rd → every 2nd → all
- 1 fast per round → 2 → 3 → 4

**By Rest**:
- :45 → :40 → :35 → :30 (tightening)
- Full recovery → moderate → short (fatigue training)

## Programming Notes

**Early Season**: Use longer rest, lower density, focus on technique
**Mid-Season**: Moderate rest, building density, maintaining technique under fatigue
**Late Season**: Shorter rest, high density, race simulation

**Recovery Indicators**:
- Can hold times round to round
- Technique stays solid
- Splits remain consistent
- Athlete feels challenged but not destroyed

**Red Flags**:
- Times falling apart mid-set
- Technique breakdown
- Unable to complete prescribed work
- Excessive soreness next day

## Adaptations by Ability Level

### Beginner Masters
- Longer rest ratios
- Fewer total reps
- More recovery swim
- Focus on finishing

### Intermediate Masters
- Standard rest as written
- Full volume
- Some stroke variety
- Building speed endurance

### Advanced Masters
- Tighter rest ratios
- Add volume (extra rounds)
- Stroke-specific work
- Racing simulation

## Sample Week Structure

**Monday**: Aerobic base (Workout #15 style)
**Wednesday**: Threshold intervals (Workout #11 style)
**Friday**: Speed/density (Workout #9 style)
**Saturday**: Volume pyramid (Workout #3 style)

**Total weekly meters**: ~8000m
**Quality sessions**: 2-3
**Recovery sessions**: 1-2

## Key Takeaways

1. **Progressive sets teach pacing** under increasing fatigue
2. **Density training** builds speed endurance
3. **Pyramid sets** develop both pacing and mental toughness
4. **Contrast work** teaches recovery while moving
5. **Self-selected intervals** build athlete ownership

**Remember**: The point isn't to survive the set. It's to execute the set with purpose, pace awareness, and technical excellence.
